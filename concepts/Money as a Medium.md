# Money as a Medium

## Brief Definition

McLuhan's reading of money as an extension of man and a storehouse, translator, and amplifier of communally achieved work and skill — a medium with a "commodity and community character" whose history runs from commodity money through metallic coin and paper/representative money to the credit card, where electric speed obsolesces money into pure information.

## Longer Explanation

For McLuhan money is not chiefly an economic instrument but a medium — a specialization of his master frame, [[Media as Extensions of Man]]. He sets aside the psychoanalytic "filthy lucre" tradition (Ferenczi, Freud) precisely because it "does not correspond sufficiently to the nature and function of money in society," and instead treats money as a member of the same family as speech, writing, the clock, and number: an outering of human faculties that reorganizes the social and sensory field. Its defining property is that it stores and carries work: "Like words and language, money is a storehouse of communally achieved work, skill, and experience" (Directly stated). Cut off from the community that gives it meaning, money is nothing — the lesson Robinson Crusoe learns at the wreck — for "Money always retains something of its commodity and community character" (Directly stated).

McLuhan tracks money through a developmental arc that doubles as a media history, and this note is the anchor for that whole arc; its successive stages are folded in here rather than minted as separate nodes. (1) **Commodity money** begins in nonliterate cultures as a staple — whales' teeth, rats, tobacco, cigarettes — that is "as much media of communication as they are commodities," a storehouse of magical power and "communal participation." (2) The growth of **currency** (metallic coin) is the move from grasping the nearest staple to "letting go" of it in order "to extend trading to the whole social complex" — the same passage from reflexive grasp to voluntary release that McLuhan finds in the child's acquisition of speech. (3) **Paper, or representative money**, is money's print-age abstraction: "Money, as a social means of extending and amplifying work and skill in an easily accessible and portable form, lost much of its magical power with the coming of representative money, or Paper money. Just as speech lost its magic with writing, and further with printing, when printed money supplanted gold the compelling aura of it disappeared." McLuhan reads representative money as "an adjunct of that specialist alphabetic technology," reducing moral values in the nineteenth century just as the alphabet reduced oral cultures to "Roman uniformity." (4) Finally the **credit card** is the electric reversal of money toward information (see below). (Interpretive synthesis: the four stages are McLuhan's, drawn out in sequence; the periodization is his.)

The specialist argument is central and links money tightly to literacy and print. "Money, like writing, has the power to specialize and to rechannel human energies and to separate functions, just as it translates and reduces one kind of work to another." Money is "a specialist technology like writing"; as writing intensifies the visual, "so money separates work from the other social functions," becoming "a language for translating the work of the farmer into the work of the barber, doctor, engineer, or plumber." McLuhan makes the dependency on print-literacy explicit: "our Western use of money as store and translator of communal work and skill has depended upon long accustomation to the written word," and a "fixed-price system" with uniform commodities "does not become possible until printing prepares the ground" — which is why "backward" countries, lacking print's "psychological conditioning in the ways of uniformity and repeatability," are slow to reach economic "takeoff" (Directly stated). Money is thus a flagship case of the visual, fragmenting, specializing logic the vault tracks through [[The Phonetic Alphabet]] and [[Specialism as Counter-Irritant]].

In the electric age this whole apparatus reverses. As work is replaced "by the sheer movement of information, money as a store of work merges with the informational forms of credit and credit card." McLuhan states the trajectory as a single steady progression: "From coin to paper currency, and from currency to credit card there is a steady progression toward commercial exchange as the movement of information itself." The credit card is money's electric reversal — the case where the overheated specialist medium flips back toward its tribal opposite: "This trend toward an inclusive information is the kind of image represented by the credit card, and approaches once more the character of tribal money. For tribal society, not knowing the specialisms of job or of work, does not specialize money either." This is a flagship instance of [[Reversal of the Overheated Medium]]: print-money, intensified, retracts past the break boundary into "inclusive" information, and "electric technology puts the very concept of money in jeopardy" (Directly stated / Strongly implied for the reversal framing).

Finally, money exemplifies McLuhan's "Midas touch" restatement of the law that every medium remakes the field around it. "The classic curse of Midas, his power of translating all he touched into gold, is in some degree the character of any medium, including language." Money turns all it touches into a common denominator, and "All technology has the Midas touch. When a community develops some extension of itself, it tends to allow all other functions to be altered to accommodate that form." Money is therefore "not a closed system, and does not have its meaning alone" (Keynes's "money as a medium") — a translator and amplifier that, like all extensions of man, exists "by virtue of the shared knowledge and skill of a community" (Directly stated).

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Exponential vs Linear]] — Kurzweil reads economic/technological growth as smooth compounding expansion; McLuhan's account insists that money intensified to its print-specialist extreme *reverses* into its inclusive tribal opposite rather than continuing linearly. `Interpretive synthesis`

## Questions It Raises

- If the credit card is money "approaching once more the character of tribal money," what does a *further* electric/digital intensification (electronic funds, programmable and crypto-money) reverse money into?
- McLuhan ties money's store-of-work function to print-literacy and visual uniformity; if that literate substrate erodes in the electric/digital order, does money cease to be a "store of work" and become only "the movement of information"?
- Is money's "commodity and community character" preserved or abolished when "electric technology puts the very concept of money in jeopardy"?
- Does reading money as a *medium* (a storehouse and translator) displace or merely supplement its standard economic reading as a store of value and means of exchange?
