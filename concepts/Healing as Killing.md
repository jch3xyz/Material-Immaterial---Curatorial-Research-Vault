# Healing as Killing

## Brief Definition

Han's titular paradox: "healing" — the magic word of American self-help — names a boundless self-optimization that, by coinciding point-for-point with the optimization of the system, destroys the soul, so that the regime's promised healing is in fact killing.

## Longer Explanation

In contemporary American self-help literature the operative word is *healing*: a therapeutic vocabulary that promises to eliminate "any and all functional weakness or mental obstacle in the name of efficiency and performance." Han argues that this healing is a ruse. The self-optimization it prescribes does not serve the good life; it serves "perfect functioning within the system," tracking the logic of the market in which "everything is made comparable and measurable." Because perpetual self-optimization "coincides point-for-point with the optimization of the system," it is "proving destructive. It is leading to mental collapse." The endpoint is not wellness but exhaustion — `Directly stated`: "Healing, it turns out, means killing."

This destruction is the operation of [[Auto-Exploitation]]: self-optimization "amounts to total self-exploitation." The subject who hunts down its own negative thoughts as a Protestant once hunted sins becomes, in Han's phrase, an ego that "grapples with itself as an enemy." The mechanism therefore does not require an external coercer; the neoliberal subject runs aground "on the imperative of self-optimization, that is, on the compulsion always to achieve more and more" — the auto-exploiting structure of the [[Achievement Society]]. Han's diagnosis here supplies the destructive terminus that motivates his later turn "beyond the subject": if the optimizing self is killing itself, the way out cannot be a better-managed self. `Strongly implied`.

Han periodizes this as a new historical phase. Where sovereignty was "the age of levying and conscription" and disciplinary society "the age of active and industrial added-value," financial capitalism destroys value "at the root," so that "the neoliberal regime is in the course of inaugurating the age of exhaustion." In this age, "the psyche itself is being exploited," and "psychic maladies such as depression and burnout define our times" — `Directly stated`. The chapter thus names exhaustion, depression, and burnout as the signature maladies of psychopolitics, the symptomatic correlates of healing-as-killing.

The deeper claim is that the damage flows from an excess of [[Positivity]], not from repression. "But the violence of positivity is just as destructive as the violence of negativity," Han writes; neoliberal psychopolitics is "destroying the human soul, which is anything but a machine of positivity (Positivmaschine)." Because "without negativity, life degrades into 'something dead'" and pain "is constitutive for experience (Erfahrung)," a life "wholly of positive emotions and the sensation of 'flow'" is "not human at all." The cruelty of the regime is that it can even exploit pain — "the only pain that is tolerated is pain that can be exploited for the purposes of optimization." Healing-as-killing is therefore the lethal form of pure positivity: it eliminates the [[Negativity]] on which the soul's "defining tautness and depth" depend, and in smoothing the self into a frictionless instrument of performance, kills it. `Interpretive synthesis`.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — In *Psychopolitics* Ch.6, "healing" is the euphemism of American self-help culture for boundless self-optimization; because that optimization serves systemic functioning rather than the good life, it culminates in mental collapse, so "healing, it turns out, means killing." The concept carries Han's periodization of "the age of exhaustion" (depression and burnout as defining maladies) and his thesis that "the violence of positivity is just as destructive as the violence of negativity."

## Related Concepts

- [[Auto-Exploitation]] — the mechanism of healing-as-killing: self-optimization "amounts to total self-exploitation"; the optimizing subject exploits and destroys itself with no external coercer.
- [[Achievement Society]] — the social form whose "compulsion always to achieve more and more" is what the subject runs aground on; healing-as-killing is its self-help theodicy.
- [[Positivity]] — the regime of pure positivity whose violence is "just as destructive as the violence of negativity"; the soul "is anything but a machine of positivity."
- [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]] — pain "is constitutive for experience (Erfahrung)"; eliminating negativity hollows out genuine experience and leaves only the dead positivity of "flow."

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Negativity]] — what healing-as-killing eradicates; for Han "negativity is what keeps life alive," and its therapeutic elimination is precisely the killing.

## Questions It Raises

- If the harm comes from an excess of positivity rather than repression, what would a non-killing form of care or "healing" even look like — and can the language of healing be reclaimed, or must it be abandoned?
- Han stages this as the destructive endpoint that motivates a turn "beyond the subject." Does locating the cure outside subjectivity risk surrendering the very agency that resistance would require?
- Is "the age of exhaustion" a genuine historical break from disciplinary society, or a continuation of biopolitical management by other (psychic) means?
- For a curatorial and art-making practice: how does the demand that even pain be "exploited for the purposes of optimization" bear on work that stakes itself on negativity, friction, and non-productive experience?
