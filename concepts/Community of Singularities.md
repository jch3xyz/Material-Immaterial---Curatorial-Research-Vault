# Community of Singularities

## Brief Definition

The affirmative social form founded by [[Fundamental Tiredness|fundamental tiredness]] — a community that "requires neither belonging nor relation [Verwandtschaft]," whose members are held together not by kinship or function but by a shared rhythm and a "friendly *and*," in a "between" of friendliness-as-indifference "where 'no one and nothing dominates or commands.'"

## Longer Explanation

For [[Byung-Chul Han]], the *community of singularities* is the positive counter-image that the closing pages of *The Burnout Society* set against the isolating, exhausted solitude of the [[Achievement Society|achievement society]]. Reading Peter Handke's "Essay on Tiredness," Han distinguishes the divisive, world-destroying "I-tiredness" — "solitary tiredness" that "destroys all that is common or shared, all proximity, and even language itself" — from a "fundamental tiredness" or "we-tiredness" that "opens a between by loosening the strictures of the ego." It is this second, inspiring tiredness, not work or shared identity, that grounds the community. As the I grows smaller, "the gravity of being shifts from the ego to the world," and the human being and things "show themselves to be connected through a friendly *and*" (`Directly stated`).

The community's distinguishing trait is that it demands nothing of its members — no shared substance, no belonging, no relation. Han is explicit: this tiredness "makes it possible to conceive of a community that requires neither belonging nor relation [Verwandtschaft]." Its members are *singularities*: each remains itself, undissolved, yet all are co-present. Han's emblem for this "all in one" is the seventeenth-century Dutch floral still life, "in which a beetle, a snail, a bee, or a butterfly sits true to life, in the flowers, and although none of these may suspect the presence of others, they are all there together at the moment." No member is subordinated to a whole; togetherness is achieved without unity, proximity without fusion (`Directly stated`).

What binds the community is not a bond at all in the usual sense but a *rhythm* or *keynote* [Takt]. Han writes that here "a particular rhythm [Takt] emerges that leads to agreement [Zusammenstimmung], proximity, and vicinity [Nachbarschaft] without familial or functional connections," and he cites Handke's image of the tired man as "a new Orpheus" around whom "the wildest beasts gather" — "Tiredness gives dispersed individuals the keynote." The mode of relation is friendliness-as-indifference: the "between" is "a space of friendliness-as-indifference, where 'no one and nothing dominates or commands,'" a "particular in-difference" that "lends them an aura of friendliness" by suspending "rigid delimitation with respect to one's surroundings" (`Directly stated`). The eschatological figure for this society to come is the "'Pentecostal company' that inspires not-doing" — pictured as "tired to a man" — which "stands opposed to the society of activity" (`Directly stated`).

The concept is the redemptive inverse of the [[Hell of the Same]]. Where the hell of the same is a regime of indistinction, accumulation, and the expelled Other, the community of singularities preserves difference (each thing "never manifested alone but always in conjunction with other things") precisely *because* it neither dominates nor demands sameness. Its "in-difference" is the opposite of the hell's deadening uniformity: it is the friendly non-coercion that lets singular things be together as singulars (`Interpretive synthesis`).

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — Develops the concept in the final chapter of *The Burnout Society* by reading Handke's "Essay on Tiredness." The community of singularities is the social yield of [[Fundamental Tiredness|fundamental tiredness]] / "we-tiredness": a non-coercive "between" requiring neither belonging nor relation, attuned by rhythm and the "friendly *and*," and figured by the Dutch still life and the "Pentecostal company." It is Han's positive answer to the [[Achievement Society|achievement society]]'s solitary exhaustion and the counter-image to the [[Hell of the Same]].
- **Peter Handke** *(via Han)* — The source of the phrase "community of singularities," of "we-tiredness," the "all in one," the Dutch still life, and the "Pentecostal company." Han adopts and recasts Handke's "immanent religion of tiredness" as a curatorial-philosophical model of community.

## Related Concepts

- [[Fundamental Tiredness]] — the founding condition: the "we-tiredness" / "fundamental tiredness" whose social form *is* the community of singularities; the two notes are two faces of one passage.
- [[Pathos of Distance]] — kin in mood: the community's "friendliness-as-indifference" and the suspension of "rigid delimitation" describe a non-dominating distance between singulars rather than fusion or familiarity.
- [[Negativity]] — the community belongs to "negative potency," the capacity of "not-to" (the Sabbath, not-doing), against the achievement society's exhausting excess of [[Positivity]].
- [[Achievement Society]] — the diagnostic background the community redeems: against "solitary tiredness," the "separating and isolating" effect of achievement.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Hell of the Same]] — the load-bearing contrast: Han's positive counter-image to the toothless, indistinct Same. The community of singularities lets difference stand because "no one and nothing dominates or commands"; the hell of the same erases it.
- [[Positivity]] — the "too-much of positivity" that generates exhaustion and solitary tiredness, against the community's "negative potency" of not-doing.

## Questions It Raises

- Can a community that "requires neither belonging nor relation" still be *organized* or curated, or is any deliberate gathering already a relapse into function and belonging?
- Is friendliness-as-indifference an ethics or only a mood — does it obligate, or merely permit co-presence?
- How does a rhythm or "keynote" [Takt] do the binding work usually assigned to identity, contract, or kinship — and can it survive the acceleration it is meant to interrupt?
- If the community is reachable only through tiredness and not-doing, is it structurally unavailable to the very achievement-subjects who most need it?
