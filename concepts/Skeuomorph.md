# Skeuomorph

## Brief Definition

A skeuomorph is a design feature that is no longer functional in itself but that refers back to a feature that was once functional; in Hayles's hands it becomes a transferable analytical tool for naming the Janus-faced transitional object that smooths the passage from one [[Conceptual Constellation]] to the next.

## Longer Explanation

Hayles borrows the skeuomorph from archaeological anthropology, where it names a vestigial ornament — the molded vinyl "stitching" on her Toyota Camry dashboard that alludes back to a fabric once actually stitched but is now formed by an injection mold. The feature does no work; it survives only as a citation of the work it used to do. Crucially, Hayles treats this not as a curiosity but as a method-concept: skeuomorphs "visibly testify to the social or psychological necessity for innovation to be tempered by replication," so deeply characteristic of how concepts and artifacts evolve that "it takes a great deal of conscious effort to avoid them." This is why the term belongs to her larger toolkit alongside [[Seriation]] (the overall waxing-and-waning *pattern* of change) and the [[Conceptual Constellation]] (the coherent *cluster* of mutually entailing ideas): the skeuomorph is the specific transitional *object* that lets one constellation hand off to another without a clean break.

The skeuomorph is Janus-faced. "Like a Janus figure, the skeuomorph looks to past and future, simultaneously reinforcing and undermining both." It works a psychodynamic that "finds the new more acceptable when it recalls the old that it is in the process of displacing and finds the traditional more comfortable when it is presented in a context that reminds us we can escape from it into the new." Hayles illustrates this with the "Catholic Turing Test" installation at SIGGRAPH '93 — a confession booth, kneeling bench and all, run by a machine algorithm — where the residual religious form both authenticated and was overturned by its computational successor. The skeuomorph thus does double conceptual labor: it authenticates the new by gesturing at the familiar, and it exerts an "inertial pull" that limits how radically the new can depart.

Within the history of [[Cybernetics]], Hayles uses the concept to explain how the field changed without Kuhnian rupture or Foucauldian epistemic break. "In the history of cybernetics, skeuomorphs acted as threshold devices, smoothing the transition between one conceptual constellation and another." Her central example is [[Homeostasis]]: a foundational premise of [[The Three Waves of Cybernetics|first-wave cybernetics]], by about 1960 it "had ceased to be an initiating premise" and instead "performed the work of a gesture or an allusion used to authenticate new elements in the emerging constellation of reflexivity." Reflexivity in turn becomes a skeuomorph in the third wave, "displaced ... by emergence" yet "lingering on as an allusion that authenticates new elements" and leaving "its imprint on the new constellation of virtuality." Each dominant concept, having lost its operative function, persists as a threshold device for the constellation that supplants it.

For the curatorial research foci, the skeuomorph is a sharp diagnostic for how the dematerialized, informational concept of the [[The Body as Data|body as data]] could be naturalized: the new disembodied paradigm becomes acceptable precisely by retaining residual, no-longer-functional gestures toward the embodied past it is in the process of erasing. The skeuomorph is, in this sense, the formal mechanism behind the larger movement of [[Posthumanism]] that Hayles tracks — the soft handoff that lets information come to seem more real than the materiality it interpenetrates.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[N. Katherine Hayles]]** — appropriates the term from archaeological anthropology and elevates it into a transferable historiographic device. For Hayles a skeuomorph is a no-longer-functional feature that alludes to a once-functional one and that, as a "threshold device," smooths conceptual transitions across [[The Three Waves of Cybernetics]] (homeostasis → reflexivity → virtuality). It is distinct from the *pattern* ([[Seriation]]) and the *cluster* ([[Conceptual Constellation]]).

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Emergence]] — not an opposite in kind but the displacing concept that turns reflexivity into a skeuomorph in the third wave; the relation dramatizes how a once-central idea becomes the vestigial allusion authenticating its own successor.
- Kuhnian paradigm shift / Foucauldian epistemic break — the models of *clean rupture* that the skeuomorph explicitly contradicts; Hayles's threshold devices describe continuity-within-change rather than incommensurable breaks.

## Questions It Raises

- If the dematerialized, informational [[The Body as Data|body-as-data]] was made acceptable by skeuomorphic gestures back toward [[Embodiment|embodiment]], which residual "stitching" remains in current discourse — and what once-functional embodied feature is it citing while erasing?
- Does the skeuomorph cut both ways in the [[Embodiment vs Information]] tension: can materiality itself persist *as* a skeuomorph within informatic paradigms, a no-longer-load-bearing allusion to the body?
- Is [[Posthumanism]] best understood not as a rupture from the [[The Liberal Humanist Subject|liberal humanist subject]] but as a seriated transition in which humanist assumptions linger on skeuomorphically, exerting an "inertial pull" on the new constellation?
- The Janus structure "simultaneously reinforcing and undermining" both past and future — does this make the skeuomorph a site of curatorial leverage, where the [[Material vs Immaterial]] handoff can be exhibited and contested rather than naturalized?
- Can the skeuomorph travel beyond Hayles's own corpus as a general tool for reading how new media authenticate themselves by simulating obsolete material forms (the "almost as many skeuomorphs as morphs" she finds at SIGGRAPH)?
