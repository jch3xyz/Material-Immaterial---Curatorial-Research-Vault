# Technoetics

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's name for the convergence of technology and consciousness — *techne* + *noetic* — the emergent culture in which the strands of art grounded in technology and scientific metaphor intertwine with the strands dedicated to the spiritual and the conceptual, so that mind and consciousness move to the foreground of art and science alike.

## Longer Explanation

Technoetics is the keystone term of Ascott's late turn. He builds it by fusing two Greek roots — *techne* (craft, technology, making) and *noetic* (pertaining to *nous*, mind, consciousness) — and reads the whole twentieth century as a preparation for their union. Surveying "the strands of art based in technology and scientific metaphor, intertwining with the strands of art dedicated to the spiritual and to the conceptual," he sees these two trajectories "preparing us for the emergence of what I call technoetic culture (techne + noetic, as I just defined it, concerning mind and consciousness)" (*The Mind of the Museum*) [`Directly stated`]. The decisive claim is a reversal of the usual story about technology and the spirit: for Ascott "it is now technology and science themselves that are foregrounding issues of consciousness, mind, and spirit" — the instruments once thought to disenchant the world have become the very means of its [[Re-enchantment]] [`Directly stated`].

For Ascott the technoetic is not only a culture but an *aesthetic* and a *principle*. He names "a technoetic aesthetic, so named because I believe we need to recognize that technology plus mind not only enables us to explore consciousness more thoroughly but may lead to distinctly new forms of art, new qualities of mind, new forms of cognition and perception" (*Weaving the Shamantic Web*) [`Directly stated`]. Consciousness becomes "both the context and content of art, and the object and subject of study"; the artist's task shifts from the production of images to the construction and navigation of mind. He is emphatic that this is the destiny of the discipline: "the technoetic principle will be at the centre of art as it develops, and consciousness in all its forms will be the field of its unfolding" [`Directly stated`].

The technoetic is the connective tissue of Ascott's late cluster of coinages. It is enabled by [[Cyberception]] — "the technologically amplified faculties of perception and cognition" — which lets mind extend into the network; he triangulates "technoetics (everything that concerns cyberception and the technology of consciousness)" with artificial life and intelligent architecture as the three coordinates of millennial culture [`Directly stated`]. It is the affective and connective condition that [[Telenoia]] celebrates (networked consciousness against industrial paranoia), and it converges with [[Network Consciousness]], the collective "hypercortex" Ascott sees emerging in the Net. In *Art @ the Edge of the NET* he restates the term through a second root: "This burgeoning awareness is technoetic: techne and gnōsis combined into a new knowledge of the world, a connective mind that is spawning new realities and new definitions of life and human identity" — binding the technological to *gnosis*, esoteric knowing, and locating the synthesis in the [[Moistmedia]] interspace where the dry world of virtuality and the wet world of biology meet [`Directly stated`].

The mystical register is deliberate and structural, not ornamental. In his glossary *Technoetic Aesthetics: 100 Terms and Definitions for the Post-Biological Era* Ascott coins the cognate **technostic** — "Having esoteric, occult, spiritual knowledge revealed through technology" — making explicit that the technoetic culture is one in which "the perennial wisdom of shamans and gnostics" is rejoined with the insights of scientists and engineers [`Directly stated`]. This is why the shaman, the *ayahuasca* vine, and "double consciousness" sit beside nanotechnology and artificial neural networks in Ascott's writing: the technoetic names the single field in which technological and ritualistic methods of expanding consciousness are treated as complementary [`Strongly implied`].

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — the originator; *techne* + *noetic* (and, equivalently, *techne* + *gnōsis*), the foregrounding of mind and consciousness as the context and content of art in the post-biological, telematic era. The "technoetic principle" and "technoetic aesthetic" are his terms; "technostic" is his cognate for esoteric knowledge revealed through technology.
- **Edward Shanken (editor)** — in his introduction frames technoetics as the late phase of Ascott's career, "the fusion of technology and consciousness," and underscores that "the artist's concept of 'technoetics' again acknowledges the complementarity of technological and ritualistic methods for expanding consciousness and creating meaning."

## Related Concepts

- [[Telenoia]] — the affective, connective state of networked consciousness that the technoetic culture celebrates in place of industrial paranoia.
- [[Moistmedia]] — the interspace of bits, atoms, neurons and genes (dry/wet) in which the technoetic synthesis is materially embodied.
- [[Network Consciousness]] — the collective mind / "hypercortex" the technoetic Net is taken to generate.

## Opposing Concepts

- The classical image-based aesthetic — Ascott declares "the 'game of images is over'"; the technoetic aesthetic of "creative consciousness and artificial life" supplants "the classical concern with the surface image of the world."
- [[Disenchantment]] — technoetics is precisely the refusal of the thesis that technology empties the world of mind and spirit.

## Questions It Raises

- If technology "foregrounds" consciousness, does the technoetic dissolve the distinction between instrument and spirit, or smuggle a mysticism into engineering under cover of a neologism?
- Can the perennial wisdom of "shamans and gnostics" be joined to nanotechnology and neural networks without either being reduced to metaphor for the other?
- Does the technoetic centring of consciousness as "the context and content of art" leave any role for the material art object, or only for systems of mind?
- Whose consciousness does the "hypercortex" instantiate — and does networked technoetic culture extend mind, or merely model it?
