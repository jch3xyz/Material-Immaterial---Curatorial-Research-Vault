# Homo Deus

> [!note] Disambiguation
> This is the **concept** note — the upgraded human-as-god, *Homo deus*, the projected successor to *Homo sapiens*. For the orientation note on the book in which Harari develops it, see [[books/Homo Deus|the book note]] (`books/Homo Deus`, forward-stub).

## Brief Definition

*Homo deus* is Yuval Noah Harari's name for the upgraded human that *Homo sapiens* is on track to become once it acquires "divine powers of creation and destruction" — divinity understood not as omnipotence but as the possession of specific Greek- or Hindu-god super-abilities, reached via biological, cyborg, or non-organic engineering.

## Longer Explanation

For Harari, the upgrade into godhood is the third of three projects he forecasts for the twenty-first-century human agenda — after immortality and happiness — and he treats it as the one that "subsumes the first two." Having brought famine, plague and war under relative control, humankind's next targets are "likely to be immortality, happiness and divinity," and the divinity project is "to acquire for us divine powers of creation and destruction, and upgrade Homo sapiens into Homo deus." Pursuing bliss and immortality already entails this upgrade, because overcoming old age and misery first requires "godlike control of [our] own biological substratum," and that same control could then re-engineer the human system "in almost any manner we like." `Directly stated`

Crucially, Harari defines the *deus* of *Homo deus* against the omnipotent biblical creator. "Divinity isn't a vague metaphysical quality. And it isn't the same as omnipotence." The relevant model is "Greek gods or Hindu devas rather than the omnipotent biblical sky father" — beings who retain "their foibles, kinks and limitations, just as Zeus and Indra had theirs," but who "could love, hate, create and destroy on a much grander scale than us." Divinity here is the historical possession of "specific super-abilities" — designing and creating living beings, transforming one's own body, controlling the environment, reading minds and communicating at a distance, travelling at high speeds, and escaping death — abilities humans are "in the business of acquiring." `Directly stated`

Harari names three engineering paths to this upgrade. "The upgrading of humans into gods may follow any of three paths: biological engineering, cyborg engineering and the engineering of non-organic beings." Biological engineering rewrites the Sapiens genome, brain circuits and biochemistry to "create new godlings"; cyborg engineering merges the organic body with bionic and nano-robotic devices, freeing it from the constraint that all parts be in direct contact; and the engineering of non-organic beings "dispenses with organic parts altogether," replacing neural networks with intelligent software. `Directly stated`

The upgrade, in Harari's account, is not a Hollywood robot apocalypse but a gradual, mundane slide: *Homo sapiens* "is likely to upgrade itself step by step, merging with robots and computers," through "innumerable mundane actions" — granting a smartphone more control, trying a more effective drug — until "they will no longer be human." Harari frames this as the logical conclusion of three centuries of humanism, "the worship of humankind," even as it "exposes humanism's inherent flaws," since the same technologies that could upgrade humans into gods "might also make humans irrelevant." `Strongly implied`

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Yuval Noah Harari]]** — coins *Homo deus* as the projected upgraded successor to *Homo sapiens*; divinity is the Greek-/Hindu-god possession of specific super-abilities, reached by biological, cyborg, or non-organic engineering, and the upgrade is the third (and subsuming) project of the new human agenda.
- **[[Meghan O'Gieblyn]]** — does not use the term *Homo deus*, but treats the same horizon under [[Transhumanism]], [[Mind Uploading]] and [[Patternism]]: the drive to escape the body and become a higher, often digital, kind of being. `Interpretive synthesis`

## Related Concepts

- [[Transhumanism]] — the movement/ideology whose end-state Harari's *Homo deus* names; the upgraded god-human is the transhumanist telos rendered as a historical forecast.
- [[Techno-Humanism]] — Harari's own later term in *Homo Deus* for the humanist branch that seeks to upgrade the human mind itself; *Homo deus* is the figure that branch aims to produce. *(forward-stub — realized when later chapters are ingested)*
- [[Posthumanism]] — the broader frame in which "the human" is displaced or succeeded; Harari's upgrade ends the reign of *Homo sapiens*.
- [[Mind Uploading]] — one concrete route toward the non-organic path Harari describes.
- [[The Cyborg]] — the figure of Harari's second (cyborg-engineering) path.
- [[Embodiment]] — at stake throughout: the biological and cyborg paths re-engineer the body, the non-organic path abandons it.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[The Liberal Humanist Subject]] — the stable, self-identical human that Harari's upgrade dissolves: once minds are re-engineered, "Homo sapiens will disappear, human history will come to an end."

## Questions It Raises

- If divinity is defined as the possession of specific super-abilities rather than omnipotence, what counts as the threshold between an enhanced *Homo sapiens* and a genuine *Homo deus*?
- Harari insists "no clear line separates healing from upgrading" — does that make the slide to *Homo deus* a matter of collective choice or of structural inevitability?
- The three paths (biological, cyborg, non-organic) imply very different relations to the body. Does *Homo deus* require embodiment at all, or does the non-organic path describe a successor that is no longer "human" in any continuous sense?
- If the upgrade "takes the long-standing humanist ideals to their logical conclusion" while exposing humanism's flaws, is *Homo deus* the fulfilment of humanism or its self-cancellation?
