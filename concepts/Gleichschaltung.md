# Gleichschaltung

## General Orientation

*Gleichschaltung* enters the vault through [[Byung-Chul Han]] in two registers, across two books, that should be kept distinct even as they name one operation. In *[[The Transparency Society]]* (ch. 1, [[Positivity]]) the term is invoked **politically**: transparency is named as the new word for *Gleichschaltung*, the "totalitarian trait" of a calibrated society that eliminates the Other to speed circulation. In *[[Psychopolitics]]* (ch. 11, "Big Data") the term is given its **statistical-genealogical root**: Han traces it back through [[Friedrich Nietzsche]]'s phrase *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden* ("increasing in becoming-equal") and the Romantic-era revolt against statistical normality, then carries it forward into Big Data as "hypertrophied sameness." The two senses are the same leveling read at different depths — the political diagnosis on top, the statistical-numerical mechanism underneath — and both converge on the figure of the [[Hell of the Same]]. (`Directly stated` that Han uses the term in both books; `Interpretive synthesis` that the two are layers of one operation.)

## Brief Definition

*Gleichschaltung* — forced synchronization or bringing-into-line — is the political-historical term [[Byung-Chul Han]] invokes to name the leveling, conformity-producing violence latent in compulsory [[Transparency]].

## Longer Explanation

Han imports the loaded German word *Gleichschaltung* — historically the term for the coordination or "bringing-into-line" of all institutions under a single political will — to expose the totalitarian shape he detects inside the seemingly benign demand for transparency. The move arrives at the climax of his opening account of [[Positivity]]: transparency, he argues, "stabilizes and speeds the system by eliminating the Other and the Alien," which "makes the society of transparency a calibrated society." It is at exactly this point that he names the "totalitarian trait" with a maxim set in quotation marks: "New word for Gleichschaltung: Transparency." (`Directly stated` that Han ties transparency to *Gleichschaltung* at the close of the chapter.)

The force of the term is that synchronization is not censorship from above but frictionlessness from within. Han's *Gleichschaltung* operates by subtraction: the calibrated society does not coerce conformity so much as eliminate whatever resists smooth circulation. "Communication reaches its maximum velocity where like responds to like, when a chain reaction of likeness occurs," and "the negativity of alterity and foreignness — in other words, the resistance of the Other — disturbs and delays the smooth communication of the Same." (`Directly stated`.) Read this way, *Gleichschaltung* is the totalitarian name for what Han elsewhere calls the [[Hell of the Same]]: a leveling that proceeds by equivalence rather than command, where "the society of transparency is an inferno of the same." (`Strongly implied` that *Gleichschaltung* and the inferno of the same name the same leveling operation under different registers — political versus economic-affective.) The argument is gathered in the vault as [[Transparency abolishes the Other and produces compulsive conformity]].

The violence here is anti-personal in a precise sense. Han writes that "compulsion for transparency flattens out the human being itself, making it a functional element within a system," and that "therein lies the violence of transparency." (`Directly stated`.) *Gleichschaltung* thus designates the conversion of singular persons into interchangeable, operational units — a flattening that, for Han, only machines can survive without injury ("Only machines are transparent"). One caution governs this note. The maxim "New word for Gleichschaltung: Transparency" appears in single quotation marks keyed to footnote 1 of the chapter and is given *without explicit in-text attribution to any named author*; the chapter's surrounding citations (Humboldt, Simmel, Schmitt) make Carl Schmitt a plausible but unconfirmed source. (`Interpretive synthesis`; this is an open review item.) The maxim should therefore be preserved verbatim, in quotation marks, and treated as an **unattributed epigraph pending endnote verification** — not as Han's own coinage and not as a confirmed quotation of a named thinker.

## Psychopolitics (Han) — transparency's compulsion to conform and Big-Data sameness

In *[[Psychopolitics]]* (ch. 11, "Big Data") Han re-deploys *Gleichschaltung* and, crucially, gives it a genealogy he withheld in *[[The Transparency Society]]*. The term's conceptual root is located in [[Friedrich Nietzsche]]'s abhorrence of statistical reason. Nietzsche, Han notes, held that "the figures of statistics prove only that man is a herd animal," and coined a phrase Han carries into the digital present: *die Menschen zunehmen im Gleichwerden*. Han glosses it as meaning "both that ‘people are increasingly growing equal’ and that ‘people batten [i.e., grow fat] as they become the sam e'." (`Directly stated` — the Nietzsche gloss is verbatim from the chapter, OCR quirks preserved.) This phrase is the conceptual seed of *Gleichschaltung*: leveling understood not first as a political coordination but as a numerical-statistical convergence toward the average, the probable, the herd.

The recurrence is explicit. Immediately after the Nietzsche passage Han writes: "Such Gleichschaltung also characterizes our contemporary society of transparency and information. When everything is made visible at once, deviations can scarcely occur. Transparency entails a compulsion to conform, which eliminates the Other, the Alien and the Deviant." (`Directly stated`.) This is the same operation named in *The Transparency Society* — the calibrated society that purges the Other to accelerate circulation (see [[Transparency abolishes the Other and produces compulsive conformity]]) — but here it is grounded in statistics rather than in the footnoted political maxim. Where the earlier book gave the political slogan ("New word for Gleichschaltung: Transparency"), *Psychopolitics* supplies the mechanism: total visibility leaves no room for deviation, so conformity follows not by decree but by the disappearance of any unobserved margin in which the singular could occur.

Big Data, for Han, intensifies this to a pathology: "Dataism is augmenting Zunehmen im Gleichwerden, or hypertrophied sameness." (`Directly stated`.) Because data-mining "calculates average values" and "has no access to what is unique," it is structurally incapable of the event, the improbable, the singular — "Big Data is wholly blind to the event." Read against the *Transparency Society* account, this confirms that *Gleichschaltung* and the [[Hell of the Same]] name one leveling in two idioms: the *Hell of the Same* is its affective-economic face (equivalence abolishing singularity), *Gleichschaltung* its political-statistical face (the herd produced by averaging). The counter-pole Han offers in the same book is the figure of the [[Idiotism|idiot]] — the one who refuses to be networked, calculated, and brought into line, and so preserves the heretical singularity that *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden* dissolves. (`Strongly implied` that the idiot is the structural opposite of the data-driven *Gleichschaltung*; the explicit treatment of the idiot is in ch. 13, not ch. 11.)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — uses *Gleichschaltung* diagnostically, not as a historical analogy to a specific regime but as a structural name for the "totalitarian trait" of compulsory transparency: a calibrated, synchronized society that levels singularity to accelerate the circulation of capital, communication, and information. In *[[Psychopolitics]]* (ch. 11) he supplies the term's statistical genealogy — [[Friedrich Nietzsche]]'s *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden* — and extends it to Big Data as "hypertrophied sameness." (`Directly stated` for Han's invocation of the term in both books.)
- **[[Friedrich Nietzsche]]** *(forward-stub; originating-thinker for the statistical root)* — the source, via *Psychopolitics* ch. 11, of the phrase *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden* ("increasing in becoming-equal") that Han treats as the conceptual root of *Gleichschaltung*. Nietzsche's revolt against statistical reason ("the figures of statistics prove only that man is a herd animal") furnishes the genealogy Han uses to ground the term in numerical averaging rather than political decree. (`Directly stated` that Han attributes the phrase to Nietzsche.)
- **[[Carl Schmitt]]** *(forward-stub; originating-thinker candidate)* — the chapter's nearest cited authority on the political register (the *arcanum*, "courage to secrecy"), which makes Schmitt a plausible source of the footnoted maxim. This attribution is **unverified**; the quotation is rendered here as an unattributed epigraph pending endnote confirmation. (`Speculative connection`.)

## Related Concepts

- [[Transparency]] — the demand whose latent totalitarian form *Gleichschaltung* names.
- [[Positivity]] — the regime of dismantled negativity that produces the synchronized, calibrated society.
- [[Hell of the Same]] — the affective-economic figure for the same leveling: equivalence abolishing singularity; in *Psychopolitics* ch. 11 it is identified with the statistical *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden*.
- [[Transparency abolishes the Other and produces compulsive conformity]] — the argument this concept underwrites.
- [[Idiotism]] — the counter-figure: the idiot's heretical singularity is what *Gleichschaltung* / *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden* dissolves (developed in *Psychopolitics* ch. 13).
- [[Friedrich Nietzsche]] — the originating thinker of *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden*, the statistical root Han assigns to the term.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Negativity]] — the resistance of the Other, distinction, and the secret that synchronization must purge; its dismantling is the precondition of *Gleichschaltung*.
- [[Pathos of Distance]] — the counter-virtue of preserved separation and singularity that *Gleichschaltung* collapses.

## Questions It Raises

- **Visibility vs understanding.** *Gleichschaltung* equates being-brought-into-line with being-made-visible. If total illumination synchronizes rather than clarifies, does compulsory visibility actively *obstruct* mutual understanding rather than enable it — and what curatorial forms could stage visibility without synchronization?
- **The body as data.** Han says transparency "flattens out the human being itself, making it a functional element within a system." When the body is rendered as data and "calibrated," is the embodied subject precisely what *Gleichschaltung* converts into an "operational element" — and is there a residue of embodiment that resists synchronization?
- **Systems and feedback.** *Gleichschaltung* describes a system that accelerates by ejecting friction ("a chain reaction of likeness"). Is this a runaway positive-feedback loop with no balancing term — and what would a *negativity* that re-introduces damping or resistance look like in systemic language?
- **Desire.** If synchronization eliminates the Other, and desire is classically directed at what is not-the-self, does *Gleichschaltung* extinguish desire by abolishing its object, leaving only the self-mirroring "like responds to like"?
- **Provenance and authority.** The maxim that anchors the term is unattributed in the chapter. How should a vault preserve the rhetorical force of an epigraph while flagging that its authority is unverified — and does the historical weight of *Gleichschaltung* survive being cited without a source?
