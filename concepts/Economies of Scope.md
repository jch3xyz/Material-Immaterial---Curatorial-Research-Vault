# Economies of Scope

## Brief Definition

For Shoshana Zuboff, economies of scope are surveillance capitalism's demand that behavioral surplus be not merely *vast* but *varied* — pursued along two dimensions, the *extension* of extraction across every corner of the real world and the *depth* of extraction into the intimate patterns of the self; necessary but insufficient for the highest-quality prediction products, they sit between economies of scale and economies of action in the supply logic of the prediction imperative.

## Longer Explanation

Economies of scope name the second term in [[Shoshana Zuboff]]'s triad of supply imperatives. Compelled to improve predictions, surveillance capitalists "had to widen and diversify their extraction architectures to accommodate new sources of surplus and new supply operations": "Economies of scale would still be vital, of course, but in this new phase, supply operations were enlarged and intensified to accommodate economies of scope and economies of action" (`Directly stated`, Ch.7). Where economies of scale demand *volume* — surplus must be vast — economies of scope demand *variation*: "behavioral surplus must be vast, but it must also be varied" (`Directly stated`, Ch.7). Scope is therefore the supply logic of varied [[Behavioral Surplus]], the requirement that feeds higher-quality [[Prediction Products]].

That variation develops along two dimensions. The first is **extension**: "the extension of extraction operations from the virtual world into the 'real' world, where we actually live our actual lives." Extension "wants your bloodstream and your bed, your breakfast conversation, your commute, your run, your refrigerator, your parking space, your living room" (`Directly stated`, Ch.7) — every corner and crevice of lived experience. The second is **depth**, "even more audacious": "highly predictive, and therefore highly lucrative, behavioral surplus would be plumbed from intimate patterns of the self," aimed "at your personality, moods, and emotions, your lies and vulnerabilities" (`Directly stated`, Ch.7). Chapter 8 develops the extension dimension as [[Rendition]] moves into the real world — "the first dimension of scope" — while Chapter 9 turns to depth (`Directly stated`, Ch.8).

Zuboff figures the two dimensions through **the net and the submarines**: "The net is cast wide over the waters of daily life, but there are also submarines exploring the depths in search of new sources of surplus prized for their rare predictive powers: your personality, emotions, and endorphins" (`Directly stated`, Ch.8). The net is extension across the surface of the real world; the submarines are the descent into the intimate self. The image binds the breadth of [[Surveillance]] to its inward reach — the same inward turn that carries scope toward the [[Quantified Self]] devices (wearables, fitness trackers) Zuboff catalogs as both surplus-extraction and behavior-change instruments.

Crucially, scope is *necessary but insufficient*. "Just as scale became necessary but insufficient for higher-quality predictions, it was also clear that economies of scope would be necessary but insufficient for the highest quality of prediction products able to sustain competitive advantage in the new markets for future behavior" (`Directly stated`, Ch.7). The shortfall opens onto the third term: "the surest way to predict behavior is to intervene at its source and shape it. The processes invented to achieve this goal are what I call economies of action" (`Directly stated`, Ch.7). Chapter 11 restates the triad as three phases of competitive intensity — scale (the [[The Extraction Imperative|extraction imperative]]), scope (the [[The Prediction Imperative|prediction imperative]]'s demand for "variation as well as volume"), and action — with scope's "two dimensions" named precisely: "The first is extension across a wide range of activities; the second is the depth of predictive detail within each activity" (`Directly stated`, Ch.11). Economies of scope are thus the pivot: they expand the *what* of extraction (everything, everywhere, and in depth) and, by their own insufficiency, drive the logic past prediction into the behavior modification of economies of action.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — the originating use. Economies of scope are the second supply imperative of surveillance capitalism: behavioral surplus must be *varied* as well as vast, pursued along the twin dimensions of extension (across the real world) and depth (into the intimate self). Figured as the net and the submarines, scope realizes [[Rendition]] and feeds [[Prediction Products]], yet remains necessary-but-insufficient — its shortfall justifies the move to economies of action and the [[The Reality Business|reality business]].

## Related Concepts

- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — economies of scope are the supply logic that makes surplus *varied*; scope is the imperative for diversity in the raw material.
- [[Prediction Products]] — better predictions require "variation as well as volume"; scope exists to raise prediction quality.
- [[The Extraction Imperative]] — expresses economies of *scale* (vast surplus); scope is the companion imperative for *varied* surplus.
- [[The Prediction Imperative]] — the imperative that demands economies of scope (and action) beyond scale, in pursuit of predictive certainty.
- [[Rendition]] — the concrete operation through which scope is realized; Ch.8 develops extension as rendition moving into the real world.
- [[The Reality Business]] — the era of "scope and action" supply operations in the real world (Ch.7's name for the phase scope inaugurates).
- [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — the economic logic whose supply triad (scale → scope → action) economies of scope occupy the middle of.
- [[Surveillance]] — scope is the dimension along which surveillance becomes total (extension) and intimate (depth). `Strongly implied`
- [[Quantified Self]] — wearables and self-tracking are exemplary scope instruments, reaching the body and inner life. `Strongly implied`

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Narrativity]] — scope flattens "every level of intimacy" into "a tidal flow of data points," the antithesis of irreducible, narrated experience. `Interpretive synthesis`

## Questions It Raises

- If scope is *necessary but insufficient*, is the escalation from scale to scope to action a logical inevitability of the prediction imperative, or a contingent business choice that could be refused?
- When the "net" of extension and the "submarines" of depth converge, is there any "corner and crevice" of experience structurally beyond scope — or does the logic require that none remain?
- Does depth-scope (personality, emotions, endorphins) differ in kind, not just degree, from extension-scope — and is the intimate self a categorically different thing to render than the "real world" surface?
- If scope's insufficiency is what licenses economies of action, can extraction ever stop short of behavior modification once the variation it supplies fails to guarantee outcomes?
