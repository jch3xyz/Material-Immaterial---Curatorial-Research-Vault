# Surveillance Capitalism

## Brief Definition

Shoshana Zuboff's master concept: a new economic logic — a "logic of accumulation" — that unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data, subordinating the production of goods and services to a global architecture of behavioral modification.

## Longer Explanation

*Surveillance capitalism* is the keystone term of *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism* and the name Zuboff gives to "the darkening of the digital dream and its rapid mutation into a voracious and utterly novel commercial project." It is not surveillance as such — not a tool, a technology, or even a set of practices — but an *economic order*, a distinct logic of accumulation whose foundational mechanism is the unilateral capture of human experience. `Directly stated`

The core operation is an act of dispossession framed as discovery. Surveillance capitalism "unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data." Some of this data improves the product or service that the user came for; the *surplus* — the [[Behavioral Surplus]] — is "declared as a proprietary behavioral surplus," processed by "machine intelligence" into prediction products, and traded in what Zuboff calls behavioral futures markets. The keystone surveillance and data mechanisms (behavioral surplus, the prediction imperative, [[Instrumentarian Power]]) receive their full exposition in later chapters; this note holds the master economic concept that organizes them. `Directly stated`

Zuboff's "definition" section frames the term across eight registers at once, three of which this note folds in rather than minting as separate thin notes: surveillance capitalism is "a parasitic economic logic in which the production of goods and services is subordinated to a new global architecture of behavioral modification"; "a rogue mutation of capitalism marked by concentrations of wealth, knowledge, and power unprecedented in human history"; and "the foundational framework of a surveillance economy." It is also "a unique logic of accumulation in which surveillance is a foundational mechanism in the transformation of investment into profit." `Directly stated`

Crucially for the vault's larger argument, surveillance capitalism is *historically conditioned*, not technologically inevitable. It "is inconceivable outside the digital milieu, but neoliberal ideology and policy also provided the habitat in which surveillance capitalism could flourish." Google is its origin case — "Google is to surveillance capitalism what the Ford Motor Company and General Motors were to massproduction–based managerial capitalism" — under whose direction "the global reach of computer mediation is repurposed as an extraction architecture." And it is no longer confined to its birthplace: "Surveillance capitalism was born digital, but … it is no longer confined to born-digital companies," migrating outward into every sector and, in the book's later arc, into "the one that we call 'real.'" `Directly stated`

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — coins and owns the term. Surveillance capitalism is for her a *logic of accumulation*: a rogue, parasitic mutation of capitalism that extracts behavioral surplus from human experience and converts it, via prediction products and behavioral futures markets, into a global architecture of behavioral modification — distinct from, but the engine of, [[Surveillance]] as apparatus and [[Instrumentarian Power]] as its form of power.
- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** (resonance, not citation) — Han's [[Digital Panopticon]] names the same compulsory transparency from the side of the watched subject who exposes itself voluntarily; Zuboff supplies the *economic motive* (surplus extraction) that Han largely brackets. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **[[Yuval Noah Harari]]** (resonance, not citation) — [[Dataism]] and [[Big Data]] describe the worldview and infrastructure that surveillance capitalism monetizes; Zuboff specifies the market form. `Interpretive synthesis`

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — surveillance capitalism intensifies the immaterial-data pole: lived, material experience is claimed and converted into "behavioral data" traded in markets, so the note belongs to the vault's object→system / material→immaterial spine.

## Questions It Raises

- If surveillance capitalism is a *logic of accumulation* rather than a technology, can it be regulated or only out-competed by a different logic?
- Zuboff insists it is "inconceivable outside the digital milieu" yet enabled by neoliberalism — which is the necessary condition, the digital infrastructure or the political-economic habitat?
- How does Zuboff's economically-grounded account relate to Han's [[Digital Panopticon]], which locates the coercion inside the subject's own desire to be seen?
- Where does behavioral surplus extraction sit on the [[Material vs Immaterial]] spectrum — is "human experience" a material thing being dispossessed, or only its data-image?
