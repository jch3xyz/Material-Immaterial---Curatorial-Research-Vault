# The Hive

## Brief Definition

The hive is Shoshana Zuboff's name for the social form of instrumentarian society: a "confluent hive mind" modeled on machine learning, in which the individual's freedom is forfeit to collective knowledge administered for others' profit, with surveillance capitalists and their "data priesthood" in charge of oversight and control.

> **Forward-anchor.** The hive is *named and introduced* in Chapter 1 and developed across Part III (Chapters 14-16, 18). The sections below establish the figure as the social-scale counterpart of [[Instrumentarian Power]] and [[Big Other]]; they are written to be **extended** by later chunks of the Zuboff ingest (the "tuning, herding, and conditioning" mechanics, the Pentland/Facebook material in subsequent chapters). Pentland is from Part III and is NOT attributed here. Do not over-build.

## Longer Explanation

For Zuboff, surveillance capitalism's second phase change shifts "focus from the real world to the social world, as society itself becomes the new object of extraction and control." Where "industrial society was imagined as a well-functioning machine, instrumentarian society is imagined as a human simulation of machine learning systems: a confluent hive mind in which each element learns and operates in concert with every other element" (Directly stated, Ch. 1). The hive is thus the *societal* materialization of instrumentarian power — the pattern that [[Instrumentarian Power]] and [[Big Other]] produce when scaled from the individual to the whole population.

The model is borrowed wholesale from machine confluence. In machine learning "all the machines in a networked system move seamlessly toward confluence, all sharing the same understanding and operating in unison," so that "Confluent action means that the 'freedom' of each individual machine is surrendered to the knowledge that they share." Zuboff turns this into a social prescription: "the machine hive becomes the role model for a new human hive in which we march in peaceful unison toward the same direction based on the same 'correct' understanding in order to construct a world free of mistakes, accidents, and random messes" (Directly stated, Ch. 14). The hive's signature is the subordination of the part to the whole: "In this human hive, individual freedom is forfeit to collective knowledge and action. Nonharmonious elements are preemptively targeted with high doses of tuning, herding, and conditioning, including the full seductive force of social persuasion and influence" (Directly stated, Ch. 14).

This is why Zuboff insists the hive is a *new collectivism*, not a return to the totalitarian state. "Today, surveillance capitalism offers a new template for our future: the machine hive in which our freedom is forfeit to perfect knowledge administered for others' profit." The "shift from society to swarm and from individuals to organisms is the cornerstone upon which the structure of an instrumentarian society rests" (Directly stated, Ch. 15). It is "a privatized instrumentarian social order ... a new form of collectivism in which it is the market, not the state, which concentrates both knowledge and freedom within its domain" (Directly stated, Ch. 18). The collective is owned and operated for profit, governed not by politics but by what Chapter 1 calls "surveillance capitalists and their data priesthood in charge of oversight and control" — a small caste possessing the knowledge and freedom that the many surrender.

Zuboff treats the hive as already-arrived rather than predicted: "What we have seen in Facebook is a living example of the third modernity that instrumentarianism proffers, defined by a new collectivism owned and operated by surveillance capital," and "Facebook is a prototype of instrumentarian society, not a prophecy. It is the first frontier of a new societal territory, and the youngest among us are its vanguard" (Directly stated, Ch. 16). The intellectual lineage runs through radical behaviorism: the confluence sacrifice is one "the behavioral theorists Planck, Meyer, and Skinner predicted," "an all-out war on accidents, mistakes, and randomness in general" — the hive as the social fulfillment of Skinner's "technology of behavior" by market means (Strongly implied, Ch. 14).

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — coins "the hive" / "human hive" as the social form of instrumentarian society: a confluence modeled on machine learning where individual freedom is forfeit to collective knowledge, governed by a "data priesthood" and operated for profit. It is the population-scale extension of [[Instrumentarian Power]] and [[Big Other]], and a *new collectivism* of the market rather than the state. (To be extended in Part III.)
- *Radical behaviorism (B. F. Skinner, via Zuboff)* — the named antecedent: the hive realizes the behaviorist "war on accidents, mistakes, and randomness" that Planck, Meyer, and Skinner anticipated. Skinner is the historical source, not a co-theorist; rendered in plain text pending any dedicated ingest.

## Related Concepts

- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — the species of power whose social, population-scale form *is* the hive; the hive is instrumentarian power scaled from the individual to society.
- [[Big Other]] — the material/computational apparatus that renders the hive possible; the "machine confluence" the human hive is patterned on.
- [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — the parent economic order; the hive is the collectivist social vision it imposes ("a totalizing collectivist vision of life in the hive").
- [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — the elemental right the hive forfeits: in the hive, certainty replaces the open, willed future, drawing "rights to the future tense ... into the fortress of surveillance capital."
- [[Dataism]] (Han / Harari) — the worldview that treats society itself as data to be optimized; the hive is its social-organizational endpoint.
- [[Digital Panopticon]] (Han) — strong resonance in the figure of pervasive observation and social pressure; but Zuboff frames the hive as a *new collectivism* of the market, not the panoptic state.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[The Liberal Humanist Subject]] — the hive dissolves the autonomous, free-willing individual: "individuality is merely vestigial," "society as we know it shades into obsolescence," and freedom is surrendered to shared knowledge.
- Totalitarianism — Zuboff distinguishes the hive's collectivism from the totalitarian state: it is the *market*, not the state, that concentrates knowledge and freedom, a "privatized" collectivism that "diverges from the long-standing values of market capitalism and market democracy."

## Questions It Raises

- If the hive is a "new collectivism" of the market rather than the state, is it a successor to totalitarianism, a hybrid, or a genuinely unprecedented form?
- What is lost when "society" becomes "swarm" and "individuals" become "organisms" — and is the loss felt, given that the hive operates through seduction and social pressure rather than violence?
- Who is the "data priesthood," and does locating oversight in a caste make the hive an oligarchy in collectivist disguise?
- If Facebook is already "a prototype, not a prophecy," what would count as resistance to a social form whose members experience it as connection and belonging?
