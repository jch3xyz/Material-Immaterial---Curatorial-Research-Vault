# Sense Ratios

## Brief Definition

The proportion among the senses in a person's perceptual life — a balance that any new medium silently rebalances, so that the deepest effect of a technology is felt not as opinion or content but as an altered pattern of perception.

## Longer Explanation

For Marshall McLuhan, "sense ratios" names the perceptual mechanism that lies beneath his master thesis. A medium does its work not by what it says but by re-proportioning the senses of whoever uses it. Crucially, this happens below the threshold of argument: "The effects of technology do not occur at the level of opinions or concepts, but alter sense ratios or patterns of perception steadily and without any resistance." Because the change bypasses opinion and concept, it cannot be refused on principle — the user is reorganized perceptually before any judgment can be formed. [`Directly stated`]

The model is quantitative but not additive. Total sensation is constant; only its internal distribution shifts. McLuhan borrows the analogy of color: "Sensation is always 100 per cent, and a color is always 100 per cent color. But the ratio among the components in the sensation or the color can differ infinitely." Intensify one channel and the others are immediately affected — "if sound, for example, is intensified, touch and taste and sight are affected at once." Each medium is therefore an extension (or "self-amputation") of the body that "demands new ratios or new equilibriums among the other organs and extensions of the body," and there is "no way of refusing to comply with the new sense ratios or sense 'closure' evoked by the TV image." [`Directly stated`]

Chapter 2 sharpens the mechanism into a psychophysical pair. Over-stimulating a single sense and starving the rest produce opposite pathologies: "the hotting-up of one sense tends to effect hypnosis, and the cooling of all senses tends to result in hallucination." This is the perceptual hinge of McLuhan's hot/cool distinction — a "hot" medium that drives one sense to "dominant command of a situation" hypnotizes, while sensory withdrawal triggers "a furious fill-in or completion of senses that is sheer hallucination." The same logic recurs through the Part II case studies: "Each new impact shifts the ratios among all the senses," and a new extension "sets up a new equilibrium among all of the senses and faculties leading… to a 'new outlook.'" [`Directly stated`]

The concept also carries McLuhan's redefinition of reason itself. Rationality is not a faculty added on top of the senses but a particular balance among them: "rationality or consciousness is itself a ratio or proportion among the sensuous components of experience, and is not something added to such sense experience." On this account a shift in the sense ratio is a shift in what a culture can count as rational — which is why McLuhan treats the phonetic alphabet's intensification of the visual as a wholesale reorganization of Western consciousness, not merely a new tool. [`Directly stated`]

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** — *Understanding Media* (1964). Sense ratios are the perceptual substrate of "the medium is the message": media act by re-proportioning the senses beneath opinion and content, with hot media tending toward hypnosis and total sensory cooling toward hallucination. Rationality is itself one such ratio, so a media-induced shift in the sense balance is a shift in consciousness.

## Related Concepts

- [[Embodiment]] — sense ratios locate the deepest media effect in the proportioning of the bodily senses, not in disembodied "information" or content; the message is registered in the sensorium.

## Opposing Concepts

- The "content" or "opinions and concepts" view of media effects — McLuhan explicitly displaces it: effects do *not* occur at the level of opinions or concepts. [`Directly stated`]

## Questions It Raises

- If media alter perception "without any resistance," what kind of agency or critique is even possible? (McLuhan's answer — only the serious artist, "an expert aware of the changes in sense perception," can encounter technology with impunity.)
- Is the hypnosis/hallucination pair a literal psychophysical claim or a diagnostic metaphor for media-saturated culture?
- If rationality is "a ratio or proportion among the sensuous components of experience," does each media regime produce its own incommensurable rationality?
- How does sense-ratio shift relate to later vault accounts of perception being reformatted by data and the screen — is McLuhan's "no way of refusing to comply" a precursor to surveillance-era arguments about non-conscious capture?
