# Guaranteed Outcomes

## Brief Definition

The specific operational target of instrumentarian power: a condition of certainty without terror, in which human behavior is tuned, herded, and conditioned toward preselected ends — the substitution of machine-secured certainty for the contingency of society.

## Longer Explanation

In Shoshana Zuboff's account of surveillance capitalism, "guaranteed outcomes" names what [[Instrumentarian Power]] is *for*. Where totalitarian power sought certainty through terror, instrumentarianism, equipped with the ubiquitous sensate apparatus she calls [[Big Other]], pursues "a condition of certainty without terror in the form of 'guaranteed outcomes'" (Directly stated). It does not seize the body for pain or murder; it works on behavior at a distance, severing our insides from our outsides so that "otherized behavior takes on a life of its own that delivers our futures to surveillance capitalism's aims and interests."

Guaranteed outcomes are produced operationally through the [[Economies of Action]] — the third phase of competition in which surveillance capitalists move beyond knowing and predicting behavior to *intervening* in it, via [[Tuning, Herding, and Conditioning]]. Outcomes are "guaranteed" precisely because chaotic, "nonharmonious," or "nonconforming" elements are preemptively targeted and tuned out of the system in advance. The owners of the [[The Means of Behavioral Modification|means of behavioral modification]] are the ones who "set the behavioral parameters that define guaranteed outcomes" (Directly stated) — certainty is not discovered but engineered, baked into Big Other's operations as automatically imposed "policies."

The concept pairs tightly with [[A Utopia of Certainty]], the canonical name for the societal endgame: where the Utopia of Certainty names the *condition* surveillance capital projects onto the whole of social life, Guaranteed Outcomes names the discrete *operational deliverable* sold into [[Behavioral Futures Markets]] and demanded by both market and state. Zuboff frames the larger trajectory as "the substitution of certainty for society" — instrumentarian power filling the void left by collapsed social trust by "substituting machines for social relations" (Directly stated). Market and state share, in her words, "a relentless drive toward guaranteed outcomes."

The cost is freedom. In the "human hive" of the instrumentarian collective, "individual freedom is forfeit to collective knowledge and action," and "We learn to sacrifice our freedom to collective knowledge imposed by others and for the sake of their guaranteed outcomes." Guaranteed outcomes are thus the inverse of, and the direct assault upon, [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — the right to act in an open, indeterminate future. To guarantee an outcome is to foreclose the very uncertainty in which human will and the future tense reside (Strongly implied).

## Questions It Raises

- If certainty is engineered by tuning out "nonconforming" elements in advance, what counts as a genuine outcome at all, and whose ends define it?

- Can a society organized around guaranteed outcomes preserve any space for the unpredictable acts that constitute freedom and politics?
- Is the "substitution of certainty for society" a loss of risk, or a loss of the social itself?
