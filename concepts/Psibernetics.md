# Psibernetics

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's 1970 neologism fusing *psi* (parapsychology) and *cyb*(ernetics): the name for a coming culture and art that arch the two apparently opposed spheres of technology and telepathy into one general-systems whole of prediction, control, and communication.

## Longer Explanation

In ch.9, "The Psibernetic Arch" (first published as an untitled contribution to *Studio International*, April 1970), Ascott proposes "a general model of the factors that will operate on culture over the next twenty-five years." The model "takes the form of an arch bridging two apparently opposed spheres: cybernetics and parapsychology. The west and east sides of the mind, so to speak; technology and telepathy; provision and prevision; cyb and psi." (`Directly stated`) The fusion of these two names is the word itself: "there is no doubt that the cybernetic era—perhaps we should call it the psibernetic era—is upon us, and that art, however obscurely, has anticipated the fact." (`Directly stated`)

The unifying frame is a [[Systems Theory|general systems]] one. "A rainbow arch unites them. A general systems approach would enable us to investigate the two aspects of a whole system of prediction, control, and communication. This arch could support a whole artistic culture." (`Directly stated`) The cybernetic pole supplies efficiency, universality, and [[Feedback Loops|feedback]] — but is "entropic" and bound by media; the psi pole supplies instantaneity but is "sporadic." Crucially, ESP is folded in not as superstition but as the *limit case of communication*: "Extra-sensory perception, in so far is it has been shown to exist in terms of telepathy and clairvoyance, is the most perfect form of communication and social feedback imaginable, although its manifestation is erratic." (`Directly stated`) Because it is medialess, it outstrips even electric media on their own terms — "Electric technology is the artificial supporting redundancy for psychic systems. ESP is more instant than instant electronic communications, because it involves no media." (`Directly stated`)

From this medialessness Ascott draws the concept's most visionary reach. The arch "can mean a kind of instant global sympathy, a galactic sympathy perhaps," and "Out-of-time behaviour, clairvoyance, and psychokinesis are, for example, the only real hope of travel around the galaxy and beyond." (`Directly stated`; the *truth* of these claims is, of course, a `Speculative connection`.) The instant-global-sympathy formulation is an early sibling to McLuhan's electric one-world — Ascott himself notes "as with TV, so with ESP, the medium is the message" — and prefigures Ascott's later [[Network Consciousness|networked-consciousness]] vocabulary (see [[The Global Village]]). (`Strongly implied`)

Psibernetics is best read as the first term in a lineage Ascott extends across his career: the cybernetic/behavioural art of the 1960s, then *psibernetics* (1970), then the telematic art of the 1980s–90s, and finally the [[Technoetics|technoetic]]/moistmedia work of the 2000s. The word recurs late in the book: in the "100 Terms" lexicon (ch.32) he glosses "Psibernetic phenomena" — "The human desire for transcendence takes many forms: telepathy, telekinesis, out-of-body experience. Now we have telematics, telepresence, and the aesthetics of apparition." (`Directly stated`) — and coins the "Psiborg," a "Psychically extended organism, human but with vastly increased powers of psychic perception." (`Directly stated`) In ch.30 the editor's footnote explicitly threads the line, noting "the parallels here between Ascott's 1970 notion of psibernetics described in 'The Psibernetic Arch' . . . his 1994 concept of [[Cyberception|cyberception]] . . . and the concept of psi-perception described here" — where psi-perception is named "In parallel with technologically aided cyberception." (`Directly stated`, Shanken/Ascott) The continuity is the reconciliation of the immaterial-psychic and the technological — a [[Re-enchantment]] of the post-industrial, cybernated world rather than its disenchantment. (`Interpretive synthesis`)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — the sole coiner. Psibernetics names a *futurible* (a creative projection, not a "surprise-free" prediction): an art of "predictive or anticipatory structure," "the art of futuribles," that need employ "neither advanced hardware nor elaborate ectoplasm" because its subject is the *process and system* underlying technological culture allied to behaviour "at the psychic level." Within the *Telematic Embrace* arc it is the bridge term between his cybernetic 1960s art and his later [[Technoetics|technoetic]] cosmology; the variants "Psibernetic phenomena," "Psiborg," and "psi-perception" are its later crystallizations.

- [[Feedback Loops]] — ESP is figured as "the most perfect form of communication and social feedback imaginable"; the cybernetic pole supplies literal feedback structure.
- [[Network Consciousness]] — "instant global sympathy, a galactic sympathy" is an early, telepathic statement of the networked-mind theme Ascott develops in his telematic phase.
- [[The Global Village]] — McLuhan's electric one-world; Ascott's "instant global sympathy" and "the medium is the message" gloss it through the psi pole.
- [[Re-enchantment]] — psibernetics re-admits the psychic, transcendent, and "occult" into a post-industrial cybernetic culture rather than purging them.
- [[Cybernetics]] — the *cyb* half of the portmanteau; the science of prediction, control, and communication Ascott marries to parapsychology.
- [[Technoetics]] — Ascott's later (2000s) term for the same project; psibernetics is its 1970 ancestor.
- [[Cyberception]] — his 1994 perceptual concept; ch.30's "psi-perception" is named in parallel with it, the editor tying both back to psibernetics.

## Opposing Concepts

- The term has no single named opposite; its dynamic opposition is internal — the [[Cyb vs Psi]] poles it sets out to reconcile rather than choose between.

## Questions It Raises

- Can ESP be treated as a *limit case of communication* (a medialess channel) without smuggling unfounded empirical claims into a media theory — i.e., where does the metaphor end and the assertion begin?
- Is "instant global sympathy, a galactic sympathy" a genuine forerunner of networked [[Network Consciousness|collective consciousness]], or does its galactic/telepathic register mark the point at which Ascott's [[Re-enchantment]] becomes mysticism?
- If psibernetic art need use "neither advanced hardware nor elaborate ectoplasm," what distinguishes it from any art "concerned to give expression to process and system" — what does the *psi* pole add that [[Cybernetics|cybernetics]] alone does not?
- How does the 1970 "Psiborg" (psychically extended organism) relate to the cybernetic-organism [[The Cyborg|cyborg]] — extension of *perception* versus extension of *body*?
