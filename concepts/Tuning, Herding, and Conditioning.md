# Tuning, Herding, and Conditioning

## Brief Definition

The three operational techniques — subliminal nudging (tuning), foreclosing a person's action alternatives by controlling their immediate context (herding), and Skinnerian operant reinforcement applied at scale (conditioning) — by which surveillance capitalism's [[Economies of Action]] reach into the world to *shape* behavior at its source, named by Zuboff in Chapter 11 as the machine processes of [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]].

## Longer Explanation

For [[Shoshana Zuboff]], tuning, herding, and conditioning are the three concrete approaches that produce [[Economies of Action]] — surveillance capitalism's turn from *knowing* behavior to *doing* things to it. In Chapter 10 ("Make Them Dance") she reports them directly from her interviews: "The scientists and engineers whom I interviewed identified three key approaches to economies of action, each one aimed at achieving behavior modification. The first two I call 'tuning' and 'herding.' The third is already familiar as what behavioral psychologists refer to as 'conditioning.'" These are not separate strategies but a combinable toolkit: "Strategies that produce economies of action vary according to the methods with which these approaches are combined and the salience of each." (`Directly stated`, Ch. 10.)

**Tuning** is influence through environmental design. It "occurs in a variety of ways. It may involve subliminal cues designed to subtly shape the flow of behavior at the precise time and place for maximally efficient influence," or it may take the form of the "nudge" of behavioral economists Richard Thaler and Cass Sunstein — "any aspect of a choice architecture that alters people's behavior in a predictable way." The decisive move is that Zuboff inverts the nudge's stated purpose: choice architecture, ostensibly arranged for the chooser's own good, is here turned so the predictable behavior accrues to the architect. As she puts it, "social situations are always already thick with tuning interventions, most of which operate outside our awareness." Tuning is thus a form of [[Telestimulation]] — Facebook's contagion and voting experiments demonstrate "tuning through suggestion" as "an effective form of telestimulation at scale," its success resting precisely on "the evasion of individual and group awareness." (`Directly stated`, Ch. 10.)

**Herding** abandons suggestion for control of the world itself. It "is a second approach that relies on controlling key elements in a person's immediate context." Rather than persuade, it removes the alternatives: "Herding enables remote orchestration of the human situation, foreclosing action alternatives and thus moving behavior along a path of heightened probability that approximates certainty." Its emblem is [[The Uncontract]] — "Shutting down a car's engine irreversibly changes the driver's immediate context, herding her out the car door" — and its slogan, from an internet-of-things developer, gives the chapter its title: "We are learning how to write the music, and then we let the music make them dance." Where tuning works *on* the subject's perception, herding works *around* it, engineering the material context (the fridge that locks, the chair that shakes, the car that will not start) so that only the chosen behavior remains possible. This is the technique that most directly realizes the [[Object vs System]] spine: an open social situation is replaced by a closed machine system whose output is guaranteed. (`Directly stated`, Ch. 10.)

**Conditioning** is the explicitly Skinnerian inheritance. Zuboff traces it to "the famous Harvard behaviorist [[B. F. Skinner]]," who "interpolated a third variable: 'reinforcement'" between stimulus and response and "mastered intricate designs or 'schedules' of reinforcement that could reliably shape precise behavioral routines." Skinner "called the application of reinforcements to shape specific behaviors 'operant conditioning.' His larger project was known as 'behavior modification' or 'behavioral engineering,' in which behavior is continuously shaped to amplify some actions at the expense of others." Surveillance capitalism realizes Skinner's dreamed-of "technology of behavior" at population scale: as a Silicon Valley education company's chief data scientist told Zuboff, "Conditioning at scale is essential to the new science of massively engineered human behavior" — smartphones and wearables let a firm "gradually master[] the schedule of reinforcements" that produces the user behaviors it "selects for dominance." This is also where surveillance-capitalist conditioning overlaps with [[Gamification]]: reward systems, recognition, and "social network incentives" as the engineered delivery of reinforcement. (`Directly stated`, Ch. 10.)

In Chapter 11 ("The Right to the Future Tense") Zuboff names the triad as the working body of [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]]: "the means of production are subordinated to an elaborate new means of behavioral modification, which relies upon a variety of machine processes, techniques, and tactics (tuning, herding, conditioning) to shape individual, group, and population behavior in ways that continuously improve their approximation to guaranteed outcomes." Because all three are "designed to elude awareness," they collapse the indeterminate open future on which willing and promising depend — directly threatening [[The Right to the Future Tense]] and underwriting [[A Utopia of Certainty]]. In Chapter 15 ("The Instrumentarian Collective") the same triad scales up from the individual to the social-network level in Alex Pentland's "social physics": idea flow is computed so that "Human behavior must be herded and penned within the parameters of the plan," a maneuver Pentland calls "tuning the network," achieved (as in the eToro finance world) "by providing small incentives or nudges to individuals" — Pentland's own word for which is "reinforcement." The triad thus migrates from commercial behavior modification into the social-engineering substrate of [[Instrumentarian Power]]. (`Directly stated`, Ch. 11, Ch. 15.)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — coins "tuning" and "herding" (reporting them from surveillance-capitalist engineers) and adopts "conditioning" from behaviorism; in Ch. 11 she names the triad as the machine processes of [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]]. The three are the operational substance of [[Economies of Action]] and, scaled to the network in Ch. 15, of [[Instrumentarian Power]]. Their shared signature is that they "elude awareness."
- **[[B. F. Skinner]]** — the source of *conditioning*. Operant conditioning, schedules of reinforcement, and the dreamed-of "technology of behavior" applied "across entire human populations" are the direct ancestor of "conditioning at scale." Zuboff treats surveillance capitalism as the (unwitting, for-profit) fulfillment of Skinner's program; Skinner remains a forward-stub author in the vault.

## Related Concepts

- [[Economies of Action]] — tuning, herding, and conditioning are the three approaches that *produce* economies of action; this note details what that note names.
- [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]] — Ch. 11 names the triad as the "machine processes, techniques, and tactics" of this apparatus.
- [[The Uncontract]] — Zuboff's exemplary *herding* technique; the car-engine shutoff that forecloses action by changing the material context.
- [[Telestimulation]] — tuning realized as suggestion/contagion "at scale"; the Facebook experiments are its proof.
- [[Gamification]] — the consumer-facing delivery system for *conditioning*: reward, recognition, and "social network incentives" as schedules of reinforcement.
- [[A Utopia of Certainty]] — the end-state the triad serves: behavior pre-shaped to "guaranteed outcomes."
- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — the species of power whose operational means the triad becomes once scaled to the network (Pentland's "tuning the network").
- [[The Prediction Imperative]] — the competitive pressure that drives knowing into shaping; the triad is how prediction guarantees itself.
- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — the extracted raw material that is fed back at its source as the substrate the triad acts upon.
- [[Society of Control]] — Deleuze's continuous *modulation* of conduct finds a literal machinic realization in tuning/herding; Zuboff's herding is starker, foreclosing action rather than modulating it.
- [[Digital Panopticon]] / [[Smart Power]] — Han's diagnoses seen from the subject's side; the triad is the apparatus side, the "how" of voluntary-yet-shaped conduct.
- [[Dataism]] / [[Surveillance]] / [[Quantified Self]] — the worldview, the master concept, and the rendering of life into data on which the triad operates.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — the elemental freedom (to will, to promise) that the triad usurps; interventions "designed to elude awareness" foreclose the open future.
- [[Free Will vs Determinism]] — the triad substitutes "the determinism of programmed machine processes" for the indeterminacy of the willing subject, working "outside the driver's awareness while she still thinks that she is free."

## Questions It Raises

- The triad's effectiveness depends on remaining unfelt ("designed to elude awareness"). What political or aesthetic remedy is possible against a power that works only so long as it is imperceptible?
- Zuboff distinguishes a benign nudge (toward the chooser's own interest) from herding (engineered for the architect's revenue) by *whose interest* the choice architecture serves. Is that line legible to the person being tuned — or only to the analyst after the fact?
- How load-bearing is the Skinnerian claim? Does "conditioning at scale" actually produce guaranteed behavior, or is "guaranteed outcomes" itself a marketing claim sold in [[Behavioral Futures Markets]]?
- Herding works through the material world (the car, the fridge, the chair); tuning and conditioning work through perception and reward. Is the triad ultimately one technique — the engineering of context — split across the boundary of the body, or three genuinely distinct mechanisms?
- Curatorially: how would an artwork render tuning/herding/conditioning *perceptible* — stage the subliminal cue, the foreclosed alternative, the schedule of reinforcement — against a subject who, by design, cannot feel them operating?
