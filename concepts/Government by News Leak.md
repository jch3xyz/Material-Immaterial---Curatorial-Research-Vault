# Government by News Leak

## Brief Definition

McLuhan's name for the political condition produced by electric speed: as information accelerates, government shifts away from representation and delegation toward immediate total community involvement, decisions are "announced before they are made," and the old contradiction between publicity and secrecy is dissolved because "top secrecy is translated into public participation and responsibility by the magic flexibility of the controlled news leak."

## Longer Explanation

In the Press chapter of *Understanding Media*, McLuhan argues that the newspaper is not a vehicle for a private "point of view" but a mosaic — a "group confessional form that provides communal participation." The mosaic does not deliver detached opinion; it commands "participation in process." Once telegraph and electric speed are layered onto this form, the press becomes "inseparable from the democratic process," because the communal image it assembles hour by hour is itself a mode of total involvement rather than a window onto events. *(Directly stated)*

This formal claim has a political extension. McLuhan holds that the velocity of electrically moved information changes the very structure of governance: "As the speed of information increases, the tendency is for politics to move away from representation and delegation of constituents toward immediate involvement of the entire community in the central acts of decision." Slow information makes "delegation and representation mandatory"; electric speed makes them obsolescent, sustained only "by a series of subterfuges and makeshifts" that look to old observers like "base betrayals" of established forms. In diplomacy the same speed "causes the decisions to be announced before they are made in order to ascertain the varying responses that might occur when such decisions actually are made" — politics by trial balloon, decision as feedback loop. *(Directly stated)*

The title figure resolves an apparent paradox that McLuhan draws from Douglas Cater's *The Fourth Branch of Government*: the press is "dedicated to the process of cleansing by publicity," and yet, "in the electronic world of the seamless web of events, most affairs must be kept secret." McLuhan's resolution is the controlled news leak: "Top secrecy is translated into public participation and responsibility by the magic flexibility of the controlled news leak." The leak is not a failure of secrecy but its instrument — a calibrated, deliberate release through which a governing apparatus tests responses, distributes responsibility, and folds the public into the act of decision without abandoning control of the message. It is, in McLuhan's phrase, "ingenious adaptation from day to day" by which "Western man is beginning to accommodate himself to the electric world of total interdependence." *(Directly stated)*

Government by news leak therefore sits beneath McLuhan's larger thesis of electric decentralization (see [[The Global Village]]) and follows directly from [[The Medium Is the Message]]: the political effects flow from the form and speed of the medium, not from the content of any particular story. It is the durable political name for what happens when [[Acceleration|the speed of information]] reorganizes a society's central acts of decision. *(Interpretive synthesis)*

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** — coins "government by news leak" as the title and political thesis of the Press chapter. The leak is the mechanism by which secrecy and publicity cease to oppose each other: the state's controlled, deliberate self-exposure becomes the means of total community involvement at electric speed.

## Related Concepts

- [[The Global Village]] — the broader condition of electric decentralization and total interdependence; government by news leak is its specifically political form.
- [[The Medium Is the Message]] — grounds the thesis: the political consequence comes from the speed and form of the electric medium, not its content.
- [[Information]] — the chapter's claim that "information is the crucial commodity" of the electric age underwrites a politics organized around the management and release of information.
- [[Acceleration]] — McLuhan's "speed of information" as the driver of the shift; cross-author resonance with Han's later (pathological) sense of acceleration.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Society of Control]] — Deleuze's diagnosis treats continuous modulation and exposure as domination; McLuhan presents the same voluntary self-exposure and participation as accommodation rather than capture. The leak read as the state's *voluntary self-exposure* aligns it provocatively with the [[Digital Panopticon]] / [[Society of Control]] cluster — the apparatus exposing itself to produce participation and responsibility. *(Speculative connection)*

## Questions It Raises

- If decisions are "announced before they are made," is the public participating in decision or merely being instrumented as a feedback signal for an apparatus that retains control of the message?
- McLuhan frames the leak as the dissolution of the secrecy/publicity paradox and as benign "accommodation." Read through the [[Digital Panopticon]] / [[Society of Control]] cluster, is the controlled leak instead a technique of control — the state's *voluntary self-exposure* as a mode of power? *(Speculative connection)*
- Does "immediate involvement of the entire community" name a deepening of democracy, or its hollowing — participation without representation, responsibility without delegation?
- The chapter closes that media owners "sense that their power is in the medium and not in the message or the program." Who controls the flexibility of the leak when the power lies in the form itself?
