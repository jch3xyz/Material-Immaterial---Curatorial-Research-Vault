# Smart as a Euphemism for Rendition

## Brief Definition

The marketing word "smart" (against a denigrated "dumb") names not a benefit to the user but a capability for [[Rendition]]: an object is "smart" precisely to the degree that its embedded intelligence is engineered to convert some corner of lived experience into behavioral data for someone else's profit.

## Longer Explanation

For [[Shoshana Zuboff]], the praise lavished on "smart" products and the contempt heaped on "dumb" alternatives is a linguistic operation that hides an economic one. Each "smart" device is reimagined as a gateway into surveillance capitalism's supply chain, and its "intelligence" is, in her words, "intelligence that is designed to render some tiny corner of lived experience as behavioral data." The euphemism inverts agency: the consumer believes the object works *for* her, when its smartness in fact equips it to work *on* her — to render, capture, and relay her experience back to its makers. `Directly stated`

Zuboff's governing figure is the marionette. However lifelike the object's responsiveness, it is "a hapless puppet dancing to the puppet master's hidden economic imperatives." The owner imagines herself in command of the smart thing; in truth both owner and object are choreographed by the unseen imperatives of [[Behavioral Surplus]] extraction and [[The Prediction Imperative]]. This is the conceptual inverse of the curatorial lesson of [[The Puppet Master Not the Puppet]]: where that metaphor insists we attend to the master and not the visible figure, the "smart" euphemism is the master's most effective camouflage — it dresses the puppet up as autonomous so the strings disappear from view. `Directly stated`

What underwrites this whole turn, for Zuboff, is the death of the product as a self-sufficient good. "The very idea of a functional, effective, affordable product or service as a sufficient basis for economic exchange is dying." A thing can no longer justify its existence merely by performing its function well; it must also be a rendition device, a sensor and a relay, "remade by the new economic requirements of connection and rendition." This reframes the object's materiality entirely: the visible product is a husk whose real value lies in the immaterial data stream it secretly opens — a clean instance of the [[Material vs Immaterial]] inversion the surveillance cluster keeps performing. `Directly stated`

In Chapter 9 the same euphemistic machinery operates one register deeper, under the banner of "personalization." The "machine invasion of human depth is prosecuted under the banner of 'personalization,'" and the friendly "personal digital assistant" is "revealed as a market avatar, another Trojan horse in which the determination to render and monetize your life is secreted under the veil of 'assistance' and embellished with the poetry of 'personalization.'" "Smart," "personalized," and "assistance" thus form a vocabulary of soft words for a hard extraction — the linguistic surface of the structural argument that [[Euphemism and secrecy are structural to surveillance capitalism not incidental]]. `Directly stated`

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — "smart" is a euphemism for rendition; each smart object is a marionette dancing to its master's hidden economic imperatives; the functional product is dying as a sufficient basis for exchange, replaced by the object-as-rendition-gateway. The euphemism is not loose marketing but a structural concealment of asymmetric power.
- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — adjacent but distinct: Han's [[Smart Power]] (*Psychopolitics*) names a *mode of domination* that is friendly, permissive, and seductive rather than repressive, getting subjects to expose and optimize themselves voluntarily. Zuboff's "smart" names a *property of objects* engineered for rendition. The two converge on the point that the "smartness" of contemporary technology is a benign-sounding cover for an extractive or controlling operation. `Interpretive synthesis`

## Questions It Raises

- If "smart" is structurally a euphemism for rendition, is there any coherent notion of a "smart" object that serves only its owner — or does embedded networked intelligence always open a rendition channel?
- The marionette figure casts both object and owner as puppets. Who, then, is the puppet master — a firm, a market, an imperative — and can the strings ever be made visible from inside the dance?
- For art and curation: when "smart" materials and responsive objects enter the gallery, do they import the rendition imperative, and how would one stage the strings — make the puppet master, not the puppet, the work?
- If the functional product is "dying as a sufficient basis for economic exchange," what becomes of the made object as such — is materiality reduced to a delivery husk for an immaterial data stream?
