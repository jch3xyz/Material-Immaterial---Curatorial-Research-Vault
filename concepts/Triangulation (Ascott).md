# Triangulation (Ascott)

## Brief Definition

Ascott's non-analytical creative *method* for "locating" something as elusive as time: instead of the binary "classical dialectic" of comparing two ideas, he brings three "threads of ideas" — ESP (media), PDP (parallel data processing), and PU (parallel universes) — into a single conjunction whose overlap "define[s] a zone of understanding."

## Longer Explanation

Triangulation is a *procedure*, not a content. Ascott opens chapter 19 by declaring that the artist's work is "tracking down the paradoxical, opening up uncertainty, and stalking the ambiguous," and that for this kind of work the inherited tool — dialectic — is inadequate. "Bringing two ideas together may be good for comparative studies, or oppositional polemic, but the classical dialectic will not get us very far in trying to locate Time" (ch. 19). His alternative borrows the surveyor's logic: just as a position is fixed by sighting it from two known points, a *concept* can be fixed by sighting it from three. "I propose to use the non-analytical creative process to locate it, by employing a form of triangulation. The three coordinates, threads of ideas, I want to bring together I shall call ESP, PDP, and PU" (ch. 19). *Directly stated.*

The three coordinates name three contemporary fields, deliberately chosen as "the three most powerful areas of action and speculation currently shaping our culture, namely, media, computer science, and quantum physics" (ch. 19). Crucially, each acronym is a *pun* that loads a second, transgressive meaning onto the field:

- **ESP** — points to **media**, but doubles as **Extra-Sensory Perception**, "those ways of knowing, perceiving, and acting that transcend or lie outside the Cartesian-Newtonian frame of being." It also encodes **E**dwin **S**. **P**orter, the filmmaker "who first discovered that time can be compressed, expanded, or reversed... by means of the technique of editing" — making montage the ancestor of the "mosaic screen of digital discontinuity" of [[Telematic Art|telematic media]].
- **PDP** — **parallel data processing**, "the second strand in the process of triangulating to locate time." Networked computation produces "a kind of schizo-time," because the computer "processes time itself," transforming nanosecond pulses into information at speeds "outside human experience." The computer becomes "an extra-sensory device dealing invisibly with the invisible."
- **PU** — the many-worlds theories of **parallel universes**, which "resonate most sympathetically with our current sensibilities" and "mirror the new understanding of the creative process in telematic culture." A fourth term, **UP** (Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle and the observer effect), is folded in: it "intriguingly models the poststructuralist description of the reader/viewer as author/artist set in a context of semantic indeterminacy."

The point of triangulating these three (or four) is not synthesis into a single thesis but the production of a *region*: "Separately, in their own right, they each constitute a formidable force in our thinking; brought together — in a word, triangulated — they define a zone of understanding that provides new insights into the nature of time and new awareness of the human potential for creative consciousness" (ch. 19). The method is thus continuous with Ascott's larger commitment to [[Negotiated Meaning|negotiation]] over assertion and to the dispersed, participatory [[The Observer|observer]] — triangulation refuses the single fixed vantage point ("a fixed point in a perceiving system, a self-standing intelligence") in favour of the multiple, layered, networked "gaze." *Directly stated / Interpretive synthesis* for the linkage to negotiation.

This note records ESP, PDP, PU, and UP as Ascott's chapter-bound *coordinates of the method*; it does not atomize them into separate concept notes (they are acronyms local to ch. 19, not durable vault entities). Their substantive *result* — the dissolution of linear, unitary time into "schizo-time," "hyper time," and "the branching time-pathways of parallel universes" — belongs to [[Telematic Time]], which this note feeds. *Interpretive synthesis.*

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — coins triangulation as a deliberately *non-analytical* (anti-dialectical) creative method, demonstrated by triangulating media / computer science / quantum physics (ESP / PDP / PU) to "locate" time. A later, generalized form recurs across his writing as the triangulation of art, technology, and consciousness (the book note records this from ch. 31, *Art @ the Edge of the NET*); chapter 19 is its most explicit single statement.

## Related Concepts

## Opposing Concepts

- The **classical dialectic** (binary comparison of two ideas) — triangulation is explicitly proposed *against* it as inadequate for locating time.
- **Representation** / "art as object" — "an art of assertion" with "a fixed separation of creator and observer," which triangulation-driven [[Simulation (Ascott)|simulation]] rejects.
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — triangulation is one of the methodological engines by which Ascott pushes art off the material object and into the immaterial, networked, "out-of-body, out-of-mind" condition; it sits on the immaterialist pole of the vault's spine.

## Questions It Raises

- Is triangulation a genuine epistemology or a rhetorical device — does sighting a concept from three "powerful areas" actually constrain it, or merely license analogy among unlike fields?
- The three coordinates are joined partly by *puns* (ESP, PDP, UP). How much of the "zone of understanding" depends on the wordplay rather than on real structural homology between media, computation, and quantum physics?
- If dialectic is too binary, why stop at three? What makes triangulation the right *number* of threads rather than a contingent choice?
- How does Ascott's celebratory, "out-of-body, out-of-mind" networked time relate to [[Marshall McLuhan|McLuhan]]'s electric "extension of the central nervous system" — and to the surveillant reading of the same networked condition on the other side of [[Material vs Immaterial]]?
