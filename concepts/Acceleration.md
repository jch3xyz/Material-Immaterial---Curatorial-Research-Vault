# Acceleration

## Brief Definition

For [[Byung-Chul Han]], acceleration is speed stripped of sense and direction — a structural feature of [[Narration vs Addition|additive processes]] that operationalize the world by abolishing the negativity which would otherwise slow it down.

## General Orientation

"Acceleration" is used in several distinct senses across the vault, and they should not be collapsed. In [[Byung-Chul Han]]'s *Transparency Society* it is a *structural* category: only additive, non-narrative, transparent processes admit acceleration, so speed is a symptom of the loss of narrative form rather than a cause (see the longer explanation below). In Han's *Psychopolitics* the same term is given an *immunological-political* sense: the acceleration of information and capital presupposes the suppression of immune response — of idiosyncrasy and otherness — so that "the Same reacts to the Same" (see [[#Psychopolitics (Han) — immunosuppression for acceleration]]). Distinct again are the *speculative* media-theoretic and systems senses flagged in "Authors and Variants" below — [[Marshall McLuhan|McLuhan]]'s electric speed, [[Guy Debord|Debord]]'s saturated spectacle, [[Donella Meadows|Meadows]]'s delays and feedback — which may complement but do not ground Han's argument. The two Han senses are continuous (both make acceleration depend on the dismantling of negativity), and the Psychopolitics section extends rather than revises the structural account.

## Longer Explanation

Han's argument is structural rather than moral. He does not lament speed as such; he insists that *only certain kinds of processes admit acceleration in the first place*. "Addition is more transparent than narration. Only a process that is additive and not narrative admits acceleration" (Ch. 5). The processor counts; the procession narrates. Rituals, ceremonies, and pilgrimages have their own temporality, rhythm, and tact, and "it would be sacrilegious to seek to accelerate a sacrificial act." [[Transparency]] is the systemic compulsion that dismantles narrative thresholds — borders, obstacles, scenes — so that everything becomes operationalizable, calculable, and therefore faster.

Acceleration is thus the corollary of [[Positivity]]. In Ch. 1, Han writes that "the system of transparency abolishes all negativity in order to accelerate itself. Tarrying with the negative has given way to racing and raving in the positive." The negativity of the Other, of distance, of the gap, would impede the smooth circulation of capital, information, and communication; like-responding-to-like reaches maximum velocity. The same logic governs the [[Exhibition Value|exhibition]] regime: "Anesthetic hypercommunication reduces complexity in order to accelerate itself. It is significantly faster than sensory communication. The senses are slow" (Ch. 2). Acceleration here is the price paid by perception itself — the [[Aesthetic Contemplation|lingering gaze]] is replaced by tactile, reflex-driven contact.

The critical move comes at the end of Ch. 5: the real diagnosis is not acceleration but [[Dis-synchrony|temporal dis-synchrony]]. "The crisis of our times is not acceleration, but rather the scattering and dissociation of temporality." Time has become additive — a series of atomized presences without narrative gravity to bind them into "fragrant molecules." Because the problem is not speed but the loss of narrative tension, [[Deceleration]] cannot be the cure: "Deceleration alone produces no tact, no rhythm, no fragrance. It does not prevent falling into emptiness." Slowing down a dis-synchronous process leaves it just as senseless; what is missing is direction, threshold, and conclusion.

## Psychopolitics (Han) — immunosuppression for acceleration

In *Psychopolitics* Han recasts acceleration in immunological terms: speed is bought at the price of the body's and the psyche's defences. The idiot is *idiosyncratic*, and idiosyncrasy is a form of immune resistance — so when communication is sped up, otherness becomes friction to be eliminated. "When communication is to be accelerated, idiosyncrasy poses an obstacle inasmuch as it amounts to an immunological defence against the Other. Idiosyncrasy stands in the way of unbounded communicative exchange. Accordingly, immunosuppression is necessary for acceleration to proceed. But now, immune responses are subjected to massive suppression so that information will circulate faster and capital will accelerate" (ch. 13, "Idiotism"). The end-state of this suppression is the [[Hell of the Same]]: "Communication achieves maximum velocity when the Same reacts to the Same. In contrast, the resistance and recalcitrance of otherness, of foreignness, troubles and impedes the smooth communication of the Same." Acceleration is therefore not neutral throughput but a regime that requires the abolition of the Other; [[Idiotism]] — silence, idiosyncrasy, the recalcitrant outside — is precisely what it must suppress, and the [[Immunological Age]] (defence against the Enemy) is the epoch this immunosuppression brings to a close (`Directly stated` for the quotations; `Interpretive synthesis` for joining acceleration to idiotism and the Immunological Age across chapters).

The corollary on the affective side appears in ch. 9, "Emotional Capitalism." Because emotion is faster than reason, accelerated production emotionalizes itself: "Rationality is slower than emotionality; it has no speed, as it were. Thus, the pressure of acceleration now is leading to a dictatorship of emotion." Here acceleration does not only suppress otherness; it displaces the slow medium of rationality — "the medium of disciplinary society" — in favour of the dynamic, situative, performative register that [[Emotional Capitalism|emotional capitalism]] can exploit. The structural claim of *The Transparency Society* (only additive, complexity-reduced processes admit acceleration; "the senses are slow") thus returns as a claim about the psyche: what is slow — idiosyncrasy, immune defence, rationality, the sensory body — is what acceleration must strip away, so that information, capital, and emotion can circulate at maximum velocity (`Directly stated` for the quotations; `Strongly implied` for reading the rationality-to-emotionality shift as continuous with the Transparency-Society account of additive speed).

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — Acceleration is a *symptom*, not a *cause*. It is what becomes possible once narrative form has been hollowed out into addition; it is therefore inseparable from his account of [[Transparency]], [[Hypercommunication]], and the [[Exhibition Value|Society of Exhibition]]. In *Psychopolitics* (chs. 9 and 13) the same structural claim is given an immunological-political form: acceleration requires the *immunosuppression* of idiosyncrasy and otherness so that "the Same reacts to the Same" (the [[Hell of the Same]]), and "the pressure of acceleration now is leading to a dictatorship of emotion" — see [[#Psychopolitics (Han) — immunosuppression for acceleration]], [[Idiotism]], [[Immunological Age]], [[Emotional Capitalism]].
- **[[Guy Debord]]** — *Speculative connection.* Debord's [[Spectacle]] shares Han's diagnosis of a regime in which appearance saturates and immediate stimulation displaces lived experience; whether Debord theorizes acceleration as structurally as Han does is not established here. See `[[Guy Debord - The Society of the Spectacle]]`.
- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** — *Speculative connection.* McLuhan's account of media-driven speed and the global nervous system in `[[Marshall McLuhan - Understanding Media]]` may furnish a media-theoretic complement to Han's structural argument; this link is a commitment, not a claim.
- **[[Donella Meadows]]** — *Speculative connection.* The systems-thinking vocabulary of `[[Donella Meadows - Thinking in Systems]]` (delays, feedback loops, flows) may help formalize Han's dis-synchrony claim, but Han himself does not invoke systems theory.

## Related Concepts

- [[Transparency]] — Acceleration is its corollary; transparency operationalizes processes so they can be sped up.
- [[Positivity]] — Acceleration follows from the dismantling of negativity ("racing and raving in the positive").
- [[Narration vs Addition|Addition vs Narration]] — The structural condition: only additive processes admit acceleration.
- [[Hypercommunication]] — The accelerated mode of communication that bypasses sensory slowness.
- [[Exhibition Value]] — The image stripped to exhibition value is faster because it is unambiguous and complexity-free.
- [[Dis-synchrony]] — Han's preferred diagnosis: the real crisis behind apparent acceleration.
- [[Tarrying with the Negative]] — The Hegelian counter-practice that acceleration has displaced.
- [[Immunological Age]] — Acceleration is incompatible with immunological reaction; "immunosuppression is necessary for acceleration to proceed" (*Psychopolitics* ch. 13).
- [[Idiotism]] — The idiot's idiosyncrasy and silence are the recalcitrant otherness that acceleration must suppress; idiotism is its affirmative counter-figure.
- [[Hell of the Same]] — The terminus of accelerated communication: maximum velocity is reached "when the Same reacts to the Same."
- [[Emotional Capitalism]] — On the affective side, "the pressure of acceleration now is leading to a dictatorship of emotion," because rationality "is slower than emotionality."

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Ritual]] — Ritual has "its own temporality, rhythm and tact" and so refuses acceleration.
- [[Pilgrimage]] — A narrative event whose path is "rich in significance"; it cannot be hurried without becoming tourism.
- [[Deceleration]] — Cannot serve as cure because the problem is dis-synchrony, not speed.
- [[Narrative]] — Narration practices selection and threshold; it is the very form that addition lacks.

## Questions It Raises

- If [[Deceleration]] is not the cure, what counterpractice does Han actually propose? Does anything in *The Transparency Society* offer a positive alternative, or only diagnosis?
- Han distinguishes [[Experience|Erfahrung]] from [[Experiencing|Erlebnis]] (Ch. 5) — is acceleration what converts the former into the latter, or are they parallel symptoms of the same structural shift?
- How does Han's claim that "only additive processes admit acceleration" relate to [[Marshall McLuhan|McLuhan]]'s account of electric speed? Do their causal arrows run in opposite directions?
- If [[Ritual]] resists acceleration by virtue of its narrativity, what happens when rituals are *imitated* in accelerated form (e.g., online ceremonies)? Are they still rituals, or already processors?
- Is "dis-synchrony" a temporal claim, a social claim, or both — and what would re-synchronization even look like for Han?
