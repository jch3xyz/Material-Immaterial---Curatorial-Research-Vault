# Immaterial Labour

## Brief Definition

For [[Byung-Chul Han]], **immaterial labour** names the mode of production proper to neoliberalism — a mutated capitalism in which what is produced is no longer material objects but immaterial ones (information, programs, communication, the whole person), so that the body is released from production and the psyche becomes the central productive force.

## Longer Explanation

In *Psychopolitics* Han makes the shift to immaterial production the economic motor of his entire diagnosis. Neoliberalism is, for him, not a break with capitalism but "a further development - indeed, a mutated form - of capitalism," and the mutation is located precisely in *what* is produced. Where disciplinary [[Biopolitics]] "socializes the body in its productive capacity," neoliberalism "is not primarily concerned with 'the biological, the somatic, the corporal'. It has discovered the psyche as a productive force." The decisive sentence: "Now, immaterial and non-physical forms of production are what determine the course of capitalism. What gets produced are not material objects, but immaterial ones - for instance, information and programs." `Directly stated`. The body, no longer "a central force of production, as it formerly did in biopolitical, disciplinary society," is "being released from the immediate process of production and turning into the object of optimization, whether along aesthetic lines or in terms of health technology." `Directly stated`.

This is the **autonomist-Marxist hook** of Han's argument, and he runs it against the autonomist tradition itself. Han accepts the post-Marxist premise that production has become immaterial, but he inverts its political conclusion. Antonio Negri's cooperative "Multitude," "exalted as the post-Marxist successor to the 'proletariat'," does *not* describe contemporary conditions; instead, "conditions are defined by the solitude of an entrepreneur who is isolated and self-combating and practises auto-exploitation voluntarily." `Directly stated`. The structural consequence Han draws is that immaterial production dissolves the class system: "When production is immaterial, everyone already owns the means of production him- or herself. The neoliberal system is no longer a class system in the proper sense." `Directly stated`. With no working class dispossessed of the means of production, "no proletariat exists under the neoliberal regime at all," allo-exploitation gives way to [[Auto-Exploitation]], and "even class struggle has transformed into an inner struggle against oneself." Immaterial labour, for Han, is therefore the economic condition of possibility of the [[Achievement Society|achievement society]] and the [[Exploitation of Freedom]]: because the body is freed from production, productivity is "not to be enhanced by overcoming physical resistance so much as by optimizing psychic or mental processes." `Interpretive synthesis` (Han states each element; the causal chaining is the synthesis).

### The Integral Person

The far edge of immaterial labour is the absorption of the **whole person** into production. In "Emotional Capitalism" Han argues that the immaterial mode of production, "in which communicative interaction plays an ever-greater role," "calls not just for cognitive competence, but also for emotional competence. In this context, the integral person is installed in the very process of production." `Directly stated`. Where disciplinary society sought to "weed out" emotion and "make a shapeless mass of dough into an unfeeling machine," immaterial production conscripts emotion as raw material: "Now, sociality, communication and even individual conduct are being exploited." Emotion, Han stresses (citing André Gorz and Eva Illouz), "comes to possess value for capitalism only when a switch to immaterial production occurs" — "Emotions have become a means of production only in our own times." `Directly stated`. The endpoint is a psychopolitics that "manages to cut and operate deep inside" by "psychopolitically steering the integral person, the person as a whole." The integral person is thus not a separate concept but the limit-figure of immaterial labour: the point at which production no longer stops at the worker's output but installs the entire person — emotions, sociality, conduct — inside the process.

### Distinguishing the two senses of "immaterial"

The scope of this note keeps Han's sense deliberately distinct from the vault's other uses of "immaterial." Han's **immaterial production** is a *Marx/Negri/Gorz* category: it concerns the *content of what is produced* under post-industrial capitalism (information, programs, communication, affect) and is fundamentally a thesis about labour, value, and exploitation. This differs from the dematerialization discussed under [[Dematerialization]] (Lippard, the disappearance of the art *object*) and from the disembodiment of information discussed under [[Embodiment]] and [[The Body as Data]] (Hayles, O'Gieblyn, the conceit that information could circulate without a material substrate). The three converge only at the [[Material vs Immaterial]] tension, where they should be cross-linked but not conflated. `Interpretive synthesis` — the distinction is the vault's, drawn to keep the autonomist-Marxist and the dematerialization/disembodiment lineages from collapsing into one another.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — Immaterial production is the economic premise of *Psychopolitics*: neoliberalism as "a mutated form of capitalism" that produces "not material objects, but immaterial ones," releases the body from production, discovers "the psyche as a productive force," and — at the limit — "installs" "the integral person... in the very process of production." Because everyone "already owns the means of production him- or herself," it abolishes the class system and converts external exploitation into [[Auto-Exploitation]]. `Directly stated`.
- **Karl Marx** — `Strongly implied`. Han argues *through* and *against* Marx: he accepts the framework of forces vs. relations of production but holds that "industrial capitalism has now mutated into neoliberalism and financial capitalism, which are implementing a post-industrial, immaterial mode of production - instead of turning into communism." Marx appears here as a forward-stub (no Marx volume in `raw/`); his role is the dialectic Han claims the immaterial mode has outrun.
- **Antonio Negri** — `Strongly implied`. Negri's "Multitude" is the autonomist-Marxist theorist of immaterial production Han names and rejects: the immaterial-labour thesis is borrowed, the revolutionary "Multitude" against "Empire" is dismissed as "yet another illusion." Forward-stub.
- **André Gorz** — `Directly stated` (cited). Han's source for the corporate installation of the whole person is Gorz's *The Immaterial: Knowledge, Value and Capital* (the Daimler-Chrysler quotation in "Emotional Capitalism" is drawn from Gorz). Forward-stub.

## Related Concepts

- [[Psychopolitics]] — immaterial production is its economic substrate; the turn to the psyche as productive force is what makes psychopolitics, rather than biopolitics, the form of government.
- [[Auto-Exploitation]] — the labour relation immaterial production installs: when "everyone already owns the means of production," exploitation becomes self-exploitation.
- [[Achievement Society]] — the social formation that immaterial, self-owned production produces; its "entrepreneur of himself" is the immaterial labourer.
- [[Exploitation of Freedom]] — the mechanism by which immaterial labour extracts value: not physical coercion but the harnessing of emotion, play, communication, and the whole person.
- [[Emotional Capitalism]] — the chapter and concept in which immaterial production is shown to make emotion "a means of production" and to install "the integral person" in production.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Biopolitics]] — `Directly stated`. The disciplinary regime immaterial labour displaces: biopolitics "fundamentally concerns the biological and the physical" and "socializes the body in its productive capacity," precisely the body that immaterial production "releases... from the immediate process of production."
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — the cross-author tension this note feeds. Han's "immaterial production" sense (content of production, labour, value) is held distinct from Lippard's dematerialization and Hayles/O'Gieblyn's disembodiment, all of which meet at this tension. `Interpretive synthesis`.

## Questions It Raises

- **Is the body really "released"?** Han says immaterial production frees the body from the production process, turning it into "the object of optimization" (fitness, cosmetic surgery, health technology). But if "sexiness and fitness represent new economic resources to be increased, marketed and exploited," is the body released from production — or re-conscripted into it under another name?
- **Does immaterial production abolish class, or only conceal it?** Han claims that when "everyone already owns the means of production," the class system dissolves and "this is what accounts for the system's stability." Is the disappearance of the proletariat a real change in the relations of production, or an ideological effect — the "illusion... that every person... is capable of unlimited self-production"?
- **Material vs immaterial.** How far should Han's labour-theoretic "immaterial" be aligned with, or insulated from, the dematerialization of the art object ([[Dematerialization]]) and the disembodiment of information ([[The Body as Data]])? Where exactly do the lineages touch, and where would conflating them mislead?
- **The integral person and the limits of exploitation.** If immaterial production "installs" the whole person — emotions, sociality, conduct — in production, is there any remainder of the person left outside the productive process? What, if anything, resists installation?
- **Autonomist inversion.** Han keeps Negri's diagnosis (immaterial production) but reverses its politics (no Multitude, only the isolated auto-exploiting entrepreneur). Is the optimistic autonomist conclusion wrong, or is Han describing a later, captured phase of the same tendency?
