# Technoetic Aesthetics

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's name for the art-specific aesthetic of the technoetic program: the displacement of the classical concern with the surface appearance and representation of things by an aesthetics of creative consciousness and artificial life — the medium of art becomes mind, not matter.

## Longer Explanation

Technoetic aesthetics is the *art-side* of [[Technoetics]] (Ascott's umbrella term for the convergence of technology and consciousness, *techne* + *noetic*). Where technoetics names a whole culture, condition, and "principle," technoetic aesthetics names what that turn does specifically to art: it shifts art's object from the appearance of the world to the cultivation of mind. The two are kept distinct here and cross-linked, mirroring the vault's [[Systems Esthetics]] vs `Systems Theory` distinction — the umbrella field versus its art-specific aesthetic [`Interpretive synthesis`].

The core formula is a displacement. In his glossary *Technoetic Aesthetics: 100 Terms and Definitions for the Post-Biological Era* Ascott defines the term in a single line: "The classical concern with the surface image of the world gives way to the technoetic aesthetics of creative consciousness and artificial life" [`Directly stated`]. The contrast is between *surface* and *consciousness*, between the appearance of a thing and the life of a mind. The same glossary restates it under the entry "Art": "While traditionally focused on the appearance of things and their representation, art is now concerned with processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence" [`Directly stated`] — art moves from the noun (the depicted object) to the verb (the process), and ultimately from representation to [[Artificial Life|artificial life]] itself.

What replaces the image is consciousness as both subject and substance. In *Weaving the Shamantic Web* Ascott argues "the primacy of consciousness as both the context and content of art, and the object and subject of study" [`Directly stated`]. This is the decisive move that makes the aesthetic *technoetic* rather than merely technological: the artwork is not a surface to be looked at but, as he puts it, "an enactment of mind" — "Art as an enactment of mind implies an intimate level of human interaction within the system, which constitutes the work of art, an art without audience in its inactive mode" [`Directly stated`]. The technoetic artist abandons the "passive voyeur, the traditional gallery viewer" for "a kind of widespread intimacy, closeness on the planetary scale." The aesthetic is therefore inseparable from interaction, reciprocity, and the system — it is the late-1990s development of the two-way, [[Reciprocal Communication|reciprocal communication]] Burnham theorized in 1968.

Ascott presents this not as one option among many but as the destiny of the discipline: "the technoetic principle will be at the centre of art as it develops, and consciousness in all its forms will be the field of its unfolding" [`Directly stated`]. The aesthetic is unbounded by medium and by discipline. He describes "a new creative practice, unnamed and unbounded," which "crosses the domains of art, science, technology, and philosophy, but is centred in none. Its principal concern is with mind, states of being, and the process of becoming" [`Directly stated`]. The proper medium of this art is therefore not paint, bronze, light, or even data, but mind — which is why technoetic aesthetics is best read as the consciousness-centred horizon Ascott proposes for [[New Media Art|new-media practice]] and for [[Art as Experiential Technology|art conceived as an experiential technology]] rather than as a style or movement [`Strongly implied`].

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — the originator. Technoetic aesthetics is the art-specific aesthetic of his late, third-phase "technoetic" turn: the supplanting of the "surface image of the world" by an aesthetics of "creative consciousness and artificial life," with consciousness as the context, content, subject, and object of art, and the artwork as an "enactment of mind."

## Opposing Concepts

- The classical image-based aesthetic — the "classical concern with the surface image of the world" and "the appearance of things and their representation," which technoetic aesthetics explicitly supersedes.
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — technoetic aesthetics sits hard on the immaterial pole of the vault's keystone tension: its medium is mind, not matter, so it stands against the material-object tradition while remaining cross-linked to that tension.

## Questions It Raises

- If the medium of art becomes mind rather than matter, what is left for the art *object*, and can an "enactment of mind" still be exhibited, collected, or conserved?
- Does displacing the "surface image" by "creative consciousness" dissolve aesthetics into philosophy of mind and systems design — and if a practice is "centred in none" of art, science, technology, or philosophy, is it still recognizably art?
- Can an aesthetic of "processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence" avoid the "phantom audience" Ascott warns against, or does it merely relocate spectatorship inside the system?
- Is technoetic aesthetics distinct from technoetics, or is the "art" qualifier a distinction without a difference once consciousness is declared the field of art's unfolding?
