# The Extrapolated Body

## Brief Definition

**The vault's synthesis term** for the body extended, recoded, prostheticized, or projected beyond its material bounds into informational, virtual, or prosthetic circuits — the gathering-figure for a cluster of moves that [[N. Katherine Hayles]] documents (and largely contests) in *How We Became Posthuman*, where the lived body is treated as a base to be enhanced, replaced, or downloaded out of.

## Longer Explanation

A note on provenance first, because it governs everything below. "The extrapolated body" is **the vault's own framing phrase**, not Hayles's. A full-text search of the source chapters confirms she never writes "extrapolated body"; the word "extrapolate" appears in her prose only in unrelated senses (extrapolating a model from tissue, extrapolating a theory to an unwarranted conclusion). This note therefore does not put the phrase in her mouth. What it does is gather the *textually supported* ideas in Hayles that the vault's term points at — the body extended, the body as prosthesis, consciousness extrapolated out of the body, body boundaries up for grabs — and label every connective move as `Interpretive synthesis` rather than as Hayles's direct claim. Confidence on the underlying Hayles material is high; confidence on the *concept as a unified thing* is `medium`, because the unity is the vault's, not hers.

The strongest anchor Hayles actually provides is the posthuman premise that the body is a base for extension. She names, as a defining assumption of the posthuman point of view, that it "thinks of the body as the original prosthesis we all learn to manipulate, so that extending or replacing the body with other prostheses becomes a continuation of a process that began before we were born" (`Directly stated`). This is the seed of what the vault calls extrapolation: once the body is recast as the *first* prosthesis, every further extension — cybernetic, informational, virtual — is licensed as more of the same. Hayles links this directly to the [[The Cyborg|cyborg]], whose construction depends on "informational pathways connecting the organic body to its prosthetic extensions," and to the [[Feedback Loops|feedback loop]], which implies "that the boundaries of the autonomous subject are up for grabs, since feedback loops can flow not only within the subject but also between the subject and the environment" (`Directly stated`). The body, in these passages, stops being a bounded given and becomes an open circuit extensible into its surroundings. `Interpretive synthesis`: reading "original prosthesis," "prosthetic extensions," and "boundaries... up for grabs" *together* as one figure of the extrapolated body is the vault's connective move, not a synthesis Hayles performs under that name.

The figure runs from soft to extreme. At the soft end is Marshall McLuhan, whom Hayles reports as theorizing media "as technological prostheses" and a process of "autoamputation" that "facilitates and requires compensating technological extensions that project the body-as-prosthesis back out into the world" (`Directly stated`, as Hayles's report of McLuhan). At the extreme end is Hans Moravec's downloading fantasy — consciousness extrapolated entirely out of the body — which opens the book: the robot surgeon reads the brain layer by layer into a computer until "the cranial cavity is empty, and the patient, now inhabiting the metallic body of the computer, wakens to find his consciousness exactly the same as it was before" (`Directly stated`, as Hayles's account of Moravec; see [[N. Katherine Hayles and Hans Moravec]]). Between the poles sits [[Virtuality|virtual reality]], where Hayles attests from experience that "subjectivity is dispersed throughout the cybernetic circuit" and "the relevant boundaries for interaction are defined less by the skin than by the feedback loops connecting body and simulation in a technobio-integrated circuit" (`Directly stated`). The crucial interpretive point — and the reason this concept is ambivalent rather than celebratory — is that Hayles endorses only the embodied end of this spectrum. Her own corrective is that virtuality is "not a question of leaving the body behind but rather of extending embodied awareness" locally and materially; extension that *recovers* the body she affirms, extrapolation that *discards* it she calls a "nightmare." So the extrapolated body, as the vault names it, sits exactly on the fault line of [[Embodiment vs Information]]: it is the body imagined as extensible-and-replaceable, against which Hayles sets the body that is instantiated, local, specific, and not replicable.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[N. Katherine Hayles]]** — supports the *components* of this concept, with care about which she endorses. Directly stated and hers: the posthuman treats "the body as the original prosthesis"; the cyborg runs on "informational pathways connecting the organic body to its prosthetic extensions"; feedback loops put "the boundaries of the autonomous subject... up for grabs." Reported by her (others' positions she largely contests): McLuhan's "technological extensions that project the body-as-prosthesis back out into the world"; Moravec's downloading of consciousness out of the cranial cavity. Her own commitment is the counter-pole: extension that recovers [[Embodiment|embodiment]], not extrapolation that leaves the body behind.
- **The phrase "extrapolated body" itself** — **vault framing, not Hayles.** Flagged plainly: she does not use it. Treat this note as a vault-level synthesis that collects her supported material under a convenient label, not as exposition of a Hayles term. Any reader following a `[[The Extrapolated Body]]` link elsewhere in the vault should read it as the vault's shorthand for "the body extended/recoded/projected beyond its material bounds," and should not quote the phrase back to Hayles.

## Related Concepts

- [[The Body as Data]] — the limit case of extrapolation: the body not merely extended but recoded as portable informational pattern, "data made flesh," such that the flesh can in principle be left behind. The extrapolated body becomes the body-as-data once extension hardens into substrate-independence.
- [[The Cyborg]] — the figure in which extrapolation is embodied; the splice "connecting the organic body to its prosthetic extensions" is literally where the body gets extended into device.
- [[Virtuality]] — the cultural condition in which extension into the cybernetic circuit becomes ordinary; Hayles's "embodied virtuality" is the *good* (body-recovering) reading of the same extension.
- [[Embodiment vs Information]] — the axis this concept sits on; extrapolation tilts toward the informational pole, embodiment toward the material.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Embodiment]] — the counter-pole and the body that *resists* extrapolation. Where the extrapolated body is extensible and replaceable, embodiment is "always instantiated, local, and specific" and "can be destroyed, but it cannot be replicated." This is the direct opposition Hayles herself draws: the body whose specificity makes it un-downloadable is precisely the rebuttal to the body imagined as "the original prosthesis." `Interpretive synthesis` only in that the vault names the opposed figure "the extrapolated body"; the opposition itself is Hayles's.

## Questions It Raises

- For a curatorial practice working with [[Material vs Immaterial]] objects: when an artwork extends the body into a sensor rig, a data-stream, or an avatar, is it *extending* embodied awareness (Hayles's affirmed sense) or *extrapolating* the body away into pattern ([[The Body as Data]])? Where, concretely, is the line in a given new-media piece?
- If the body is "the original prosthesis," does new-media art that prostheticizes the body merely continue a process "that began before we were born," or does it stage a break — and which framing does the work itself invite the viewer to adopt?
- Hayles affirms extension that is "highly specific, local, and material" and rejects extension that "leav[es] the body behind." Can an installation hold both at once — exhilarating dispersal "throughout the cybernetic circuit" *and* the irreplaceable specificity of embodiment — or does the medium structurally privilege one?
- Because "the extrapolated body" is the vault's term and not Hayles's, what is gained and lost by curating under a coined concept? Does naming the cluster clarify the research, or does it risk attributing to Hayles a unity she deliberately withholds?
