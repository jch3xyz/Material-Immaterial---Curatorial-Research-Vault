# The Cybernet

## Brief Definition

The cybernet is Roy Ascott's name for the planetary sum of all interactive computer-mediated systems and telematic networks, treated not as an external tool but as a part of the human sensory apparatus — the substrate that carries [[Cyberception]] and realizes [[The Holomatic Principle]].

## Longer Explanation

For Ascott the cybernet is not infrastructure we use but a faculty we have grown. "The cybernet, the sum of all the interactive computer-mediated systems and telematic networks in the world, is part of our sensory apparatus" (Directly stated). Because it belongs to the apparatus of sensation rather than standing outside it, the cybernet performs a double redefinition: it "redefines our individual body, just as it connects all our bodies into a planetary whole." The single organism and the planetary collective are reconfigured in the same gesture — the boundary of the body is no longer the skin but the reach of the net.

The cybernet is the *carrier*; [[Cyberception]] is the human side of the same coupling. Where cyberception is the augmented faculty of perceiving and conceiving across the network, the cybernet is the medium that faculty perceives *through* and *into*. Ascott makes the dependency explicit: "Cyberception gives us access to the holomatic media of the cybernet" (Directly stated). This is why [[The Holomatic Principle]] is a property of the cybernet itself — every access node holds, equally and at the same time, all the data flowing through the network, so to be at any one interface is to be in the virtual presence of all the others. The cybernet is what makes that telematic unity a material fact of the medium rather than a metaphor.

Ascott characterizes the cybernet capaciously as "the sum of all those artificial systems of probing, communicating, remembering, and constructing that data processing, satellite links, remote sensing, and telerobotics variously serve in the enhancement of our being" (Directly stated). It is at once a sensory, mnemonic, and *constructive* organ — "also the agent of realisation and construction," extending through robotic systems, intelligent environments, and artificial organisms. The cybernet thus reaches outward as a planetary nervous system: "We are networked to the universe, our nervous systems are suffusing the cosmos" (Directly stated). This image is the literalization, in Ascott's 1994 telematics, of the cosmic-scale extension McLuhan had earlier framed as [[The Global Village]] — the electric extension of the central nervous system, now named as a concrete planetary substrate (Interpretive synthesis).

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — the cybernet is the named planetary substrate: the worldwide aggregate of interactive, computer-mediated, telematic systems, treated as part of the human sensory apparatus. It both individuates (redefines the body) and totalizes (connects all bodies into a planetary whole), and it is the carrier on which [[Cyberception]] operates and through which [[The Holomatic Principle]] holds.
- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** (resonance, not citation) — the cybernet realizes in built telematic networks what McLuhan theorized as the electric extension of the central nervous system and the planetary compression of [[The Global Village]]; Ascott's "our nervous systems are suffusing the cosmos" extends the McLuhanite figure to cosmic scale (Speculative connection).

## Related Concepts

- [[Cyberception]] — the human faculty; the cybernet is its carrier and the medium it perceives through.
- [[The Holomatic Principle]] — a structural property of the cybernet (every node holds the whole); cyberception gives access to its holomatic media.
- [[Network Consciousness]] — the distributed mind that arises across the cybernet; the cybernet is its physical/technical substrate.
- [[The Global Village]] — McLuhan's earlier name for the planetary compression the cybernet concretizes.
- [[Cybernetics]] — the control-and-communication science whose computer-mediated systems the cybernet aggregates.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — the cybernet sits across this tension rather than on one side: it is a literal material infrastructure (satellite links, data processing, telerobotics) that nonetheless dematerializes the body's boundary into "the limitless boundaries of cyberspace," replacing dense materiality with "environmental fluidity."

## Questions It Raises

- If the cybernet is "part of our sensory apparatus," is disconnection a form of sensory deprivation — and who controls the apparatus?
- Can a substrate that simultaneously "redefines our individual body" and "connects all our bodies into a planetary whole" preserve any individuated subject, or does it dissolve the individual into [[Network Consciousness]]?
- Where Ascott reads the cybernet as an enhancement of being, what is the relation between his celebratory planetary nervous system and the surveillant, control-oriented readings of the same networked substrate (the [[Digital Panopticon]], Dataism)?
