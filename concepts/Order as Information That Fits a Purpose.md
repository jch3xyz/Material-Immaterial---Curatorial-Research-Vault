# Order as Information That Fits a Purpose

## Brief Definition

Kurzweil's definition of **order** as information measured not by quantity but by *fitness to a purpose* — the metaphysical hinge that lets him claim evolution has a direction (toward ever-greater order) without committing him to the weaker claim that it simply piles up complexity.

## Longer Explanation

In Chapter 2 of *[[The Singularity Is Near]]*, [[Ray Kurzweil]] builds a careful ladder of distinctions before asserting that evolution is directional. Orderliness is not order; complexity is not order; information is not noise. A predictably alternating pattern such as `0101010...` is *orderly* yet carries almost no information. **"Noise"** is a random sequence: unpredictable but informationless. **[[Information]]** is also unpredictable — but meaningful in a process, "such as the DNA code of an organism or the bits in a computer program." Order, finally, is the term that ties information to *ends*: "Order is information that fits a purpose. The measure of order is the measure of how well the information fits the purpose. In the evolution of lifeforms, the purpose is to survive." (Directly stated.)

The decisive move is that order is **purpose-relative and graded by success**, not by size. Measuring complexity has proposed metrics; measuring order requires "a measure of 'success' that would be tailored to each situation" — what an evolutionary algorithm calls a *utility function*. This is why Kurzweil insists that **increasing complexity is not the goal of evolution**: "Evolution results in better answers, not necessarily more complicated ones. Sometimes a superior solution is a simpler one." (Directly stated.) Improving a solution "usually increases but sometimes decreases complexity — [yet] increases order," and so, summarizing, "evolution increases order, which mayor [*sic*, OCR for 'may or'] may not increase complexity (but usually does)." Order, not complexity, is the conserved upward quantity, and "in [the] evolutionary process order increases exponentially" — the bridge that joins this concept to the [[The Law of Accelerating Returns|Law of Accelerating Returns]]. (Directly stated; bracketed correction flags OCR noise in the raw file.)

The same notion of *purposeful versus random* organization silently structures Chapter 3's computation triad (rock / human brain / "ultimate laptop"). The atoms in an ordinary rock are furiously active — "all of this activity represents computation, even if not very meaningfully organized" — yet "the rock is not performing any useful work aside from perhaps acting as a paperweight or a decoration," because "the structure of the atoms in the rock is for the most part effectively random." Reorganize "the particles in a more purposeful manner" and the *same matter* becomes a planet-beating computer. The explicit aim of these comparisons is "to assess just how far biological evolution has been able to go from systems with essentially no intelligence (that is, an ordinary rock, which performs no useful computation) to the ultimate ability of matter to perform purposeful computation." (Strongly implied: Ch. 3 never re-invokes the phrase "order is information that fits a purpose," but its rock/laptop distinction *is* that distinction — random vs. purposeful organization of identical matter — applied to physics. Interpretive synthesis that Ch. 2 and Ch. 3 turn on one idea.)

This concept is the ground on which Kurzweil's larger commitments stand. If order is purposeful information and matter's destiny is "purposeful computation," then mind is a pattern (see [[Patternism]]), the universe is a substrate for computation (see [[The Computational Universe]]), and history has a measurable, accelerating arrow. It is also where Kurzweil is most exposed: "purpose" and "success" are imported as given (survival, engine efficiency, economic value), so the apparent objectivity of "order" rests on a prior, unargued choice of utility function. (Interpretive synthesis.)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Ray Kurzweil]]** — *The Singularity Is Near*, Ch. 2 (canonical home) and Ch. 3. Order = information that fits a purpose; measured by fitness/utility, not magnitude. Evolution increases order, which *usually but not always* increases complexity. In biology the purpose is survival; in an evolutionary algorithm it is the programmer-supplied utility function; in technology it can be economic success. Ch. 3 transposes the random-vs-purposeful contrast onto physics (the rock vs. the "ultimate laptop").
- **[[N. Katherine Hayles]]** — supplies the critical counter-frame. In *How We Became Posthuman* information is reconceived through [[Pattern and Randomness]], and Hayles resists the move that treats meaningful pattern as substrate-independent and self-evidently directional. Where Kurzweil reads order *off* a purpose, Hayles reads the very notion of disembodied, purpose-fitting information as a historically contingent construction. (Interpretive synthesis; no Kurzweil quote in Hayles' text — connection by the vault, not by either author.)
- **[[Yuval Noah Harari]]** — *Homo Deus* shares Kurzweil's premise that [[Organisms Are Algorithms]] processing [[Information]], and the [[Dataism]] creed treats "value = contribution to data processing." Kurzweil's "order that fits a purpose" is a near-cousin of Dataism's purpose-laden valuation of information flow, though Harari narrates it as an emerging *religion* rather than a law of nature. (Strongly implied parallel; Harari does not cite this passage.)

## Related Concepts

- [[Information]] — the genus; order is its purpose-fitted species. Kurzweil's chain runs noise → information → order.
- [[The Law of Accelerating Returns]] — order is the quantity LOAR claims grows exponentially; this note supplies LOAR's definition of what is accelerating.
- [[Paradigm Shift and the S-Curve]] — the *mechanism* by which order accumulates; each paradigm is a new, more efficient way of embodying purposeful information.
- [[Patternism]] — if mind is purposeful information (order), it is a pattern that can in principle be preserved across substrates.
- [[The Computational Universe]] — Ch. 3's claim that matter's "ultimate ability" is "purposeful computation" is the cosmological extension of this concept.
- [[Emergence]] — rising order is Kurzweil's optimistic gloss on what emergence produces over evolutionary time.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Pattern and Randomness]] — Hayles' dyad. Kurzweil treats the pattern (order) as the meaningful, directional term and randomness as mere noise to be overcome; Hayles holds pattern and randomness in productive, non-hierarchical tension and denies that meaningful information floats free of its material embodiment.

## Questions It Raises

- If order is purpose-relative, who or what sets the purpose? Survival and utility functions are *given* in Kurzweil's account — does the objectivity of "order" then quietly smuggle in a chosen value?
- Can "the ability of matter to perform purposeful computation" name a purpose intrinsic to matter, or only one projected onto matter by an observer?
- If a superior solution can be a *simpler* one, why does the Singularity narrative still privilege escalating complexity and capacity?
- Does treating mind as purpose-fitting information (order) presuppose [[Patternism]], or independently support it?
