# The Shamantic Web

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's coinage — a deliberate fusion of *shamanic* and *semantic* — naming the bio-telematic art practice that interweaves the navigation of consciousness with the construction of meaning.

## Longer Explanation

The shamantic web is the figure Ascott uses to name what bio-telematic art is doing. In the title essay of chapter 30, "Weaving the Shamantic Web," he describes a practice that braids together two activities he takes to be one: the shamanic navigation of states of consciousness, and the semantic construction of meaning. The portmanteau is exact and intentional — "combining the sense of shamanic and semantic, the navigation of consciousness and the construction of meaning" (Directly stated). The "web" is the telematic network itself, woven not only of cables and protocols but of minds in transit between fields of experience.

Ascott grounds the figure in fieldwork rather than metaphor. He pairs immersion in advanced virtual-reality systems with initiation into Amazonian ritual — "under the influence of the computer and of the plant, albeit an extremely powerful computer and a particularly potent plant (ayahuasca, the 'vine of the soul')" — in order to research an apparent parallelism between shamanic space and telematic space (Directly stated). What links them is what he elsewhere calls [[Cyberception]] and the "double consciousness": the shaman is at once in the everyday world and navigating other psychic worlds; the telematic artist moves through cyberspace while remaining accommodated within the material world. The shamantic web is the art that holds both gazes open at once.

This double commitment is why the concept sits at the heart of Ascott's [[Technoetics]] — his term for the technologically enabled study and construction of consciousness. The shamantic web reframes the network not as an information channel but as a medium for the structuring of psychic forces. Ascott draws the contrast through the shamanic ceremony, which is "performative but is not intended as a public performance" — an enactment of mind, not a spectacle for a "phantom audience" (Directly stated). Interactive art on the shamantic web should likewise be "an enactment of mind," an "art without audience in its inactive mode," speaking to "a kind of widespread intimacy, closeness on the planetary scale" (Directly stated). The construction of meaning is not interpretation of a pre-given world but its negotiation and re-creation through interaction — what Ascott's "hermeneutic error" entry calls writing "our own reality" (Strongly implied).

Chapter 32, the alphabetical glossary "Technoetic Aesthetics: 100 Terms and Definitions for the Post-Biological Era," distills the same fusion under the entry *Shamantics*: "Foregrounding the semantic aspect of shamanism in the technoetic context" (Directly stated). Where the chapter-30 figure stresses the *shamanic* pole (the navigation of consciousness), the glossary entry stresses the *semantic* pole (the construction of meaning) — the two faces of the one coinage, which the [[The Post-Biological Era|post-biological]] artist works simultaneously.

- [[Telematic Art]] — the networked art practice; "bio-telematic art" is the variant Ascott says is weaving the shamantic web.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — the shamantic web works against any settled materiality, treating the network as a medium for immaterial psychic forces and the negotiated construction of reality rather than for the transmission of fixed, embodied objects.

## Questions It Raises

- If meaning is "negotiated through interaction" rather than read off a pre-given world, what becomes of the artwork as a stable, attributable object?
- Does pairing "plant technology" (ayahuasca) with computer technology as equivalent routes to "double consciousness" risk flattening a specific Indigenous ritual practice into a metaphor for cyberspace?
- Can an "art without audience" — an enactment of mind that refuses the "phantom audience" — still function as public, sharable culture, or only as private psychic enactment?
- Is the navigation of consciousness genuinely continuous with the construction of meaning, or does the portmanteau paper over a gap between altered states and semantic content?
