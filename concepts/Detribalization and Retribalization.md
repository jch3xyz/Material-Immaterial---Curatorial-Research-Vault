# Detribalization and Retribalization

## Brief Definition

McLuhan's paired claim that specialist, uniform, repetitive (hot, mechanical) technologies — phonetic literacy, print, money, the clock, the wheel — fragment and *detribalize* traditional collective life, while the nonspecialist electric technology of much greater speed *retribalizes*, restoring an intense, total, tribal involvement: this reversal is the mechanism that produces the global village.

## Longer Explanation

For McLuhan the two terms name a single reversible process keyed to the *speed* and *definition* of a medium. Hot, "specialist" technologies — the phonetic alphabet, print, money, the wheel, the mechanical clock — break the closed, all-involving tribal world into fragmented, lineal, visual, individualist functions. He states the principle most crisply at the end of "Media Cold and Hot": "Specialist technologies detribalize. The nonspecia ist electric technology retribalizes" — the verb pair that gives this concept its name (`Directly stated`; the second sentence is garbled in the raw OCR — the intended text reads "The nonspecialist electric technology retribalizes"). The detribalizing engine is above all phonetic literacy: in `Number: Profile of the Crowd` he writes that "Phonetic letters and number were the first means of fragmenting and detribalizing man," and in `The Written Word: An Eye for an Ear` the new literate "is emotionally free to separate from the tribute and to become a civilized individual." (`Directly stated`)

Retribalization is detribalization run in reverse at electric speed. A hot medium fragments; "a very much greater speedup, such as occurs with electricity, may serve to restore a tribal pattern of intense involvement such as took place with the introduction of radio in Europe" (`Directly stated`). The decisive variable is rate: McLuhan repeatedly couples retribalization to the *instant* character of electric information, which "does not enlarge, but involves, the family of man in the cohesive state of village living" (`Number`). The classic case study is radio — the "tribal drum" whose "power to retribalize mankind, its almost instant reversal of individualism into collectivism, Fascist or Marxist, has gone unnoticed" — which makes plain that for McLuhan retribalization is value-neutral and can be politically violent, not a sentimental return to community. (`Directly stated`)

The reversal is symmetrical and global: as the West retribalizes, the formerly tribal world is being detribalized by the West's older hot technology. "Western man is himself being de-Westernized by his own new speed-up, as much as the Africans are being detribalized by our old print and industrial technology" (`Roads and Paper Routes`, `Directly stated`). McLuhan reads this as a literal cultural break-boundary — a "break boundary at which the system suddenly changes" — and gives it a mythic form through Joyce: "Western man enters his tribal, or Finn, cycle once more, following the track of the old Finn, but wide awake this time as we re-enter the tribal night" (Ch3, `Directly stated`). The "wide awake this time" supplies the difference between the original preliterate condition and the new one: electric retribalization is a *conscious* re-entry into total involvement, which is why McLuhan can say in `Games` that "We have begun retribalizing with the same painful groping with which a preliterate society begins to read and write" (`Directly stated`).

The concept is minted separately from `The Global Village` because Chapter 2 states the underlying *mechanism* most crisply, where the Global Village names its *result*. The two are tightly bound: in `Roads and Paper Routes` the detribalizing speed-up of wheel and road built the empires that fragmented the city-state, and the electric speed-up reassembles "all its mechanized bits into an organic whole. This is the new world of the global village" (`Strongly implied` for the explicit causal pairing between this concept and that one).

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** — coins the verb pair *detribalize / retribalize* in *Understanding Media* and applies it across Part II case studies (the alphabet and print in `The Written Word`, number in `Number`, the clock in `Clocks`, the wheel and road in `Roads and Paper Routes`, gambling and team sports in `Games`, and above all radio in `Radio: The Tribal Drum`). For McLuhan detribalization correlates with hot/specialist/visual/lineal media and retribalization with cool/nonspecialist/electric/acoustic media; the discriminating factor is the *speed* of information movement. (`Directly stated`)

## Related Concepts

- [[The Global Village]] — the *result* of retribalization: electric implosion contracts the world "to village size," reassembling fragmented functions into "an organic whole." This concept is the mechanism behind that one.
- [[Media Hot and Cold]] — the underlying media taxonomy: hot, specialist, high-definition media detribalize; cool, nonspecialist, low-definition, participational media (and electric media generally) retribalize.
- [[The Medium Is the Message]] — detribalization/retribalization is an *effect of the medium's form*, independent of program content: "the effects of radio are quite independent of its programming" (`Radio`).

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Media Hot and Cold]] — listed both as related and as the axis of opposition *within* the concept: the two poles (detribalize / retribalize) line up with the two poles of the hot/cool distinction. The opposition is internal to the process rather than to a separate rival concept.

## Questions It Raises

- If retribalization is value-neutral — equally capable of producing "collectivism, Fascist or Marxist" — what, if anything, distinguishes a benign global village from electronic mass mobilization?
- McLuhan insists the new tribal involvement is entered "wide awake." Is conscious, mediated retribalization actually different in kind from preliterate tribal involvement, or only in self-awareness?
- The detribalizing technologies (alphabet, print, clock) underwrote individual conscience, privacy, and "point of view." If electric speed dissolves them, what becomes of the individualist subject those technologies created?
- Does the symmetry hold — is the "de-Westernization" of the West by its own speed-up genuinely parallel to the detribalization of formerly tribal cultures by Western print and industry, or does McLuhan flatten a real asymmetry of power?
