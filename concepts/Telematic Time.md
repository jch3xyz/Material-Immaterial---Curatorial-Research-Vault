# Telematic Time

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's name for the networked, asynchronous, non-linear temporality of telematic culture — a "schizo-time" of "layered phase-space, discontinuous periodicity" and "branching time-pathways" lived in "deep data space," where the dispersal of intelligence across a global network dissolves the single fixed perceiving point that linear time presupposes.

## Longer Explanation

For Ascott, linear time is an artifact of a particular perceiving system: it "implies a fixed point in a perceiving system, a self-standing intelligence." Once intelligence is no longer located at a single self-standing point but is **dispersed** across a planetary network of terminals and processing clusters, time itself changes character. He states the condition precisely: "Time becomes something other when our intelligence is dispersed, when the perceiving system is global in its gaze, and when interactions between parts of the network are asynchronic rather than serial or simultaneous." (Directly stated.) Telematic time is thus a *consequence* of [[Network Consciousness]] and [[Distributed Cognition]] — when authorship is dispersed and "memory becomes time-free and global in its dimensions," serial and simultaneous time give way to something else.

Ascott triangulates this "new time" through three strands — ESP (media), PDP (parallel data processing), and PU (parallel universes) — and names the result: "Time, the new time, a kind of schizo-time, dominates the process." (Directly stated.) The figure is not deficit but proliferation: the discontinuity of the computer's clock-pulsed, "discrete units" of operation, the "mosaic screen of digital discontinuity," and the "layering and associative interweaving of data" all model a time that is non-linear, non-local, and branching rather than flowing. The culminating formula gathers these into a single image: "schizo-time, layered phase-space, discontinuous periodicity takes us into hyper time, the branching time-pathways of parallel universes." (Directly stated.)

This temporality is **affirmative and creative**, not pathological. Ascott reads it through the many-worlds interpretation of quantum physics (PU): "As artists, we move through parallel universes, shifting in and out of timeframes, phase shifts, tunnelling through one set of realities into other worlds." The telematic artwork is never encountered twice the same way — "to insert oneself into the flow of data … is never to interact with the same set of data … twice," a Heraclitean river. To pursue these aspirations "in deep data space, in the digital space-time of the computer, fully PDP and networked, is to bring art into resonant co-existence with physics." Time becomes a medium to "play with," to "layer," and to recombine in "imaginative discontinuities" — a generative, branching field, the temporal correlate of [[Data Space]]. (Strongly implied — Ascott frames the postmodern impetus "to play with time, to layer experience" as not yet "exhausted" and maps it positively onto telematic practice.)

It is essential to hold telematic time **distinct from** [[Acceleration]] in the Han/McLuhan sense. Han's acceleration is a pathology of speed and the loss of narrative duration; Ascott's telematic time is not faster linear time but *non-linear* time — its disruption of the line is the source of its creative power, not a symptom of crisis. Where acceleration names a temporal disorder to be diagnosed, schizo-time names a temporal expansion to be inhabited. (Interpretive synthesis — the contrast is drawn across two distinct vault sources; Ascott himself does not name acceleration.)

## Related Concepts

- [[Data Space]] — the spatial correlate; telematic time is the temporality *of* deep data space, "the digital space-time of the computer."
- [[Network Consciousness]] — the dispersed, global perceiving system whose emergence *causes* the shift away from linear time.
- [[Distributed Cognition]] — "intelligence is amplified, authorship is dispersed"; the dispersal of intelligence is the precondition for telematic time.
- [[Telematic Art]] — the practice that "play[s] with time" in branching, non-repeating, asynchronous structures.
- [[Telematic Discourse]] — the asynchronous "negotiat[ion] for meaning" across networks that telematic time conditions.
- [[The Telematic Embrace]] — the affirmative networked encounter within which this temporality is lived.
- [[Virtuality]] — "actual or virtual," "neither precisely here nor there"; telematic time is bound to the virtual/actual oscillation of the many-worlds frame.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Acceleration]] — the keystone contrast. Han's (and McLuhan's) acceleration is *pathological speed* — faster linear time, the erosion of duration and narrative. Ascott's telematic time is *non-linear, branching, affirmative* — not the line sped up but the line dissolved. Linear time presupposes "a fixed point in a perceiving system"; telematic time arises precisely where that fixed point is gone.

## Questions It Raises

- If linear time "implies a fixed point in a perceiving system," does the dissolution of that point dissolve the experiencing subject as well, or relocate it into the network?
- Is "schizo-time" a genuinely new lived temporality, or a metaphor borrowed from quantum physics and clinical pathology to aestheticize the asynchrony of network latency?
- Can Ascott's affirmative branching time and Han's pathological acceleration both be true of the same networked condition — is the difference in the time itself, or only in how it is inhabited?
- Does grounding art's temporality in the many-worlds interpretation commit the work to a contested physics, and what survives if that interpretation is rejected?
