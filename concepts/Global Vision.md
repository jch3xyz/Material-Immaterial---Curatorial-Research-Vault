# Global Vision

## Brief Definition

The new, holistic, integrative *order of perception* constituted by the telematic network — a planet-spanning "distributed sensorium and distributed intelligence" that together provide a global-scale viewing of structures, systems, and events. It is the **perceptual register** of the network, distinct from the network's [[Network Consciousness|consciousness]].

## Longer Explanation

For [[Roy Ascott]], a telematic network "is more than the sum of its parts, more than a computer communications web." Its surplus is a transformation of *perception* itself. The new order of perception the network constitutes "can be called 'global vision,' since its distributed sensorium and distributed intelligence — networked across the whole planet … together provide for a holistic, integrative viewing of structures, systems, and events that is global in its scope" (Directly stated). Global Vision is therefore not a metaphor for being well-informed; it names a literal extension and *re-engineering* of the human perceptual apparatus, an "artificial extension of human intelligence and perception" that "not only amplifies human perception but is in the process of changing it" (Directly stated).

The shift from observation to participation is the precondition of Global Vision. Borrowing John Wheeler's quantum analogy — "cross out that old word 'observer' and put in its place 'participator'" — Ascott declares that "the focus of the aesthetic shifts from the observed object to participating subject, from the analysis of observed systems to the (second-order) cybernetics of observing systems: the canon of the immaterial and participatory" (Directly stated). Perception is no longer the passive reception of a world "sitting out there" behind plate glass; it is "the product of active negotiation rather than passive reception," carried out "within this evolving symbiosis of human/machine" through "the worldwide artificial reticular sensorium" (Directly stated). This is why Global Vision belongs to the perceptual register specifically: it is the *seeing* proper to a participator embedded in the data field, not the *consciousness* that emerges from the network (which Ascott treats separately as a planetary "network consciousness" or "noosphere"), and not the later, sharpened faculty he will name [[Cyberception]] (Interpretive synthesis).

Ascott images Global Vision spatially as the **horizontal**, against the vertical of Renaissance perspective. The vertical, "head-to-toe viewing of Renaissance space," is a "tunnel vision," "emblems often as not of aggression, competition, and dominance." The horizontal, by contrast, "is a metaphor for the bird's-eye view, the all-over, all-embracing, holistic systems view of structures, relationships, and events — viewing that can include the ironic, the fuzzy, and the ambiguous," and "this is precisely the condition of perception and insight to which telematic networking aspires" (Directly stated). Global Vision thus participates in "the overarching ambition of both art and science throughout this century: to make the invisible visible" — the computer "deals invisibly with the invisible," stretching perception "laterally away from the tunnel vision, from the Cartesian sight lines of the old deterministic era," so that "our sensory experience becomes extrasensory" (Directly stated).

Crucially, Global Vision is **produced, not merely received**. "Telematic culture means, in short, that we do not think, see, or feel in isolation." The network "amplifies the individual's capacity … for more informed perception, by enabling her to participate in the *production* of global vision through networked interaction with other minds, other sensibilities, other sensing and thinking systems across the planet" (Directly stated). Perception here is distributed across human and artificial nodes — the seeing-correlate of [[Distributed Cognition]] and of Ascott's [[Reciprocal Communication]].

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** (*Telematic Embrace*, ch. 20, 1990) — the coiner. Global Vision is the perceptual order of the telematic network: the distributed sensorium/intelligence yielding a holistic, horizontal, planet-scale way of seeing, predicated on the observer→participator shift and on "the canon of the immaterial and participatory." It is one of three coordinated Ascott terms: [[Network Consciousness]] (the consciousness register), Global Vision (the perceptual register), and the later refinement [[Cyberception]] (Directly stated / Interpretive synthesis on the three-term coordination).
- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** (variant precedent, not Ascott's term) — Ascott's horizontal "global vision" extends McLuhan's [[The Global Village]] and his account of electric media as a [[The Technological Extension of Consciousness|technological extension of consciousness]]; both describe a planetary nervous system that abolishes detachment and conscripts the whole sensorium. McLuhan frames the same reversal as a tension between [[Detachment vs Participation|detachment and participation]] (Interpretive synthesis).

## Related Concepts

- [[Network Consciousness]] — the **consciousness** register of the same network; Global Vision is its **perceptual** correlate. Kept distinct by design (the consciousness note vs. the perception note); the network's "subtle body"/"noosphere"/"planetary consciousness" is consciousness, while its "distributed sensorium … holistic, integrative viewing" is perception.
- [[Cyberception]] — Ascott's **later refinement** (later chapters) of telematic perception; Global Vision is the earlier, broader formulation in ch. 20. Cross-linked as the maturation of the same perceptual thesis.
- [[Telematic Art]] / [[Telematic Art]] — the practice and the cultural formation whose perceptual register Global Vision names.
- [[Distributed Cognition]] — Global Vision is, in effect, distributed *perception*: a sensorium spread across human and artificial nodes.
- [[The Global Village]] — McLuhan's planetary, retribalizing perceptual field that Ascott's horizontal "global vision" extends and digitizes.
- [[The Technological Extension of Consciousness]] — McLuhan's electric extension of the central nervous system; Ascott's "artificial extension of human intelligence and perception" is its perceptual specification.
- [[Reciprocal Communication]] — the two-way interactive model that makes participatory (hence distributed) perception possible.
- [[New Media Art]] / [[Art as Experiential Technology]] — the art-historical categories (realized by Burnham, extended by Ascott) within which Global Vision is the perceptual stake.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Detachment vs Participation]] — McLuhan's tension names exactly what Global Vision overcomes: the Cartesian "observer safely separated" by "a 20 centimetre slab of plate glass." Global Vision is perception *after* the observer becomes the participator; the vertical "tunnel vision" of detached Renaissance/Cartesian sight is its opposite pole.

## Questions It Raises

- If perception is "produced" collectively at the interface, in what sense does an individual still see for herself? Ascott insists the network "amplifies the individual's capacity," but does distributed perception preserve or dissolve a singular point of view?
- Global Vision is offered as emancipatory — making the invisible visible, escaping tunnel vision. How does it sit beside the surveillance reading of the same "worldwide artificial reticular sensorium" (the [[Digital Panopticon]], Dataism)? Is a planetary distributed sensorium a liberation of perception or its capture?
- What is the precise boundary between Global Vision (perception) and [[Network Consciousness]] (consciousness)? Ascott runs them close together — where does enhanced seeing end and emergent planetary mind begin?
- Is Global Vision continuous with [[Cyberception]], or does the later term mark a genuine conceptual break in how Ascott theorizes telematic perception?
