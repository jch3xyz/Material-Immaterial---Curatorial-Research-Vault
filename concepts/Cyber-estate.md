# Cyber-estate

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's coinage for "the interfaces, networks, circuits and sensors of telematic educational structures" — the immaterial infrastructure of the [[The Planetary Collegium|Planetary Collegium]], set explicitly against "real estate."

## Longer Explanation

Cyber-estate is one of the four "new ways of being and doing" Ascott names in his 1994 essay *The Planetary Collegium: Art and Education in the Post-Biological Era* (alongside [[Telenoia]], [[Inter-Reality]], and [[Cyberception]]). Where the other three name qualities of consciousness, presence, and perception, cyber-estate names the *infrastructural* term: it is the material-immaterial substrate on which the emergent learning organism runs. Ascott defines it telegraphically — "Cyber-estate: the interfaces, networks, circuits and sensors of telematic educational structures. Not real estate!" (`Directly stated`, ch. 26) — and the punning closure ("Not real estate!") carries the whole argument. The coinage is built by substitution: the academy of the object-era was constituted by its *real estate* (the campus, the atelier, the lecture hall, the ivory tower); the academy of the telematic era is constituted instead by its *cyber-estate* (the connective apparatus of the net).

The term is therefore the infrastructural correlate of the dematerialization Ascott is tracking across the chapter, in which "the aesthetic of the material object" gives way first to "electronic flow" and then to "the metaphor of emergence." Cyber-estate is what the institution becomes when it dematerializes: not a place one occupies but a network one is "on." Ascott makes this dissolution explicit in his closing argument about how the academy must react to market and on-line pressures: "The dissolution of the university as real estate will not occur overnight. University investment in cyber-estate is only just beginning. But our plans as educationalists must be anticipatory" (`Directly stated`, ch. 26). The real-estate/cyber-estate pair thus does double duty — it diagnoses an ongoing institutional transformation and prescribes where investment and imagination should now go.

Of Ascott's four 1994 coinages, cyber-estate is the smallest and most chapter-bound — it does not recur with the load-bearing frequency of [[Telenoia]] or [[Cyberception]], and reads as a tactical, even rhetorical, coinage minted for the education argument rather than a standing pillar of his vocabulary. It is nonetheless durable because it names a real and persistent thing the other three do not: the *infrastructure* of telematic culture, the circuits and sensors and interfaces that the consciousness-terms presuppose but do not themselves describe. It is the term in which Ascott's idealist, consciousness-first program acknowledges that mind-at-large still runs on hardware — sensors, circuits, networks — even as that hardware is reframed from owned property into connective flow.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — sole originator and user. Cyber-estate is his coinage, introduced in the 1994 *Planetary Collegium* essay as one of four "new ways of being and doing." The single canonical spelling is hyphenated, "Cyber-estate," following Ascott's own usage. The term is anchored in this chapter; it does not recur across the *Telematic Embrace* corpus with the frequency of his keystone coinages.

## Related Concepts

- [[The Planetary Collegium]] — cyber-estate is its infrastructure; the Collegium is the "emergent organism" of distributed learning, cyber-estate the "interfaces, networks, circuits and sensors" that organism inhabits instead of a physical campus.
- [[Telenoia]] — the sibling coinage from the same passage; telenoia names the *consciousness* of distributed connectivity, cyber-estate the *apparatus* that carries it.
- [[Inter-Reality]] — the sibling coinage naming the fuzzy state between the virtual and the real; cyber-estate is part of the infrastructural ground (alongside VR, AL, and natural systems) within which inter-reality is located.
- [[Cyberception]] — the sibling coinage naming the augmented perceptual faculty; the "sensors" of cyber-estate are among the technological extensions through which cyberception operates.
- [[Dematerialization]] — cyber-estate is the institutional/infrastructural form of dematerialization: the university dematerialized from real estate into network.
- [[The Global Village]] — McLuhan's earlier name for the electric, all-at-once collectivity whose connective wiring cyber-estate names as the new ground of education.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — cyber-estate is the explicitly *immaterial* pole of the real-estate/cyber-estate opposition Ascott coins; "Not real estate!" is a compressed assertion of the material-to-immaterial shift, extending the cross-author tension to the institution itself.

## Questions It Raises

- "Not real estate!" — but the interfaces, networks, circuits and sensors of cyber-estate are themselves owned, powered, and physically sited (servers, fiber, devices). Does the coinage obscure the very material substrate Ascott elsewhere insists telematic art does not abandon?
- Ascott offers cyber-estate as liberation from the ivory tower and the campus; yet in the same chapter he warns that corporations are "turning their attention to global education as the next big market." If the university dematerializes into cyber-estate, who owns the cyber-estate?
- Is cyber-estate a durable concept on the order of telenoia and cyberception, or a tactical pun bound to the 1994 education argument? What is lost, and what gained, when an institution is reconceived as infrastructure rather than place?
