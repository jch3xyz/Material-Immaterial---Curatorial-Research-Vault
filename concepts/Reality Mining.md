# Reality Mining

## Brief Definition

The 2005 method coined by MIT's Alex (Sandy) Pentland and his student Nathan Eagle: exploiting the ubiquitous mobile-phone infrastructure together with wearable behavioral monitors to uncover rules and structure in the behavior of individuals and organizations — the move that extended behavioral dispossession from virtual experience to actual and social experience, and pointed the way toward the "reality business."

## Longer Explanation

In Zuboff's account, reality mining is a technological and cultural landmark in the expansion of surveillance capitalism's raw-material supply. Pentland and Eagle saw that it would be possible to exploit the increasingly "ubiquitous infrastructure" of mobile phones and combine those data with new streams from wearable behavioral monitors. The result, Zuboff writes, "was a radical new solution that Pentland and Eagle called 'reality mining.'" Mentor and student demonstrated that cell-phone data "can be used to uncover regular rules and structure in the behavior of both individuals and organizations" — thereby furthering the capture and analysis of [[Behavioral Surplus]]. (`Directly stated`)

What makes reality mining pivotal for Zuboff is its *vector*: it announced that "reality" itself was "now fair and feasible game for surplus capture, search, extraction, [[Rendition|rendition]], datafication, analysis, prediction, and intervention." This points "the way toward the larger shift in the nature of behavioral dispossession from virtual, to actual, to social experience." Where earlier surveillance capitalism mined the virtual exhaust of online clicks and searches, reality mining instruments the body and the street — the "digital breadcrumbs" left as people go about their daily lives — and finally targets society itself as a "first class object" for computation and control. (`Directly stated`)

For Pentland, this was never neutral science. From the start, Zuboff notes, he "understood reality mining as the gateway to a new universe of commercial opportunities," asserting in 2004 that wearable devices with "computational horsepower" would provide the "foundation" for reality mining as an "exciting new suite of business applications." Businesses could use a privileged grasp of "reality" to shape behavior toward their objectives. The practices reality mining inaugurated, Zuboff argues, "would eventually become the '[[The Reality Business|reality business]].'" (`Directly stated`)

Zuboff situates reality mining within Pentland's larger program — his "[[Instrumentarian Power|instrumentarian]]" worldview and his 2014 *Social Physics*, which "completes Skinner" by outfitting and measuring human behavior pervasively for modification, control, and profit. Reality mining is thus the practical instrument of [[Applied Utopistics|applied utopistics]]: the "God's-eye view," the [[Big Data|big-data]] dream of total, continuous knowledge of "virtually all of humanity." It is the method by which lived reality is converted into surplus — the connective tissue between [[Affective Computing|affective computing]], digital omniscience, and the eventual datafication of the social. (`Interpretive synthesis`)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — uses "reality mining" as a *diagnostic* term, quoting Pentland and Eagle's own coinage to mark the historical threshold at which [[Behavioral Surplus|behavioral surplus]] capture leapt "from virtual, to actual, to social experience." For Zuboff it is the bridge between online [[Rendition|rendition]] and the [[The Reality Business|reality business]], and a contemporary signal of the instrumentarian worldview taking hold among data scientists.
- **Alex "Sandy" Pentland (via Zuboff)** — the term's celebrant: reality mining is "an exciting new suite of business applications," a "God's-eye view," and the empirical foundation of [[Instrumentarian Power|social physics]]. Zuboff reports his framing in order to "infer the theory behind the practice," not to endorse it.

## Related Concepts

- [[The Reality Business]] — the commercial enterprise reality mining inaugurates; reality mining is its method, the reality business its market.
- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — the raw material reality mining captures and extends from online exhaust to bodily and social behavior.
- [[Rendition]] — the conversion of human experience into data; reality mining is rendition pushed into the "actual" and "social" registers.
- [[Big Data]] — the aggregate of "digital breadcrumbs" reality mining produces and feeds.
- [[Quantified Self]] — the self-tracking culture whose wearable monitors reality mining repurposes from voluntary self-measurement into involuntary commercial surplus.
- [[Surveillance]] — reality mining is a concrete technique of ubiquitous, sensor-based surveillance.
- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — the form of power reality mining serves; Pentland's "social physics" is its theory.
- [[Affective Computing]] — a parallel rendition technology (Picard) that, like reality mining, extends capture into embodied and emotional experience.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[The Right to Sanctuary]] — the claim to inviolable space that reality mining's "God's-eye view" and ambition to mine the "actual" and the "social" would abolish.

## Questions It Raises

- If behavioral dispossession moves "from virtual, to actual, to social experience," is there any register of human life left outside the reach of surplus capture?
- Reality mining repurposes the wearable instruments of the [[Quantified Self]] — does self-tracking inevitably become hetero-tracking once the same data become commercially valuable?
- Pentland frames reality mining as a "God's-eye view" for the "greater good" — whose good, when the means of behavioral modification are owned by surveillance capitalists?
- What is the material status of "reality" once it is declared "fair and feasible game" for extraction — does the term name lived experience, or only its datafied double? (cross-link [[Material vs Immaterial]])
