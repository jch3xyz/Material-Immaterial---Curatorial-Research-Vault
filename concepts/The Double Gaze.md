# The Double Gaze

## Brief Definition

The perceptual correlate of double consciousness: the faculty of seeing, at one and the same time, both the inward realities of psychic/virtual space and the outward surfaces of the everyday world — and, in its later reciprocal form, of being seen, heard, and felt by a responsive environment that returns the gaze.

## Longer Explanation

For [[Roy Ascott]], the double gaze names the *seeing* that belongs to a divided, navigable field of awareness. Where double consciousness is the *state* of being simultaneously present in two distinct fields of experience, the double gaze is its visual operation: it is, in his words, "seeing at once both inward realities and the outward surfaces of the world." Ascott introduces it in the shamanic register of "Weaving the Shamantic Web" — the pagé who in altered states "sees the world through different eyes" — and explicitly twins this psi-perception with the technologically aided faculty of [[Cyberception]]. The double gaze is thus the perceptual capacity that cyberception and shamanic vision share. `Directly stated`

Ascott grounds the concept in first-person report. Under ayahuasca he enters "a state of double consciousness," able to move freely between his familiar self and "a totally separate state of being," inhabiting at once two bodies — the everyday phenomenological body and a second body "made up of a mass of multicoloured particles." His "visual field, [his] double gaze, alternated, at choice, between the coherent space of everyday reality and a fractal universe." The double gaze is therefore not a fusion but an alternation held under will: the artist navigates between registers rather than collapsing them. `Directly stated`

The stakes are ontological. Because "the prosthesis of vision can be at one and the same time instrumental in constructing what is envisioned" — "to see in the mind's eye is to realize in the material world" — the two worlds the double gaze opens "become less distinctly separate. The immaterial and material lose their categorical distinction." Here the double gaze becomes Ascott's perceptual engine for the move from [[Apparition|appearance to apparition]] and for the dissolution staged by [[Material vs Immaterial]]: seeing is no longer reception of surfaces but active, constructive participation in what comes to be. `Strongly implied`

By the lexicon of "Technoetic Aesthetics" the gaze has become *reciprocal*. Under the entry "Double gazing," Ascott writes that we now "see, hear, and feel in ways unknown to biological man, just as the environment increasingly hears, sees, and feels us." The technological figure is exact: "With retina-tracking lasers, the artist's gaze is returned; the walls have ears, and buildings will speak volumes." The double gaze is no longer only the subject's two-way vision but a circuit between subject and a sentient, instrumented environment — the perceptual face of Ascott's [[Moistmedia]] world, where surveillance and aesthetic responsiveness are folded into one another. `Directly stated`

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — The double gaze is the perceptual correlate of double consciousness. In ch.30 ("Weaving the Shamantic Web") it is the alternating inward/outward vision shared by the shaman's psi-perception and the artist's [[Cyberception]]; in ch.32 ("Technoetic Aesthetics") it becomes reciprocal "double gazing," where the instrumented environment sees, hears, and feels us in return (retina-tracking lasers return the gaze). `Directly stated`

## Related Concepts

- [[Cyberception]] — the technologically aided faculty of which the double gaze is the visual mode; Ascott twins it explicitly with the shaman's psi-perception, both being "a matter of the double gaze."
- [[Apparition]] — what the double gaze brings forth: a constructive seeing that "realize[s] in the material world" rather than merely registering surfaces (appearance).
- [[Technoetics]] — Ascott's science of consciousness-and-technology; the double gaze is one of its constitutive perceptual capacities.
- [[Moistmedia]] — the bio-electronic, responsive environment in which the reciprocal "double gazing" of ch.32 (the walls that hear, the buildings that speak) takes place.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Appearance vs Apparition]] — the double gaze is what tips perception from the pole of appearance (the "outward surfaces of the world") toward apparition; it holds the tension by seeing both at once rather than resolving it.

## Questions It Raises

- If "the prosthesis of vision" both perceives and constructs what is envisioned, can the double gaze still be called perception, or has it become a kind of making?
- The reciprocal "double gazing" of ch.32 fuses aesthetic responsiveness with surveillance — the environment that sees us. Is the returned gaze emancipatory (a new repertoire of senses) or a figure of the [[Digital Panopticon]]?
- Ascott derives the double gaze from a hallucinogenic state held "at choice." How far does volitional alternation survive once the gaze is offloaded to retina-tracking machines that look back?
