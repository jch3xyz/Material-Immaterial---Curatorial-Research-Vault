# The Reality Business

## Brief Definition

Shoshana Zuboff's name for the phase of surveillance capitalism in which extraction migrates from the online environment into the physical world, so that *all aspects of human experience* — every place, body, gesture, mood, and depth of the self — are claimed as raw-material supplies and targeted for rendering into behavioral data.

## Longer Explanation

The reality business is a *forward-anchor* concept in *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*: Zuboff names it in the book's opening orientation (Part I) as the subject of Part II, where it is developed under the operative term **rendition**. In Chapter 1 she frames it as the migration of the extractive logic — first perfected on clicks, searches, and posts — out of the screen and into lived space: "Here we explore this new reality business, as all aspects of human experience are claimed as raw-material supplies and targeted for rendering into behavioral data" (`Directly stated`). What had been a business of harvesting the digital traces of behavior becomes a business of annexing reality itself.

The driver is competitive: as rivalry over [[Prediction Products]] that "approximate certainty" intensifies, surveillance capitalists need supplies that are not merely abundant but *predictive*, which means closer to the source. In Chapter 7 Zuboff dates the threshold to a new level of competitive intensity defined by **scope** (the variety and reach of supply) and **action** (intervention in behavior), which "ratchets up the invasive character of supply operations and initiates a new era of surveillance commerce that I call the reality business" (`Directly stated`). The reality business therefore couples economies of scope and action with machine-based extraction architectures planted in and permeating the real world — the fulfillment, she argues, of ubiquitous computing's old promise that computational processes will "weave themselves into the fabric of everyday life," now turned to the interests of surveillance capitalists. Its ambition is nothing less than "the annexation of 'reality' to its kingdom of conquered human experience" (`Directly stated`).

Rendition is how the reality business operates. It has two dimensions. **Extension** (Chapter 8) casts the net wide across the surface of daily life: "Extension wants every corner and crevice, every utterance and gesture on the path to dispossession. All that is moist and alive must hand over its facts" (`Directly stated`) — the migration of [[The Body as Data]] and the [[Quantified Self]] from voluntary self-tracking into involuntary, ambient capture. **Depth** (Chapter 9) sends "submarines" into the interior — personality, emotions, endorphins, the "self" — rendering even inwardness as measurable behavior. Zuboff describes the escalation as a sequence of crawls: "from web crawling to reality crawling to life crawling to self crawling" (`Directly stated`).

The reality business does not stop at knowing reality; it moves to shaping it. In Chapter 5 Zuboff narrates Google Maps and Street View as "the migration from an online data source to a real-world monitor to an advisor to an active shepherd — from knowledge to influence to control," a copilot whose repertoire is to "push and pull, suggest, nudge, cajole, shame, seduce" (`Directly stated`). Chapter 10 supplies the demonstration: John Hanke's *Pokémon Go* "proved that surveillance capitalism could operate in the real world much as it does in the virtual one, using its unilateral knowledge (scale and scope) to shape your behavior now (action) in order to more accurately predict your behavior later" (`Directly stated`). The reality business is thus continuous with [[Behavioral Surplus]] and [[Behavioral Futures Markets]]: it is the real-world frontier where surplus is mined and futures in human behavior are traded.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — coins *the reality business* as the name for surveillance capitalism's migration off the screen and into the physical world, realized through *rendition* (extension into space and bodies; depth into the self) and consummated through *economies of action* that intervene in behavior. It is the moment extraction stops being a feature of the online environment and becomes a claim on reality as such.
- Resonant but distinct: [[Big Data]] and [[Dataism]] (Han, Harari) name the *datafication* of the world as worldview and ideology; Zuboff's reality business specifies the *commercial logic of accumulation* that drives datafication into physical space and converts it into prediction products and revenue.

## Related Concepts

- [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — the reality business is its second-phase elaboration: extraction migrating from the online environment to the real world.
- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — the raw material the reality business chases into ever more intimate corners and depths.
- [[Behavioral Futures Markets]] — the markets the reality business stocks; real-world, real-time trading in behavioral futures.
- [[Prediction Products]] — competition over products that "approximate certainty" is the pressure that pushes extraction into reality.
- [[The Body as Data]] — extension's primary target: the living body's "facts" handed over to the supply chain.
- [[Quantified Self]] — voluntary measurement turned, by the reality business, into involuntary ambient rendition.
- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — the form of power the reality business instruments through ubiquitous architectures of extraction and execution.
- [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]] — economies of action, the reality business's turn from knowing behavior to shaping it.
- [[The Division of Learning in Society]] — the asymmetry of knowledge that lets the reality business observe what was unobservable and do what was undoable.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — Zuboff's name for the elemental human capacity (to imagine, intend, promise) that the reality business, by shaping behavior now to guarantee outcomes later, directly threatens.
- [[Surveillance]] — the reality business radicalizes ordinary surveillance into ubiquitous, ambient, world-pervading capture; the contrast marks the difference between watching reality and annexing it.

## Questions It Raises

- If "all that is moist and alive must hand over its facts," what (if anything) of human experience remains outside the supply chain — and can a "right to sanctuary" or to the future tense be defended against ambient extraction?
- Where is the line between *rendering* reality (knowing it) and *making* reality (shaping behavior to guarantee outcomes)? Does the reality business erase that line?
- How does the migration of extraction into physical space change the [[Material vs Immaterial]] relation — is the body's "facts" the point where immaterial data re-materializes as the contested ground?
- Is datafication of the world inevitable, as the surveillance capitalists' "inevitabilists" insist, or is the reality business a contingent commercial choice that could be refused?
