# Immunological Age

## Brief Definition

For [[Byung-Chul Han]], the **Immunological Age** is the historical paradigm in which experience is structured by *immunological negativity* — a defence reaction against the Other, the Alien, the Enemy, organized on a Manichaean opposition of Good and Evil. Shock, as a violent intervention against an adversary, is its emblematic operation.

## Longer Explanation

Han introduces the term in the chapter on **Shock**, reading Naomi Klein's *Shock Doctrine* through the figure of Dr Ewen Cameron ("Doctor Shock"), the Montreal psychiatrist whose CIA-funded electroshock experiments aimed to "de-brand" and "re-brand" the psyche. Cameron's anticommunism, his likening of patients to "communist prisoners of war," and the structure of his practice all rest, Han writes, on "the Manichaean opposition between Good and Evil": "Evil would be wiped away, eradicated, and replaced by Good." This is what makes Cameron exemplary of an *epoch*: "As Doctor Shock, Cameron represents a phenomenon of the Immunological Age. The immunological intervention of shock therapy takes aim at the Other, the Alien, the Enemy" (`Directly stated`).

The Immunological Age is thus defined by **[[Negativity]]** — the negativity of defence, threat, and annihilation. Shock therapy "owes its efficacy to paralyzing and annihilating the contents of the psyche"; "its essential trait is negativity." Han marks this as a *disciplinary* and biopolitical mode of power, distinct from the neoliberal regime that succeeds it. Where the immunological/disciplinary register works by negative threat against an Enemy, neoliberal psychopolitics "is dominated by [[Positivity]]. Instead of working with negative threats, it works with positive stimuli." The Immunological Age names, in other words, the era *before* the dissolution of immunological boundaries — the era of the recognizable adversary against whom a defence can still be mounted (`Strongly implied`).

Chapter 13, **Idiotism**, supplies the concept's inverse and clarifies why Han periodizes it as passing. Acceleration is incompatible with immunological reaction: "When communication is to be accelerated, idiosyncrasy poses an obstacle inasmuch as it amounts to an immunological defence against the Other." Because idiosyncrasy "stands in the way of unbounded communicative exchange," Han concludes that "immunosuppression is necessary for [[Acceleration]] to proceed." The accelerated, hyper-connected present therefore *suppresses* immune response so that "information will circulate faster and capital will accelerate." Its terminal condition is not defence against the Other but its abolition: "Communication achieves maximum velocity when the Same reacts to the Same" — the [[Hell of the Same]], where "the resistance and recalcitrance of otherness, of foreignness, troubles and impedes the smooth communication of the Same" (`Directly stated`).

Read across the two chapters, the Immunological Age is one term of a two-stage diagnosis: immunological negativity (defence, shock, the Enemy, disciplinary society) gives way to post-immunological positivity (smoothness, the Same, accelerated communication, neoliberal psychopolitics). The Other that the Immunological Age combated is, in the present, no longer expelled but dissolved — there is no Enemy left to immunize against (`Interpretive synthesis`).

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — *Psychopolitics*: the Immunological Age is the paradigm of immunological negativity, defence against the Other/Alien/Enemy on a Manichaean Good/Evil structure (ch. 7, Cameron as its emblem). Its inverse is the accelerated present, which requires the *immunosuppression* of idiosyncrasy and otherness so that "the Same reacts to the Same" (ch. 13). The concept belongs to a durable Han periodization that distinguishes a negative, immunological epoch from a positive, post-immunological one.

## Related Concepts

- [[Negativity]] — the Immunological Age is the epoch *of* negativity (threat, defence, annihilation); shock's "essential trait is negativity."
- [[Hell of the Same]] — the post-immunological terminus, reached once immune defence against the Other is suppressed and "the Same reacts to the Same."
- [[Acceleration]] — incompatible with immunological reaction; "immunosuppression is necessary for acceleration to proceed."
- [[Smoothness]] — the smooth communication of the Same that immunological recalcitrance "troubles and impedes."
- [[Society of Control]] — neoliberal "total communication and total surveillance," the post-immunological power against which idiotism is set.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Positivity]] — the structural opposite epoch: neoliberal psychopolitics "is dominated by positivity… it works with positive stimuli," seducing rather than shocking the soul.
- *The Same* (post-immunological condition) — abolishes the Other/Enemy that the Immunological Age was organized to repel; rendered here as plain text pending a dedicated note.

## Questions It Raises

- If the present is post-immunological, does the disappearance of the Enemy also remove the very *negativity* through which resistance, distance, and otherness were possible?
- Is "immunosuppression for the sake of acceleration" a description of a power that no longer needs an outside — and what political form does opposition take once defence against the Other is itself the obstacle?
- How does the immunological model of the Other relate to Han's later, fuller periodizations (e.g. in *The Burnout Society*)? (Cross-book; flagged for consolidation.)
- Does Han's Manichaean reading of Cold War shock practice over-tidy the historical record, or is the Good/Evil structure doing diagnostic rather than merely descriptive work?
