# Inter-Reality

## Brief Definition

Roy Ascott's name for the "fuzzy state between the virtual and the real" — the everyday, half-real, half-virtual zone, located between virtual reality, artificial life, and natural systems, in which our social, cultural and educational life increasingly takes place.

## Longer Explanation

Inter-Reality (abbreviated IR) is Ascott's term for the lived condition of a population that no longer sits cleanly on either side of the screen. In "The Planetary Collegium" (ch.26) he describes students who are "half in school and half in cyberspace," who "live between the virtual and the real" and are "on the Internet more often than out of it"; this dissolution of the boundary between online and offline is, for Ascott, "the advent of inter-reality (IR), the space we are all most likely to inhabit for the next many years" (Directly stated). IR is therefore not a special destination one enters and exits — it is the ordinary medium of existence once telematic networks saturate culture.

Crucially, Ascott situates IR *relationally*, between three coordinates: "IR is located between VR, AL, and natural systems" — that is, between [[Full-Immersion Virtual Reality]], [[Artificial Life]], and the biological/ecological world (Directly stated). It is neither the total immersion of VR nor unmediated nature; it is the unstable, negotiated overlap. His insistence "Not phoney reality!" marks the polemical edge of the term: IR is not a counterfeit or degraded version of the real but a genuine new ontological field, where "reality is seen as the product of human negotiation and construction" and the former passive viewer "now negotiates and navigates what is largely a world of her own making" (Directly stated).

Inter-Reality is one of "Four new ways of being and doing" Ascott names as arising within telematic culture and feeding his vision of a planetary collegium, alongside [[Telenoia]] ("distributed mind-at-large"), Cyber-estate (the network's interfaces and sensors as the academy's new infrastructure), and [[Cyberception]] ("emergent human faculty of technologically augmented perception/conception") (Directly stated). Within this quartet IR is the *spatial/ontological* member — the where, against telenoia's distributed mind, cyberception's augmented faculty, and cyber-estate's material substrate.

IR belongs to the constructivist, anti-representational thrust of Ascott's late work: the university becomes "an interface to inter-reality, ... a doorway to a radical constructivism, a way into building their own world," and art's task shifts from "providing content and meaning" to creating "contexts and systems" the viewer manipulates (Strongly implied — Ascott draws IR into his pedagogy of self-constructed worlds). As a half-real, half-virtual presence realised "in telepresence, set in cyberspace," IR is a precursor to the later, more biological *moist* convergence of his "the future will be moist!" period (Interpretive synthesis — the chapter predates and anticipates that turn; the dry-digital / wet-biological fusion is developed in later chapters, here only foreshadowed by the placement of IR "between VR, AL, and natural systems").

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Roy Ascott]]** — coins Inter-Reality (IR) in "The Planetary Collegium" as a "fuzzy state between the virtual and the real," located between VR, AL, and natural systems; one of his four "new ways of being and doing," and the everyday space "we are all most likely to inhabit." Emphatically *not* "phoney reality": a real, constructed, negotiated field, the precursor to his later moist convergence.

## Related Concepts

- [[The Distributed Self]] — the self that inhabits IR: half-real, half-virtual, distributed across the network rather than localized in one body or place.
- [[Virtuality]] — IR is defined *against* the purely virtual; it is the overlap of the virtual with the real, not the virtual alone.
- [[Telepresence]] — the mode through which half-real, half-virtual presence is "realised" within IR ("new forms of human presence, half real, half-virtual ... realised in telepresence").
- [[Nature II]] — Ascott's reconceived, technologically mediated nature; IR's siting "between VR, AL, and natural systems" feeds his later rethinking of the natural as a second, constructed order.
- [[Cyberception]] — fellow member of the four "new ways of being and doing"; the augmented faculty of perception by which the inhabitant of IR apprehends it.
- [[Telenoia]] — fellow member of the four; the distributed "mind-at-large" whose collective negotiations produce the constructed reality of IR.
- [[Artificial Life]] — one of the three coordinates between which IR is located.
- [[Emergence]] — Ascott frames the cultural field of IR through "the metaphor of emergence" and "unbounded neural networks, in an infinite connectivity"; IR is an emergent, fuzzy state.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Full-Immersion Virtual Reality]] — IR is explicitly *not* total immersion; it is the in-between, the persistent overlap of virtual and real, and is positioned between VR and natural systems rather than wholly inside the virtual.

## Questions It Raises

- If IR is "the space we are all most likely to inhabit," what becomes of any appeal to an unmediated "real" outside it — is there still a stable referent against which the virtual is measured?
- Ascott insists IR is "Not phoney reality!" — but on what grounds is a negotiated, constructed reality *not* less real? What distinguishes constructive fuzziness from mere simulation?
- How does IR relate to the later "moist" merging of the digital and the biological? Is IR a transitional stage on the way to moist media, or a permanent condition?
- If the viewer "navigates what is largely a world of her own making," who or what constrains that world — and does shared inter-reality require shared construction (telenoia) to avoid solipsism?
