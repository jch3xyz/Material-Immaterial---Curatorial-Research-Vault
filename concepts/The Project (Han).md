# The Project (Han)

## Brief Definition

The contemporary self that no longer experiences itself as a subjugated subject but as a *project* (*Projekt*) — perpetually refashioning and reinventing itself — and that, in the very feeling of freedom this brings, internalizes constraint as compulsive achievement and optimization.

## Longer Explanation

For Byung-Chul Han, *the project* names the self-conception and affect under neoliberalism, not a labour-form. Where the disciplinary order produced *subjects* — those "cast down," subjugated from without — the neoliberal order produces selves that "do not deem ourselves subjugated subjects, but rather projects, always refashioning and reinventing ourselves." The decisive point is phenomenological: the passage from subject to project is *felt as freedom*. Han is explicit that this feeling is real and that it is precisely what makes the formation efficient (`Directly stated`).

The freedom is, however, a vehicle of a subtler subjugation. Deeming itself free of "external and alien limitations," the I subjugates itself instead to "internal limitations and self-constraints" that take "the form of compulsive achievement and optimization." Han calls this "a more efficient kind of subjectivation and subjugation": coercion is no longer imposed by a master or a prohibitive *Should* but generated from within by the limitless *Can* (`Directly stated`). This is why *the project* is distinct from [[Achievement Society]]: the achievement-society names the labour-form and its compulsion to perform, whereas *the project* names the self-understanding — the sense of free, unlimited self-production — that lets that compulsion be experienced as autonomy rather than domination (`Interpretive synthesis`).

In "The Mole and the Snake," Han maps the concept onto Deleuze's animals of control. The mole — the labourer of disciplinary confinement — "moves through predetermined spaces" and so "is a subjugated subject." The snake, the animal of the neoliberal regime, is by contrast a *project*: "the snake is a project inasmuch as it creates space through the course it steers." The figure literalizes the self-conception: the project does not merely occupy given space but appears to *generate* its own space and trajectory, which is exactly the form the feeling of freedom takes (`Directly stated`). Yet Han insists the passage "from subject to project" is "not setting out for an entirely new way of life" but "a mutation, indeed an intensification, of capitalism, which remains one and the same" — the snake's self-steering yields more productivity, and "above all, the snake embodies the guilt and debts" of the neoliberal regime (`Directly stated`).

The unlimited self-production the project imagines is therefore an illusion that stabilizes the system: the person "as a project free to fashion him- or herself at will" believes itself "capable of unlimited self-production," and this belief, dispersing class into isolated entrepreneurs of the self, "renders impossible any social revolution" and ensures "no political We is even possible" (`Directly stated`). The affect of freedom is, in this sense, the most economical instrument of domination.

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Byung-Chul Han]]** — *Project* (*Projekt*) is the self-image of the neoliberal subject in *Psychopolitics*: the I that takes itself to be free, self-refashioning, and limitlessly self-producing, and that thereby internalizes coercion as compulsive achievement and optimization. Han pairs it with Deleuze's *snake* (control society) against the *mole* (disciplinary society), making *project* the counter-term to *subject*.

## Related Concepts

- [[Achievement Society]] — the labour-form whose compulsion the project's felt freedom underwrites; the project is its self-conception, not its synonym.
- [[Auto-Exploitation]] — the project, "master and slave in one," exploits itself voluntarily; its sense of freedom is what makes self-exploitation possible.
- [[Exploitation of Freedom]] — the project is the subjective face of the neoliberal exploitation of freedom: only freely given, freely felt activity yields maximal returns.
- [[Society of Control]] — the snake-as-project is the animal of control society; the project belongs to the post-disciplinary order of open, self-steering movement.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Freedom vs Control]] — the project is the precise site of this tension: it is the form in which freedom *switches over into* control, coercion arising from within the feeling of liberty.
- [[Subject vs Project]] — the opposition the concept grounds: the subjugated *subject* (the mole, cast down, moving in predetermined space) versus the self-fashioning *project* (the snake, creating space through its course), where the "freer" pole is the more efficiently subjugated.

## Questions It Raises

- If the feeling of freedom is itself the mechanism of subjugation, can the project be critiqued from within its own self-understanding, or only from outside it?
- Han locates the snake's "creating space through the course it steers" as the mark of the project — to what extent is this generativity real autonomy versus a phenomenological effect of intensified productivity?
- If "no political We is even possible" once selves become isolated projects, what form could collective resistance take that does not presuppose the subject Han says neoliberalism has dissolved?
- How does *the project* relate to curatorial and artistic self-presentation, where "the project" is also the literal unit of creative labour and self-branding?
