# The One Voice

## Brief Definition

Shoshana Zuboff's name for the winner-take-all prize of the voice-interface race — the dominant, if not exclusive, medium through which humans engage the apparatus — which would confer on its owner the power to corner and monetize the largest share of human experience.

## Longer Explanation

In Chapter 9, "Rendition from the Depths," Zuboff tracks the competition among the tech giants' "personal digital assistants" — Microsoft's Cortana, Google Now / Google Assistant and its Home device, Amazon's Alexa with its Echo and Dot hubs, and Apple's Siri (whose original developers built Samsung's "Viv"). She reads this contest not as a convenience race but as a race to control raw-material supply. "Conversation" is the coveted medium because reliable voice capture renders rivers of casual talk that surveillance capitalism converts into [[Behavioral Surplus]] — surplus from what you say and from how you say it. The competitor whose interface rises to dominance becomes "the One Voice": the run time and new interface through which we access and engage the apparatus as it engages us. `Directly stated`

The strategic logic is the [[The Extraction Imperative|extraction imperative]] taken to totality: "the logic of competition is to corner as much supply as possible," and so the contestants are driven toward an "urge toward totality" — to become "the dominant, if not exclusive, medium." Whoever wins the coronation is described as "the colossus of behavioral surplus pipelines with a potentially insurmountable competitive advantage in its ability to corner and kidnap the dominant share of human experience," and would be rewarded with "the ability to anticipate and monetize all the moments of all the people during all the days." The One Voice thus names a specific extractive mechanism of the personalization race, not merely a product category. `Directly stated`

Crucially, Zuboff frames the One Voice as the apotheosis of "personalization." The personal assistant is "a market avatar, another Trojan horse in which the determination to render and monetize your life is secreted under the veil of 'assistance' and embellished with the poetry of 'personalization.'" Casual talk "helps to blur the boundaries between 'it' — the apparatus saturated with commercial agents — and us"; in conversation "we imagine friendship," and the more we treat the apparatus as confidante the more experience we surrender to [[Rendition]]. The One Voice is therefore continuous with [[Smart as a Euphemism for Rendition]]: the affective, frictionless interface ("Let there be new running shoes") is the supply mechanism that drives [[Prediction Products]] into [[Behavioral Futures Markets]]. `Directly stated`

The stakes are developmental and existential, not just commercial. Zuboff imagines a future in which "children learn the principles of the One Voice," an omnipresent run time that executes commands, anticipates desires, and shapes possibilities, "with its fractious, eager marketplace-of-you concealed under its skirts." In that world "intimacy as we have known it is compromised, if not eliminated," "solitude is deleted," and children "learn first that there are no boundaries between self and market." The One Voice is thus the interface form of [[Surveillance Capitalism]]'s deepest ambition: a single medium standing between the self and all of life. `Directly stated`

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** (*The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*, ch. 9) — coins "the One Voice" as the winner-take-all prize of the personal-digital-assistant race. It is the dominant or exclusive conversational interface to the apparatus, conferring an "insurmountable competitive advantage" in cornering the supply of human experience for behavioral surplus, prediction, and monetization.

## Related Concepts

- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — the One Voice is described as "the colossus of behavioral surplus pipelines"; voice capture is its supply engine.
- [[Rendition]] — the One Voice maximizes how much of life is rendered into data; "the more experience we allow it to render, the richer its supply operations grow."
- [[The Extraction Imperative]] — the race to the One Voice is the extraction imperative's "urge toward totality" applied to the conversational interface.
- [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — the One Voice is the interface telos of the surveillance-capitalist economic logic.
- [[Behavioral Futures Markets]] — the talk captured by the winning voice feeds prediction products sold into these markets ("hosting behavioral futures markets in your front seat").
- [[Prediction Products]] — "personalization derives from prediction"; the One Voice manufactures ever-richer prediction inputs.
- [[Smart as a Euphemism for Rendition]] — "personalization" / "assistance" is the euphemistic veil under which the One Voice secrets its rendition operations.
- [[Surveillance]] — the One Voice extends surveillance into casual speech, the home, and the unconscious ("affective computing").

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Quantified Self]] — where the quantified-self movement framed self-tracking as a tool of self-knowledge and autonomy, the One Voice inverts it: the dominant interface renders the self for the market, so that "there are no boundaries between self and market" (`Interpretive synthesis` — Zuboff narrates personalization's capture of self-illuminating tools, not a direct One-Voice/quantified-self contrast).

## Questions It Raises

- If a single conversational interface becomes the dominant or exclusive medium of access to "the apparatus," what forms of opacity, refusal, or exit remain possible to the speaker?
- Does the friendliness of voice — "in conversation we imagine friendship" — make the One Voice a more effective instrument of dispossession than the screen, precisely because it dissolves the boundary between "it" and "us"?
- What becomes of intimacy, solitude, and the self when, as Zuboff projects, children grow up unable to imagine a world without the One Voice?
- Is the One Voice the natural endpoint of the [[The Extraction Imperative|extraction imperative]], or does the prediction imperative push past voice toward even more direct capture ("merely thinking a thought or waving a finger")?
