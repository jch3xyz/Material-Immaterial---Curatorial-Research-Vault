# Techno-Religion

## Brief Definition

For Harari, a techno-religion is an ideology that promises the old religious prizes — salvation, happiness, peace, prosperity, even eternal life — not through gods or a cosmic plan but through technology and economics, with socialism as "the first techno-religion in history" and the twenty-first century's two new techno-religions emerging not from caves or madrasas but from research laboratories.

## Longer Explanation

Harari treats *religion* functionally — as any system that confers meaning and authority and thereby sustains social order — rather than as belief in supernatural beings (a move elaborated in [[Religion as Social Order]]). On this reading, an ideology that promises the goods of religion through worldly, technological means is still structurally a religion. A *techno-religion* is precisely such a creed: it keeps the form of religious promise while swapping its content from the heavenly to the technical. Socialism is Harari's inaugural case. The socialists, he writes, "created a brave new religion for a brave new world," promising "salvation through technology and economics, thus establishing the first techno-religion in history." With Marx, the dividing line of ideological discourse shifted from views about God to views about production methods. `Directly stated`

The category is forward-looking as much as historical. Harari argues that the next world religions "are unlikely to emerge from the caves of Afghanistan or from the madrasas of the Middle East" but "from research laboratories" — that, just as socialism took over the world by promising salvation through steam and electricity, new techno-religions may conquer the world "by promising salvation through algorithms and genes." The exemplary site is therefore not the Islamic State or the Bible Belt but Silicon Valley, where "hi-tech gurus are brewing for us brave new religions that have little to do with God, and everything to do with technology," offering the old prizes "here on earth with the help of technology, rather than after death with the help of celestial beings." `Directly stated`

These twenty-first-century techno-religions, Harari states, "can be divided into two main types: techno-humanism and data religion." [[Techno-Humanism]] is the more conservative creed: it still sees humans as the apex of creation and clings to many traditional humanist values, but holds that *Homo sapiens* has run its course and so proposes to use technology to upgrade humans into *Homo deus*. [[Dataism]] (the "data religion") is the bolder creed that severs the humanist umbilical cord and worships information rather than gods or man. This concept note is the umbrella; the two species are developed in their own notes, and later chapters of *Homo Deus* extend the data-religion material. `Directly stated`

Genealogically, Harari nests the techno-religions within humanism's own schism. He presents humanism itself as "a revolutionary new creed" — "the humanist religion worships humanity" — that fragmented into [[Liberal Humanism (Harari)]], socialist humanism, and [[Evolutionary Humanism]]. Techno-humanism is explicitly "an updated variant on the old dreams of evolutionary humanism," pursuing superhumans through genetic engineering, nanotechnology, and brain–computer interfaces rather than selective breeding and ethnic cleansing. The techno-religions are thus continuous with [[Humanism (Harari)]] even as data religion threatens to abolish the human will it once sanctified. `Directly stated` / `Strongly implied`

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Yuval Noah Harari]]** — coins *techno-religion* as an umbrella term in *Homo Deus*. The historical instance is socialism ("the first techno-religion in history"); the contemporary instances are techno-humanism and data religion, both expected to emerge from research laboratories and to promise salvation "through algorithms and genes." Harari's functional definition of religion (meaning + authority, not the supernatural) is what licenses calling these ideologies religions at all.

## Related Concepts

- [[Techno-Humanism]] — one of the two contemporary techno-religions; the conservative branch that upgrades humans into *Homo deus*.
- [[Dataism]] — the other contemporary techno-religion ("data religion"); worships information and supersedes the human.
- [[Evolutionary Humanism]] — the humanist sect of which techno-humanism is "an updated variant."

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Liberal Humanism (Harari)]] — the still-theistic-in-form humanism that techno-humanism declares historically exhausted; "liberal humanism makes way for techno-humanism."

## Questions It Raises

- If a "techno-religion" promises salvation through technology while abandoning God and the cosmic plan, what (if anything) distinguishes it from a secular ideology — is "religion" here an analysis or a polemic?
- Socialism, techno-humanism, and data religion all claim the mantle of salvation through technology — does grouping such different projects under one label illuminate or flatten their differences?
- If techno-humanism sanctifies the human will yet builds technologies to engineer that will, does the category contain its own self-undoing — the point at which a techno-religion must mutate into data religion?
- Why the *laboratory* rather than the cave or the madrasa as the birthplace of new religions — what does relocating the sacred to research institutions imply about authority in a data-driven society?
