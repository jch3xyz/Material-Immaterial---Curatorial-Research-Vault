# Economies of Action

## Brief Definition

The most radical tier of surveillance capitalism's supply operations: machine processes configured to intervene in the state of play in the real world — nudging, tuning, herding, manipulating, and modifying behavior at its source — so as to enhance the certainty of prediction not merely by knowing but "by doing things."

## Longer Explanation

For [[Shoshana Zuboff]], economies of action are the third and most consequential member of a supply triad. Economies of scale (more behavioral data) and economies of scope (more *varied* data — extension and depth) sharpen prediction by gathering surplus; economies of action go further by reaching into the world to *shape* the behavior being predicted. As Zuboff puts it, "the surest way to predict behavior is to intervene at its source and shape it," and "the processes invented to achieve this goal are what I call economies of action." This is the load-bearing innovation that compels extraction to be twinned with **execution**: the apparatus must not only render the world as data but act back upon it. (`Directly stated`, Ch. 7.)

The mechanism is concrete and "muscular." Machine processes "are configured to intervene in the state of play in the real world among real people and things," and "these interventions are designed to enhance certainty *by doing things*: they nudge, tune, herd, manipulate, and modify behavior in specific directions" — inserting a phrase into a Facebook feed, timing the appearance of a BUY button, shutting down a car engine when an insurance payment is late. In Chapter 10 Zuboff's engineers name this capability "actuation": sensors become actuators, and "the real aim is ubiquitous intervention, action, and control." This phase "represents the completion of the new means of behavior modification," a "decisive and necessary evolution of the surveillance capitalist 'means of production' toward a more complex, iterative, and muscular operational system." (`Directly stated`, Ch. 7, Ch. 10.)

Zuboff's interviewees identify three approaches that produce economies of action, each aimed at behavior modification: **tuning** (subliminal cues and the Thaler–Sunstein "nudge" embedded in choice architecture, but twisted so the nudge accrues to the architect, not the individual), **herding** (controlling key elements of a person's immediate context to foreclose action alternatives — "we are learning how to write the music, and then we let the music make them dance"), and **conditioning** (Skinnerian operant reinforcement applied "at scale"). Together these subordinate the means of production to [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]]. (`Directly stated`, Ch. 10.)

This is why economies of action sit at the heart of Zuboff's political stakes. In Chapter 11 she frames them as the third phase of competitive intensity, in which "surveillance capitalists discovered the necessity of economies of action based on new methods that go beyond tracking, capturing, analyzing, and predicting behavior in order to intervene in the state of play and actively shape behavior at the source." Because they replace "the indeterminacy of social processes with the determinism of programmed machine processes," they directly threaten [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — collapsing the open future into "an infinite present of mere behavior, in which there can be no subjects and no projects: only objects." Economies of action are thus the operational core of [[Instrumentarian Power]] and the means by which [[Behavioral Surplus]] is "repurposed as the means to shape and compel" experience for the sake of guaranteed outcomes. (`Directly stated` / `Strongly implied`, Ch. 11, Ch. 7.)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Shoshana Zuboff]]** — the coiner. Economies of action complete the supply triad (scale, scope, action) and mark surveillance capitalism's turn from *knowing* to *doing*: ubiquitous intervention via tuning, herding, and conditioning, enforced through the architecture of extraction-and-execution. They constitute [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]] and underwrite [[Behavioral Futures Markets]] by manufacturing the certainty that is sold there.

## Related Concepts

- [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]] — economies of action are the supply process that *produces* this apparatus; Zuboff calls their arrival "the completion of the new means of behavior modification."
- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — the raw material; economies of action redirect surplus back at its source "to interrupt and shape behavior."
- [[Behavioral Futures Markets]] — the demand side; intervention manufactures the guaranteed outcomes that buyers bet on.
- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — economies of action are its operational means; the power "to know and to modify" behavior toward others' ends.
- [[Prediction Products]] — what economies of scale and scope yield; economies of action raise their quality by shaping the predicted behavior.
- [[Society of Control]] — Deleuze's modulating, continuously-tuning control finds a literal machinic realization in tuning/herding/conditioning; Zuboff's actuation extends control from monitoring into real-time behavioral enforcement.
- [[Digital Panopticon]] — Han's voluntary self-exposure supplies the surplus that economies of action then act upon; surveillance here is not only seeing but doing.
- [[Dataism]] — the worldview that legitimates rendering and acting upon all behavior as data flows.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — the elemental human freedom that economies of action usurp; intervention "at the source" forecloses the open future on which willing and promising depend.

## Questions It Raises

- If prediction is perfected by *causing* the behavior predicted, has "prediction" collapsed into control — and does the distinction between forecasting and engineering still hold?
- Where is the line between a benign "nudge" toward the chooser's own interest and a herding intervention engineered for the architect's revenue? Zuboff locates the difference in *whose interest* the choice architecture serves — is that line legible to the person being tuned?
- Economies of action operate, "as MacKay advised, outside the driver's awareness while she still thinks that she is free." What political remedy is possible against a power whose effectiveness depends on remaining unfelt?
- How does Zuboff's machinic actuation differ from, and extend, Deleuze's [[Society of Control]] and Han's [[Digital Panopticon]] — is this the realization those diagnoses anticipated, or a categorical break?
