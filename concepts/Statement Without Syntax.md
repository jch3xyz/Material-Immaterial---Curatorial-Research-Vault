# Statement Without Syntax

## Brief Definition

McLuhan's term for the photograph as a nonverbal mode of self-delineation — "statement by gesture, by mime, and by gestaJt" — whose logic is neither verbal nor syntactical, and which therefore leaves literary culture helpless before it.

## Longer Explanation

For McLuhan the photograph delineates the world directly, "by carrying the pictorial delineation of natural objects much further than paint or language could do." In doing so it confers "a means of self delineation of objects, of 'statement without syntax,'" — a kind of declaration that needs neither the line-by-line ordering of writing nor the grammar of speech. Where the woodcut and engraving had "delineated the world by an arrangement of lines and points that had syntax oi a very elaborate kind," the photograph dispenses with that arrangement: it states without sequencing. This is what makes it a problem for the inheritors of the [[The Phonetic Alphabet|phonetic alphabet]] — the [[Typographic Man|typographic man]] trained to read meaning off a line. "Literate man is not only numb and vague in the presence of film or photo, but he intensifies his ineptness by a defensive arrogance." (`Directly stated`)

The deep claim is one of sensory restoration. McLuhan reads the phonetic alphabet as a technology that amputated gesture and sound from the word, abstracting meaning into a visual sequence. The photograph and its development in the movie reverse that abstraction: they "restored gesture to the human technology of recording experience." The "snapshot of arrested human postures" turns attention to "physical and psychic posture" as never before, so that "the age of the photograph has become the age of gesture and mime and dance, as no other age has ever been." Statement without syntax is thus the photograph speaking in the older, suppressed register of the body — posture, mime, gestalt — rather than in the line. (`Directly stated`; the link to the alphabet's amputation of gesture is `Strongly implied`.)

This restored register supplies the raw material of depth psychology. The "physical and psychic gestalts, or 'still' shots, with which they worked were much owing to the posture world revealed by the photograph"; "Freud and Jung built their observations on the interpretation of the languages of both individual and collective postures and gestures." McLuhan extends the point: where rhetoric handled the individual posture of a private speaker, "myth and Jungian archetypes are collective postures of the mind with which the written form could not cope, any more than it could command mime and gesture." The photograph, biased toward neither the private nor the line, can arrest collective as well as individual posture. (`Directly stated`.)

Finally, statement without syntax opens an inner landscape. By delineating objects so completely, the photograph "gave the impetus to a delineation of the inner' world." The same nonverbal, nonsyntactical gesture that captures outer posture also captures "our inner postures and the dialogue with ourselves" — so that "the age of Jung and Freud is, above all, the age of the photograph." This is the concept's distinctive twist: a medium with no grammar nonetheless makes a statement, and by abolishing the syntactical it reaches what the syntactical never could — the wordless gestalt of body and mind. (`Directly stated` for the inner-world claim; the framing of it as the chapter's most distinctive idea is `Interpretive synthesis`.)

## Authors and Variants

- **[[Marshall McLuhan]]** — coins "statement without syntax" in *Understanding Media* (ch. 20, "The Photograph: The Brothel-without-Walls") to name the photograph's nonverbal, nonsyntactical logic. It is a special case of his general thesis that each medium reorganizes the [[Sense Ratios|sense ratios]] (here: away from the visual sequence of print, back toward gesture and the tactile/kinetic gestalt), and a counter-pole to the literacy of [[Typographic Man|typographic man]].

## Related Concepts

- [[The Phonetic Alphabet]] — the technology that "severed the spoken word from its aspects of sound and gesture"; statement without syntax is the photograph reversing that amputation by restoring gesture to recording.
- [[Typographic Man]] — the line-bred reader whom statement without syntax leaves "quite helpless"; the literacy that cannot cope with the nonsyntactical photo.
- [[Sense Ratios]] — the photograph's shift from visual sequence to gestural gestalt is a reorganization of the sense ratios.
- [[Media as Extensions of Man]] — the photograph as an extension that restores a suppressed sense (gesture) to the technology of recording experience.
- [[The Medium Is the Message]] — the photograph's "message" is precisely its nonverbal logic, not the scenes it depicts.

## Opposing Concepts

- [[Typographic Man]] — the verbal, syntactical, line-by-line order of print culture is exactly what statement without syntax bypasses; the two are McLuhan's antithesis in this chapter.

## Questions It Raises

- If a medium can "state" without syntax, what counts as meaning when meaning belongs "very little to the semantic universe, and not at all to the republic of letters"?
- Is the photograph's restoration of gesture a genuine recovery of embodied experience, or — given Joyce's "abnihilization of the etym" and the "substitution of shadows for substance" — a new abstraction that only mimics the body?
- Why should the nonsyntactical image be the medium that makes the *inner* world legible, supplying the gestalts Freud and Jung worked from?
- Does the helplessness of "literary culture" before the photo generalize to every new medium's encounter with the literacy of the medium it displaces?
