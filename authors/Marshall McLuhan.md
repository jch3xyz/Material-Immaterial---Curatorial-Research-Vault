## Central Concerns

McLuhan's governing preoccupation is that media are not neutral conduits for content but *extensions of man* whose form, independent of any message they carry, reorganizes the whole human sensorium and social field: "the personal and social consequences of any medium—that is, of any extension of ourselves—result from the new scale that is introduced into our affairs by each extension of ourselves, or by any new technology." From this follows his most famous claim, that *the medium is the message*, and its corollary, that "the 'content' of any medium is always another medium." He reads Western history as a movement from the *mechanical* age of "explosion"—fragmentation, specialism, the uniform-and-continuous habits bred by the phonetic alphabet and print—into the *electric* age of "implosion," in which "we have extended our central nervous system itself in a global embrace, abolishing both space and time," collapsing the planet into "the global village." His sorting of media into *hot* and *cool* (by definition and audience participation), his account of how any *overheated* medium reverses into its opposite, and his physiological model of extension-as-*autoamputation* (Narcissus as narcosis) all serve a single project: to make visible the formal effects of media that ordinarily "alter sense ratios or patterns of perception steadily and without any resistance."

## Method

- **Form over content; the medium as the unit of study.** McLuhan insists the proper object of study is "not only the 'content' but the medium and the cultural matrix within which the particular medium operates," because "it is only too typical that the 'content' of any medium blinds us to the character of the medium." The opposing view—"that it is how they are used that counts"—is "the numb stance of the technological idiot."
- **Effect, not meaning.** Following Kenneth Boulding ("the meaning of a message is the change which it produces in the image"), he holds that "concern with effect rather than meaning is a basic change of our electric time, for effect involves the total situation."
- **The concentric / configurational mode against the lineal.** He works by "endless intersection of planes," arguing "no medium has its meaning or existence alone, but only in constant interplay with other media"—a deliberately nonlineal method matched to the electric age it describes.
- **The artist as early-warning system.** "The serious artist is the only person able to encounter technology with impunity, just because he is an expert aware of the changes in sense perception."

## Major Concepts

- [[The Medium Is the Message]] — "in operational and practical fact, the medium is the message"; the message of any medium is "the change of scale or pace or pattern that it introduces into human affairs."
- [[Media as Extensions of Man]] — every technology is "an extension of ourselves"; "our human senses, of which all media are extensions, are also fixed charges on our personal energies." (The vault definition note is [[Extensions of Man (McLuhan)]].)
- [[The Content of Any Medium Is Another Medium]] — "the 'content' of any medium is always another medium. The content of writing is speech, just as the written word is the content of print, and print is the content of the telegraph."
- [[The Global Village]] — "As electrically contracted, the globe is no more than a village"; electric speed brings "all social and political functions together in a sudden implosion."
- [[Explosion vs Implosion]] — "After three thousand years of explosion, by means of fragmentary and mechanical technologies, the Western world is imploding"; "the stepping-up of speed from the mechanical to the instant electric form reverses explosion into implosion."
- [[Sense Ratios]] — "the effects of technology... alter sense ratios or patterns of perception steadily and without any resistance"; any extension "demands new ratios or new equilibriums among the other organs and extensions of the body."
- [[Media Hot and Cold]] — "A hot medium is one that extends one single sense in 'high definition'... Hot media are, therefore, low in participation, and cool media are high in participation." (Definition note: [[Hot and Cool Media (McLuhan)]].)
- [[Reversal of the Overheated Medium]] — "When all the available resources and energies have been played up in an organism or in any structure there is some kind of reversal of pattern"; "the principle that during the stages of their development all things appear under forms opposite to those that they finally present."
- [[The Break Boundary]] — Boulding's "break boundary at which the system suddenly changes into another or passes some point of no return in its dynamic processes." (Definition note: [[The Break Boundary (Boulding via McLuhan)]].)
- [[Detribalization and Retribalization]] — "Specialist technologies detribalize. The nonspecialist electric technology retribalizes."
- [[Typographic Man]] — the uniform, continuous, individualist subject produced by the phonetic alphabet and print: "Print created individualism and nationalism in the sixteenth century"; "we have confused reason with literacy, and rationalism with a single technology."
- [[Autoamputation]] — extensions as self-amputation: "Any extension of ourselves they regard as 'autoamputation'... resorted to by the body when the perceptual power cannot locate or avoid the cause of irritation."
- [[Narcosis]] — the numbness that accompanies every extension: "Narcissus... is from the Greek word narcosis, or numbness... Self-amputation forbids self-recognition."
- [[Man as Servomechanism of His Extensions]] — "By continuously embracing technologies, we relate ourselves to them as servo-mechanisms... An Indian is the servo-mechanism of his canoe, as the cowboy of his horse or the executive of his clock."
- [[The Electric Age as Extension of the Central Nervous System]] — "with electric technology, man extended, or set outside himself, a live model of the central nervous system itself"; "an organism that now wears its brain outside its skull and its nerves outside its hide."
- [[Hybrid Energy]] — "It is from such intensive hybrid exchange and strife of ideas and forms that the greatest social energies are released, and from which arise the greatest technologies."
- [[Media as Translators]] — "All media are active metaphors in their power to translate experience into new forms"; "What we call 'mechanization' is a translation of nature, and of our own natures, into amplified and specialized forms."
- [[The Technological Extension of Consciousness]] — "the final phase of the extensions of man—the technological simulation of consciousness, when the creative process of knowing will be collectively and corporately extended to the whole of human society."
- [[The Artist as Integral Awareness]] — "The serious artist is the only person able to encounter technology with impunity, just because he is an expert aware of the changes in sense perception."

## Major Arguments

- [[The medium not its content is the message]] — "it is the medium that shapes and controls the scale and form of human association and action. The content or uses of such media are as diverse as they are ineffectual in shaping the form of human association."
- [[The neutral-instrument view of technology is the numb stance of the technological idiot]] — against Sarnoff's "The products of modern science are not in themselves good or bad; it is the way they are used that determines their value" ("the voice of the current somnambulism"), McLuhan: "Our conventional response to all media, namely that it is how they are used that counts, is the numb stance of the technological idiot."
- [[Electric speed reverses explosion into implosion]] — "the stepping-up of speed from the mechanical to the instant electric form reverses explosion into implosion"; the mechanical age "extended our bodies in space," the electric age "extended our central nervous system itself in a global embrace."
- [[Every medium has a break boundary triggered by cross-fertilization with another medium]] — "One of the most common causes of breaks in any system is the cross-fertilization with another system, such as happened to print with the steam press, or with radio and movies (that yielded the talkies)."
- [[All media must be studied together because any extension reorganizes the whole psychic and social field]] — "There is little possibility of answering such questions about the extensions of man without considering all of them together. Any extension, whether of skin, hand, or foot, affects the whole psychic and social complex."

## Characteristic Metaphors

- [[The Burglar's Meat]] — "the 'content' of a medium is like the juicy piece of meat carried by the burglar to distract the watchdog of the mind"; content as the decoy that lets the medium do its work unnoticed.
- [[The Electric Light]] — "The electric light is pure information. It is a medium without a message, as it were, unless it is used to spell out some verbal ad or name"; the limit-case proving "the medium is the message."
- [[Railway vs the Electric Grid]] — "It is like the difference between a railway system and an electric grid system: the one requires rail-heads and big urban centers... Electric power... permits any place to be a center"—the figure of centralization giving way to decentralization.
- [[Hypnosis vs Hallucination]] — "the hotting-up of one sense tends to effect hypnosis, and the cooling of all senses tends to result in hallucination"—the perceptual extremes of overheated and underloaded media.
- The Narcissus mirror — the youth who "mistook his own reflection in the water for another person... became the servomechanism of his own extended or repeated image"; McLuhan's master figure for technological numbness.

## Disagreements or Tensions

- [[Mechanical vs Electric]] — the periodizing axis of the whole book: the mechanical age of "explosion" (fragmentation, specialism, "uniform and continuous patterns") against the electric age of "implosion" and "organic interrelation." "The electric tape succeeds the assembly line."
- [[Hot vs Cool Media]] — high-definition / low-participation against low-definition / high-participation; "the hot form excludes, and the cool one includes."
- [[Fragmentation vs Wholeness]] — "the technique of fragmentation that is the essence of machine technology" against "the aspiration of our time for wholeness, empathy and depth of awareness... a natural adjunct of electric technology."
- [[Detachment vs Participation]] — the literate Westerner's "art of carrying out the most dangerous social operations with complete detachment" against the electric age in which "we necessarily participate, in depth, in the consequences of our every action."
