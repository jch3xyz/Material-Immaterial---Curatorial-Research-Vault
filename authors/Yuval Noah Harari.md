## Central Concerns

Harari writes a "brief history of tomorrow": having argued that humanity is "bringing famine, plague and war under control," he asks "an unprecedented question: what are we going to do with ourselves?" — and answers that the next human agenda is to upgrade *Homo sapiens* into *Homo deus*, seeking "immortality, bliss and divine powers of creation" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/01 The New Human Agenda]]). His master thread is the claim that "organisms are algorithms" and that "life is data processing" — a thesis he calls [[Dataism]], the [[Information|information]]-religion in which "the universe consists of data flows" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). From this he derives the book's two most unsettling diagnoses: that human worth has rested on shared fictions rather than on any "human spark" or soul, and that the modern alliance of science and humanism is about to be dissolved by "the decoupling of intelligence from consciousness," after which "non-conscious but highly intelligent algorithms may soon know us better than we know ourselves" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]], [[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). He is preoccupied, in short, with meaning, authority, and value in a world where the [[The Liberal Humanist Subject|liberal humanist subject]] is losing its economic and epistemic monopoly.

## Major Concepts

- [[Dataism]] — Harari is the **originator of the term** as it enters the vault. "Dataism says that the universe consists of data flows, and the value of any phenomenon or entity is determined by its contribution to data processing" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). Note: the existing [[Dataism]] note currently carries [[Byung-Chul Han|Han's]] critical reframing (dataism as "nihilism," "data totalitarianism"); Harari is the source Han is answering. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Information]] — Harari's "Information Wants to be Free": "The supreme value of this new religion is 'information flow'… Hence Dataism upholds the freedom of information as the greatest good of all." A shared cross-author concept here read as a *cosmology of flow* rather than (as in Han/Hayles) a substance or a signal. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Narrativity]] / [[Narration vs Addition]] — In *Homo Deus* the human is defined as a story-teller: "Humans think they make history, but history actually revolves around the web of stories" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/06 The Storytellers]]). This is the affirmative counterpart to Han's worry that data are "additive, not narrative"; Harari shows narrative *building* power before Dataism dissolves it. (`Directly stated` / `Interpretive synthesis` on the Han linkage)
- [[The Liberal Humanist Subject]] — The individual whose "free choices are the ultimate source of authority," and which Harari argues is being made "obsolete" by three developments in [[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]]. A cross-author concept (Hayles, O'Gieblyn); Harari supplies its political-economic obituary. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Algorithmic Determinism]] — "Organisms are algorithms. Every animal — including *Homo sapiens* — is an assemblage of organic algorithms shaped by natural selection" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]]). A cross-author concept (O'Gieblyn); Harari is the most literal proponent of the formula in the vault. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — Harari narrates the shift "from a material-based economy into a knowledge-based economy," where "the main source of wealth is knowledge" and "you cannot acquire knowledge" through war ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/01 The New Human Agenda]]); and the deeper claim that "algorithmic calculations are not affected by the materials from which you build the calculator" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]]). A new author-section on the vault's central four-author tension. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Posthumanism]] / [[Transhumanism]] / [[Mind Uploading]] — *Homo Deus* is a popular synthesis of the upgrade project: "humanism makes it difficult for us to imagine a post-human destiny" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). Cross-author concepts (Hayles, O'Gieblyn, [[Ray Kurzweil|Kurzweil]]); Harari connects them to a *religious* reading of Silicon Valley. (`Directly stated` on the post-human horizon; `Interpretive synthesis` on the note linkages)

## Major Arguments

- **Humans agree to give up meaning in exchange for power.** "The entire contract can be summarised in a single phrase: humans agree to give up meaning in exchange for power" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/08 The Modern Covenant]]). The modern deal, and humanism's "cunning escape clause" that lets us "find meaning without deriving it from a great cosmic plan" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/09 The Humanist Revolution]]). (`Directly stated`)
- **There is no human spark; the soul cannot survive Darwin.** "Darwin has deprived us of our souls. If you really understand the theory of evolution, you understand that there is no soul" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/04 The Human Spark]]). Human privilege rests on a "powerful myth," not on a discoverable essence. (`Directly stated`)
- **Human power is built on shared fictions, organised algorithmically.** Sapiens "live in triple-layered reality" that adds "stories about money, gods, nations and corporations"; "writing has thus enabled humans to organise entire societies in an algorithmic fashion," each person "only a small step in a huge algorithm" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/06 The Storytellers]]). (`Directly stated`)
- **Intelligence is decoupling from consciousness.** "Humans are in danger of losing their value, because intelligence is decoupling from consciousness… intelligence is mandatory but consciousness is optional" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]]). The hinge argument of the book. (`Directly stated`)
- **The liberal subject is being made obsolete (economically, then philosophically).** Three developments — humans losing economic/military usefulness, the system valuing humans only collectively, and a "new elite of upgraded superhumans" — "might make this belief obsolete" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]]). (`Directly stated`)
- **Politics and economics are competing data-processing systems.** "Free-market capitalism and state-controlled communism aren't competing ideologies… they are competing data-processing systems"; "capitalism won the Cold War because distributed data processing works better than centralised data processing" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). (`Directly stated`)
- **Dataism is the first genuinely new value since 1789 — freedom of information.** "Dataism is the first movement since 1789 that created a really novel value: freedom of information," a value "given to information," not to humans, that "may impinge on the traditional freedom of expression" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). (`Directly stated`)
- **Dataism threatens to do to Sapiens what Sapiens did to other animals.** Once "humans lose their functional importance to the network… humanity will turn out to be just a ripple within the cosmic data flow" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). (`Directly stated`)

- **The new motto: "Record, Upload, Share."** "If you experience something — record it. If you record something — upload it. If you upload something — share it" — Dataist devotion replacing the humanist private diary ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). Resonates with [[The Like Button]] in the Han corpus. (`Directly stated`)

## Disagreements or Tensions

- [[Narration vs Addition]] — Harari and [[Byung-Chul Han]] split on the *value* of the same diagnosis. Harari: humans are story-tellers, and Dataism replaces narration with the data flow; he reports this with cool ambivalence. Han: the loss of narration *is* the catastrophe — "Data and numbers are not narrative; they are additive… Dataism is nihilism." Same opposition, opposite verdict. (`Interpretive synthesis`)
- [[Free Will vs Determinism]] — Harari's "organisms are algorithms" pushes hard toward determinism: "the algorithms know how each person is going to vote" and Dataism "may sideline humans" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). This is the existing cross-author tension (O'Gieblyn) read at population scale. (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Liberal Humanist Subject|Liberal Humanist Subject vs Posthuman]] — Harari sits with [[N. Katherine Hayles]] and [[Meghan O'Gieblyn]] in announcing the subject's eclipse, but where Hayles defends embodiment and O'Gieblyn defends meaning, Harari mostly declines to defend anything — "what, if anything, would be lost by replacing conscious intelligence with superior non-conscious algorithms?" ([[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/14 The Data Religion]]). (`Interpretive synthesis`)
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — Harari's "algorithmic calculations are not affected by the materials from which you build the calculator" is the strongest *immaterialist* (substrate-independence) statement in the vault; it stands against the materialist insistence (Hayles, Lippard) that the substrate matters. (`Directly stated` for the quote; `Interpretive synthesis` for the cross-author opposition)
