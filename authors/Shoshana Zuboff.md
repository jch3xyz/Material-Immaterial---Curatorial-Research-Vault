# Shoshana Zuboff

Shoshana Zuboff is the social psychologist and Harvard Business School professor emerita whose *[[The Age of Surveillance Capitalism]]: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power* (New York: PublicAffairs, 2019) names and anatomizes a "rogue mutation of capitalism" that "claims human experience as free raw material for hidden commercial practices of extraction, prediction, and sales." This note covers Zuboff as she appears in that book — the vault's primary source for the surveillance cluster, realizing the long-committed forward-stubs [[Surveillance]] and [[Quantified Self]] and extending the data/surveillance notes the Han and Harari ingests grounded secondhand.

## Books in the Vault

- [[The Age of Surveillance Capitalism]]

## Central Concerns

Zuboff is preoccupied with the discovery, naming, and confrontation of an *unprecedented* economic order. Her central object is [[Surveillance Capitalism]]: a parasitic logic that "unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data," declares the surplus a proprietary [[Behavioral Surplus|behavioral surplus]], feeds it into "machine intelligence," and fabricates [[Prediction Products|prediction products]] sold in [[Behavioral Futures Markets|behavioral futures markets]] — "a new kind of marketplace for behavioral predictions." Her insistence is that this is "not technology; it is a logic that imbues technology and commands it into action," which is why she hunts "the puppet master, not the puppet."

Her second great theme is the migration "from knowledge to power." Once surveillance capitalists learn that the most predictive surplus comes from "intervening in the state of play," the project shifts: "it is no longer enough to automate information flows about us; the goal now is to automate us." This births [[The Means of Behavioral Modification|the means of behavioral modification]] and a new "species of power" she calls [[Instrumentarian Power|instrumentarianism]], operating through [[Big Other|Big Other]] — "the sensate, computational, connected puppet that renders, monitors, computes, and modifies human behavior." Around this she builds an account of the threat to social order ([[The Division of Learning in Society|the division of learning in society]], "the axial principle of social order in an information civilization") and a set of countervailing rights: [[The Right to the Future Tense|the right to the future tense]] ("the individual's ability to imagine, intend, promise, and construct a future") and [[The Right to Sanctuary|the right to sanctuary]]. Throughout, she frames the whole as a political emergency — "best understood as a coup from above: an overthrow of the people's sovereignty."

## Major Concepts

- [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — the keystone: "A new economic order that claims human experience as free raw material for hidden commercial practices of extraction, prediction, and sales... A parasitic economic logic in which the production of goods and services is subordinated to a new global architecture of behavioral modification." See [[Surveillance Capitalism (Zuboff)]]. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — "These behavioral data available for uses beyond service improvement constituted a surplus, and it was on the strength of this behavioral surplus that the young company would find its way to the 'sustained and exponential profits'"; "a behavioral surplus, was the game-changing, zero-cost asset that was diverted from service improvement toward a genuine and highly lucrative market exchange." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Prediction Products]] — surplus "fabricated into prediction products that anticipate what you will do now, soon, and later." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Behavioral Futures Markets]] — "these prediction products are traded in a new kind of marketplace for behavioral predictions that I call behavioral futures markets. Surveillance capitalists have grown immensely wealthy from these trading operations." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]] — "the production of goods and services is subordinated to a new global architecture of behavioral modification"; "ownership of the new means of behavioral modification eclipses ownership of the means of production as the fountainhead of capitalist wealth and power in the twenty-first century." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — "the instrumentation and instrumentalization of behavior for the purposes of modification, prediction, monetization, and control"; "Instrumentarian power knows and shapes human behavior toward others' ends." See [[Instrumentarian Power (Zuboff)]]. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Big Other]] — "I now name the apparatus Big Other: it is the sensate, computational, connected puppet that renders, monitors, computes, and modifies human behavior... replacing the engineering of souls with the engineering of behavior." See [[Big Other (Zuboff)]]. (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Division of Learning in Society]] — "the axial principle of social order in an information civilization"; resolved into three questions: "Who knows?... Who decides?... Who decides who decides?" (`Directly stated`)
- [[Information Civilization]] — the order whose ordering principle is the division of learning; "the division of learning in society—the axial principle of social order in an information civilization." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Right to the Future Tense]] — "the individual's ability to imagine, intend, promise, and construct a future... an essential condition of free will and, more poignantly, of the inner resources from which we draw the will to will." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Right to Sanctuary]] — "the principle of sanctuary... has stood as an antidote to power since the beginning of the human story"; the Greek *asylon*, "unplunderable," founding "the notion of a sanctuary as an inviolable space." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Hive]] — "the hive and its larger architecture of Big Other plunge us into an intolerable world of 'no exit'"; life "meticulously crafted by the science of behavioral engineering" for "the youngest among us." (`Directly stated`)
- [[A Utopia of Certainty]] — instrumentarian power "aims for a condition of certainty without terror in the form of 'guaranteed outcomes'"; "a movement that aims to impose a new collective order based on total certainty." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Coup from Above]] — "An expropriation of critical human rights that is best understood as a coup from above: an overthrow of the people's sovereignty." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Unprecedented]] — "it is unprecedented. The unprecedented is necessarily unrecognizable"; the framing problem of the whole book. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Inevitabilism]] — "The rhetoric of inevitability is so 'ubiquitous' that within the tech community it can be considered a full-blown ideology of inevitabilism"; "Inevitabilism precludes choice and voluntary participation." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Reality Business]] — "this new reality business, as all aspects of human experience are claimed as raw-material supplies and targeted for rendering into behavioral data." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Psychic Numbing]] — "The evidence of our psychic numbing is that only a few decades ago US society denounced mass behavior-modification techniques as unacceptable threats to individual autonomy and the democratic order." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Second Modernity]] · [[Individualization]] — the "centuries-long process known as 'individualization'... the human signature of the modern era"; "the new conditions of the 'second modernity' produced a new kind of individual." See [[Individualization (Zuboff)]]. (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Collision]] · [[Conditions of Existence]] — the Apple miracle and surveillance capitalism each owe their success "to the destructive collision of two opposing historical forces": the shift "from the mass to the individual" against "the neoliberal economic paradigm"; people seeking "to live effectively in our 'conditions of existence'" (after Durkheim). (`Directly stated`)
- [[The Apple Inversion]] — Apple's flip of "this century-old industrial logic," its "unprecedented fusion of capitalism and the digital"; "Napster hacked the music industry, but Apple appeared to have hacked capitalism." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Neoliberalism]] · [[The Double Movement]] · [[The Shareholder Value Movement]] — the political-economic backdrop: neoliberal "formal indifference," the survival of "the double movement" (Polanyi) "in some form" in northern Europe, and the shareholder-value reorientation of the firm. See [[The Double Movement (Polanyi via Zuboff)]]. (`Directly stated` / `Strongly implied`)
- [[Economic Mutation]] — surveillance capitalism as "a new mutation" and "the emerging logic of accumulation"; framed against Schumpeter's account of capitalist mutation. See [[Economic Mutation (Schumpeter via Zuboff)]]. (`Directly stated` / `Interpretive synthesis`)
- [[The Right to Be Forgotten]] — the 2011 Spanish challenge to Google, when "Spanish citizens asserted their rights to a human future" — a counter-assertion of sanctuary and the future tense. (`Directly stated`)
- [[Contracts of Adhesion]] — the displacement of the social contract by "the uncontract" and take-it-or-leave-it terms that "bypass" decision rights. (`Strongly implied`)

## Major Arguments

- [[Surveillance capitalism is a logic not a technology]] — "Surveillance capitalism is not technology; it is a logic that imbues technology and commands it into action... It is capitalism that assigns the price tag of subjugation and helplessness, not the technology." (`Directly stated`)
- [[We are not the product but the raw material of surveillance capitalism]] — "Forget the cliché that if it's free, 'You are the product.' You are not the product; you are the abandoned carcass. The 'product' derives from the surplus that is ripped from your life." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The goal is no longer to know our behavior but to automate us]] — "With this reorientation from knowledge to power, it is no longer enough to automate information flows about us; the goal now is to automate us." (`Directly stated`)
- [[The unprecedented eludes contest because existing categories make it invisible]] — "When we encounter something unprecedented, we automatically interpret it through the lenses of familiar categories, thereby rendering invisible precisely that which is unprecedented... existing lenses illuminate the familiar, thus obscuring the original by turning the unprecedented into an extension of the past." (`Directly stated`)
- [[Breaking up Google or Facebook would not interrupt surveillance capitalism]] — "calls to break up Google or Facebook on monopoly grounds could easily result in establishing multiple surveillance capitalist firms, though at a diminished scale, and thus clear the way for more surveillance capitalist competitors." (`Directly stated`)

## Characteristic Metaphors

- *The puppet master and the puppet* — the governing figure: "we hunt the puppet master, not the puppet"; the "instrumentation" is "the puppet: the ubiquitous connected material architecture of sensate computation," while "instrumentalization" names "the social relations that orient the puppet masters." (Quoted under [[Big Other]] / [[Instrumentarian Power]].) (`Directly stated`)
- *The one-way mirror* — surveillance as "asymmetries of knowledge and power": "A one-way mirror embodies the specific social relations of surveillance based on asymmetries of knowledge and power." (Quoted under [[Behavioral Surplus]].) (`Directly stated`)
- *Raw material / extraction* — the extractive register throughout: human experience as "free raw material," "raw-material supplies," surplus that "leaches" and is "ripped from your life"; Big Other "poaches our behavior for surplus and leaves behind all the meaning," likened to "the monstrous slaughter of elephants for ivory." (Quoted under [[The Reality Business]] / [[The Means of Behavioral Modification]].) (`Directly stated`)
- *The Hive / no exit* — collective life under instrumentarian power as a beehive: "the mesmerizing social disciplines of the hive," an "intolerable world of 'no exit.'" (Quoted under [[The Hive]].) (`Directly stated`)
- *The unplunderable wall* — sanctuary as inviolable space: the Greek *asylon*, "unplunderable"; "It's the wall, not the tunnels, that requires our attention." (Quoted under [[The Right to Sanctuary]].) (`Directly stated`)

## Related Authors

- [[Byung-Chul Han]] — *Interpretive synthesis*: Zuboff's [[Big Other]] and [[Instrumentarian Power|instrumentarian power]] are the surveillance-capitalist machinery underlying Han's [[Digital Panopticon|digital panopticon]] and his account of [[Dataism]] and [[Smart Power|smart power]]; both diagnose a power that runs on voluntary self-exposure rather than terror — Zuboff's "certainty without terror," Han's "friendly Big Brother." Han names the affective regime; Zuboff names the economic logic ("a logic not a technology"). Drawn by this vault.
- [[Yuval Noah Harari]] — *Interpretive synthesis*: Harari's [[Dataism]] and "[[Algorithms That Know Us Better Than We Know Ourselves|the algorithms that know us better than we know ourselves]]" describe, from the worldview side, what Zuboff describes from the market side — the [[Behavioral Futures Markets|behavioral futures markets]] that turn "from knowledge to power." Both warn the goal becomes prediction-then-modification. Drawn by this vault.
- [[Ray Kurzweil]] — *Interpretive synthesis*: Kurzweil *celebrates* the pervasive instrumented environment (smart dust, the ubiquitous sensorium) that Zuboff anatomizes as [[Big Other]]; he treats [[Privacy vs Security|privacy]] as a tractable trade-off where Zuboff sees a [[The Coup from Above|coup]]. The vault's standing pro/contra pairing on ubiquitous computing. Drawn by this vault.
- [[B. F. Skinner]] — Zuboff's central genealogical source: instrumentarianism "was forged in the controversial intellectual domain known as 'radical behaviorism,' most notably championed by B. F. Skinner and his dream of a 'technology of behavior'"; Big Other "encodes the viewpoint of the Other-One" Skinner theorized, and *Walden Two* supplies her instrumentarian "[[A Utopia of Certainty|utopia of certainty]]." (forward-stub)
- [[Hannah Arendt]] — the analyst of totalitarianism Zuboff invokes both to contrast instrumentarianism with totalitarian power and for her "faculty of freedom... the sheer capacity to begin," which underwrites [[The Right to the Future Tense]]. (forward-stub)
- [[Karl Marx]] — the source of the "means of production" frame Zuboff inverts ("means of behavioral modification eclipses ownership of the means of production") and a touchstone of [[Inevitabilism]] ("Its fall and the victory of the proletariat are equally inevitable"). (forward-stub)
- [[Émile Durkheim]] — the sociologist of the division of labor whom Zuboff extends into [[The Division of Learning in Society]] and whose "[[Conditions of Existence|conditions of existence]]" and "always intensifying 'violence of the struggle'" ground her account of why capitalism aligns (or fails to align) with human need. (forward-stub)
- [[Hal Varian]] — Google's chief economist, Zuboff's recurring primary witness for surveillance capitalism's self-understanding; she rejects his framing of foundational rights as "quaint accessories." (forward-stub)
- [[Alex Pentland]] — the MIT social-physics theorist whose celebration of "social pressure and tuning" on Facebook Zuboff treats as the manifesto of [[The Hive]] and the [[Instrumentarian Power|instrumentarian collective]]. (forward-stub)

## Disagreements or Tensions

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — Zuboff's whole apparatus turns experience into immaterial "behavioral data" and predictions ("data that is itself immaterial"), the rendering of lived, embodied reality into informational surplus — her economic-power inflection of the vault's nine-author tension.
- [[Object vs System]] — surveillance capitalism subordinates products and the firm-as-object to a planetary *system* of extraction and modification ("Big Other"); the smart object is "a kind of marionette" of the larger architecture.
- [[Knowledge vs Power]] — the organizing opposition of her late chapters: the shift "from knowledge to power," from automating information *about* us to automating *us*; surveillance capitalists "know everything about us, whereas their operations are designed to be unknowable to us."
- [[Freedom vs Control]] — Zuboff's [[The Right to the Future Tense|right to the future tense]] and freedom-of-will set against instrumentarianism's "guaranteed outcomes" and "certainty without terror"; "the future collapses into an infinite present of mere behavior."
- [[Instrumentarian Power vs Totalitarianism]] — her core conceptual distinction: instrumentarian power is "the precise antithesis of Orwell's Big Brother," "a market project that converges with the digital," not "a political project that converged with economics" — and equating the two "impedes our understanding as well as our ability to resist."
