# Source Summary: 12. Beyond the Subject

**Book:** [[Psychopolitics]]
**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Raw file:** [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/12 Beyond the Subject]]

## Orientation

This is the closing chapter of *Psychopolitics* (pp. 77–79), and it performs the book's constructive, emancipatory turn. Where the preceding chapters diagnose neoliberal [[Smart Power|psychopolitics]] — the [[Smart Power]] that colonizes the psyche, gamifies labor, mines emotion, and renders the soul transparent and predictable through Big Data — this final chapter sketches the figure of resistance, named only obliquely as a mode of existence "beyond the subject." The chapter is short and citationally dense, routed almost entirely through [[Friedrich Nietzsche|Nietzsche]] and [[Michel Foucault|Foucault]], and it inverts every term the book has used to describe domination: calculability becomes the event's incalculability, the colonized psyche becomes the de-psychologized self, and subjection becomes the wrenching-free of the subject.

The argument moves in three steps. First, Han recovers [[The Event]] (*Ereignis*) — via Nietzsche's "naturification" (*Vernatürlichung*) and Foucault's eventful conception of history — as that which is "absolutely sudden and thwarting," incalculable and unpredictable, and which "breaks the subject open and wrests it from subjection." The event is precisely what psychopolitics, with its predictive Big Data positivism, seeks to abolish; its incalculability is its emancipatory force (the structural complement to the earlier thesis that [[Big Data is blind to the event and so blind to the future]]). Second, Han re-mobilizes the book's recurring distinction between *Erfahrung* and *Erlebnis* ([[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]]): experience (*Erfahrung*), founded on discontinuity and transformation, "wrench[es] the subject from itself," whereas the neoliberal psychopolitics of experiencing or emotion (*Erlebnis*) "only ensnares the subject deeper and deeper in the state of subjection." Han etymologizes the subject as the one "cast under," subjected by a higher instance, so that liberation means tearing the subject out of subjection.

Third, Han offers his positive program: the Foucauldian art of living as a "practice of freedom," which unfolds as de-psychologization — "the art of killing psychology," the creation of "unnamed indi­vidualities, beings, relations, qualities." Because neoliberal psychopolitics is "a technology of domination that stabilizes and perpetuates the prevailing system by means of psychological programming and steering," the praxis of freedom must disarm it by de-psychologizing and "de-voiding" (*ent-leeren*) the subject, opening onto "a mode of existence that still has no name: an unwritten future." The chapter is the conceptual ground of the [[Beyond the Subject|beyond-the-subject]] figure and the hinge between Han's critique of psychopolitics and the idiot/idiotism figure developed in the book's final chapter (not named here). Notably, Han's entire positive program is fully Foucauldian, even though elsewhere in the book he argues that neoliberal psychopolitics supersedes Foucauldian biopolitics — a productive tension carried by [[Byung-Chul Han and Michel Foucault]].

## Key Concepts

- [[The Event]] — the absolutely sudden, incalculable rupture (*Ereignis*) that annuls the standing order, breaks the subject open, and wrests it from subjection; the outside of the calculable, predictable world that psychopolitics and Big Data produce.
- [[Beyond the Subject]] — the chapter's titular figure of resistance: the subject as *subjectum* ("cast under" / subjected), and its liberation as a de-voiding (*ent-leeren*) that opens onto a nameless, unwritten mode of existence.
- De-psychologization — the praxis of freedom proper to the art of living; "the art of killing psychology," which disarms psychopolitics by emptying the subject of the programmed, steered psyche on which neoliberal power operates. *(No canonical note yet; rendered as plain text per link policy.)*
- The Art of Living (practice of freedom) — following Foucault, an art of existence that "brings forth an entirely different mode of existence," standing opposed to the "psychological terror" through which subjugating subjectivation occurs. *(No canonical note yet; plain text.)*
- [[Smart Power|Neoliberal psychopolitics]] — here defined as "a technology of domination that stabilizes and perpetuates the prevailing system by means of psychological programming and steering"; the antagonist that de-psychologization is posed to disarm.

## Key Arguments

- The event's incalculability is what wrests the subject from subjection — psychopolitics rules through calculation and prediction; the event "defies all calculation and prediction," so it marks the outside and limit of psychopolitical power. *(No standalone note yet; cf. [[Big Data is blind to the event and so blind to the future]].)*
- Erfahrung frees the subject while Erlebnis (emotion) deepens subjection — transformative experience dissolves the subject and frees it from itself, whereas the neoliberal psychopolitics of emotion ensnares it ever deeper. *(No standalone note yet; carried by [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]].)*
- De-psychologization is the praxis of freedom that disarms psychopolitics — because psychopolitics operates *through* the psyche, the way to disarm it is to remove its object by de-psychologizing and "de-voiding" the subject. Structural echo of [[Compulsive transparency stabilizes the existing system]] and complement to [[Freedom itself is producing coercion]]. *(No standalone note yet; plain text.)*
- The de-voided subject opens onto a nameless, unwritten future — emptying the subject yields not a freer subject but something past the subject-form, "a mode of existence that still has no name." *(No standalone note yet; plain text.)*

## Important Definitions

- [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]] — re-glossed here via Foucault: *Erfahrung* = discontinuity, transformation, and the "wrenching" of the subject from itself; *Erlebnis* = emotion/experiencing = deepened subjection. This chapter extends the existing definition with the Foucauldian "wrenching" gloss and the explicit alignment of *Erlebnis* with neoliberal psychopolitics.
- [[Psychopolitics (Han)]] — anchored here by Han's one-sentence definition of neoliberal psychopolitics as "a technology of domination that stabilizes and perpetuates the prevailing system by means of psychological programming and steering."
- Subject as subjection (*subjectum*) — Han's etymological definition: "Being a subject means being subjected, being cast under, by a higher instance." *(Held as a passage within [[Beyond the Subject]]; no standalone note.)*
- Event (Han via Foucault) — defined twice: Han's own gloss (incalculable rupture that "annuls what has held until now" and "brings into play an outside") and Foucault's quoted gloss (the reversal of a relationship of forces, the usurpation of power). *(Carried by [[The Event]]; no standalone definition note.)*

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- The event as natural disaster / act of God — the event's incalculable abruptness figured as "just as incalculable and abrupt as a natural disaster or act of God." *(One-off image; folded into [[The Event]].)*
- Breaking the subject open / wrenching and tearing — the event "breaks the subject open," and experience is a "wrenching" and "tearing" out of subjection (toward "annihilation," "dissolution"): a consistent figural register of violence and rupture against the sealed, programmed neoliberal psyche.
- De-voiding / emptying (*ent-leeren*) — liberation imaged as emptying-out rather than filling-up, inverting the productivist logic of self-optimization. *(Image inside [[Beyond the Subject]].)*
- No strong recurring symbol; the "unwritten future" / "mode of existence that still has no name" functions as a deliberate withholding of figuration appropriate to a resistance defined as un-nameable.

## Major Tensions

- [[Freedom vs Control]] — the chapter is the emancipatory counter-pole to the book's "freedom itself as the crisis" thesis: here freedom is reclaimed as a Foucauldian *practice* rather than exposed as a ruse.
- [[Positivity vs Negativity]] — *Erfahrung* and the event carry [[Negativity]] and discontinuity; *Erlebnis* and emotion fall on the side of [[Positivity]]. The chapter is another instance of Han's negativity-as-resistance motif.
- [[Subject vs Object]] — adjacent but distinct: the chapter's axis is subjection vs liberation (subject vs beyond-the-subject), not the subject/object ontology of the O'Gieblyn tension.
- Calculability vs the Event (prediction vs rupture) — the core opposition of the chapter: psychopolitical prediction/calculation against the event that "defies all calculation and prediction." *(No canonical tension note yet; plain text.)*

## Candidate Essay Uses

- A counter-pole to surveillance/Big Data essays: the incalculable event as the structural outside of predictive power. Where earlier chapters argue [[Big Data is blind to the event and so blind to the future]], this chapter reads that blindness as the *site* of resistance — the unpredictable rupture that no dataist regime can pre-empt. Useful for an essay on art's relation to the event, the accident, and the non-calculable.
- A frame for thinking the de-psychologized, "unnamed" self against the optimized, self-tracking subject — relevant to curatorial work on embodiment, self-exposure, and refusal. The "art of living" as the killing of psychology can be set against the wellness/quantified-self apparatus the book critiques.
- A test case for Han's ambivalent debt to Foucault: an essay could hold this chapter's wholly Foucauldian "practice of freedom" against [[Foucault failed to make the turn to psychopolitics]] to ask whether Han's resistance program is consistent with his claim that psychopolitics has outrun biopolitics. See [[Byung-Chul Han and Michel Foucault]] and the tension [[Biopolitics vs Psychopolitics]].
- A bridge to the material/immaterial axis: de-voiding (*ent-leeren*) as emptying rather than producing offers a counter-image to the productivist logic of accumulation and exhibition — a vocabulary for dematerialization read as subtraction rather than mere disappearance.
