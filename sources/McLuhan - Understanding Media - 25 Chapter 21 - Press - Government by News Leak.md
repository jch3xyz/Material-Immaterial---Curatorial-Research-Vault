# Source Summary: Press — Government by News Leak

**Book:** [[Understanding Media]]

## Orientation

This is a Part II case-study chapter of *Understanding Media*: McLuhan turns the apparatus built in Part I onto the newspaper. The organizing claim is that the press is not a vehicle for a private "point of view" but a [[The Mosaic|mosaic]] — "a group confessional form that provides communal participation," opposed in its very format to the book, "a private confessional form that provides a 'point of view.'" The newspaper page is a field of discontinuous, juxtaposed items unified only by a dateline, and the daily juxtaposition is what "gives the press its complex dimension of human interest." Because the mosaic "means, not a detached 'point of view,' but participation in process," McLuhan concludes that "the press is inseparable from the democratic process, but quite expendable from a literary or book point of view." This is a direct application of his master thesis [[The Medium Is the Message]] (the closing line: media owners "sense that their power is in the medium and not in the message or the program") and of [[Media as Extensions of Man]].

The title figure — [[Government by News Leak]] — is McLuhan's name for the political consequence of electric speed. As "the speed of information increases," politics moves "away from representation and delegation of constituents toward immediate involvement of the entire community in the central acts of decision"; in diplomacy, decisions are "announced before they are made" to test responses. The apparent paradox that the press cleanses by publicity while "most affairs must be kept secret" (drawn from Douglas Cater's *The Fourth Branch of Government*) is resolved because "Top secrecy is translated into public participation and responsibility by the magic flexibility of the controlled news leak." This is the specifically political form of McLuhan's [[The Global Village]] thesis of electric "total interdependence."

The chapter also restages McLuhan's standing themes through journalistic material: the newspaper as a hot medium that needs "bad news for the sake of intensity and reader participation" ([[Media Hot and Cold]]); the press as the [[The Content of Any Medium Is Another Medium|mosaic successor to the book-form]] whose "technology is also a mosaic of all the technologies of the community"; the crossing of telegraph, rotary press, linotype, and typewriter as [[Hybrid Energy]]; the cross-cultural difference between "oral Russia" and "visual America" ([[Acoustic vs Visual Space]], [[Detribalization and Retribalization]]); and the recurring failure of perception by which critics (Newton Minow, Daniel Boorstin) "give attention to the program 'content' of our media while ignoring the form" ([[Numbness vs Awareness]]). Rhetorically the chapter is a sustained defense of the press's "frivolity" and "seamy side" against the "book-oriented man," whose hostility to the press McLuhan diagnoses as the resentment of "the merely private and fragmentary point of view" toward "the big communal power of the press."

## Key Arguments

- The press is a mosaic, participational ("group confessional") form opposed in its very format to the book's private "point of view," and is therefore "inseparable from the democratic process."
- Electric speed forces "government by news leak": politics shifts from representation/delegation toward immediate total involvement, and secrecy is converted into participation by the controlled leak.
- Media power lies in the form, not the content; reformers (Newton Minow, Boorstin) who attack content while ignoring form misunderstand the medium ([[The medium not its content is the message]]).
- The newspaper is a hot medium that requires bad news for intensity; ads are "always good news" and need bad news to balance them.
- In the electric/automation age "information is the crucial commodity" and the ad-supported press is "paid learning."
- The press has totally diverse effects in different cultures (oral vs. visual); to assume it functions identically across cultures "is not really in touch with the medium."
- All media — not just modern ones — make and stage a fictive image of the community; Boorstin's "pseudo-event" charge betrays ignorance of pre-electric media.

## Important Definitions

- "Human interest" (technical/mosaic sense) — "a technical term meaning that which happens when multiple book pages or multiple information items are arranged in a mosaic on one sheet."
- The mosaic / corporate (communal) image — "the mode of the corporate or collective image," whose participation is "communal rather than private, inclusive rather than exclusive."
- Equitone — Addison and Steele's print-matched prose technique: "maintaining a single level of tone and attitude to the reader throughout the entire composition."
- The dateline — "the only organizing principle of the newspaper image of the community."

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- [[Government by News Leak|The controlled news leak]] — "the magic flexibility of the controlled news leak"; top secrecy converted into public participation.
- The press agent as ventriloquist / the newspaper as his dummy (and as a painter's palette) — managed news: "He can make it say what he wants."
- "Buying a piece of the reader" / the free show — advertisers "buy a piece of the reader, listener, or viewer as definitely as if they hired our homes for a public meeting."
- "The book arrives too late" (Lamartine) — the press's instantaneity against the book's belatedness.
- The battery radio on the camel — radio's "fury of oral agitation" rejuvenating the Bedouin tribes, against the press's visual nationalism.
- The wallpaper newspaper / the magazine-papered igloo / the kitchen-floor paper — the press persisting as environment and mosaic even when not read for content.
- The classified ads (and stock-market quotations) — "the bedrock of the press."

## Major Tensions

- [[Fragmentation vs Wholeness]] — the book as "a private voice" / "point of view" vs. the press as "a communal mosaic or corporate image"; "as incompatible as any two media could be."
- [[Hot vs Cool Media]] — the newspaper "is a hot medium" requiring bad news for intensity, yet its mosaic form commands high participation (McLuhan's own internal complexity).
- [[Numbness vs Awareness]] — the form-blind critic / "book-oriented man" attends to content; "Unawareness of the nature of the press in its subliminal or latent action is as common among politicians as among political scientists."
- [[Mechanical vs Electric]] — print's uniform "equitone" vs. the telegraph that "broke language away again from the printed word" into "headlines, journalese, and telegraphese."
- [[Detachment vs Participation]] — the detached private reader vs. the press mosaic's "participation in process."
