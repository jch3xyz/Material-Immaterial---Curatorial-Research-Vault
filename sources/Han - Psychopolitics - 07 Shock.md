# Source Summary: 7. Shock

**Book:** [[Psychopolitics]]
**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Raw file:** [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/07 Shock]]

## Orientation

This short chapter is a polemic against Naomi Klein's *The Shock Doctrine*. Han takes up Klein's two "Doctor Shocks" — the CIA-funded Montreal psychiatrist Ewen Cameron, who used electroshock, sensory deprivation, and drugs to "de-brand" and "re-brand" the psyche from a "clean slate," and Milton Friedman, "the theologian of the neoliberal market," who exploits post-catastrophe trauma to "depattern" whole societies back to "pure capitalism." Han concedes that Klein has accurately described one technology of power, but argues she has mistaken a *disciplinary* technique for the actual logic of neoliberalism.

Shock, for Han, is essentially *negative*: it paralyzes, annihilates, empties, "wipes the psyche clean." It belongs to the disciplinary and [[Biopolitics|biopolitical]] order — it is "orthopaedic," coercive, immunological, oriented against an Other or Enemy. Cameron, as Doctor Shock, "represents a phenomenon of the [[Immunological Age]]," and his isolation chambers are literally "a panopticon far crueller than Bentham's." The chapter thus reads shock as a residue of an earlier epoch of immunological [[Negativity]], not as the engine of contemporary power.

Against this, Han installs his central thesis: actual neoliberal [[Psychopolitics]] is dominated by [[Positivity]], not negativity. It does not shock, repress, forbid, or "depattern" — it seduces, flatters, pleases, anticipates. It "works with positive stimuli" and "enlists Liking" instead of administering "bitter medicine." It is [[Smart Power|SmartPolitics]]: pre-emptive, protocolling, prognostic, pleasing. Klein's conspiratorial, shock-centered frame "blinds her to the actual workings of neoliberal psychopolitics." The chapter functions as a hinge: it clears away the wrong (negative, disciplinary, shock-based) model so Han can foreground the right one (positive, seductive, smart), carrying the positivity/negativity axis of *The Transparency Society* into the explicit theory of psychopolitical power.

## Key Concepts

- [[Psychopolitics]] — the book's title concept; here characterized operationally and by contrast: neoliberal power does not coerce or paralyze the soul but seduces, flatters, pleases, and pre-empts it.
- [[Smart Power]] — Han's "SmartPolitics," the positive, pleasing, fulfilling modality of neoliberal power that "seeks to please and fulfil, not to repress."
- [[Positivity]] — the defining mode of psychopolitics: it works with positive stimuli, Liking, and flattery rather than negative threats.
- [[Negativity]] — the "essential trait" of shock: electroshock works by "paralyzing and annihilating the contents of the psyche."
- [[Immunological Age]] — Han's periodization in which shock is immunological intervention against the Other, the Alien, the Enemy, on a Manichaean Good/Evil structure.
- [[Biopolitics]] — shock therapy as a coercive, "orthopaedic" measure belonging to disciplinary society, not to neoliberalism.
- [[Society of Control]] — the pre-emptive, anticipatory, modulating logic that Han poses against shock's after-the-fact cancellation of action.

## Key Arguments

- [[Naomi Klein's shock model misreads neoliberal power]] — Klein accurately describes a real disciplinary, immunological technology of power, but because shock is essentially negative it cannot be the engine of neoliberalism, which operates through positive, seductive psychopolitics.

## Important Definitions

- [[Psychopolitics (Han)]] — neoliberal power as a positive technology that seduces, flatters, pre-empts, and protocols desire, in contrast to the negative, paralyzing operation of shock.
- [[Smart Power (Han)]] — the pleasing, fulfilling, non-repressive ("SmartPolitics") modality of neoliberal psychopolitics coined in this chapter's closing sentence.

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- [[The Panopticon]] — Cameron's hospital as "a panopticon far crueller than Bentham's"; disciplinary architecture taken to its cruelest extreme.
- [[The Like Button]] — the conceptual seed of "enlists Liking": positive affect, not fear, as the medium of power.

## Major Tensions

- [[Positivity vs Negativity]] — the organizing axis: shock as negativity (threat, paralysis, annihilation, depatterning) versus neoliberal psychopolitics as positivity (stimulus, Liking, flattery, seduction).
- [[Biopolitics vs Psychopolitics]] — shock as disciplinary, biopolitical, coercive, orthopaedic versus neoliberalism as psychopolitics; the book's central Foucault-to-Han move.
- [[Discipline vs Control]] — shock cancels and annihilates action after the fact, while psychopolitics prognosticates and pre-empts it, acting ahead of action.

## Candidate Essay Uses

- A surveillance-and-power essay contrasting the *negative* disciplinary panopticon (Cameron's literal torture-panopticon, "far crueller than Bentham's") with the *positive*, voluntary, seductive control of the digital present — the through-line from *The Transparency Society* into *Psychopolitics*.
