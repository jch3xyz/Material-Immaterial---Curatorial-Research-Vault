# Source Summary: Chapter 6 — Healing as Killing

**Book:** [[Psychopolitics]]
**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Raw file:** [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/06 Healing as Killing]]

## Orientation

This short chapter (source pages 29–32) carries the book's most compressed paradox in its title: under neoliberal [[Psychopolitics]], "Healing, it turns out, means killing" (`Directly stated`). Han's target is the therapeutic-industrial apparatus of self-improvement — "Countless self-management workshops, motivational retreats and seminars on personality or mental training" — which promises "boundless self-optimization and heightened efficiency" but in fact functions as a refined technique of domination. The decisive move of neoliberalism, for Han, is that it has "discovered integral human being as the object of exploitation": power now capitalizes not merely on working time but on the whole person, on all the attention the individual commands, and on "his or her very life."

Han periodizes this as a third regime. The age of sovereignty levied and seized; disciplinary society banked on industrial production and "active and industrial added-value"; but under "the financial capitalism of our day, value is being destroyed at the root." This inaugurates "the age of exhaustion," in which "the psyche itself is being exploited" and whose defining maladies are "depression and burnout." The chapter's argumentative engine is [[Self-Optimization]]: because it "coincides point-for-point with the optimization of the system," and is driven not by "concern for the good life" but by "the logic of quantifying success on the market," self-optimization "amounts to total self-exploitation." It is here that the book's [[Auto-Exploitation]] thesis is realized at the level of the psyche, and where the [[Achievement Society|achievement-subject]] is shown "running aground on the imperative of self-optimization."

The chapter's philosophical spine is the defense of [[Negativity]] against the totalitarian reach of [[Positivity]]. Han argues that "It is impossible to subordinate human personhood to the dictates of positivity entirely": without negativity, life degrades into "something dead" (Hegel), "Pain is constitutive for experience (Erfahrung)" — invoking the distinction tracked in [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]] — and a life of pure positive emotion and "flow" "is not human at all." He mobilizes [[Hegel]] and [[Friedrich Nietzsche]] for negativity's necessity, then shows the regime absorbing even this: the optimization imperative "manages to exploit pain," tolerating only the productive, optimizable pain of CANI ("Constant, Never-Ending Improvement"). The closing thesis equates two violences — "the violence of positivity is just as destructive as the violence of negativity" — and casts the consciousness industry of neoliberal psychopolitics as the destroyer of a soul that "is anything but a machine of positivity (Positivmaschine)." Han also frames self-optimization as a secularized [[Michel Foucault|Foucauldian]] "technology of subjectivation and domination," heir to Protestant self-examination, where "instead of searching out sins, one hunts down negative thoughts."

## Key Arguments

- Self-optimization is total self-exploitation, not care for the good life — it follows from "systemic constraints" and "the logic of quantifying success on the market," coinciding point-for-point with optimization of the system (`Directly stated`).
- Healing means killing — "healing," the keyword of American self-help, is a boundless [[Self-Optimization]] that "is proving destructive" and "leading to mental collapse" (`Directly stated`).
- The violence of positivity is as destructive as the violence of negativity — totalized [[Positivity]], the soul figured as Positivmaschine, exercises its own destruction of the soul (`Directly stated`).
- [[Negativity]] is constitutive of life, experience, and the soul — without it, life is "something dead" (Hegel); pain is constitutive of Erfahrung; pure "flow" "is not human at all" (`Directly stated`).
- The optimization regime exploits even pain — it tolerates "only pain that can be exploited for the purposes of optimization," instrumentalizing suffering as the productive "pressure of dissatisfaction" (`Directly stated`).
- Self-optimization is a secularized Protestant technology of subjectivation — it resembles Protestant self-examination and self-monitoring, "a technology of subjectivation and domination," now hunting "negative thoughts" instead of "sins" (`Strongly implied`).

## Important Definitions

- Healing (Han's diagnostic sense) — in American self-help, "healing" refers to "self-optimization that is supposed to therapeutically eliminate any and all functional weakness or mental obstacle in the name of efficiency and performance."
- The age of exhaustion — the regime inaugurated by financial-capitalist value-destruction "in the course of inaugurating the age of exhaustion," in which "the psyche itself is being exploited" and "psychic maladies such as depression and burnout define our times."
- Positivmaschine ("machine of positivity") — Han's coinage for the soul reduced to a pure-positivity device, which he negates: "the human soul, which is anything but a machine of positivity (Positivmaschine)."

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- [[Healing as Killing]] — the central oxymoronic figure: therapeutic "healing" as lethal self-exploitation
- The soul as a "machine of positivity" (Positivmaschine) — the soul figured (and rejected) as a positivity-machine, to insist on its constitutive negativity
- The neoliberal subject "running aground" — a nautical/grounding image for the achievement-subject foundering on the optimization imperative
- Hunting down negative thoughts / the ego grappling with itself as an enemy — the self at war with itself, auto-policing displacing the confessional hunt for sins
- The new Gospel of limitless achievement and optimization — the managerial-motivational creed figured as religion, preached by fundamentalist-preachers-as-managers

## Major Tensions

- [[Positivity vs Negativity]] — the governing opposition: the regime of positivity against the constitutive necessity of negativity (pain, suffering, unhappiness) for life, experience, and the soul
- [[Freedom vs Control]] — the achievement-subject's voluntary self-optimization (freedom) is precisely what exhausts and destroys it (control)
- Healing vs Killing — the titular paradox of cure-as-destruction
- Good life vs Market logic — self-optimization is driven not by "concern for the good life" but by "the logic of quantifying success on the market"
