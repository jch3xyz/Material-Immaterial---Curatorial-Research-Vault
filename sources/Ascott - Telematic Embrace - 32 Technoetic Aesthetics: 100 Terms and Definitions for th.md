## Orientation

"Technoetic Aesthetics: 100 Terms and Definitions for the Post-Biological Era" (1996; originally published in Japanese in Ascott's *Art & Telematics: Toward the Construction of New Aesthetics*, trans. E. Fujihara, Tokyo: NTT Publishing Co., 1998) is not an expository essay but a **glossary** — a hundred short, aphoristic dictionary entries, alphabetical from "Art" to "Zen." It is the keystone vocabulary of Ascott's late, third-phase thought, distilling his telematic and cybernetic theory into maximally portable, often punning, neologisms (Cyburbs, Telemadic, Ayahu-ascii, Phreno-fractals, State-of-the-heart). Where the surrounding essays argue, this chapter simply *names*: it coins or redefines the terms the rest of Ascott's project runs on — [[Technoetics]], [[Cyberception]], [[Moistmedia]], [[Telenoia]], [[Telepresence]], [[The Double Gaze]], [[The Telematic Imperative]], [[The Post-Biological Era]], [[Psibernetics]], and [[The Planetary Collegium]].

The rhetorical posture is programmatic and visionary, compressed almost to the point of slogan. The opening entries set the whole agenda in two lines: art, "while traditionally focused on the appearance of things and their representation," is "now concerned with processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence"; and "Classical aesthetics dealt with the behaviour of forms; the new aesthetic deals with forms of behaviour." From this twin pivot — from noun to verb, from surface to process — the lexicon restates Ascott's central theses: that consciousness, not matter, is now the medium of art ("the technoetic aesthetics of creative consciousness and artificial life"); that the self is decentred, distributed, and multiple ("I connect, therefore I am multiple"); that the sole imperative of a borderless world is to connect; and that technology re-enchants reality, reintegrating the shamanic, the paranormal, and the spiritual. The chapter's signature synthesis is the "Variable reality" triad — dry (VR), wet (nurtured nature), and the emergent **moist** of artificial life — Ascott's clearest lexical seed of the slogan "the future will be moist."

Because it is a 100-entry list, the chapter is the densest single source of Ascott's coined CONCEPTS and DEFINITIONS in the whole volume, and the ideal place to harvest verbatim definitional quotes. Most entries, however, are one-off puns and not durable vault concepts; this summary links only the ~15 terms that recur across Ascott's essays and the vault's existing clusters, and quotes the rest as flavour. For the [[Material vs Immaterial]] tension the chapter is a substantive contribution: the "Immateriality" entry loops dematerialisation back into "re-materialisation of culture in the form of artificial life," while "Supervenience" insists that mind, "although ubiquitous and always present, ... requires matter to become manifest."

## Key Concepts

- [[Technoetics]] — the fusion of technology and consciousness; "esoteric, occult, spiritual knowledge revealed through technology" (the "Technostic" entry). The umbrella term of Ascott's third phase, named in the chapter title.
- [[Technoetic Aesthetics]] — the displacement of the classical concern with surface appearance by an aesthetics of creative consciousness and artificial life. Kept DISTINCT from [[Technoetics]] (the broad worldview).
- [[Cyberception]] — "the emergent human faculty of technologically augmented cognition and perception"; the central perceptual term of Ascott's third phase.
- [[Moistmedia]] — the "Variable reality" triad: dry (VR), wet (nurtured nature), and the emergent moist of biotechnological artificial life; the convergence the chapter seeds with "the future will be moist."
- [[Telenoia]] — "the networked consciousness of global connectivity"; the deliberate opposite of industrial-era paranoia.
- [[Telepresence]] — presence distributed throughout the Internet; being "both here and there, out-of-body and reembodied, dematerialised, and reconfigured at one and the same time."
- [[The Telematic Imperative]] — when geographical boundaries dissolve, "the only imperative is to connect"; "even the self is permeable."
- [[The Double Gaze]] — the reciprocal gaze (the environment sees/hears/feels us as we see it) and the simultaneous access to two fields, "psychic space and cyberspace" (the "Double gazing" and "Double consciousness" entries).
- [[The Post-Biological Era]] — the epoch in which the old disputes of modernism and poststructuralism "dissolve before the prospect of postbiological life."
- [[Psibernetics]] — the convergence of psi (telepathy, telekinesis, out-of-body experience) with telematics; the psibernetic phenomena and the "Psiborg," the "psychically extended organism."
- [[The Planetary Collegium]] — Ascott's networked-learning institution, succeeding the university as the university succeeded the monastery.
- [[Network Consciousness]] — personal neural networks merging with global networks "to create a new space of consciousness"; the World-mind, Hypercortex, and Noetic networks of the lexicon.
- [[The Distributed Self]] — the decentred, "constructively schizophrenic" Cyberself; "I connect, therefore I am multiple."
- [[Nature II]] — the replacement of "the old authorised nature" by artificial life, assisted by bio-telematic connectivity.
- [[The Shamantic Web]] — "Shamantics," the foregrounding of the semantic aspect of shamanism in the technoetic context; the shamanic/altered-states strand (Enactment, Ayahu-ascii, Sipápuni).
- [[Connectivism]] — "connectivism is the way of the technoetic artist"; the connective ethos converging the artificial and the natural; carries Ascott's five-fold path (connectivity, immersion, interaction, transformation, emergence).
- [[The Post-Biological Body]] — the body as "the site of bionic transformation at which we can recreate ourselves and redefine what it is to be human."
- [[Emergence]] — the fifth step of the "Tao of telematics," and a redefinition of art's object as "processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence."
- [[New Media Art]] — extended by this whole late-1990s post-biological, online-arts vocabulary (the realized committed stub; cross-author).
- [[Art as Experiential Technology]] — extended here: "Subtle, intimate, ubiquitous ... the three conditions to which all interactive art aspires."
- [[Reciprocal Communication]] — extended here via "Double gazing" (the returned gaze) and the trivial/nontrivial interactivity distinction.

## Key Arguments

- [[Art as the behaviour of forms has become art as a form of behaviour]] — "Classical aesthetics dealt with the behaviour of forms; the new aesthetic deals with forms of behaviour."
- [[Western art's preoccupation with appearance is dying]] — art has shifted from "the appearance of things and their representation" to "processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence."
- [[Process replaces product just as system supersedes structure]] — the technoetic aesthetic of creative consciousness and artificial life supersedes the classical concern with the surface image.
- [[We write our own reality and negotiate meaning through interaction]] — the "Hermeneutic error" entry: the world is no longer a text to be interpreted but a reality we write through interaction.
- [[The unified individual is dead the networked self is multiple]] — "Freud is dead and the myth of the unified individual has been destroyed"; "I connect, therefore I am multiple."
- [[Dematerialisation rematerialises culture as artificial life]] — "the dematerialisation of art ... leads inexorably to the re-materialisation of culture in the form of artificial life."
- [[Mind is supervenient on its interfaces but requires matter to become manifest]] — the "Supervenience" entry, a key Material vs Immaterial statement.
- [[Art is shifting from the dry digital and wet biological to a third moist domain]] — the "Variable reality" triad; the moistmedia thesis stated lexically.
- [[Cyberception makes immaterial process perceptible as palpably as material objects]] — cross-linked from the "Cyberception" definition.

## Major Tensions

- [[Dry vs Wet vs Moist]] — the "Variable reality" triad; the book's signature synthesis-tension and the seed of [[Moistmedia]].
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — Ascott's contribution: "Immateriality" loops dematerialisation into re-materialisation as artificial life, while "Supervenience" insists mind "requires matter to become manifest."
- [[Telenoia vs Paranoia]] — networked consciousness vs the alienated, secretive, private self of industrial culture.
- [[Trivial vs Nontrivial Interactivity]] — the closed, finite-data-set form (FDS, artist in control) vs the open-ended form (OES, user/environment in control).
- [[Appearance vs Apparition]] — the classical aesthetics of surface vs the technoetic aesthetics of process, becoming, and "apparition."
- [[Object vs System]] — the participatory/networked pole (open-ended, evolutive systems in the Net) beside Burnham's object→system spine.

## Candidate Essay Uses

- **The lexicon as method.** Use the glossary form itself — art defined by a hundred neologisms rather than a thesis — as a case study in how new-media theory builds vocabulary faster than it builds arguments; set Ascott's coinages beside Burnham's `Systems Esthetics` and Hayles's `Flickering Signifier` as terms that did or did not survive.
- **"The future will be moist."** Build an essay on [[Moistmedia]] and the [[Dry vs Wet vs Moist]] triad as a third term that breaks the material/immaterial binary — the digital does not end in pure information but loops back into the wet/living via artificial life. Pair with Lippard's `Dematerialization` and Burnham's object→system.
- **Re-enchantment of the machine.** Read the technoetic/shamanic strand (Technostic, [[Psibernetics]], [[The Shamantic Web]], Paranature, Ki) against O'Gieblyn's `Re-enchantment` as twin late-modern re-sacralisations of technology.
- **Telenoia vs the panopticon.** Set [[Telenoia]] and the [[The Telematic Imperative|telematic imperative]] ("the only imperative is to connect") against Han's `Digital Panopticon` and Harari's Dataism — the same connective infrastructure read as conviviality versus domination.
- **The distributed self after the liberal subject.** Use "I connect, therefore I am multiple" and the Cyberself to triangulate Hayles's `The Liberal Humanist Subject` (its negation) and Haraway's `Fractured Identities` (its sibling), noting that Ascott's "out-of-body ... dematerialised" telepresence courts exactly the disembodiment Hayles warns against.
- **Reciprocal interactivity.** Trace [[Trivial vs Nontrivial Interactivity]] (FDS vs OES) from Burnham's `One-Way Stimulation vs Two-Way Communication` and `Reciprocal Communication` to contemporary debates over "interactive" art that retains a "phantom audience."
