# Source Summary: The Society of Exhibition

**Book:** [[The Transparency Society]]
**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Raw file:** [[raw/Byung-Chul Han - The Transparency Society/05 Ch2 - Exhibition]]

## Orientation

Chapter 2 extends Walter Benjamin's pairing of [[Cult Value]] and [[Exhibition Value]] into a diagnosis of late capitalism as a [[Exhibition Value|Society of Exhibition]]. Han argues that under the regime of [[Transparency]], cult value — which depended on negativity, secrecy, distance, and the sheer fact of an object's existence — collapses into exhibition value, which exists only insofar as it produces attention. This third value cannot be located within Marx's opposition between [[Use Value]] and [[Exchange Value]]; it is a new metric proper to the [[Exhibition Value|Attention Economy]], and its fulfillment marks, for Han, the fulfillment of capitalism itself.

The chapter then runs this logic through three registers: the photographic image, the human body, and communication. Benjamin's claim that cult value finds "its last refuge" in the human countenance becomes, in Han, the historical observation that the [[Countenance]] has been replaced by the [[Face]] — a flat, transparent commodity-surface tuned for Facebook and Photoshop. [[Digital Photography]] becomes the emblematic medium of [[Positivity]]: without negative, without darkroom, without mortality, without the "this-is-how-it-was" that Barthes located at the heart of photographic experience. The result is a temporality without [[Destiny]] and an image without [[Mourning]].

The chapter's rhetorical posture is polemical and diagnostic. Han equates exhibition with [[Pornography (Han)]], pornography with [[Obscenity]], and obscenity with transparency itself, in order to show that hypervisibility is not neutral exposure but a violent suspension of dwelling, distance, and aesthetic contemplation. The closing pages move from Levinas's [[The Other|Other]] through Heidegger's [[Dwelling]] to Baudrillard's "promiscuity of the look," staging transparency as an [[Anesthetic Aestheticization]] that accelerates the senses past the threshold of reflection.

## Key Concepts

- [[Exhibition Value]] — value that accrues only insofar as objects are seen; the third value beyond Marx's use/exchange pair, generated by [[Exhibition Value|Attention Capital]].
- [[Cult Value]] — value rooted in existence rather than visibility, secured by negativity, secrecy, and fencing-off; the structure transparency dissolves.
- [[Exhibition Value|Attention Capital]] (*Aufmerksamkeitskapital*) — the resource that solicitation [Werben] is mobilized to heighten when dwelling becomes impossible.
- [[Pornography (Han)]] — Han's name for the mode of being in which everything is turned outward, stripped, and put on show; the logical form of exhibition.
- [[Obscenity]] — hypervisibility that lacks the negativity of the hidden; the disappearance of things into "the more visible than visible."
- [[Transparency]] — the imperative that all distance, secret, and Otherness be eliminated for the sake of accelerated circulation.
- [[Digital Photography]] — Han's emblem of purely positive imaging; corollary of a way of living that dispenses with negativity.
- [[Face]] vs [[Countenance]] — the commodity surface stripped of aura versus the Levinasian site of transcendence.
- [[Anesthetic Aestheticization]] — visual communication that occurs through "infection, abreaction, or reflex" and short-circuits aesthetic reflection.
- [[Tactility]] — distanceless "epidermal contiguity of eye and image" that replaces the contemplative gaze.

## Key Arguments

- [[Exhibition Value Exceeds the Marxian Frame]] — exhibition value is neither use nor exchange value; it is generated by attention alone, and signals the fulfillment of capitalism (Directly stated).
- [[Cult Value Migrated to the Countenance, Then Vanished]] — Benjamin lodged cult value's last refuge in the human countenance; under Facebook and Photoshop, even the countenance is reduced to the face as commodity (Directly stated).
- [[Digital Photography Abolishes Negativity]] — without negative, darkroom, or mortality, digital photography has no semantic or temporal density and therefore "says nothing" (Directly stated).
- [[Exhibition Is Exploitation]] — the imperative to display destroys [[Dwelling]] in body and world; the body cannot be inhabited but only exhibited and exploited (Directly stated).
- [[Transparency Equals Pornography Equals Obscenity]] — hypervisibility, hypercommunication, and the pornographic display of body and soul are structurally identical forms of obscenity (Directly stated).
- [[Anesthetic Acceleration of the Senses]] — exhibition-value images reduce complexity to accelerate communication; the senses are too slow for hypercommunication and are therefore bypassed (Directly stated).
- [[Distancelessness Is Not Proximity]] — transparency's de-distantiation destroys the "pure nearness which sustains the distance" rather than producing closeness (Interpretive synthesis of Han via Heidegger).

## Important Definitions

- [[Exhibition Value (Han)]] — "It is not use value because it stands removed from the sphere of utility; it is not exchange value because it does not reflect any labor. It exists thanks only to the attention it produces."
- [[Face (Han)]] — "a visage on display, 'strip[ped] of its aura' . . . the commodity form of the 'human countenance.'"
- [[Dwelling (Han via Heidegger)]] — "to be at peace, to be brought to peace, to remain in peace [zufrieden sein, zum Frieden gebracht, in ihm bleiben]."
- [[Obscenity (Han via Baudrillard)]] — "things visible do not come to an end in obscurity and silence — instead they fade into the more visible than visible: obscenity."
- [[Tactility (Han via Baudrillard)]] — "contact without physicality, 'epidermal contiguity of eye and image,' just a breath away."

## Major Tensions

- [[Cult Value vs Exhibition Value (Han via Benjamin)|Cult Value vs Exhibition Value]] — existence-based negativity versus visibility-based positivity.
- [[Countenance vs Face]] — Levinasian transcendence of the Other versus the immanent commodity surface.
- [[Dwelling vs Solicitation]] — Heideggerian peace-in-place versus the perpetual advertising of self for [[Exhibition Value|Attention Capital]].
- [[Aura vs Transparency]] — the "appearance of a distance" versus the elimination of distance.
- [[Aesthetic Distance vs Tactility]] — the contemplative gaze versus distanceless epidermal contiguity.
- [[Mourning vs Positivity]] — Barthes's "this-is-how-it-was" versus digital photography's deathless present.

## Candidate Essay Uses

- A curatorial essay on how the museum's display logic — which Han would diagnose as exhibition value pushed to absolute — has begun to colonize artworks whose meaning depended on cult value (icons, reliquaries, ritual objects), and what curatorial strategies might re-introduce the negativity of the hidden.
- An essay on the [[Selfie]] and the [[Profile Picture]] as the technical fulfillment of Han's "face": tracing the migration from Benjamin's photographic portrait through Barthes's *that-has-been* to the Facebook/Photoshop face that has no negative.
- An essay pairing Han's "exhibition = pornography = obscenity" with Debord's [[The Society of the Spectacle]] to ask whether transparency is the spectacle's intensification or its negation — testing the claim that hypervisibility is not more visibility but the death of the visible.
