# Source Summary: 10 Table

**Book:** [[Telematic Embrace]]

## Orientation

"Table" is a short 1975 essay (originally published in *House: The Journal of the London Architecture Club* 1, no. 1) that sits squarely in Ascott's pre-telematic, cybernetic/behaviourist phase. It takes the domestic *table* as a model object and reads it not as a thing but as a behavioural matrix — a horizontal "arena for behaviours," "a medium of exchange," "the grounds for change." The essay opens with a semiotics of domestic behaviour (house, kitchen, table as nested analogues of the universe; utensils as "pure ideas, realised in hardware"), then builds the table into Ascott's working model of the open-system artwork. (Confidence: Directly stated.)

The argumentative spine is a sweeping art-historical polemic opposing the **horizontal table-top** (holistic, multi-viewpoint, transactional, "bird's-eye," cosmic, the "mystical matrix" of myth/religion and the Tarot) to the **vertical plane** of the Renaissance "picture on the wall" / the mirror — "the archetypal convention of a whole culture of separation, competition, self-love" installed by perspective and the vanishing point. Ascott then extends the polemic to broadcast television and "video art," whose social and cultural "bankruptcy" he blames on verticality, against which he proposes horizontal, all-round, top-and-bottomless table-top video as a route to "holistic consciousness." (Confidence: Directly stated.)

The piece closes with Ascott's signature behaviourist credo: art is now "a form of behaviour (rather than a simple array of images or objects)," it "aspires to the condition of exchange," it is an open system with "no final outcome," and "Art, then, resides not on a surface or within material objects alone, but between the behaviours of all parts of the system." This is the object→system / image→behaviour move in miniature — the participatory, behavioural counterpart to Burnham's [[Systems Esthetics]] of the object — and a sharp anticipation of Ascott's later anti-spectatorial, network-consciousness arguments: the table-top as a many-to-many transactional surface prefigures the telematic network. (Confidence: Directly stated for the credo; Interpretive synthesis for the placement against Burnham and the telematic anticipation.)

- **The object→system shift from the behaviour side.** Use "Table" beside Burnham's [[Systems Esthetics]] to stage the two poles of 1960s cybernetic art: Burnham reads the object's dissolution into system; Ascott reads the same move as art becoming behaviour/transaction.
