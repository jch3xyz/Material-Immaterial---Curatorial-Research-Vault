# Source Summary: The Society of Tiredness

**Book:** [[The Burnout Society]]
**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Raw file:** [[raw/Han - The Burnout Society/08 The Society of Tiredness]]

## Orientation

"The Society of Tiredness" (printed pp. 30–34, trans. Erik Butler) is the closing chapter and the constructive culmination of *The Burnout Society*. Having diagnosed the [[Achievement Society|achievement society]] as a self-exploiting, hyperactive [[Doping Society|doping society]] poor in negativity and dominated by [[Positivity|excess positivity]], Han here makes a therapeutic, almost redemptive turn: not all tiredness is the pathological exhaustion of burnout. The chapter is the affirmative counter-image to the whole book — the redemptive use of negativity against the tyranny of the positive.

The argument is built almost entirely as a close reading of Peter Handke's "Essay on Tiredness," and so this chapter is the load-bearing source for the vault's [[Byung-Chul Han and Peter Handke]] relationship. From Handke, Han draws his governing distinction between a destructive, isolating, speechless **I-tiredness** (the "solitary," "divisive" tiredness of the exhausted ego, the tiredness of *positive potency* that "makes one incapable of doing something") and a healing, world-trusting, eloquent **we-tiredness** or [[Fundamental Tiredness|fundamental tiredness]] (the tiredness of [[Negative Potency|negative potency]], "namely of not-to"). This deep tiredness loosens the strictures of the ego, opens a "between" of friendliness-as-indifference, restores the duality of seeing/being-seen and touching/being-touched, and founds a [[Community of Singularities|community of singularities]] that requires "neither belonging nor relation."

The chapter opens by extending the book's negative diagnosis — neuroenhancement, the *Leistungsmaschine*, the "psychic infarctions" caused by a "too-much of positivity" — before pivoting to its Handkean redemption. This makes it a dual-purpose source: it consolidates the book's signature thesis ([[The pathologies of the present are infarctions not infections]]) while supplying its single most original positive concept. Han threads the affirmative pole through the Sabbath as "the day of not-to," through Heidegger's *Gelassenheit* and the suspension of care (see [[Byung-Chul Han and Martin Heidegger]]), and through Handke's "Pentecostal company" ([[The Pentecostal Company]]), proposing that the society of the future might be reimagined not as the society of activeness but as a "society of tiredness."

## Key Concepts

- [[Doping Society]] — achievement society intensifying into a "society of activeness"; the body and the human "as a whole" reduced to a performance-machine [Leistungsmaschine] that must function without disturbance; neuroenhancement lets one "achieve without achieving."
- [[Fundamental Tiredness]] — the keystone affirmative concept: "we-tiredness," "tiredness that trusts in the world," a "singular capacity" that "inspires" rather than exhausts; tiredness of negative potency.
- [[Negative Potency]] — the power "of not-to"; the redemptive inversion of capacity-to. Inspiring tiredness is the tiredness of negative potency; the Sabbath, *Gelassenheit*, and the "use of the useless" are its figures.
- [[Community of Singularities]] — the affirmative social form opened by fundamental tiredness: a community connected through a "friendly *and*," requiring "neither belonging nor relation," prefigured in a Dutch still life.
- [[Positivity]] — restated here as the cause of tiredness and exhaustion: a "too-much of positivity" producing "psychic infarctions," not an immunological reaction to the negative Other.
- [[Negativity]] — the "poverty in negativity" of the achievement world; the absent dimension whose recovery (as not-to, as fundamental tiredness) is the chapter's redemptive wager.
- [[Achievement Society]] — extended here into the "society of activeness" [Aktivgesellschaft] that generates "excessive tiredness and exhaustion."
- [[Auto-Exploitation]] — the doping society as self-administered performance enhancement in the body itself.
- [[Acceleration]] — "the economy of efficiency and acceleration" that makes slow forms disappear; opposed to the long, slow gaze of the tired person.
- [[Vita Contemplativa]] — the inspired not-doing of fundamental tiredness as the recovery of the contemplative against absolutized activity.

## Key Arguments

- [[The society to come is a society of tiredness]] — the programmatic closing move: the "Pentecostal company" of inspired not-doing stands opposed to the society of activity and offers a synonym for the society of the future.
- [[The pathologies of the present are infarctions not infections]] — exhaustion and tiredness are caused by excess positivity, not by an immunological encounter with the negative Other; "performance enhancement leads to psychic infarctions."

## Important Definitions

- We-tiredness vs I-tiredness — defined contrastively: "I am not tired 'of you' . . . but rather I am tired 'with you.'" (carried in [[Fundamental Tiredness]] and the [[I-Tiredness vs We-Tiredness]] tension).
- Positive vs negative potency of tiredness — exhaustion is the tiredness of positive potency (the can that renders one incapable); inspiration is the tiredness of negative potency (the not-to) (carried in [[Negative Potency]] and [[Positive Potency vs Negative Potency]]).

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- [[The Pentecostal Company]] — Handke's image of the disciples "tired to a man," whose inspiration tells them "not so much what they should, as what they need not, be."
- The Dutch seventeenth-century floral still life — Handke's emblem of the "all in one," beetle, snail, bee, and butterfly coexisting "at the moment, my moment"; image of the community of singularities (plain text; no note minted this pass).
- The Sabbath / the Seventh Day — the "day of not-to," the interval [Zwischenzeit], the "use of the useless"; figure of negative potency (handled inside [[Negative Potency]]; plain text here).

## Major Tensions

- [[I-Tiredness vs We-Tiredness]] — the chapter's organizing opposition: solitary, divisive, mute, violent, world-destroying tiredness versus world-trusting, eloquent, community-founding fundamental tiredness.
- [[Positive Potency vs Negative Potency]] — exhaustion as failed positive potency versus inspiration as the not-to; cross-linked to [[Can vs Should]].

## Candidate Essay Uses

- A redemptive counter-figure to burnout for the vault's material/immaterial and embodiment threads: fundamental tiredness as the recovery of slow, embodied "long and slow forms" against the dematerializing "economy of efficiency and acceleration."
- The Dutch still life as a curatorial model of coexistence-without-relation — a "community of singularities" connected by a "friendly *and*" — read against Han's [[Hell of the Same]]; an essay on installation, the group show, or the still life as a politics of the *and*.
- "Negative potency / the power not-to" as a critique of the surveillance/optimization regime: the not-to as resistance to the imperative to perform, set beside the [[Digital Panopticon|digital panopticon]] and Dataist self-measurement.
- The Sabbath as "the day of not-to" and the "use of the useless": a frame for thinking exhibition time, slowness, and non-instrumental attention against the "in-order-to" of productivist culture.
- Tiredness as disarming — "resolution yields to a state of calm" — as a quietly anti-decisionist aesthetics of the gaze, useful for an essay on attention, looking, and the suspension of the will to grasp.
