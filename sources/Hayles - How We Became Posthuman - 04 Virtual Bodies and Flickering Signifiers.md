# Source Summary: Virtual Bodies and Flickering Signifiers

## Orientation

Chapter 2 is where Hayles theorizes the linguistic and semiotic consequences of the move named in the Prologue: the abstraction of [[Information]] from its material substrate. Her governing claim is that information technologies do not merely change how texts are produced and stored — they "fundamentally alter the relation of signified to signifier." Carrying Lacan's print-bound floating signifiers "one step further," she coins the chapter's title figure, the [[Flickering Signifier|flickering signifier]]: a signifier that no longer floats above an absent signified but exists as "a flexible chain of markers bound together by the arbitrary relations specified by the relevant codes," prone to "unexpected metamorphoses, attenuations, and dispersions." The deeper structural argument is that a new dialectic — pattern/randomness — is displacing the Western tradition's presence/absence, and that this epistemic shift toward dematerialization registers simultaneously "as a change in the body (the material substrate) and as a change in the message (the codes of representation)." Hayles names the whole network of relations binding bodies, texts, and technologies [[Informatics]], crediting [[Donna Haraway]].

The chapter's rhetorical posture braids two registers Hayles insists are "massively parallel and highly interdigitated." On the theoretical side she stages a precise structural analogy: just as castration is the psycholinguistic catastrophe of the presence/absence dialectic, [[Mutation]] is the catastrophe of pattern/randomness — "Mutation is the catastrophe in the pattern/randomness dialectic analogous to castration in the presence/absence dialectic." This is the chapter's signature move, glossed through Cronenberg's *The Fly*: the operative transition is "not from male to female-as-castrated-male but from human to something radically other than human," so that "flickering signification brings together language with a psychodynamics based on the symbolic moment when the human confronts the posthuman." She develops a precise definition of information that holds pattern *and* randomness together (after Shannon and Weaver), and tracks the social symptom of the shift from possession to access — "the constraining factor separating the haves from the have-nots is not so much possession as access."

On the literary side, the chapter reads a sequence of "information narratives" — texts "in which the displacement is most apparent." [[William Gibson]]'s *Neuromancer* trilogy supplies cyberspace, the [[Data Made Flesh|"data made flesh"]] formula, and the pov-as-disembodied-marker; [[Don DeLillo]]'s *White Noise* shows private space penetrated by informational patterns and a subjectivity made of "the digitized randomness of miscellaneous bits"; [[Italo Calvino]]'s *If on a winter's night a traveler* dramatizes "print interruptus," a print book's terror of being digitized and "reduced to a body of meaningless data"; and [[William Burroughs]]'s *Naked Lunch* yields the junkie's body as "harbinger of the postmodern mutant." Crucially, even as Hayles narrates this dematerialization she refuses to take the "illusion of erasure" as given: "information in fact derives its efficacy from the material infrastructures it appears to obscure." The chapter therefore holds, in tension, both the seduction of [[The Body as Data|the body as information]] and the corrective insistence on [[Embodiment]]. ## Key Concepts

- [[Flickering Signifier|Flickering Signifiers]] — signifiers as flexible chains of coded markers prone to "unexpected metamorphoses, attenuations, and dispersions"; Lacan's floating signifier carried "one step further" by information technologies.
- [[Informatics]] — Hayles' term (after [[Donna Haraway]]) for "the technologies of information as well as the biological, social, linguistic, and cultural changes that initiate, accompany, and complicate their development."
- [[Information]] — defined here as depending on both pattern and randomness, predictability and unpredictability; "information in the technical sense has nothing to do with meaning."
- [[Pattern and Randomness]] — the dialectic in which pattern and randomness are "complements or supplements," not opposites; the emergent ground of informatic signification.
- [[Mutation]] — "the bifurcation point at which the interplay between pattern and randomness causes the system to evolve in a new direction"; the pattern/randomness catastrophe paralleling castration.
- [[The Body as Data]] — bodies "constituted as information" that can be "sold" and "fundamentally reconstituted in response to market pressures."
- [[Embodiment]] — the "resistant materiality" that books and humans "have something to lose" if read solely as informational patterns; "Embodiment can be destroyed, but it cannot be replicated."
- [[Posthumanism]] — the construction that "appears when computation rather than possessive individualism is taken as the ground of being."
- [[Feedback Loops]] — production, signification, consumption, bodily experience, and representation in "constant feedback and feedforward loops with the others"; the VR user spliced into "a direct feedback loop with a computer."
- [[The Cyborg]] — the narrator reconstrued "not so much as a scribe as a cyborg authorized to access the relevant codes"; the decoder whose "physicality is also data made flesh."

## Key Arguments

- [[Information technologies create flickering signifiers]] — by carrying Lacanian floating signifiers further, coding chains make the signifier "a flexible chain of markers" prone to metamorphosis and dispersion. (Directly stated)
- [[Pattern and randomness are displacing presence and absence]] — an epistemic shift toward dematerialization, registering simultaneously in the body (substrate) and the message (codes). (Directly stated)
- [[Mutation is the pattern/randomness catastrophe analogous to castration]] — it marks "a rupture of pattern so extreme that the expectation of continuous replication can no longer be sustained," staging the human confronting the posthuman. (Directly stated)
- [[Access displaces possession]] — because information "is not a conserved quantity," the dividing line shifts from owning a presence to accessing a pattern. (Directly stated)
- [[Dematerialization does not erase the material world]] — "information in fact derives its efficacy from the material infrastructures it appears to obscure"; the "illusion of erasure should be the subject of inquiry, not a presupposition." (Directly stated)
- [[Embodiment cannot be replicated]] — information and humanity alike "cannot exist apart from the embodiment that brings it into being"; once embodiment's specific form is gone, "no amount of massaging data will bring it back." (Directly stated)

## Important Definitions

- [[Flickering Signifier|Flickering Signifiers]] (Hayles' coinage) — "information technologies create what I will call flickering signifiers, characterized by their tendency toward unexpected metamorphoses, attenuations, and dispersions."
- [[Informatics]] (Hayles, after Haraway) — "the technologies of information as well as the biological, social, linguistic, and cultural changes that initiate, accompany, and complicate their development."
- Information narratives (Hayles) — "those texts in which the displacement is most apparent"; they "show, in exaggerated form, changes that are more subtly present in other texts as well."

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- [[Data Made Flesh]] — Gibson's "data made flesh in the mazes of the black market"; information as origin, physicality as derivative manifestation.
- The text-as-flickering-image — the screen text "as an image drawn in a medium as fluid and changeable as water," against the typewriter's "spatially discrete, durably inscribed, and flat" mark.
- Print interruptus — Calvino's print book's "fear that once it has been digitized, the computer will garble its body."
- Plate tectonics of language — flickering signifiers signal "an important shift in the plate tectonics of language."
- The body as "meat" — Gibson's Case regarding his body "as so much 'meat' that exists primarily to sustain his consciousness."

## Major Tensions

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — "materiality and immateriality are joined in a complex tension that is a source of exultation and strong anxiety."
- [[Embodiment vs Information]] — the "dream or nightmare of the body as information" against the "material interfaces and technologies that make disembodiment such a powerful illusion."
- Pattern vs presence — "pattern and presence are opposites existing in antagonistic relation," which Hayles reframes as potentially "mutually enhancing and supportive."
- Access vs possession — credentialing patterns versus physical exclusion/inclusion as the structuring element of subjectivity and law.
