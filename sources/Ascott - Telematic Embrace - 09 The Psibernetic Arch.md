# Source Summary: 09 The Psibernetic Arch

## Orientation

"The Psibernetic Arch" is a short, programmatic Ascott essay first published — untitled — as a contribution to *Studio International*, April 1970 (here dated 1970, source pages 164–167). It is futurology written by an artist: Ascott opens by distinguishing the "surprise-free" predictions that economists, sociologists, and astrologers manufacture for clients from the artist's freer practice, for whom "the generating of futuribles is essentially a far less constrained affair and may become a creative act." Rather than forecast specific futures, he proposes "a general model of the factors that will operate on culture over the next twenty-five years" — and that model is an arch. (Confidence: Directly stated.)

The governing figure is a **rainbow arch bridging two apparently opposed spheres: cybernetics and parapsychology** — "technology and telepathy; provision and prevision; cyb and psi." Across this arch Ascott projects a coming "**psibernetic** era / culture," a general-systems fusion of cybernetic prediction-control-communication with extrasensory perception (telepathy, clairvoyance, psychokinesis), which he calls "the most perfect form of communication and social feedback imaginable, although its manifestation is erratic." He grounds the prediction in 1960s science-and-state context — the Soviet reclassification of cybernetics as the science of government and its planned "Unified Information System" (1970–75), L. L. Vasiliev's psi research at the Leningrad Laboratory of Biological Cybernetics, J. B. Rhine at Duke, Helmut Schmidt's random-event-generator ESP work at Boeing, and Herman Kahn's scenario-sketching at the Hudson Institute. (Confidence: Directly stated for the figure and the cited context; the psibernetic culture is, in Ascott's own words, "a prediction.")

Within Ascott's arc the chapter sits at the late-1960s cybernetic-art pole, extending his thesis of "a general behavioural quality in twentieth-century art that admits of cybernetic analysis" and cross-referencing his [[Cybernetic Art Matrix]] (CAM, developed in chapter 3) — while prefiguring the network/consciousness preoccupations of his later telematic and technoetic work ("instant global sympathy, a galactic sympathy perhaps"). The 1970 coinage "psibernetic" is the conceptual ancestor of Ascott's later [[Technoetic Aesthetics|technoetics]]: here cybernetics is wedded not yet to telecommunications networks but to *psychic* communication. The essay closes personally and practically, with Ascott describing painting "at what amounts to the level of trance" against "a deliberate grid," ouija boards and a wired planchette feeding a domed light-and-sound environment ("a kind of system with a psychic brain"), and the speculative prospect of "linking a sensitive clairvoyant to a computer." (Confidence: Directly stated for the self-description; the placement in Ascott's lineage is Interpretive synthesis.)

## Key Concepts

- [[Psibernetics]] — Ascott's titular coinage fusing *psi* (parapsychology) and *cyb*(ernetics): the coming culture spanning the arch between technology and telepathy, in which art expresses "process and system underlying technological culture" allied with "behaviour at the psychic level."
- [[Futuribles]] — the artist's freer generation of alternative futures as a creative act; "the art of futuribles," "an art of predictive or anticipatory structure, which creates alternative futures."

- [[Cybernetic Art Matrix]] — the CAM blueprint (chapter 3) invoked as the social macrocosm to the studio microcosm, in which "subsystems tend to become automatic … and a psychic consciousness of the human group within it evolves."
- [[Trance and Automatism]] — Ascott's working method: random behaviour against a grid, automatic writing, ouija boards and the wired planchette, "pictomancy," "a kind of system with a psychic brain."
- [[Network Consciousness]] — forward bridge: ESP as "instant global sympathy, a galactic sympathy" and the evolving "psychic consciousness of the human group" anticipate Ascott's later networked-mind theme.
- [[New Media Art]] — extended: Ascott's anticipatory, behavioural, psibernetic art as a primary anchor of the realized committed stub.

## Key Arguments

- Generating alternative futures (futuribles) is, for the artist, a creative act rather than surprise-free prediction — captured by [[Futuribles]]. (Directly stated.)
- A coming psibernetic culture will arch cybernetics and parapsychology, and art has already "anticipated anticipation" — captured by [[Psibernetics]] and [[The Psibernetic Arch]]. (Strongly implied / framed by Ascott as "a prediction.")
- The new art need not employ the media of either pole — "Neither advanced hardware nor elaborate ectoplasm need be involved!" — but expresses the process and system underlying technological culture — captured by [[The Cybernetic Vision]] and [[Psibernetics]]. (Directly stated.)
- A cybernated economy of abundance frees humans "to choose and anticipate," and art has anticipated this leisure-to-anticipate — folded into [[Psibernetics]] / [[Futuribles]]. (Directly stated as prediction; utopian/1970-optimistic — Speculative connection on the abundance premise.)
- ESP, being medialess, is the limit-case of perfect feedback and even "the only real hope of travel around the galaxy" — folded into [[Psibernetics]]. (Speculative connection — Ascott's visionary register.)
