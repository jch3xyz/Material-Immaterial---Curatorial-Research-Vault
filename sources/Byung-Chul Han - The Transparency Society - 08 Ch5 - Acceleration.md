# Source Summary: Chapter 5 — The Society of Acceleration

**Book:** [[The Transparency Society]]
**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Raw file:** [[raw/Byung-Chul Han - The Transparency Society/08 Ch5 - Acceleration]]

## Orientation

Chapter 5 reframes the familiar diagnosis of contemporary "acceleration" by arguing that speed is a symptom, not the disease. Drawing on [[Jean-Paul Sartre|Sartre]]'s theory of obscenity (the body without reference, without direction, reduced to facticity), Han transposes obscenity onto the social body: hyperactivity, hyperproduction, and hypercommunication accelerate "beyond purpose," and this purposeless surplus, not velocity as such, is what is obscene. The chapter's pivotal move is the etymological pairing of [[Procession vs Processor]] — both derive from *procedere*, "to step forward," but the procession is narrative and the processor merely additive. Only addition admits acceleration; narration has its own tact and rhythm and cannot be sped up.

From this hinge, Han develops a series of contrasts that all turn on the absence of narrativity: [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)|Erfahrung vs Erlebnis]] (transformative experience vs. self-identical experiencing), [[Memory vs Storage]] (historicized rearrangement vs. additive accumulation), and [[Pilgrim vs Tourist]] (the way as significance-laden transition vs. the here-and-now of empty passage). Thresholds, transitions, and obstacles — the [[Negativity]] that constitutes "the topology of passion" — are precisely what the [[Compulsion for Transparency]] dismantles when it smooths and levels space. Without thresholds, meaning cannot arise; without narration, an ending becomes "absolute loss" rather than completion.

The chapter closes with a Proustian image: beauty is "a late bloomer," a [[Phosphorescence of Time]] that emerges only as reminiscence, in temporal layers and deposits. "Transparency does not phosphoresce." Han's final diagnosis — and the chapter's rhetorical pivot — is that the crisis is not acceleration but the "scattering and dissociation of temporality" into atomized presences. Because the problem is structural (loss of narrative gravity), deceleration is no cure: slowing down atoms still leaves them atoms.

## Key Concepts

- [[Hyperacceleration]] — movement that "accelerates just for its own sake," stripped of sense and goal.
- [[Narrativity]] — the binding tension of selection, direction, and tact; the missing ingredient in a transparent society.
- [[Additivity]] — the purely cumulative logic of the processor, of storage, of information.
- [[Negativity]] — obstacle, transition, transformation; what thinking, experience, and meaning require.
- [[Phosphorescence of Time]] — Proustian afterglow; beauty as belated reminiscence, not immediate stimulation.
- [[Compulsion for Transparency]] — the drive that dismantles thresholds, smooths space, annihilates fragrance.
- [[Fragrance of Time]] — the aromatic, atmospheric quality of layered, narrated temporality; opposed to transparent immediacy.
- [[Temporal Dis-synchrony]] — time buzzing without direction, disintegrating into atomized presences.

## Key Arguments

- [[The crisis is not acceleration but loss of narrativity]] — speed is symptom; the real disease is temporal scattering and the collapse of narrative gravity.
- Only additive processes admit acceleration — rituals, ceremonies, and narrations have their own tact and cannot be sped up; processors can.
- [[Transparency is a neoliberal dispositive that dismantles negativity]] — by smoothing and leveling space, the transparency regime evacuates the negativity from which meaning arises.
- [[The crisis is not acceleration but loss of narrativity]] — because the problem is structural rather than tempo-based, slowing down produces "no tact, no rhythm, no fragrance."
- Memory differs from storage by historicity — memory traces are constantly rearranged; stored data is self-identical and ahistorical.

## Important Definitions

- Obscenity (Sartre, via Han) — a body, or social body, "reduced to the mere facticity of the flesh," without reference, direction, or situation.
- [[Procession vs Processor]] — a narrative event with strong direction, scenographically marked, harnessed by narration.
- [[Procession vs Processor]] — the purely additive *procedere* that "tells nothing" and "simply counts."
- [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]] — transformative experience whose negativity holds "consequences that exude transformative power."
- [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)]] — experiencing that "leaves what exists as it stands."
- Pilgrimage — a narrative itinerary "rich in significance," charged with atonement, healing, thanksgiving.

## Important Metaphors / Symbols

- Junkshop of Memory — the positivized memory as a storage unit "stuffed full of 'images of all kinds and origins, used and worn-out symbols piled up any-old-how.'"
- Phosphorescence of Beauty — beauty as quiet afterglow, late bloomer, layered deposit that glows belatedly.
- Atoms and Molecules of Time — atomized presences require "a figurative attraction, a narrative gravity" to unite into "fragrant molecules."
- The Pilgrim — figure of narrative temporality, on the way to a "there" of well-being or salvation.
- The Tourist — figure of denarrativized presentism, stuck in the here-and-now, traversing empty passage.
- The Threshold — zone of mystery, transformation, fear, yearning — the "topology of passion."

- A curatorial essay on **exhibition pacing and the threshold** — using Han's procession/processor and pilgrim/tourist pairs to argue that an exhibition's meaning lives in its transitions and obstacles, not in maximally legible flow. Smooth, transparent galleries risk producing only [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)|Erlebnis]], not [[Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)|Erfahrung]].
