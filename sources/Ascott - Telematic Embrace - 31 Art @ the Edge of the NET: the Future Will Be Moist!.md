## Orientation

This is the keystone "moistmedia" essay of *Telematic Embrace* — the primary anchor for the entire late-period (1999–2000) Ascott vocabulary of MOIST that earlier-chapter analyses repeatedly deferred to "the technoetics chapters." It is an Ascott primary text (revised and expanded from the 1999 São Paulo keynote "Wired, Wet, Moist: Art @ the Edge of the Net" and the 2000 Vienna "Moist Manifesto"), not Shanken's editorial apparatus; only notes 4 and 5, signed "—Ed.," are the editor's. The essay opens with the verse manifesto itself — a litany of definitions ("MOIST SPACE is where dry pixels and wet molecules converge"; "MOIST MEDIA comprises bits, atoms, neurons, and genes"; "MOIST MIND is technoetic multiconsciousness") — and then unfolds that litany into a programmatic prose vision of twenty-first-century art.

Ascott's central move is a historical analogy pushed one stage further. Just as twentieth-century interactive media "replaced the cult of the objet d'art with a process-based culture," so the new century brings a further shift "as silicon and pixels merge with molecules and matter." Between the **dry** world of virtuality and the **wet** world of biology lies a third, **moist** domain — "a new interspace of potentiality and promise." [[Moistmedia]] ("comprising bits, atoms, neurons, and genes") becomes the substrate of a "transformative art concerned with the construction of a fluid reality." The convergence is digital / biological / spiritual at once ("MOIST ART is digitally dry, biologically wet, and spiritually numinous") and it is **technoetic** — techne and gnōsis fused into "a connective mind that is spawning new realities and new definitions of life and human identity." This is Ascott's mature restatement of Burnham's object→system thesis, now driven into the biological and spiritual register: appearance gives way to behaviour, structure to system, and "Engineering will embrace ontology."

Rhetorically the essay is a visionary-but-grounded manifesto. Ascott names the methodological category that organizes it — [[Visionary Pragmatism]], "flying with your feet on the ground… working with the future in the present" — and frames the whole text as "a textual portal to the future," closing with "remarks [that] will be inclusive rather than conclusive." Its posture is emphatically optimistic, "corner[ing] the nihilism and despair of late postmodernism" with a "bye-bye Baudrillard" and announcing the move "from the industrial age of paranoia into the [[Telenoia]] of universal connectivity and transparency." Across its sections it co-anchors the [[Cyberbotany]] / ayahuasca strand, the [[The Planetary Collegium]] as "a paradigmatic embodiment of the nascent moist culture," and [[The Building of Sentience]] as the architectural goal of the moist century.
