# A Cyborg Manifesto

**Author:** [[Donna Haraway]]
**Raw folder:** `raw/Haraway - A Cyborg Manifesto/`
**Full title:** *A Cyborg Manifesto: Science, Technology, and Socialist-Feminism in the Late Twentieth Century* (1985; cited here from *Manifestly Haraway*, University of Minnesota Press, 2016)

## Brief Orientation

*A Cyborg Manifesto* is Donna Haraway's 1985 socialist-feminist essay built as "an ironic political myth faithful to feminism, socialism, and materialism." Its governing figure is [[The Cyborg]] — "A cyborg is a cybernetic organism, a hybrid of machine and organism, a creature of social reality as well as a creature of fiction" (*Directly stated*, ch.01) — installed not as metaphor but as condition: "we are all chimeras, theorized and fabricated hybrids of machine and organism—in short, cyborgs. The cyborg is our ontology; it gives us our politics." The cyborg is "a condensed image of both imagination and material reality," which makes the essay a primary feminist articulation of the vault's [[Material vs Immaterial]] tension (machines that are "nothing but signals," "made of sunshine"; people who are "both material and opaque"; "Cyborgs are ether, quintessence").

The argument moves in two registers at once: diagnosis and possibility. Haraway names the historical condition the cyborg inhabits — [[The Informatics of Domination]], her coinage for the shift "from the comfortable old hierarchical dominations to the scary new networks," in which both communications sciences and modern biologies enact a common move, [[The World as a Problem of Coding]], condensed in the military emblem [[C3I]]. She grounds that abstraction in concrete labor and bodies through [[The Homework Economy]] and [[Women in the Integrated Circuit]], where the new networks land on actual women's days as feminization, surveillance, and "citizenship by telematics." This is the vault's earliest (1985) statement of the surveillance and dataist condition that [[Byung-Chul Han]] and [[Yuval Noah Harari]] later diagnose.

The essay is equally a critique of identity politics. Against essentialist feminism it sets [[Fractured Identities]] and [[Affinity Not Identity]] — "another response through coalition—affinity, not identity" — with [[Women of Color as a Cyborg Identity]] (via Chela Sandoval and Katie King) as the exemplary coalitional subject. Running through all of it is [[The Refusal of Innocence]]: the cyborg "would not recognize the Garden of Eden," has "no origin story in the Western sense," and refuses organic wholeness, victimhood, and return to nature. The closing section turns this into a poetics — [[Cyborg Writing]], "the power to survive... on the basis of seizing the tools to mark the world that marked them as other" — and ends on the manifesto's most-quoted line, the symbol [[I Would Rather Be a Cyborg Than a Goddess]] and the figure [[Regeneration vs Rebirth]].

For this vault, the manifesto is the keystone source that several notes had grounded only secondhand in [[N. Katherine Hayles]] — who borrowed the term "informatics" and the cyborg figure directly from Haraway. Ingesting it realizes Donna Haraway from forward-stub to primary author and re-anchors [[The Cyborg]], [[Informatics]], and the [[N. Katherine Hayles and Donna Haraway]] reference in their originating text. It also stages an early, parallel demotion of Foucault ([[Donna Haraway and Michel Foucault]]) that rhymes with Han's later "Foucault failed to make the turn to psychopolitics."

## Major Concepts

- [[The Cyborg]] — the cybernetic organism as ironic, blasphemous, postgender political myth (Haraway is the originating source; the existing note gains a primary Haraway author-section)
- [[The Informatics of Domination]] — the historic shift from organic hierarchical dualisms to coded command-control-communication networks (Haraway's coinage; the source of Hayles's "informatics")
- [[The World as a Problem of Coding]] — the common move by which communications sciences and modern biologies dissolve all heterogeneity into instrumental, translatable code
- [[The Breached Boundaries]] — human/animal, organism/machine, physical/nonphysical: the boundary breakdowns that make cyborg analysis possible
- [[The Homework Economy]] — the worldwide restructuring of work as "literally female and feminized" (term from Richard Gordon, enlarged by Haraway)
- [[Women in the Integrated Circuit]] — women's dispersed, networked, surveilled positions in computerized multinational capitalism; "no 'place'... only geometries of difference and contradiction"
- [[Fractured Identities]] — gender, race, and class as contradictory, partial, historically constituted, incapable of grounding essential unity
- [[Affinity Not Identity]] — political coalition built through chosen affinity and kinship rather than natural identification
- [[Oppositional Consciousness]] — Chela Sandoval's model (via Haraway) of political identity formed by those refused stable category-membership
- [[Women of Color as a Cyborg Identity]] — "a potent subjectivity synthesized from fusions of 'outsider' identities"; the exemplary affinity-identity
- [[The Refusal of Innocence]] — the rejection of origin myths, the Garden, original unity, organic wholeness, and victimhood-as-the-only-ground-for-insight
- [[Cyborg Writing]] — seizing the tools of signification that marked you as other; "the struggle against perfect communication"

## Major Arguments

- [[The cyborg is our ontology and it gives us our politics]] — "we are all chimeras, theorized and fabricated hybrids of machine and organism"; the cyborg is not optional metaphor but our condition, and a politics follows from it.
- [[Take pleasure in the confusion of boundaries and responsibility for their construction]] — the manifesto's double imperative: pleasure in the confusion of boundaries, responsibility in their construction.
- [[The cyborg has no origin story in the Western sense]] — it skips the myth of original unity, "would not recognize the Garden of Eden," and is "completely without innocence" (grounds the refused-origin-myths and Frankenstein/Eden material).
- [[There is no natural basis for the unity of women]] — gender, race, and class are fractured and contradictory; unity is "an achievement, not a natural fact" (carries the affinity-not-identity claim and the MacKinnon / totalizing-feminism critique).
- [[The world is being translated into a problem of coding]] — communications sciences and biologies recode all heterogeneity into instrumental, exchangeable information; the move that constitutes the informatics of domination.
- [[The homework economy is made possible by not caused by the new technologies]] — her careful refusal of technological determinism.
- [[The machine is us an aspect of our embodiment]] — "the machine is us, our processes, an aspect of our embodiment"; we are responsible for boundaries, and we are they.

## Important Definitions

- [[Cyborg (Haraway)]] — "A cyborg is a cybernetic organism, a hybrid of machine and organism, a creature of social reality as well as a creature of fiction" (ch.01)
- [[The Informatics of Domination (Haraway)]] — the shift from "the comfortable old hierarchical dominations" of white capitalist patriarchy to coded, networked command-control-communication (ch.03)
- [[The Homework Economy (Haraway after Gordon)]] — work restructured to "the characteristics formerly ascribed to female jobs," integrating factory, home, and market "on a new scale" (term from Gordon; ch.04)

## Key Metaphors

- [[Machines Made of Sunshine]] — microelectronic machines as immaterial light, "nothing but signals," against people who are "both material and opaque" (carries the floating-signifiers and "irreverent upstart god" images)
- [[The Salamander (Regeneration)]] — the regrown limb, "monstrous, duplicated, potent," as the figure of cyborg reconstitution opposed to flawless reproductive rebirth

## Key Symbols

- [[I Would Rather Be a Cyborg Than a Goddess]] — the manifesto's signature closing slogan: the ironic, blasphemous, partial cyborg chosen over the organicist Mother / Goddess of holistic, feminist-pagan politics
- [[Blasphemy]] — the stance of the manifesto, "faithful as blasphemy is faithful"; "Blasphemy is not apostasy"
- [[C3I]] — command-control-communication-intelligence; the militarized emblem of the coded-control regime (the refused origin myths — Frankenstein's monster and the Garden of Eden — are treated under [[The Refusal of Innocence]])

## Major Tensions

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — the cyborg as "a condensed image of both imagination and material reality"; bodies dissolving into networked, telematic, surveilled flows (Haraway joins this multi-author tension)
- [[Domination vs Possibility]] — the signature "double vision": a cyborg world as both "the final imposition of a grid of control" and the ground of unfeared kinship (carries the identity-vs-affinity and totality-vs-partiality oppositions, captured in [[Affinity Not Identity]] and [[The Refusal of Innocence]])
- [[Cyborg vs Goddess]] — technological/ironic/partial/blasphemous vs organic/holistic/reproductive/pagan
- [[Regeneration vs Rebirth]] — reconstitution after injury (monstrous, partial) vs the reproductive matrix's demand for flawless rebirth
