# Psychopolitics

**Author:** [[Byung-Chul Han]]
**Full title:** *Psychopolitics: Neoliberalism and New Technologies of Power*
**Translator:** Erik Butler
**Raw folder:** `raw/Han - Psychopolitics/`

## Brief Orientation

*Psychopolitics* is Han's diagnosis of the technology of power proper to neoliberalism. Its thesis is that power no longer works against freedom but *through* it: "Neoliberalism represents a highly efficient, indeed an intelligent, system for exploiting freedom" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]). Where the older [[Biopolitics|biopolitical]] regime disciplined the body, the neoliberal regime targets the psyche — it motivates, optimizes, and seduces rather than prohibiting. The book names this regime [[Psychopolitics]]: a [[Smart Power|smart, friendly power]] that "presents itself as freedom" and so makes the subjugated subject believe it is free.

The argument is dialectical, and continuous with [[The Transparency Society]]: freedom switches into coercion. The self-exploiting [[Achievement Society|achievement-subject]] "deems itself free" but "in reality it is a slave… an absolute slave," because it "willingly exploits itself without a master" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]). This [[Auto-Exploitation|auto-exploitation]] is more efficient than the older [[Allo-Exploitation|allo-exploitation]] precisely because it feels like self-realization. Han borrows [[Gilles Deleuze|Deleuze's]] periodization from the "Postscript on the Societies of Control" — the mole of the disciplinary enclosure giving way to the snake of open, modulating control ([[The Mole and the Snake]]) — and corrects [[Michel Foucault|Foucault]], whose [[Biopolitics]] (he argues) belongs strictly to disciplinary society and cannot describe a capitalism that has gone immaterial ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/05 Foucault's Dilemma]]).

The second half of the book follows this power into its concrete techniques: therapeutic [[Self-Optimization|self-optimization]] that turns [[Healing as Killing|healing into killing]]; the [[Friendly Big Brother|friendly]] [[Digital Panopticon|digital panopticon]] that inverts Orwell from negativity to positivity; [[Emotional Capitalism|emotional capitalism]] that exploits the [[Feeling vs Emotion vs Affect (Han)|performativity of emotion]]; the [[Gamification|gamification]] of work and communication; and above all [[Big Data]], the "highly efficient psychopolitical instrument" that opens a [[The Digital Unconscious|digital unconscious]] and, by making behaviour predictable, "has announced the end of the person who possesses free will" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]). [[Dataism]] is named as a "second Enlightenment" that repeats the dialectic of enlightenment "as a barbarism of data."

The book closes not with resistance-as-counter-power but with a praxis of freedom: against the totalizing communication of psychopolitics, Han proposes [[De-psychologization|de-psychologization]], the [[The Event|incalculable event]] that breaks the subject open, and a life [[Beyond the Subject|beyond the subject]] — figured finally as [[Idiotism|idiotism]], the heretical [[The Idiot|idiot]] who "veils himself in silence" and "communicates with the In-communicable" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/13 Idiotism]]). It is for readers who want a compact vocabulary for how contemporary power steers the psyche under the sign of freedom, and who already suspect that self-optimization, sharing, and the "capitalism of Like" are forms of domination.

## Major Concepts

- [[Psychopolitics]] — the book's title concept: neoliberal power that targets not the body but the psyche, steering free will rather than repressing it. "We are entering the age of digital psychopolitics. It means passing from passive surveillance to active steering" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]).
- [[Smart Power]] — the dominant, permissive technology of power: "Smart power with a liberal, friendly appearance — power that stimulates and seduces — is more compelling than power that imposes, threatens and decrees" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/02 Smart Power]]).
- [[Biopolitics]] — Foucault's body-and-population power, which Han historicizes and bounds: it belongs to disciplinary society and "is incapable of enabling subtle interventions in the psyche."
- [[Exploitation of Freedom]] — the signature move: power "does not negate or repress freedom so much as exploit it"; this book is its most explicit statement.
- [[Auto-Exploitation]] — the achievement-subject exploits itself "willingly — and even passionately," as "an entrepreneur of himself."
- [[Allo-Exploitation]] — the older, inefficient exploitation against the will; "allo-exploitation yields scant returns."
- [[Achievement Society]] — the self-optimizing achievement-subject who "deems itself free" but "is a slave… an absolute slave."
- [[Self-Optimization]] — therapeutic self-improvement that "amounts to total self-exploitation"; a "technology of subjectivation" with religious traits.
- [[Healing as Killing]] — the titular paradox of self-optimization as destruction of the soul.
- [[Emotional Capitalism]] — capitalism that exploits emotion as a means of production: "Emotional capitalism exploits precisely these qualities."
- [[Gamification]] — the enlistment of play and games to heighten productivity; "the gamification of work exploits homo ludens."
- [[Human Capital]] — the self capitalized; "human beings… are now transforming themselves into Capital."
- [[Immaterial Production]] — the post-industrial mode under which "the body is released from the immediate process of production," dissolving the proletariat. Cross-links the vault's [[Material vs Immaterial]] tension.
- [[Big Data]] — "a highly efficient psychopolitical instrument" that makes behaviour predictable and the future "calculable and controllable."
- [[Dataism]] — the new faith of the "second Enlightenment"; "Dataism is nihilism. It gives up on any and all meaning."
- [[The Digital Unconscious]] — the "micropsychical web of relations" Big Data makes accessible below the waking mind, opening the unconscious to psychopolitical exploitation.
- [[The Ban-opticon]] — the sorting apparatus by which Big Data divides humanity into a "digital class society."
- [[Pre-emptive Power]] — power that "anticipates and pre-empts action," intervening "in a prospective fashion," "even faster than free will."
- [[Society of Control]] — the Deleuzian neoliberal regime of open, modulating control superseding the disciplinary enclosure.
- [[Positivity]] — the affirmative mode of neoliberal power, which "beams forth in positivity"; the "violence of positivity."
- [[Negativity]] — the inhibitive mode of disciplinary power; also what "keeps life alive" and is "constitutive for experience (Erfahrung)."
- [[Smoothness]] — the frictionlessness of smart power's "subtle, supple and smart forms."
- [[Transparency]] — exteriorization that "transforms the negativity of freely made decisions into the positivity of factual states."
- [[Hypercommunication]] — total communication fused with control: "communication and control have become one."
- [[Hell of the Same]] — the "Inferno of the Same" produced by the immunosuppression of otherness.
- [[Acceleration]] — coupled here to the immunosuppression of negativity that total communication requires.
- [[De-psychologization]] — "the art of killing psychology"; the praxis of freedom that disarms psychopolitics.
- [[Beyond the Subject]] — the de-voided (*ent-leert*) subject opening "onto a mode of existence that still has no name: an unwritten future."
- [[The Event]] — the incalculable rupture that "breaks the subject open and wrests it from subjection."
- [[Idiotism]] — the heretical practice of freedom against the violence of consensus: "By nature, the idiot is unallied, un-networked, and uninformed."

## Major Arguments

- [[The freedom of Can is more coercive than the disciplinary Should]] — "The freedom of Can generates even more coercion than the disciplinarian Should… Can has none [no limit]."
- [[Neoliberalism exploits freedom because coercion is inefficient]] — "It is inefficient to exploit people against their will. Allo-exploitation yields scant returns."
- [[The auto-exploiting subject is an absolute slave]] — "In so far as it willingly exploits itself without a master, it is an absolute slave."
- [[The highest form of power is invisible not coercive]] — "Wherever power does not come into view at all, it exists without question. The greater power is, the more quietly it works."
- [[Neoliberal power seduces rather than represses]] — it operates "not by means of forbidding and depriving, but by pleasing and fulfilling."
- [[The shift to control society is an intensification of capitalism not a liberation]] — "a mutation, indeed an intensification, of capitalism, which remains one and the same."
- [[Power has shifted from the disciplinary body to the neoliberal soul]] — "the disciplinary regime organizes itself as a 'body'… The neoliberal regime, in contrast, seems like a 'soul'."

- [[Big Data reaches the psyche where statistics cannot]] — the census yields demographic but "not psychological" material; digital psychopolitics "manages to intervene in psychic processes."
- [[Self-optimization is total self-exploitation]] — "perpetual self-optimization… is proving destructive. It is leading to mental collapse."
- [[Healing means killing under neoliberal self-optimization]] — "Healing, it turns out, means killing."
- [[The violence of positivity is as destructive as the violence of negativity]] — neoliberal psychopolitics "is destroying the human soul, which is anything but a machine of positivity."
- [[Naomi Klein's shock model misreads neoliberal power]] — shock is "a disciplinary not a neoliberal technique."
- [[The digital panopticon runs on voluntary self-exposure|The digital panopticon inverts Orwell from negativity to positivity]] — friendly surveillance is more efficient than repressive surveillance.
- [[Communication and control have become one]] — the new control society fuses communication with control.
- [[Emotional capitalism exploits the performativity of emotion]] — "Emotional capitalism exploits precisely these qualities."
- [[Emotion becomes a means of production only under immaterial capitalism]] — the "emotion boom" derives from an economic process.
- [[Gamification subjugates the player through the act of playing]] — "The player subjugates him- or herself to the order of domination in the very act of playing."
- [[The realm of necessity colonizes the realm of freedom]] — under Capital "the realm of necessity comes to colonize the realm of freedom."
- [[Big Data ends the free-willed person by making the future predictable]] — "Big Data has announced the end of the person who possesses free will."

- [[The event is incalculable and breaks the subject open]] — the event "defies all calculation and prediction" and "wrests it [the subject] from subjection."
- [[Idiotism is a practice of freedom against psychopolitics]] — "In light of compulsive and coercive communication and conformism, idiotism represents a practice of freedom."
- [[Resistance is silence not expression]] — "the idiot veils himself in silence… Idiotism erects spaces for guarding silence."

## Important Definitions

- [[Psychopolitics (Han)]] — neoliberal power that exploits the psyche and "passes from passive surveillance to active steering."
- [[Smart Power (Han)]] — "Smart power with a liberal, friendly appearance — power that stimulates and seduces — is more compelling than power that imposes, threatens and decrees."
- [[Biopolitics]] — disciplinary power over the body and population in its productive capacity; "the biological, the somatic, the corporal."
- [[Dataism (Han)]] — the data-faith of the "second Enlightenment": "everything must become data and information."
- [[Feeling vs Emotion vs Affect (Han)]] — the taxonomy underwriting emotional capitalism: feeling is objective, narratable, durative; emotion is "performative," "intentional and goal-oriented"; affect is "eruptive" and momentary; *Stimmung* (mood) is objective and static.

## Key Metaphors

- [[The Mole and the Snake]] — the burrowing mole of disciplinary enclosure vs. the space-making snake of neoliberal control: "The snake is the animal of neoliberal control society… it makes space by means of its own movement."
- [[The Smartphone as Rosary]] — "As a subjectivation-apparatus, the smartphone works like a rosary… Like is the digital Amen" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]).
- [[Hell of the Same]] — the "Inferno of the Same"; the levelling produced when otherness is immunosuppressed.

## Major Tensions

- [[Freedom vs Control]] — the book's master tension: power "presents itself as freedom" and exploits it. "Freedom itself, which is supposed to be the opposite of constraint, is producing coercion."
- [[Positivity vs Negativity]] — disciplinary power "commanded by negativity" vs. the neoliberal regime that "beams forth in positivity."
- [[Biopolitics vs Psychopolitics]] — the deepest periodizing opposition: power over the "body" vs. power over the "soul" / psyche.
- [[Subject vs Project]] — the disciplinary "subjugated subject" vs. the neoliberal "project"; intensification, not liberation.
- [[Can vs Should]] — the unlimited compulsion of "Can" vs. the bounded prohibition of "Should."
- [[Rationality vs Emotionality]] — the disciplinary suppression of emotion vs. the neoliberal exploitation of it; "man is a creature of sentiment."
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — material production vs. "immaterial and non-physical forms of production"; the economic/labour inflection of the vault's cross-author tension.
- [[Narration vs Addition]] — narration vs. the additive, non-narrative data of dataism; "counting is not recounting."
- [[Immanence vs Transcendence]] — "The transcendence of Capital stands in the way of life as immanence."
- [[Intelligence vs Idiotism]] — system-immanent intelligence (which "is not freedom") vs. the idiot's access to the wholly Other.

## References to Other Authors

- [[Byung-Chul Han and Karl Marx]] — Marx is both ally and target: freedom as relation ("only in the community… is personal freedom possible," *The German Ideology*) and individual freedom as "a ruse — a trick of capital" (*Grundrisse*, *Capital* Vol. 1), but Han breaks with Marx on the *Aufhebung*, the proletariat, and revolution ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]], [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/10 Gamification]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Gilles Deleuze]] — the "Postscript on the Societies of Control" supplies the mole/snake and discipline-to-control periodization Han adopts and inflects toward debt and psychopolitics ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/03 The Mole and the Snake]], [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/13 Idiotism]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Michel Foucault]] — the book's central interlocutor: Han adopts Foucault's biopolitics and "technologies of the self," then marks their limit ("the turn to psychopolitics" Foucault "failed to make") ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/04 Biopolitics]], [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/05 Foucault's Dilemma]], [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/12 Beyond the Subject]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Walter Benjamin]] — extended here through "Capitalism as Religion" ("the first case of a cult that creates guilt, not atonement") ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Hegel]] — the master–slave dialectic invoked and inverted: "counter to what Hegel assumed, labouring does not make the slave free… the totalization of labour" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/01 The Crisis of Freedom]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Jeremy Bentham]] — the panopticon as the disciplinary foil to the digital panopticon; "Bentham's Big Brother" ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/04 Biopolitics]], [[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/11 Big Data]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and George Orwell]] — "Big Brother" borrowed and inverted into the "Friendly Big Brother" of the positivized digital panopticon ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/08 Friendly Big Brother]]).
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Naomi Klein]] — Han critiques Klein's shock doctrine as a misreading: shock is a disciplinary, negative technique, not the seductive form of neoliberal power ([[raw/Han - Psychopolitics/07 Shock]]).
