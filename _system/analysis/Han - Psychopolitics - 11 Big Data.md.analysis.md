## Executive Summary

This is the longest and most pivotal chapter of *Psychopolitics*: Han's full theory of **Big Data as digital psychopolitics**. He opens with Bentham's "Columbus and his egg" to ask whether Big Data is the panopticon's successor — and answers that it is far more than that. Where Bentham's panopticon was *perspectival* (it had blind spots where prisoners could harbour secret wishes), digital surveillance is **aperspectival**: it eliminates blind spots and "can peer into the human soul itself." This marks the shift from a power that watches behaviour to a power that steers the psyche.

Han then diagnoses **Dataism** (David Brooks's coinage, riding Chris Anderson's "The End of Theory") as a **"second Enlightenment"** and a new **faith / ideology**: the dogma that everything measurable must be measured, that data is a "transparent and reliable lens," that with enough data "the numbers speak for themselves" and theory becomes superfluous. Invoking Adorno and Horkheimer's *Dialectic of Enlightenment*, he argues the second Enlightenment repeats the first's fatal dialectic — illumination tipping into a new barbarism (the "barbarism of data"), data-transparency as "false clarity," "another name for myth." Dataism is "digital Dadaism" and "nihilism": it abandons meaning, because **data are additive, not narrative**; **counting is not recounting**.

The chapter proceeds through a sequence of sub-arguments. **Quantified Self** is a "Dadaist technology" that empties the self of meaning — "Self Knowledge through Numbers" yields no answer to "Who am I?" because a sense of self comes from *giving an account*, not from counting. Self-tracking has become **self-surveillance**: the auto-exploiting "entrepreneur of the self" "carries around its own labour camp," is "a panopticon of itself." **Life Logged in Full** (Web 3.0 / internet of things) brings "digital control society to completion," producing a "total memory of the Digital": unlike forgetful Big Brother, "Big Data never forgets." In US elections, **micro-targeting** is "the practical microphysics of power, micro-targeting is data-driven psychopolitics" — and digital psychopolitics can "intervene in psychic processes in a prospective fashion," possibly "faster than free will," which "would herald the end of freedom." **The Digital Unconscious** (analogizing Benjamin's "optical unconscious") proposes that Big Data can read desires we don't know we harbour — making the Freudian *id* (Es) "into an ego to be exploited psychopolitically." **Big Business** shows Big Data as Big Brother *and* Big Business merging surveillance state and market (Acxiom, the 360-degree customer view, the "ban-opticon" that sorts humans into "waste"). **Forgetting** contrasts living, narrative, rewritten human memory (Freud's retranscription) with undead, additive digital memory. **Spirit** is the Hegelian core: correlation is the most primitive level of the *Logic*; Big Data lacks causation, reciprocity, and above all the **Concept** — so "totalized data-knowledge amounts to absolute ignorance: the absolute zero of Spirit," and the "end of theory" means "taking leave of Spirit." **The Event** historicizes Dataism against the eighteenth-century euphoria for statistics (Arbuthnot, Kant, Rousseau's *volonté générale*), against which Romanticism and Nietzsche revolted: statistics expresses only the "herd," *Zunehmen im Gleichwerden* (growing equal / battening as one becomes the same), a *Gleichschaltung* of sameness. Big Data calculates averages and is therefore **blind to the singular, the improbable, the event — and so blind to the future itself.**

The chapter is the hinge where Han's transparency-society thesis becomes an explicit **psychopolitics**: surveillance that reaches the unconscious, monetizes the person, and forecloses freedom by predicting and pre-empting the will.

## Definitions

Terms explicitly defined or strongly clarified in the chapter:

- **Dataism** — the new "faith"/"philosophy" that "everything that can be measured should be measured; that data is a transparent and reliable lens… that data will help us do remarkable things - like foretell the future" (Brooks, quoted); Han adds: "dataism itself is an ideology… leading to digital totalitarianism" and "Dataism is nihilism."
- **The ban-opticon** — "a dispositive that identifies persons who stand outside the system or are hostile to it, and excludes them" (vs the panopticon, which disciplines incarcerated insiders); "ensures the system's security and efficiency."
- **Aperspectival (digital) surveillance** — surveillance that "does not suffer from the perspectival limitations characterizing analogue optical systems… eliminates all blind spots… can peer into the human soul itself."
- **The digital unconscious** — "a microphysical or micropsychical web of relations" disclosed by data-mining, "behind the framework of consciousness," made of "actomes" (micro-actions "that elude detection by the waking mind") and "collective patterns of behaviour."
- **Correlation vs causation (Hegelian)** — "A strong correlation between A and B means that if A changes, a change also occurs in B… Correlation represents a relation of probability, not of necessity… A causes B" (causation = necessity); above both stands reciprocity (Wechselwirkung) and the Concept.
- **The Concept (Begriff) / syllogism (Schluss)** — "The Concept is C, which comprehends within itself… A and B"; "Syllogism is not addition, but narration"; "'Everything is a syllogism (Schluss)' means 'Everything is a concept.'"
- **Quantified Self** — self-measurement via body sensors under the motto "Self Knowledge through Numbers," which Han redefines as "a Dadaist technology… it empties the self of any and all meaning."
- **Publicatio sui (Tertullian, via Foucault)** — antique self-display/self-examination that "meant committing to the search for truth," "an ethics of the self" — contrasted with ethics-less digital self-tracking.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Biopolitics vs Psychopolitics** — power over the body/population (census, demography) vs power over the psyche (micro-targeting, prediction). The book's master tension, made explicit here.
  - Source: > "Biopolitics is incapable of enabling subtle interventions in the psyche. In contrast, digital psychopolitics manages to intervene in psychic processes."
- **Perspectival vs Aperspectival surveillance** — Bentham's blind-spotted optics vs digital optics with no blind spots.
  - Source: > "Digital surveillance proves so efficient because it is aperspectival."
- **Counting vs Recounting / Addition vs Narration** — data enumerate; meaning, memory, selfhood, theory recount. The chapter's structural spine.
  - Source: > "Counting is not recounting."
- **Correlation vs Causation (vs the Concept)** — probability vs necessity vs comprehending Spirit.
  - Source: > "Correlation represents a relation of probability, not of necessity… That is the difference between correlation and causation."
- **Absolute knowledge vs Absolute ignorance** — Big Data's "omniscience" (All-Wissen) is, for Hegel, "absolute ignorance" (Un-Wissen).
  - Source: > "The absolute knowledge intimated by Big Data coincides with absolute ignorance."
- **The Average / Probable vs The Singular / Event** — statistics sees the herd and the likely; history is made by the unlikely and singular.
  - Source: > "Big Data has no access to what is unique. Big Data is wholly blind to the event."
- **Living memory vs Undead digital memory** — rewriting/forgetting vs seamless accumulation; animacy vs the undead.
  - Source: > "Memory constitutes a dynamic, living process… Digital memory consists of indifferent - as it were, undead - points of presence."
- **Self-knowledge vs Self-measurement** — "Who am I?" vs the data pile; account-giving vs data.
  - Source: > "no insight into the self can result from data and numbers alone."
- **Ethics/truth of the self (publicatio sui) vs ethics-less self-tracking** — antique care of the self vs digital self-surveillance.
  - Source: > "dataism's self-tracking is devoid of all ethics and truth; it amounts simply to a technology for self-monitoring."
- **Discipline vs Exclusion (panopticon vs ban-opticon)** — to discipline insiders vs to exile the valueless.
  - Source: > "The classical panopticon served to discipline. In contrast, the ban-opticon ensures the system's security and efficiency."
- **Freedom / free will vs Prediction-Control** — will vs a psychopolitics "faster than free will."
  - Source: > "it is even faster than free will… this would herald the end of freedom."
- **Sameness (Gleichwerden) vs the Other/Deviant** — statistical normality and "compulsion to conform" vs the singular Other, Alien, Deviant.
  - Source: > "Transparency entails a compulsion to conform, which eliminates the Other, the Alien and the Deviant."
