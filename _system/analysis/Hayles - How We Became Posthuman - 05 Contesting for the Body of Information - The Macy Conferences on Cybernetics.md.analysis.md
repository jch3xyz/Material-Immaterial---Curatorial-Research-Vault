## Executive Summary

This chapter is Hayles's historical core argument for the central thesis of the book: the claim that information became conceptualized as a *disembodied* entity — a pattern that can move "unchanged between different material substrates" — was not inevitable but the contingent outcome of situated debates within a specific U.S. techno-scientific community. Her case study is the Macy Conferences on Cybernetics (1946–1953), which she treats as a "crossroads for the traffic in cybernetic models and artifacts."

She argues the reification of information was contested on three fronts: (1) the *theoretical* construction of information (Shannon/Wiener's probability-function definition, divorced from meaning, versus Donald MacKay's rival meaning-and-context theory); (2) the construction of *human neural structure* as information flow (the McCulloch-Pitts neuron and the "Platonic backhand" that makes embodiment derivative of logical form); and (3) the construction of *artifacts* — Shannon's electronic rat, Ashby's homeostat, Wiener's Moth and Bedbug, Grey Walter's tortoise — that "made the flows 'real'" and constructed "the human as cyborg." At each front, reification won not for lack of opposition but because it was more *quantifiable*; the alternatives (meaning, embodiment, context) "ballooned into an unmanageable project."

A second, organizing thread is Hayles's three-wave periodization machinery: she shows how the *homeostasis constellation* (first wave) crystallized at Macy, how *reflexivity* (second wave) was repeatedly raised — by MacKay, by Lawrence Kubie's psychoanalysis, by Frank Fremont-Smith's insistence on the observer — and was "relegated to the category of 'nonscience'" because including the observer threatened reliable quantification and collapsed the speaker into the spoken. The chapter traces how Gregory Bateson and Heinz von Foerster later rehabilitated reflexivity in a form that did not depend on psychoanalysis (Bateson's "we are our epistemology"), and gestures forward to Humberto Maturana's *autopoiesis* (third wave, Ch. 6).

The chapter closes with the figure of Janet Freed/Freud, the conference transcriptionist, whom Hayles reads as the embodied, materially-laboring "outside observer" erased from the record — a concrete demonstration that "information is never disembodied" and that decontextualization is a privilege of those who "command the labors of others."

## Key Entities

People:
- **Norbert Wiener** — co-founder of cybernetics; here, with von Neumann, "made clear that the important entity in the man-machine equation was information, not energy"; source of the betting/bookie information-theory exposition. Forward-stub candidate.
- **Claude Shannon** — defined information as a dimensionless probability function divorced from meaning; "meticulously careful" about limiting its scope; builder of the maze-solving electronic rat. Forward-stub candidate.
- **John von Neumann** — led the "triumph of information over materiality" at the first conference; discussed heat dissipation from vacuum tubes.
- **Warren McCulloch** — chaired the conferences; neurophysiologist; co-creator of the McCulloch-Pitts neuron; sought an "empirical epistemology"; delivered the anti-Freudian "The Past of a Delusion"; central tragic/heroic figure of the chapter.
- **Walter Pitts** — McCulloch's collaborator; proved a neural net can compute anything a Turing machine can.
- **Donald MacKay** — British researcher; proposed an information theory including meaning, distinguishing *selective* (metrons) and *structural* (logons) information; tied to reflexivity.
- **Nicolas S. Tzannes** — information theorist who observed Shannon/Wiener define information by *what it is*, MacKay by *what it does*.

## Definitions

Terms explicitly defined or strongly clarified in the chapter:

- **Information (Shannon-Wiener)** — a choice of one message from a range of possible messages, quantified as the log (base 2) of the number of elements in the message set; formulated "entirely without reference to what information means."
  - Key passage: > "Note that the theory is formulated entirely without reference to what information means. Only the probabilities of message elements enter into the equations."

- **Selective information (MacKay)** — "information calculated by considering the selection of message elements from a set" (measured in "metrons"); the Shannon-Bavelas sense.
  - Key passage: > "He proposed that both Shannon and Bavelas were concerned with what he called 'selective information,' that is, information calculated by considering the selection of message elements from a set."

- **Structural information (MacKay)** — "a message about how to interpret a message—that is, it is a metacommunication" (measured in "logons"); calculated through changes in the receiver's mind, so semantics enters.
  - Key passage: > "Structural information indicates how selective information is to be understood; it is a message about how to interpret a message—that is, it is a metacommunication."

- **What information *is* vs. what it *does*** (Tzannes) — Shannon/Wiener define information by what it is (a thing); MacKay by what it does (an action implying context and embodiment).
  - Key passage: > "He pointed out that whereas Shannon and Wiener define information in terms of what it is, MacKay defines it in terms of what it does."

- **Signal vs. sign (McCulloch)** — a signal "always implies its occasion" (refers to a real external event); a sign is "an enduring affair which has lost its essential temporal reference" (e.g., a reverberating-loop firing, hallucination, phantom limb).
  - Key passage: > "A signal 'always implies its occasion,' but a sign is an 'enduring affair which has lost its essential temporal reference.'"

- **McCulloch-Pitts neuron** — a model neuron with excitatory/inhibitory inputs and a firing threshold, connectable into nets "capable of signifying logical propositions"; "a liminal object, part abstraction and part embodied actuality."
  - Key passage: > "McCulloch's central insight was that neurons connected in this way are capable of signifying logical propositions."

- **Homeostat (Ashby)** — "a device that sought to return to a steady state when disturbed," configurable to simulate organism-plus-environment.
  - Key passage: > "Ross Ashby's homeostat, a device that sought to return to a steady state when disturbed."

- **Reflexivity (as it operated at Macy)** — initially "a nebulous cluster that was not explicitly named as such"; the inclusion of the observer producing an infinite regress; later "we are our epistemology."
  - Key passage: > "during the Macy Conferences reflexivity was a nebulous cluster that was not explicitly named as such."

- **Autopoiesis (Maturana) — forward gloss** — the inner world "is a metaphor for nothing other than its own creation of itself as a system."
  - Key passage: > "He went into the inner world and insisted that it can't be turned inside out, that it is a metaphor for nothing other than its own creation of itself as a system. This is the theory of autopoiesis"

## Arguments

- **Claim: The disembodiment of information was contingent, not inevitable — the product of situated debates, not a law of nature.**
  - Reasoning structure: Historicize the "black box." Recover the contesting alternatives (MacKay, Kubie, Fremont-Smith) to show reification "was triumphant not because it had no opposition but because... it seem[ed] a better choice."
  - Evidence: The three fronts; MacKay's defeat; the role of quantifiability and McCarthy-era postwar "normalcy."
  - Source passage: > "Conceptualizing information as a disembodied entity was not an arbitrary decision, but neither was it inevitable."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The disembodiment of information was contingent not inevitable`

- **Claim: Divorcing information from meaning was a sound *local* engineering choice that became an *ideology* when decontextualized.**
  - Reasoning structure: Shannon/Wiener needed a context-invariant value, so they bracketed semantics; the "raining" example shows why. The error is extrapolation: "The theory decontextualizes information; Moravec decontextualizes the theory."
  - Evidence: Shannon's own caution to Bavelas; the windowless-office/street-corner example.
  - Source passage: > "Thus, a simplification necessitated by engineering considerations becomes an ideology in which a reified concept of information is treated as if it were fully commensurate with the complexities of human thought."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Information divorced from meaning is an engineering choice mistaken for an ideology`

- **Claim: Reflexivity lost at Macy because it was chained to subjectivity, which threatened quantification and thus the status of "science."**
  - Reasoning structure: Including the observer (MacKay's structural information; Kubie's unconscious; Fremont-Smith's observer) opens an infinite regress and "psychological complexity," making reliable quantification "elusive or impossible." In a McCarthy-era atmosphere, homeostasis "implied a return to normalcy."
  - Evidence: Fremont-Smith vs. Stroud exchange; Kubie's "last stand"; McCulloch's "The Past of a Delusion."
  - Source passage: > "The fear is that under these conditions, reliable quantification becomes elusive or impossible and science slips into subjectivity, which to many conferees meant that it was not real science at all."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Reflexivity was relegated to nonscience because it could not be quantified`

- **Claim: The Platonic backhand makes embodiment derivative of abstract form, masking a contingent abstraction as a preexisting law.**
  - Reasoning structure: A model abstracted *from* embodied mechanisms is rhetorically reversed so that the abstraction appears to *precede and govern* them; universality is bought by "black-boxing" specific mechanisms.
  - Evidence: McCulloch's Dec. 1947 letter to Teuber ("a law so general"); Nye's feminist history of logic; the 1:1 map (Carroll/Borges).
  - Source passage: > "Rhetorically, however, McCulloch presents the theory as though it preexisted specific mechanisms and then was later imperfectly instantiated in them. This backhanded swing invests the theory with a coercive power that cannot be ignored"
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The Platonic backhand makes embodiment derivative of logical form`

- **Claim: Cybernetic artifacts construct the human as machine even when the analogy fails — the comparison itself does the constructing.**
  - Reasoning structure: Whether humans and machines are judged alike or unlike, "ranging human intelligence alongside an intelligent machine puts the two into a relay system that constitutes the human as a special kind of information[-processor]." The chicken/tractor example.
  - Evidence: The electronic rat; the von Foerster/Mead/Teuber introduction admitting limitations yet still using the analog.
  - Source passage: > "If I say a chicken is not like a tractor, I have characterized the chicken in terms of the tractor, no less than when I assert that the two are alike"
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Comparing humans to machines constructs the human as machine even when the analogy fails`

- **Claim: What can be exactly specified can be mechanized; therefore the "uniquely human" residue collapses into the non-specifiable qualities of language (ambiguity, metaphor).**
  - Reasoning structure: Von Neumann/Pitts thesis ("as long as you definitely specify what you want the machine to do... you can build a machine to do it") implies the human remainder is exactly the linguistic excess that resists specification.
  - Evidence: Pitts at the ninth conference; McCulloch's *Embodiments of Mind*.
  - Source passage: > "the residue of the uniquely human becomes coextensive with the linguistic qualities that interfere with precise specification—ambiguity, metaphoric play, multiple encoding, and allusive exchanges between one symbol system and another."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The uniquely human collapses into what language cannot exactly specify`

- **Claim: Decontextualization/reification is a class- and gender-situated privilege — the material labor of embodiment is borne by those (women, staff) erased from the record.**
  - Reasoning structure: Via Dorothy Smith: men who command others' labor experience their words as self-acting because they do not perform the labor. Janet Freed embodies the suppressed materiality.
  - Evidence: Fremont-Smith's "You write and get a lovely smile"; the "his resources, her time" of transcription; Freed effacing herself in the record.
  - Source passage: > "men of a certain class are prone to decontextualization and reification because they are in a position to command the labors of others."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated / Interpretive synthesis where Hayles extends it to Freed)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Decontextualization is a privilege of those who command others' labor`

## Metaphors

- **Constellation** — concepts as networks ("constellations") in which the era's emphasis (homeostasis, then reflexivity) is the organizing star pattern.
  - > "Earlier I called these networks 'constellations,' suggesting that during the Macy period, the emphasis was on homeostasis."

- **The Platonic backhand / chess imagery** — McCulloch as a "knight" mounting a "two-pronged attack," catching Teuber's "queen"; the rhetorical "backhanded swing" / "backhand volley."
  - > "Now McCulloch—like a knight that, moved from the diagonal to attack the queen, exposes the queen to the bishop's attack as well—has caught Teuber in a two-pronged attack."

- **The 1:1 map (Carroll / Borges)** — a model as complex as reality defeats the purpose of modeling; the "king's mad cartographer."
  - > "he imagines a king's mad cartographer who is satisfied only when he creates a map that covers the entire kingdom, reflecting its every detail in a scale of 1:1."

- **Information "weightless as sunshine"** — the reified, dematerialized quantity.
  - > "Stripped of context, it becomes a mathematical quantity weightless as sunshine, moving in a rarefied realm of pure probability, not tied down to bodies or material instantiations."

- **The shattered safety-glass windshield** — what happens to controlled context once the observer is admitted.
  - > "cracks in the frame radiate outward until the perspectives that controlled context are fractured as irretrievably as a safety-glass windshield hit by a large rock."

- **Tar baby** — psychoanalytic reflexivity that clings the more one pushes it away.
  - > "turning what should be scientific debate into a tar baby that clung to them the more they tried to push it away."

- **Brothers / siblings under the skin** — the cybernetic equivalence of human and machine.
  - > "the momentous conclusion that humans and robots are siblings under the skin."

- **The suture / "setting in the flesh"** — binding the abstract model to the body; the McCulloch-Pitts neuron as cybernetics' "setting in the flesh."
  - > "A suture is needed to bind the flesh more tightly to the model."

- **McCulloch as Moses** — could lead others to the brink of the new (reflexive) epistemology but not enter it.
  - > "McCulloch represents a Moses-like figure who could lead others to the brink of this new epistemology but was unable to enter into it himself."

- **The kitchen table** — recurring scene of embodied, contextual reflection (Bateson/Mead/Brand 1976; Hayles 1996; Catherine Bateson) opposed to the disembodied conference transcript.
  - > "Like Bateson, Mead, and Brand sitting at a kitchen table on that March morning in 1976, I am sitting at my kitchen table in March 1996."

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Homeostasis vs. Reflexivity** — the two "rival constellations"; thing/action, stability/change, signal-noise/signifying-difference, instrumental-language/ambiguity, objectivity/subjectivity.
  - > "In the choice between what information is and what it does, we can see the rival constellations of homeostasis and reflexivity beginning to take shape."

- **Information vs. meaning (Shannon-Wiener vs. MacKay)** — context-invariant quantity vs. meaning-bearing, receiver-relative metacommunication.
  - > "subjectivity, far from being a morass to be avoided, is precisely what enables information and meaning to be connected."

- **Logical form vs. embodiment** — held in "dynamic tension" within McCulloch himself; abstraction "is not the actuality" (Teuber).
  - > "a commitment to embodiment exists in dynamic tension with an equally strong proclivity to see embodiment as the instantiation of abstract propositions."

- **Objective/scientific vs. subjective/nonscience** — "objective" coded as science, "subjective" as "a morass of unquantifiable feelings."
  - > "'objective' was associated with being scientific, whereas 'subjective' was a code word implying that one had fallen into a morass of unquantifiable feelings that might be magnificent but were certainly not science."

- **Universality vs. specificity** — universality achieved by erasing particularity; a gap reopens at "a level of specificity at which differences create a significant gap."
  - > "It depends on how the perspective is constructed."

- **Disembodied information vs. material instantiation** — the chapter's master tension, resolved rhetorically in Freed's body.
  - > "information is never disembodied, that messages don't flow by themselves"

- **Simplicity of model vs. complexity of phenomena** — the model's "very lack of complicating detail meant that the model stood for much more than it physically enacted" (J. Z. Young's warning).
  - > "the model's simplicity facilitated the overlay of assumptions onto the artifact"
