## Executive Summary

The final chapter of *The Agony of Eros* extends Han's central thesis — eros as the relation to the atopic Other — from love and politics into **thought itself**. Its argument: *theory in the emphatic sense is erotic*. Genuine thinking requires the negativity of the Other, the resistance of the incalculable, the seduction down "untrodden paths"; without eros, thinking degenerates into "mere work," pure repetition, the additive comparison of data. Han opens with Heidegger's letter to his wife naming Eros ("the oldest of the gods according to Parmenides") as what moves thinking onto "untrodden paths," and closes with Plato's Socrates as the *atopos* seducer and with Deleuze and Guattari's claim that the friend/lover is "a condition of possibility of thought itself."

The polemical target is **Chris Anderson's 2008** *Wired* essay, also titled "The End of Theory," which argued that massively abundant data makes theoretical models — even causality — superfluous ("With enough data, the numbers speak for themselves"). Han inverts the title: for him it is not theory that has ended but the emphatic *capacity for theory*, killed by the flood of positive information. He distinguishes **information (positive, additive, cumulative, cognized)** from **insight/recognition (negative, exclusive, transformative)**, and **Erlebnis (additive experiencing)** from **Erfahrung (transformative experience that occurs once)**. "Google science" — the balancing and comparison of data without theory, correlation over causality — is diagnosed as the end of theory of the emphatic sort, an entropy-raising deluge of noise in the society of transparency and information. This chapter is the vault's primary primary-source bridge between Han's *Agony of Eros* eros/Other vocabulary and his *Psychopolitics* Dataism/Big Data critique.

## Arguments

- **Theory in the emphatic sense is erotic; without eros, thinking degenerates into mere calculation that reproduces the Same.**
  - Reasoning structure: eros = seduction by the atopic Other = the negativity/resistance that drives thinking onto untrodden paths. Remove the Other (calculation, data) and thinking loses its negativity, becoming additive, repetitive "work on the positive." Genealogy: Heidegger (Eros moves thinking) → Plato (Socrates as atopos, Eros as philosophos, Eros son of Poros) → Deleuze & Guattari (the friend/lover as condition of possibility of thought).
  - Evidence: Heidegger's letter; the *Symposium* (Socrates/Alcibiades/Marsyas; Poros; philosophos); *What Is Philosophy?*
  - Source passage: > "Without seduction by the atopic Other, which sparks erotic desire, thinking withers into mere work, which always reproduces the Same."
  - Confidence: high (directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Theory in the emphatic sense is erotic` (new) — or fold into the realized `Eros` concept. Cross-link to `Big Data is blind to the event and so blind to the future`.

- **Big Data / "Google science" abolishes theory of the emphatic sort; positive, additive information produces neither insight nor truth.**
  - Reasoning structure: Anderson claims abundant data makes models (and causality) superfluous — correlation replaces causality, "the numbers speak for themselves." Han: this rests on "a weak and simplistic conception of theory." Strong theory is not a model but a primordial decision; it is prior to data ("things given"), transcends the positivity of facts, reduces entropy. "There is no such thing as data-driven thinking. Only calculation is data driven." Positive science is "additive or detective—not narrative or hermeneutic."
  - Evidence: Anderson's *Wired* essay quoted directly; the entropy/ritual argument; the information-vs-insight distinction.
  - Source passage: > "Data-based, positive science ('Google science'), which amounts to merely balancing out and comparing data, is putting an end to theory of the emphatic sort. It is additive or detective—not narrative or hermeneutic."
  - Source passage: > "Anderson's thesis rests on a weak and simplistic conception of theory. Theory offers more than a model or a hypothesis to be proven or disproven by means of experimentation."
  - Confidence: high (directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: update existing `arguments/Big Data is blind to the event and so blind to the future` with an Agony-of-Eros section (the Anderson refutation is the same anti-Dataism argument, now grounded in a primary text on theory/eros); alternatively mint `arguments/Correlation cannot replace theory because thinking is negativity not calculation`. Recommend UPDATE the existing argument to avoid duplication, cross-link to `Dataism` / `Counting vs Recounting`.

- **Massive information raises entropy and noise; therefore in the data deluge theory is more necessary than ever.**
  - Reasoning structure: information is positive/additive → it sprawls, runs together, deforms; theory (like ceremony/ritual) confers form, reduces entropy. The literary/artistic crisis (Butor) is a symptom of the same "crisis of the spirit," caused by the "tremendous noise" of new communication.
  - Evidence: the entropy/ceremony argument; Butor via *Die Zeit*.
  - Source passage: > "Today, the volume of data and information, proliferating without end, is pulling science away from thought on a massive scale."
  - Source passage: > "For the last ten or twenty years, almost nothing has been happening in literature. There's a tide of publications, but an intellectual standstill. The reason is a crisis of communication."
  - Confidence: high (directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: fold into the argument note above / `Narrativity` / `Transparency`. Butor quote → candidate citation.

- **Logos is powerless without eros; philosophy is the translation of eros into logos.**
  - Reasoning structure: at "the very beginning of philosophy and theory, logos and eros entered into such an intimate relation." Socrates seduces by words alone (unlike Pericles, whose words lack "the erotic power of seduction"); his discourse is daimonic because of "the negativity of atopia," yet does not end in aporia because Eros is son of Poros ("way").
  - Evidence: the *Symposium* (Alcibiades, Marsyas, Poros, philosophos); Deleuze & Guattari.
  - Source passage: > "Logos is powerless without the force of eros."

## Metaphors

- **The beating wings of Eros** — Heidegger: "The beat of that god's wings moves me every time I take a substantial step in my thinking and venture onto untrodden paths." Han: thinking grows "more powerful" and "uncannier" when "moved by the beating wings of Eros." (The image of eros as the god whose wing-beat propels thought.)
- **Untrodden paths / a clearing cut through untrodden terrain** — eros "leads and seduces thinking down untrodden paths"; theory "as highly selective narration … cuts a clearing of differentiation through untrodden terrain." (The path/clearing metaphor for genuine thinking vs the flat, mapped positivity of data.)
- **Thinking is an expedition into quietness** — "Thinking demands calm. Thinking is an expedition into quietness." (Set against the "racket"/noise of massive information.)
- **Socrates as the satyr Marsyas / the snakebite** — Socrates "seduces and intoxicates by means of words alone"; Alcibiades is wounded by his "speeches of wisdom … as if a snake had bitten him." (The erotic wound of true theory.)
- **"The numbers speak for themselves"** — Anderson's slogan (quoted), the emblem of the Dataist fantasy Han refutes.
- **Google science / making a racket** — "Rampant, massive information—an excess of positivity—makes a racket." (Information as noise.)

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Calculation vs Thinking** — "There is no such thing as data-driven thinking. Only calculation is data driven. The negativity of the incalculable is inscribed in thinking." (Maps onto `Positivity vs Negativity`, `Counting vs Recounting`, `Narration vs Addition`.)
- **Information vs Insight (Cognition vs Recognition)** — additive/cumulative/inconsequential information vs the exclusive, transformative, status-quo-shaking negativity of insight.
- **Erlebnis vs Erfahrung** — additive experiencing vs the once-occurring transformative experience that alone reaches the wholly Other (`Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (Han)`).
- **Positivity vs Negativity** — "work on the positive" that reproduces the Same vs the negativity of the atopic Other/the incalculable that inspires "disquiet or unrest" (`Positivity vs Negativity`, `Narration vs Addition`).
- **Correlation vs Causality** — Anderson's "Correlation is more important than causality" (data affinity/dependency patterns replacing causal/theoretical models), which Han rejects.
- **Noise vs Quietness (Spirit as unrest)** — the high noise level of the transparency/information society vs "Thinking is an expedition into quietness." A productive paradox: "Spirit—which originally meant unrest—owes its spiritedness … to negativity" — so spirit is *unrest* yet thinking demands *calm*; the unrest is the negativity of the Other, not the racket of information.
