## Executive Summary

This short chapter is Han's dedicated statement of pornography *as the antagonist of eros* — the fifth movement of *The Agony of Eros*, following melancholia (ch. 2), the power not-to (ch. 3), and bare life (ch. 4). Where the equivalent chapter in *The Transparency Society* defined the pornographic as a structural-formal condition (the abolition of veil, secret, distance, and hermeneutic depth), this chapter re-frames pornography around three tightly-linked theses:

1. **Porn is bare life on display, and it annihilates sexuality itself.** Pornography is "more effective than morality" at destroying sex: it is not an excess of sex but the presence of "no sex at all" — the "anticipation of dead sex in living sexuality" (Baudrillard). Today even *real* sex is turning into porn. This ties the chapter directly back to ch. 4 ("Bare Life"): porn is the display-form of bare, survival-reduced life.

2. **The pornographication of the world is the profanation of the world — and Han turns Agamben's *own* concept of profanation against him.** The chapter is essentially a sustained polemic against Giorgio Agamben's essay "In Praise of Profanation" (from *Profanations*, 2007) and against Agamben's *Nudities*. Agamben celebrates profanation as the liberation of things from consecration into "means without ends," and reads the exhibition of nudity (fashion models, porn stars) as opening the face to "a new form of erotic communication." Han argues that Agamben's thesis of secularization *blinds* him: exhibition does not profane in Agamben's redemptive sense — it *destroys cult value and replaces it with exhibition value*; the exhibited, expressionless, secret-less face is not erotic but "obscene and pornographic."

3. **Profanation unfolds as deritualization and desacralization — the destruction of the ritual spaces, distances, and scenic illusions on which eros depends.** Drawing on Bataille's "holy eroticism" and Baudrillard's opposition of seduction to love, Han argues that pornography "completes the deritualization of love," and that Agamben's profanation actively *promotes* this by suspecting all ritual sequestration of being mere compulsion.

The chapter's philosophical core is the museum/temple, tourism/pilgrimage, and cult-value/exhibition-value contrasts — Han deploys Benjamin (implicitly) and Heidegger (dwelling, the "non-sites" of tourism) to argue that setting-apart, distance, and place are not repressions to be liberated but the very conditions of the sacred and the erotic. Capitalism aggravates all of this: "Capitalism is aggravating the pornographication of society by making everything a commodity and putting it on display."

For the vault, this chapter is primarily an **EXTENSION of the existing `Pornography (Han)` note** (adding the Agony-of-Eros / profanation / deritualization layer beside the existing Transparency-Society, Psychopolitics, and Debord-Spectacle layers), and a strong secondary anchor for the forward-stubs `Eros` and `The Other` (porn as the antagonist of eros), plus `Exhibition Value` / `Cult Value vs Exhibition Value (Han via Benjamin)`, `The Veil`, and the `Byung-Chul Han and Jean Baudrillard` reference. It also opens (or reinforces) forward-stub-worthy material on **Profanation (Agamben)**, **Deritualization / Desacralization**, and **Ritual**.

## Definitions

- **Porn (Han, via Baudrillard)** — not a genre of explicit imagery but the mode in which sexuality "fades … into the more sexual than sex: porn"; obscene not by excess of sex but because "it contains no sex at all"; "the anticipation of dead sex in living sexuality."
  - Source passage: > "'Sexuality does not fade into sublimation, repression and morality, but fades much more surely into the more sexual than sex: porn.'"
  - Key passage (Han's own gloss): > "Today, sexuality is not threatened by that 'pure reason' which puritanically avoids sex as something 'dirty,' but by pornography. Porn is not sex in virtual space. Today, even real sex is turning into porn."

- **Profanation (Agamben, as Han reports it)** — "using things that have been set aside for the gods through consecration (sacrare) and stand removed from regular contact"; practicing "a special form of negligence" toward what has been set apart; liberating things into "means without ends."
  - Source passage: > "'Profanation' means using things that have been set aside for the gods through consecration (sacrare) and stand removed from regular contact. It involves practicing 'a special form of negligence' with respect to what has been set apart in this manner."

- **The pornographic face (Han)** — a face "loaded with exhibition-value to the point of bursting," yet "without mystery or expression — reduced simply to being on display"; it "says nothing," has "no expressivity or mystery," and is therefore "obscene and pornographic."
  - Source passage: > "A naked face without mystery or expression—reduced simply to being on display—is obscene and pornographic."

## Arguments

- **Claim: Porn destroys sexuality more effectively than morality does — because it is not an excess of sex but its absence (dead sex in living sexuality).**
  - Reasoning structure: inversion of the puritanical hypothesis — the threat to sex is not repression/"pure reason" but hyper-exposure; porn is the "more sexual than sex" in which sex itself dies.
  - Evidence: Baudrillard's *Fatal Strategies* ("fades much more surely into the more sexual than sex: porn"; "anticipation of dead sex in living sexuality").
  - Source passage: > "The antagonist of eros, it annihilates even sexuality. In this respect, it is more effective than morality"
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: capture in the `Pornography (Han)` extension; candidate argument name: `Porn destroys sexuality more effectively than morality` (optional — Human Review).

- **Claim: Agamben's celebration of profanation fails, because exhibition does not free things for a "new use" but destroys their cult value and replaces it with exhibition value.**
  - Reasoning structure: Han grants Agamben's descriptive frame (setting-apart, consecration) but denies the redemptive conclusion; the museum and tourism are the counter-examples — they profane by *destroying* aura/cult value and place, not by liberating.
  - Evidence: museum vs. temple (musealization destroys cult value → exhibition value); tourism's "non-sites" vs. pilgrimage's places; Heidegger on dwelling and the "divine"; the exhibited porn-star/model face that "says nothing."
  - Source passage: > "Contra Agamben, a face loaded with exhibition-value to the point of bursting promises no 'new collective use of sexuality.' Indeed, exhibition destroys any and all possibilities for erotic communication."
  - Source passage: > "On this score, Agamben's 'profanation' amounts to just so much profanity."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: candidate argument `Exhibition destroys cult value it does not profane it into new use` (Han contra Agamben) — Human Review; at minimum captured in the extension.

- **Claim: Pornography completes the deritualization of love; ritual distance and scenic illusion, not their liberation, are the condition of eros.**
  - Reasoning structure: Han aligns himself with Bataille's "holy eroticism" (ritualized communication) and Baudrillard's seduction (ritual, scenic illusion, deceptive appearances) against the modern reduction of love to "warmth, intimacy, and pleasant arousal"; profanation (Agamben) *promotes* deritualization by suspecting ritual sequestration.
  - Evidence: Bataille's festivals/ceremonial games; Baudrillard — "Ritual is in the realm of seduction. Love is born from the destruction of ritual forms."
  - Source passage: > "By the same token, pornography is eliminating erotic seduction, which toys with scenic illusion and deceptive appearances."
  - Source passage: > "Agamben's profanation even promotes the deritualization and pornographication of the world in that it suspects ritual spaces of constituting compulsive forms of sequestration."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: capture in the extension; candidate argument `Pornography completes the deritualization of love` (optional).

- **Claim: Capitalism aggravates the pornographication of society by commodifying and exhibiting everything.**
  - Reasoning structure: the profanation-as-exhibition-value logic is generalized to the commodity form; capitalism, "knowing no other use for sexuality," profanes eros into porn.
  - Evidence: > "Capitalism is aggravating the pornographication of society by making everything a commodity and putting it on display. Knowing no other use for sexuality, it profanes eros—into porn."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: capture in the extension; cross-links `Exhibition Value`, `Spectacle`, and the commodity-as-display line to Debord (already in the `Pornography (Han)` Debord section).

## Metaphors

- **The world as "one big museum"** — Han's image (reporting Agamben's tourism/pilgrimage analogy) of a world that has become one continuous museum through which sightseers take "restless trips." Used to figure the destruction of cult value / place.
  - Source passage: > "sightseers' restless trips through a world that has become one big museum."

- **The cat playing with a ball of yarn** — Agamben's example (which Han quotes and rejects) of profanation-as-deactivation: predatory behavior used "in vain," "opened up for a new, possible use."
  - Source passage: > "The cat who plays with a ball of yarn as if it were a mouse … knowingly uses the characteristic behaviors of predatory activity … in vain."
  - (This is Agamben's metaphor, reported by Han, not endorsed — flag as Agamben's.)

- **The face "loaded until it bursts with exhibition-value"** — Agamben's phrase (again reported and inverted by Han): the model/porn-star face nullified of expressivity, loaded with exhibition value.
  - Source passage: > "the face is loaded until it bursts with exhibition-value."

## Symbols

- **The naked/pornographic face** — the recurring figure of the chapter: the expressionless, secret-less face reduced to pure display ("one's own absolute mediality," in Agamben's words) — for Agamben the site of a new erotic communication, for Han the very type of the obscene.
- **The veil / the secret** — the (absent) positive term: "the erotic is never free of secrecy." Its removal is what makes the pornographic.
- **The temple / museum and pilgrimage / tourism** — paired sites of the sacred-at-a-remove vs. its exhibition-value dissolution.

- **Han contra Agamben** — the internal contradiction Han presses: Agamben's own secularization thesis "blinds him" to a phenomenon that "even stands opposed" to religious practice; profanation, meant to liberate, actually promotes pornographication.
