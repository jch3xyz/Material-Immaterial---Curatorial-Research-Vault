## Executive Summary

This chapter introduces and develops **Rendition** — Zuboff's name for the concrete operational practices by which human experience is claimed and translated into behavioral data. Where earlier chapters established that surveillance capitalism *claims* human experience as free raw material, this chapter ("and the next") drills into the often-overlooked intermediate step: the gap between experience and data, and the operations engineered to cross it. Zuboff frames the chapter around a double meaning of the verb "render" — (1) the *causal* sense of forming one thing out of another (rendering oil from fat, translation), and (2) the *surrender* sense, in which the thing changed gives itself over to the process ("render unto Caesar"). Surveillance capitalism, she argues, must work both sides: its technologies render experience into data outside our awareness, while every encounter with a digital interface makes our experience available to "datafication."

The chapter is organized in two sections. **Section I (Terms of Sur-render)** establishes rendition as "Step One" — the operationalization of the "original sin" of dispossession — and surveys the migration of rendition into ordinary products: the iRobot Roomba (selling home floor-plan maps), the Sleep Number bed, Alphabet's Nest thermostat, "smart" everything, the "internet of things," and ultra-ubiquitous "smart skin." It diagnoses the coercive contract logic ("Bend the knee or we degrade your purchase"), the death of the "functional, effective, affordable product" as a sufficient basis for exchange, and the meaninglessness of "opt in / opt out." **Section II (Body Rendition)** narrows to the rendition of the body: location tracking via the phone (geotags, geofencing, "life pattern marketing," Google's "Your Timeline," Android's covert tower-triangulation), the de-anonymization of "anonymized" metadata ("databases of ruin"), wearables, m-health and fitness/diabetes apps (privacy policies that are "more aptly referred to as surveillance policies"), and biometric facial recognition (Facebook's resistance to the Illinois Biometric Privacy Act). It closes by recasting rendition as conquest under "the dictatorship of no alternatives," and flags that Chapter 9 will move from this *extension* dimension (scope's first dimension) into the *depth* dimension (personality, emotions, endorphins).

This is the chapter that most directly grounds the vault's surveillance-cluster forward-stubs in primary text. **Rendition** is the keystone new concept; **The Reality Business** / extension-into-the-physical-world is realized here as the migration of extraction from the online to the real world; and the chapter realizes the long-committed `Surveillance` and `Quantified Self` stubs (the body/health-tracking material is the Quantified Self anchor). It deeply extends Han's `Digital Panopticon`, Harari's `Dataism`, the cross-author `Big Data` and `The Body as Data` notes, and resonates with Kurzweil's embraced ubiquitous computing ("smart dust," the instrumented body).

  - Evidence: Aware Home (rendition under individual sovereignty); the asymmetric coercive contracts of Roomba/Nest/Sleep Number.
  - Source passage: > "The lesson of the Aware Home is that there can be rendition without surveillance capitalism. However, the lesson of this chapter and the next is that there can be no surveillance capitalism without rendition."

- **Privacy policies are surveillance policies** — the documents that claim to protect privacy in fact authorize, conceal, and normalize extraction; consent is a fig leaf.
  - Reasoning structure: empirical inversion (studies show policies enable, not restrict, sharing) + renaming.
  - Evidence: diabetes-app study (79% of apps with policies shared data, half disclosed it); 1,000-contract Nest ecosystem; 14-second median read time vs 45 minutes for comprehension.
  - Source passage: > "privacy policies are more aptly referred to as surveillance policies, and that is what I suggest we call them."

- **Consent under surveillance capitalism is coercion, not choice ("Bend the knee or we degrade your purchase")** — opt-in/opt-out is meaningless because refusal degrades or disables the product; rendition is unilateral and compelled.
  - Reasoning structure: Requirimiento analogy (Spanish conquest's coercive legal ritual) → the modern "abusive contract."

- **Privacy vs Surveillance (Revenue)** — transparency and privacy are *friction* to surveillance capitalism; the cross-author `Privacy vs Security` tension (Kurzweil) is adjacent but distinct — here it is privacy vs the *logic of accumulation*. Recommend a `Privacy vs Surveillance` tension or extend an existing one.
- **Individual Sovereignty over Data vs Unilateral Rendition** — the Aware Home's three principles (the individual decides what is rendered, the data enriches the individual, the individual is sole arbiter of use) vs surveillance capitalism's unilateral, secret extraction. The keystone normative contrast.
- **The Functional Product vs The Product-as-Gateway** — the dying ideal of a product as sufficient economic exchange vs the product reimagined as an extraction gateway.
- **Material/Embodied Experience vs Behavioral Data** — the body as lived experience vs the body "reimagined as a behaving object." (Connects to the cross-author `Material vs Immaterial` and `The Body as Data`.)
