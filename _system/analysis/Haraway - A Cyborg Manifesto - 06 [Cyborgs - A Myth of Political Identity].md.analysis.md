## Executive Summary

This is the concluding section of *A Cyborg Manifesto*. Having (in earlier sections) defined the cyborg, mapped the historical shift to the **informatics of domination**, and located women in the homework economy and the integrated circuit, Haraway now turns to **myth and identity**: she builds "a myth about identity and boundaries that might inform late-twentieth-century political imaginations." The section advances two converging arguments through two overlapping groups of texts: (1) the construction of **"women of color"** as a **cyborg identity** built on **affinity, not natural identity**, anchored in the figures of **Sister Outsider** and **Malinche**; and (2) the **monstrous selves of feminist science fiction** (Russ, Delany, Tiptree, Varley, Butler, McIntyre) as cyborg myths that refuse "innocent wholeness."

The throughline is a **refusal of innocence, origin, and organic wholeness**. Haraway attacks the **founding myth of original wholeness** — Eden, the Fall, the apocalyptic "return to a deathly oneness" — and proposes **Cyborg Writing** as "the power to survive, not on the basis of original innocence, but on the basis of seizing the tools to mark the world that marked them as other." She recapitulates the **persistent Western dualisms** ("self/other, mind/body, culture/nature...") and shows how **high-tech culture challenges these dualisms**: "It is not clear who makes and who is made... The machine is us, our processes, an aspect of our embodiment." She closes with **regeneration vs rebirth** (the salamander regrowing a possibly-monstrous limb) and the manifesto's most-quoted line: **"I would rather be a cyborg than a goddess."** Throughout, the **material/immaterial entanglement** is foregrounded (the cyborg as "a condensed image of both imagination and material reality" — established in §01 and consummated here as bodies "textualized... as code problems on the grid of C3I").

This section is the PRIMARY source for THE CYBORG as a political myth, and the sole source for several durable concepts (Cyborg Writing, Affinity not Identity, Women of Color as Cyborg Identity, Regeneration vs Rebirth, the Refusal of Innocence). It is the canonical anchor for the manifesto's signature symbol/slogan.

## Arguments

- **Claim: Cyborg writing is survival by seizing the tools that marked you as other, not a return to original innocence.**
  - Reasoning structure: Writing has been central to the Western myth of the oral/written, primitive/civilized divide and to phallogocentrism's "unique and perfect name." For colonized and othered groups, literacy is "a special mark," acquired "through a history of risking death." Therefore cyborg writing must refuse the Fall / pre-linguistic wholeness and instead weaponize the conqueror's tools.
  - Evidence: Malinche, master of languages and mistress of Cortés; Cherríe Moraga's "self-consciously spliced, a chimera of English and Spanish"; the poetry of U.S. women of color "repeatedly about writing, about access to the power to signify."
  - Source passage: > "Cyborg writing is about the power to survive, not on the basis of original innocence, but on the basis of seizing the tools to mark the world that marked them as other."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Cyborg writing is seizing the tools that marked you as other.md`

- **Claim: Cyborg politics reject the dream of a common language and embrace noise, pollution, and the partial.**
  - Reasoning structure: A single perfect code that "translates all meaning perfectly" is "the central dogma of phallogocentrism." Resistance therefore lies in imperfect, polluted, illegitimate fusions, not in purity or perfect communication.
  - Evidence: The closing "dream not of a common language, but of a powerful infidel heteroglossia."
  - Source passage: > "That is why cyborg politics insist on noise and advocate pollution, rejoicing in the illegitimate fusions of animal and machine."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Cyborg politics reject perfect communication and embrace noise and pollution.md`

- **Claim: Effective politics must refuse to be grounded in innocence, victimization, purity, or closeness to nature.**
  - Reasoning structure: Feminisms and Marxisms "have run aground" on the Western imperative to build a revolutionary subject from a hierarchy of oppressions claiming moral superiority and closeness to nature. Recognizing oneself as "fully implicated in the world" frees politics from identification, vanguardism, and "mothering."
  - Evidence: The critique of ecofeminism/feminist paganism's organicism; the reframing of women of color and "real-life cyborgs" (Aihwa Ong's Southeast Asian electronics workers) as agents "actively rewriting the texts of their bodies and societies" rather than victims.
  - Source passage: > "Feminisms and Marxisms have run aground on Western epistemological imperatives to construct a revolutionary subject from the perspective of a hierarchy of oppressions and/or a latent position of moral superiority, innocence, and greater closeness to nature."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Feminist politics must refuse the ground of innocence and victimization.md`

- **Claim: High-tech culture dissolves the foundational Western dualisms; the machine is us, an aspect of our embodiment.**
  - Reasoning structure: In machines that "resolve into coding practices" it is unclear who makes and who is made, what is mind and what is body; biological organisms "have become biotic systems, communications devices like others"; therefore there is "no fundamental, ontological separation" of machine and organism, and the machine/organism relationship of animation-or-mechanization is "obsolete."
  - Evidence: Rachel the replicant in *Blade Runner*; the "trance state" of computer users; paraplegics' "intense experiences of complex hybridization"; McCaffrey's *The Ship Who Sang*.
  - Source passage: > "The machine is not an it to be animated, worshipped, and dominated. The machine is us, our processes, an aspect of our embodiment."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The machine is us not an other to dominate.md` (or fold into Embodiment / Material vs Immaterial — see Human Review).

- **Claim: Cyborgs are about regeneration, not rebirth; we require reconstitution, not return to wholeness.**
  - Reasoning structure: Organicist, holistic politics depend on "metaphors of rebirth" and "the resources of reproductive sex." The cyborg is "suspicious of the reproductive matrix." The salamander's regenerated limb — "monstrous, duplicated, potent" — models reconstitution after injury without the demand for flawless rebirth.
  - Evidence: The salamander regrowing structure and function "with the constant possibility of twinning or other odd topographical productions."
  - Source passage: > "We have all been injured, profoundly. We require regeneration, not rebirth, and the possibilities for our reconstitution include the utopian dream of the hope for a monstrous world without gender."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/We require regeneration not rebirth.md` (paired concept: `concepts/Regeneration vs Rebirth.md`).

- **Claim: Universal totalizing theory is a major mistake; responsibility for science and technology means refusing both organic holism and a demonology of technology.**
  - Reasoning structure: The two "crucial arguments" Haraway names: (1) totalizing theory "misses most of reality"; (2) taking responsibility means "refusing an antiscience metaphysics, a demonology of technology" while still naming science/technology as "a matrix of complex dominations." The cyborg image offers "a way out of the maze of dualisms."
  - Evidence: The double vision — technology as both "human satisfaction" and "complex dominations."
  - Source passage: > "first, the production of universal, totalizing theory is a major mistake that misses most of reality, probably always, but certainly now; and second, taking responsibility for the social relations of science and technology means refusing an antiscience metaphysics, a demonology of technology..."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Totalizing theory misses reality and a demonology of technology must be refused.md`
