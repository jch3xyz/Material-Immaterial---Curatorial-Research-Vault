## Executive Summary

This 1990 essay is Ascott's most concentrated meditation on **time** in telematic culture, and the chapter where he most explicitly maps the new physics onto his theory of networked art. Refusing the "classical dialectic" of comparison, he proposes to *locate* time by a non-analytical, creative method of **triangulation**, bringing together three "threads of ideas" he abbreviates ESP, PDP, and PU. **ESP** points to *media* — at once "Extra-Sensory Perception" (modes of knowing "outside the Cartesian-Newtonian frame of being") and the initials of Edwin S. Porter (the filmic discovery that time can be "compressed, expanded, or reversed" by editing), the lineage running through Griffith's parallel editing to "the mosaic screen of digital discontinuity… which we are now beginning to exploit through telematic media." **PDP** is *parallel data processing* / computer science — the computer as a machine that "works with time" (Bolter's "Turing's man"), processing discrete nanosecond pulses into information at speeds "outside human experience," and, networked globally, generating a "schizo-time" and the conditions for a "world culture" and a "global consciousness." **PU** is *parallel universes* / the many-worlds interpretation of quantum physics — where the observer "creates the reality of the act by observation," where photons "spend no time in our world," and which "resonate[s] most sympathetically with our current sensibilities," modeling "the production of realities… in telematic culture."

The payoff is the chapter's keystone argument: **telematic art moves beyond both object art and time-based art, and rejects representation in favour of simulation.** Representation, Ascott argues, demands the art object, a fixed creator/observer separation, coherence in space and time, and a passive observing subject — it "masks the inherent instability, the chaos, of process in the world." Telematic art instead simulates "the unstable, chaotic, fleeting, and numinous quality of its subject," replacing assertion with "negotiation, participation, collaboration," and rendering "what is invisible visible." Against the "deconstructive pessimism of late postmodern art" he holds out hope for a "radically constructive art" of "parallel time-worlds." The user — "neither autonomous creator nor passive viewer" — moving through these worlds is "like Schrödinger's Cat, neither precisely here nor there, actual or virtual," and "like Heraclitus… can never put our foot into the same river twice."

For the vault, this chapter is a primary anchor for a new concept cluster around **simulation vs representation**, **telematic time / schizo-time / hyper time**, the **computer as an extra-sensory device**, and Ascott's **triangulation** method; it strongly EXTENDS `Telematic Art`, `Network Consciousness`, the existing eight-author `Material vs Immaterial` tension (here the object→system, representation→simulation spine), and cross-links to McLuhan (the "mosaic screen"/discontinuity), Hayles/Haraway (the dispersed, out-of-body networked mind; the world as code), O'Gieblyn (the "seamless universal web of which… only mystics and madmen would speak," the new physics, re-enchantment), and the Burnham systems cluster (object art / "beyond object art").

## Definitions

- **Telematics (Ascott, glossed here)** — "beyond its technical designation of the convergence of computer and telecommunications systems—effectively out-of-body, out-of mind… asynchronous connectivity, whereby individuals can communicate (negotiate for meaning) through electronic networks, regardless of where they are located in the world or at what 'real' time they log on." (An Ascott re-gloss; folds into `Telematic Art` / the existing `Telematics (Nora and Minc via Ascott)` definition.)
- **Triangulation (Ascott)** — "to use the non-analytical creative process to locate it [time], by employing a form of triangulation. The three coordinates, threads of ideas… ESP, PDP, and PU." (Functional definition of the method.)
- **Simulation (telematic art's mode, by opposition to representation)** — telematic art "rejects representation in favour of simulation," seeking to "simulate in its digital space-time the unstable, chaotic, fleeting, and numinous quality of its subject" and "to render what is invisible visible." (Definition by contrast; the keystone.)
- **Representation (Ascott's antagonist sense)** — "Representation demands work that is determinate, coherent, and consistent. It produces an art of assertion… in which the observing subject must be essentially passive." (Defined as the old order's mode.)
- **Schizo-time / hyper time (Ascott)** — "the new time, a kind of schizo-time"; "layered phase-space, discontinuous periodicity… hyper time, the branching time-pathways of parallel universes." (The telematic temporality.)
