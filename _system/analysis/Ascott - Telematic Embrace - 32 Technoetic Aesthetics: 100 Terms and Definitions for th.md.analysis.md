## Executive Summary

This chapter is a **glossary / lexicon**: a hundred short, aphoristic dictionary entries (1996) coining or redefining terms for what Ascott calls the "Post-Biological Era." Unlike Ascott's expository essays, it is a list of compressed definitions, many of them puns and neologisms (Cyburbs, Cyberstress, Telemadic, Ayahu-ascii, Phreno-fractals). It functions as the keystone vocabulary of Ascott's late, third-phase thought — **technoetics** (the fusion of technology and consciousness), **cyberception** (technologically augmented perception/cognition), **moistmedia** (the dry/digital + wet/biological convergence, here phrased as "Variable reality": dry/wet/moist), the **fivefold path** (connectivity, immersion, interaction, transformation, emergence), **telenoia** (the networked opposite of paranoia), the **double gaze** ("Double gazing" and "Double consciousness"), and the **Planetary Collegium**. The entries restate Ascott's signature theses in maximally portable form: that art has shifted from the *appearance* of things to *processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence*; that consciousness, not matter, is now the medium of art; that the self is decentred, distributed, multiple ("I connect, therefore I am multiple"); and that technology re-enchants reality, reintegrating the paranormal, the shamanic, and the spiritual ("the future will be moist"). For the ingest, this chapter is the densest single source of Ascott's coined CONCEPTS and DEFINITIONS, and the clearest place to anchor cyberception, technoetics/technoetic aesthetics, moistmedia, telenoia, the double gaze, the connectivity/immersion/interaction/transformation/emergence triad, and the Planetary Collegium.

## Arguments

- **Claim: Art has shifted from the appearance/representation of things to the processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence.**
  - Reasoning structure: programmatic redefinition of art's object — from noun (appearance) to verb (process); restates the whole behaviourist→telematic→technoetic arc in one line.
  - Evidence: the "Art" and "Behaviour" and "Technoetic aesthetics" entries.
  - Source passage: > "While traditionally focused on the appearance of things and their representation, art is now concerned with processes of interaction, transformation, and emergence."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Art has moved from the appearance of things to processes of interaction transformation and emergence`

- **Claim: The classical concern with surface appearance gives way to an aesthetics of consciousness — the proper medium of art is now mind/consciousness, not matter.**
  - Reasoning structure: the technoetic turn; consciousness (noetikos) becomes the medium and subject of art.
  - Evidence: the "Technoetic aesthetics" and "Cross-sectoral sensibility" entries ("Its principal concern is with mind, states of being, and the process of becoming").
  - Source passage: > "The classical concern with the surface image of the world gives way to the technoetic aesthetics of creative consciousness and artificial life."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The medium of technoetic art is consciousness not surface appearance`

- **Claim: Telematic networking produces a celebratory networked consciousness (telenoia) that supersedes industrial-era paranoia.**
  - Reasoning structure: diagnostic inversion — the private, secretive, alienated self of industrial culture is replaced by an open, connected, planetary consciousness.
  - Evidence: the "Telenoia," "Telematic Imperative," and "Connectitude" entries.
  - Source passage: > "Telenoia celebrates the networked consciousness of global connectivity. It replaces the anxious, alienated, secretive, and neurotically private paranoia of the old industrial culture."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Telenoia replaces the paranoia of industrial culture with networked consciousness`

- **Claim: The unified individual is dead; the self is decentred, distributed, and multiple — connection multiplies rather than unifies the self.**
  - Reasoning structure: Cartesian inversion ("I connect, therefore I am multiple") + a post-Freudian claim about the dissolution of the unified subject.
  - Evidence: "Cyberself," "Non-linear identity," "Phreno-fractals."
  - Source passage: > "Freud is dead and the myth of the unified individual has been destroyed. We are each made up of many selves: de-centred, distributed, and tele-schizophrenic."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The networked self is decentred distributed and multiple not unified` (cross-link Hayles The Liberal Humanist Subject, Haraway Fractured Identities)

- **Claim: Reality is no longer a fixed text to be interpreted but is written and negotiated through interaction ("hermeneutic error").**
  - Reasoning structure: constructivist epistemology — meaning is produced, not read off a pre-given world.
  - Evidence: the "Hermeneutic error" and "Radical constructivism/Radical connectivism" entries.
  - Source passage: > "In the pre-telematic era, we thought the world was full of meaning, a text to be interpreted, a great book waiting to be read. Now we see that we write our own reality and negotiate for meaning through interaction."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/We write our own reality and negotiate meaning through interaction`

- **Claim: Dematerialisation of art leads inexorably to re-materialisation of culture as artificial life (immateriality → moist).**
  - Reasoning structure: dialectical reversal — the dry/immaterial does not end in pure information but loops back into the wet/living via biotechnology; "the future will be moist."
  - Evidence: "Immateriality," "Variable reality," "Nature II," "Bionicity."
  - Source passage: > "The dematerialisation of art, telemedia, and virtual reality leads inexorably to the re-materialisation of culture in the form of artificial life."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Dematerialisation rematerialises as artificial life` (cross-link Lippard Dematerialization, Burnham, Material vs Immaterial)

- **Claim: Mind is supervenient on the activity of its interfaces, whether organic or telematic, but requires matter to become manifest.**
  - Reasoning structure: a philosophy-of-mind position folded into the technoetic program — consciousness is ubiquitous but needs an interface (organic OR machinic) to appear.
  - Evidence: the "Supervenience," "Ki," and "Spiritual software" entries.
  - Source passage: > "Mind is supervenient on the activity of its interfaces, whether organic or telematic, and although mind is ubiquitous and always present, it requires matter to become manifest."
  - Confidence: medium (compressed lexicon entry; strongly stated but undeveloped here)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Mind is supervenient on its interfaces organic or telematic` (cross-link O'Gieblyn Panpsychism / The Hard Problem of Consciousness, Material vs Immaterial)

- **Claim: The future will be moist — dry digital and wet biological converge in a third, emergent state (artificial life).**
  - Reasoning structure: triadic synthesis (dry/wet → moist); the core moistmedia thesis stated lexically.
  - Evidence: "Variable reality," "Datapool" ("Post-biological life forms grow in these datapools just as proto organisms emerged from primeval rock pools").
  - Source passage: > "Dry reality is found in the arid spaces of VR. Wet reality is the nature that we nurture. Moist reality emerges from the biotechnology of artificial life."
  - Confidence: high

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Dry vs Wet vs Moist (the variable-reality triad)** — VR's arid dryness vs nurtured wet nature vs the emergent moist of artificial life. This is the book's signature synthesis-tension and the seed of `Moistmedia`.
- **Material vs Immaterial** — "Immateriality" (dematerialisation) loops into re-materialisation as artificial life; "Supervenience" insists mind "requires matter to become manifest." Strong feed into the existing eight-author `Material vs Immaterial` tension.
- **Trivial vs Nontrivial interactivity (FDS vs OES)** — closed finite-data-set interaction vs open-ended, user/environment-in-control systems. Cross-links Burnham's `One-Way Stimulation vs Two-Way Communication`.
- **Appearance vs Process / Apparition** — the classical aesthetics of appearance/surface vs the technoetic aesthetics of process, becoming, and "apparition" ("the aesthetics of apparition" in "Psibernetic phenomena"). Seeds the Apparition vs Appearance pair flagged in the focus brief.
- **Telenoia vs Paranoia** — networked consciousness vs the alienated secretive private self of industrial culture.
- **Unified self vs Multiple/distributed self** — "Freud is dead"; "I connect, therefore I am multiple."
- **Connectionism vs Connectivism** — the cognitive scientist's way vs the technoetic artist's way ("Connectivity" entry).
