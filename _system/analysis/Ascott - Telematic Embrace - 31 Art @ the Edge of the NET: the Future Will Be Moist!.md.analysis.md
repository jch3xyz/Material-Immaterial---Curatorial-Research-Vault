## Executive Summary

This is the **keystone "moistmedia" essay** of *Telematic Embrace* and the primary anchor for the entire late-period (1990s–2000s) Ascott cluster of MOIST. The essay opens with the verse "Moist Manifesto" (a litany of definitions: "MOIST SPACE is where dry pixels and wet molecules converge"; "MOIST MEDIA comprises bits, atoms, neurons, and genes"; "MOIST MIND is technoetic multiconsciousness") and then expands it into a programmatic prose vision for twenty-first-century art.

Ascott's central claim: just as twentieth-century interactive media "replaced the cult of the objet d'art with a process-based culture," the twenty-first century brings a further shift — "as silicon and pixels merge with molecules and matter." Between the **dry** world of virtuality and the **wet** world of biology lies a third, **moist** domain, "a new interspace of potentiality and promise." MOISTMEDIA — "comprising bits, atoms, neurons, and genes" — will be the substrate of a new transformative art "concerned with the construction of a fluid reality." This convergence is fundamentally a convergence of **digital / biological / spiritual** ("MOIST ART is digitally dry, biologically wet, and spiritually numinous"), and it is **technoetic**: "techne and gnōsis combined into a new knowledge of the world, a connective mind that is spawning new realities and new definitions of life and human identity."

Around this core the essay develops several durable, named Ascott concepts that the vault's earlier-chapter analyses have repeatedly DEFERRED to "the later moist/technoetics chapters" — this is that chapter:
1. **Moistmedia / Moist Manifesto** (the title figure; the digital/biological/spiritual convergence). PRIMARY ANCHOR.
2. **Visionary Pragmatism** — "flying with your feet on the ground… working with the future in the present" — Ascott's name (drawn through Richard Rorty's pragmatism) for what artists do; the essay's organizing methodological category. PRIMARY ANCHOR.
3. **The Planetary Collegium** — the post-institutional, networked, transdisciplinary research organism (seven planetary nodes, the evolution of CAiiA-STAR), here treated as "a paradigmatic embodiment of the nascent moist culture." (Dedicated anchor is ch. 26; this chapter is a major co-source — recommend EXTEND.)
4. **Telenoia** — "the telenoia of universal connectivity and transparency," the networked opposite of paranoia. (Dedicated anchor is ch. 23; EXTEND.)
5. **Cyberception** (and "psi-perception") — perception extended by technology; "our newly evolved cyberception and our rediscovered psi-perception." (Dedicated anchor is ch. 27; EXTEND.)
6. **Technoetics / Technoetic Aesthetics** — techne + gnōsis; "technoetic multiconsciousness." (Dedicated anchor is ch. 32; EXTEND.)
7. **Cyberbotany** and the psychoactive-plant / ayahuasca strand (the ayahuascatec.net project) — vegetal reality + virtual reality → "a new ontology." (Cross-links to the Shamantic Web, ch. 30.)
8. **The Building of Sentience / moist architecture** — architecture that shifts "at the level of behaviour rather than form," "Engineering will embrace ontology," "the goal will be the building of sentience."
9. **The Transformative Vocation** — art reframed (via Roberto Mangabeira Unger) as a "transformative vocation" replacing the "honourable calling."

For the vault, this chapter is the strongest primary-text anchor for the MOIST cluster and a major EXTEND source for the technoetics / cyberception / telenoia / Planetary Collegium notes. It cross-links hard to: the just-realized Burnham art cluster (Burnham's object→system "process-based culture" is explicitly echoed in Ascott's "replaced the cult of the objet d'art with a process-based culture"); McLuhan ("the spread of intelligence to every part of the built environment," the global nervous system → network/technoetic consciousness); Hayles/Haraway (the post-biological, transformable, prosthetic body; "Human Body Version 2.0"-style demand for "prostheses and genetic intervention"); Kurzweil/Harari (the post-biological culture, ubiquitous computing, intelligence pervading matter); and O'Gieblyn (re-enchantment — "spiritually numinous," sentience "pervades its every living part," panpsychist re-enchantment of nature).

## Arguments

- **Claim: Art is shifting from the dry digital and the wet biological to a third, moist domain — moistmedia — that will be the substrate of twenty-first-century art.**
  - Reasoning structure: historical analogy (20th c. interactive media replaced the objet d'art with process culture) → extrapolation (silicon/pixels now merge with molecules/matter) → the moist interspace.
  - Evidence: the "Moist Manifesto" litany; the dry/wet/moist tripartition; "bits, atoms, neurons, and genes."
  - Source passage: > "Just as the development of interactive media in the past century transformed the world of print and broadcasting, and replaced the cult of the objet d'art with a process-based culture, so, at the start of this century, we see a further artistic shift, as silicon and pixels merge with molecules and matter."
