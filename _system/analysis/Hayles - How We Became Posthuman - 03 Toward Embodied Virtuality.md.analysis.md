> Note: front-matter labels this `chapter: 1`, titled "Toward Embodied Virtuality." Functionally this is the book's **prologue/introduction** — it sketches the entire argument, the three stories, the three waves of cybernetics, and Hayles's methodological toolkit (seriation, skeuomorph, constellation). It is the densest single source for the book's core machinery and should be treated as the anchor for most concept notes.

## Executive Summary

This is the programmatic introduction to *How We Became Posthuman*. Hayles announces her central project: to contest the cultural construction of **information as a disembodied pattern** that can "circulate unchanged among different material substrates," and to recover the **embodiment** that this construction systematically erases. She is provoked by Hans Moravec's fantasy of downloading consciousness into a computer, which she reads as a "nightmare" symptomatic of a wider belief that mind can be separated from body.

She organizes the book around **three interrelated stories**: (1) how information "lost its body" (came to be conceptualized as separate from material form); (2) how the **cyborg** was constructed as cultural icon and technological artifact after WWII; and (3) how the historically specific construction "the human" gave way to "the **posthuman**." She defines the posthuman as a *point of view* with four assumptions (pattern over instantiation; consciousness as epiphenomenon; body as the "original prosthesis"; seamless articulation with intelligent machines), and crucially argues the posthuman *shares* the **liberal humanist subject's erasure of embodiment** — both privilege mind/cognition over body.

She lays out the historical arc as **three waves of cybernetics**: first wave (1945–1960, **homeostasis**, the Macy Conferences); second wave (1960–1980, **reflexivity**, the **observer**, second-order cybernetics, **autopoiesis**); third wave (1980–present, **virtuality**, emergence, **artificial life**). She introduces her methodological vocabulary from archaeological anthropology — **seriation**, **skeuomorph**, **conceptual constellation** — to argue that conceptual change is patchwork overlapping innovation/replication, not Kuhnian paradigm shift or Foucauldian epistemic break. She defines **virtuality** as "the cultural perception that material objects are interpenetrated by information patterns," and presents **Shannon's** information theory (information as "a pattern, not a presence," with no necessary connection to meaning) as the technical hinge of the whole construction, contrasted with **MacKay's** rejected meaning-based alternative. She names her critique of the "Platonic backhand and forehand" (abstraction reified as the Real), insists "for information to exist, it must always be instantiated in a medium," and defends **narrative** and literary reading (Wolfe's *Limbo*, Dick, Gibson, Bear, Stephenson, Powers) as more embodied forms of discourse against the "teleology of disembodiment."

- **Information (as disembodied pattern)** — information conceptualized as an entity separable from any material substrate, free to circulate unchanged; "a pattern, not a presence."

## Arguments

- **Claim: Information was constructed as disembodied — it "lost its body."** The defining belief of the present cultural moment is that information can circulate unchanged across material substrates; this required erasing embodiment.
  - Reasoning structure: historical-genealogical; traces the belief from Wiener through Moravec/Minsky/Star Trek and shows it is a *construction*, not a fact.
  - Evidence: Moravec's download fantasy; Wiener "telegraph a human being"; molecular biology treating information as essential code.
  - Source passage: > "In fact, adefining characteristic ofthe present cultural moment is the belief that information can circulate unchanged among different material substrates."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Information lost its body — it was constructed as disembodied pattern`.

- **Claim: Both the liberal humanist subject and the cybernetic posthuman erase embodiment.** The posthuman, far from simply overthrowing the liberal subject, continues its privileging of mind/cognition over body.
  - Reasoning structure: continuity-amid-rupture; uses the anoretic (Gillian Brown) and Gibson's "data made flesh" as illustrations.
  - Evidence: liberal subject "possessed a body but was not usually represented as being a body"; anorexia as extreme self-possession; Neuromancer's "data made flesh."
  - Source passage: > "To the extent that the posthuman constructs embodiment as the instantiation of thought/information, it continues the liberal tradition rather than disrupts it."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The posthuman shares the liberal subject's erasure of embodiment`.

- **Claim: The information/materiality separation is an imaginary act that must be contested.** Hayles contests not information theory itself but the hierarchy that subordinates materiality to information.
  - Reasoning structure: deconstructive; "Platonic backhand and forehand" both reify abstraction as the Real; invokes Derrida's supplementarity.
  - Evidence: Shannon's equations on a printed page; Genome Project maps; CRT screens — all require a medium.
  - Source passage: > "It is this materiality/ information separation that I want to contest—not the cellular automata model, information theory, or a host of related theories in themselves."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Information always requires material instantiation`.

- **Claim: Cybernetics developed in three waves — homeostasis, reflexivity, virtuality.** A periodization tracking the interplay between embodiment and arguments for disembodiment.
  - Reasoning structure: historical schema; each wave defined by a central concept; transitions mediated by skeuomorphs.
  - Evidence: Macy Conferences (homeostasis); von Foerster/Maturana/Varela (reflexivity/autopoiesis); artificial life/cellular automata (virtuality/emergence).
  - Source passage: > "The first, from 1945 to 1960, took homeostasis as a central concept; the second, going roughly from 1960 to 1980, revolved around reflexivity; and the third, stretching from 1980 to the present, highlights virtuality."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Cybernetics developed in three waves — homeostasis, reflexivity, virtuality`.

- **Claim: The disembodiment of information was not inevitable.** It was the contingent outcome of historically specific negotiations (e.g., Shannon's pattern-definition winning over MacKay's meaning-definition for reasons of quantifiability and generality), not technological determinism.
  - Reasoning structure: anti-teleological; narrative used as a method to re-embody history; Shannon-vs-MacKay as the exemplary contingent fork.
  - Evidence: MacKay's meaning-based definition required unmeasurable psychological states; Shannon's won by "reliable quantification and theoretical generality"; Shannon himself cautioned against over-extension.
  - Source passage: > "Though overdetermined, the disembodiment ofinformation was not inevitable, any more than it is inevitable we continue to accept the idea that we are essentially informational patterns."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The disembodiment of information was contingent, not inevitable`.

- **Claim: Narrative is a more embodied form of discourse than systems theory, and so a resource against the teleology of disembodiment.**
  - Reasoning structure: methodological; literature and science read together to "entangle abstract form and material particularity."
  - Evidence: chronological/located/personified properties of narrative; Lyotard's incredulity toward metanarrative repurposed against the disembodiment metanarrative.
  - Source passage: > "With its chronological thrust, polymorphous digressions, located actions, and personified agents, narrative is a more embodied form of discourse than is analytically driven systems theory."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Narrative is a more embodied discourse than systems theory`.

- **Claim: "We became posthuman" is multiply ironic; human and posthuman coexist.** The transformations were never clean breaks; they "reinscribed traditional ideas and assumptions even as they articulated something new."
  - Reasoning structure: titular gloss; the past tense and plural are both ironic.
  - Source passage: > "Rather, 'human' and 'posthuman' coexist in shifting configurations that vary with historically specific contexts."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Human and posthuman coexist in shifting configurations`.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Information vs. materiality (pattern vs. presence)** — the master duality the book exists to contest.
  - > "Central to this argument is a conceptualization that sees information and materiality as distinct entities."
  - Poles: information / materiality; pattern / presence; immaterial / material. Link `[[Material vs Immaterial]]`, `[[Embodiment vs Information]]`.
- **Embodiment vs. disembodiment** — the recovered term against the dominant teleology.
  - > "I view the present moment as a critical juncture when interventions might be made to keep disembodiment from being rewritten, once again, into prevailing concepts of subjectivity."
- **Human vs. posthuman** — coexisting, not sequential; the past tense is ironic.
- **Liberal humanist subject vs. posthuman subject** — discontinuity (distributed cognition, owned parts, no a priori self-will) yet shared erasure of embodiment.
  - > "the posthuman's collective heterogeneous quality implies a distributed cognition located in disparate parts"
- **Homeostasis vs. reflexivity vs. autopoiesis/virtuality** — the three-wave succession; earlier concepts persist as skeuomorphs.
- **Observer inside vs. outside the system** — first- vs. second-order cybernetics; reflexivity dissolves objectivism (Kubie's double-encoding threat).
- **Shannon (pattern, no meaning) vs. MacKay (information as meaning/change in receiver)** — the contingent fork that decided what "information" would mean.
  - > "Donald MacKay, a British researcher, argued for an alternative definition that linked information with change in a receiver's mindset and thus with meaning."
- **Pleasure vs. terror of the posthuman** — Hayles's affective ambivalence ("evokes terror and excites pleasure"); her "nightmare" vs. her "dream" version of the posthuman.
- **Technological determinism vs. historical contingency** — narrative deployed to replace teleology with contingent stories.
- **Wiener's intent (extend liberal humanism) vs. cybernetics' inexorable logic (undermine the liberal subject)** — an internal tension within the founding moment.
  - > "For Wiencr, cybernetics was a means to extend liberal humanism, not subvert it."
