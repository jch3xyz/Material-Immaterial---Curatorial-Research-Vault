## Executive Summary

This chapter diagnoses a shift from the eighteenth-century theatrum mundi — a public sphere structured by ritualized, theatrical distance — to a contemporary "society of intimacy" in which psychological interiority is exhibited rather than represented. Drawing heavily on Richard Sennett's *The Fall of Public Man*, Han argues that intimacy is "the psychological formula of transparency": by laying the soul bare, subjects believe they achieve authenticity, but in doing so they dismantle the public sphere, mask, ritual, and play. Digital media (social media, personalized search) intensify this by creating a "space of absolute closeness" with no outside, no negativity, no Other. Politics is psychologized, the public sphere becomes an exhibition space, and the subject — narcissistically deprived of self-distance — drowns in borderless self-intimacy and depression. The chapter functions as Han's account of the affective/psychological substrate of the broader transparency regime: distance, mask, ritual, and narrative are eroded together.

## Arguments

- **Claim**: Intimacy is not opposed to transparency but is its psychological-affective form.
  - Reasoning structure: Han identifies the cultural ideology that authenticity = self-revelation, then names this ideology as the subjective correlate of the transparency regime.
  - Evidence: The chapter's central naming sentence; supported by the analysis of ritual, mask, and distance erosion.
  - Source passage: > "Intimacy is the psychological formula of transparency. One believes that one attains transparency of the soul by revealing intimate feelings and emotions, by laying the soul bare."
  - Confidence: high (directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Intimacy as the psychological formula of transparency.md`

- **Claim**: Modernity replaces theatrical representation with pornographic exhibition, transforming the public sphere from theater into market.
  - Reasoning structure: Historical contrast (theatrum mundi vs. market); typology of two modes of display (represent vs. exhibit).
  - Evidence: Opening paragraph; Sennett citation.
  - Source passage: > "The world today is no theater where actions and feelings are represented and interpreted, but a market on which intimacies are exhibited, sold, and consumed. The theater is a site of representation, whereas the market is a site of exhibition. Today theatrical representation is yielding to pornographic exhibition."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Theater to market — representation to exhibition.md`

- **Claim**: Social media and personalized search constitute a closed intimate sphere with no outside, no Other, no negativity — and therefore no possibility of change.
  - Reasoning structure: Diagnostic; from algorithmic personalization to political-ontological consequence (loss of the public sphere, loss of negativity).
  - Evidence: Direct claim about personalization eliminating the outside.
  - Source passage: > "Social media and personalized search engines set up, in the internet, a space of absolute closeness [Nahraum]; here the outside has been eliminated. One encounters only oneself and one's own life. No negativity stands available to make change possible."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Digital closeness eliminates negativity.md`

- **Claim**: The tyranny of intimacy psychologizes politics — politicians are measured by persona rather than action.
  - Reasoning structure: Causal: void left by lost public sphere is filled by intimate display.
  - Evidence: Direct claim plus the staging/persona analysis.
  - Source passage: > "The tyranny of intimacy psychologizes and personalizes everything. Even politics cannot escape its grasp. Accordingly, politicians are no longer measured by their actions. Instead, general interest concerns their persons; this entails compulsive staging on their part."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Intimacy psychologizes politics.md`

- **Claim**: Sociability requires distance; intimacy destroys distance and therefore sociability.

  - Evidence: Han's final paragraph synthesizing Sennett's narcissism analysis.

## Metaphors

- **The world as theater (theatrum mundi)** — historical metaphor for the eighteenth-century public sphere, now lost.
  - > "The world of the eighteenth century was a theatrum mundi. The public sphere resembled a stage."
- **The world as market** — replacement figure; intimacies as goods exhibited, sold, consumed.
  - > "a market on which intimacies are exhibited, sold, and consumed."
- **The stage vs. the market** — paired metaphor governing the chapter.
- **The pornographic** — figure for distanceless, exhibitionist display; not literal pornography but a mode.
  - > "the pornographic lack of distance."
- **Laying bare / denuding / nakedness** — body metaphor for transparency.
  - > "by laying the soul bare." / "render it naked."
- **The mask (persona / per-sonare)** — voice acquires form by passing through a mask; the transparency society strips this off.
- **Comfort zone** — the internet as an intimate comfort zone, distance eliminated.
  - > "The internet transforms into an intimate sphere or comfort zone."
- **Drowning in self-intimacy** — figure for narcissistic collapse.
  - > "the narcissist drowns in his borderless self-intimacy."
- **Hazy borders** — for the dissolved boundaries of the narcissistic self.
- **Exhibition space (vs. communal action)** — the public sphere as exhibition hall.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Theater vs. Market** — representation vs. exhibition; the chapter's structuring opposition.
  - > "The theater is a site of representation, whereas the market is a site of exhibition."
- **Distance vs. Intimacy** — sociability requires distance; intimacy destroys it.
  - > "human beings become sociable when they preserve distance from one another. Intimacy, in contrast, destroys distance."
- **Mask vs. Nakedness / Persona vs. Exposure** — symbolic appearance vs. denuding.
- **Ritual vs. Psychology** — objective rule-bound expressive forms vs. subjective psychic states.
  - > "Objective rules, not subjective psychic states, determine play and ritual."
- **Erfahrung vs. Erlebnis** — Other-facing experience vs. self-encountering lived experience.
- **Public Sphere vs. Intimate Sphere / Comfort Zone** — Öffentlichkeit vs. Nahraum.
- **Negativity vs. Positivity** — the absence of negativity in the intimate digital sphere; (this term likely runs across the whole book — flag for cross-chapter handling).
- **Representation vs. Exhibition** — paired throughout Han's transparency diagnosis.
- **Self vs. Other** — narcissism eliminates the Other; intimacy is encounter only with oneself.
