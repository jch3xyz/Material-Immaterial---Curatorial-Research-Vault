## Executive Summary

This is the closing chapter (pp. 77–79) of *Psychopolitics*, and it functions as the constructive, emancipatory turn that the whole book has been building toward. Where the preceding chapters diagnose neoliberal psychopolitics — smart/friendly power that colonizes the psyche, gamifies labor, mines emotion, and renders the soul transparent and predictable through Big Data — this final chapter sketches the figure of resistance. Han names that figure obliquely: a mode of existence "beyond the subject."

The argument moves in three steps, all routed through Nietzsche and Foucault. First, Han recovers the **EVENT** (*Ereignis*) — drawing on Nietzsche's "naturification" (*Vernatürlichung*) and Foucault's eventful conception of history — as that which is "absolutely sudden and thwarting," incalculable and unpredictable, which "breaks the subject open and wrests it from subjection." The event is precisely what psychopolitics, with its predictive Big Data positivism, seeks to abolish; its incalculability is its emancipatory force. Second, Han re-mobilizes his recurring distinction between **EXPERIENCE (*Erfahrung*) and EXPERIENCING (*Erlebnis*)**: *Erfahrung*, founded on discontinuity and transformation, "wrench[es] the subject from itself," whereas the neoliberal psychopolitics of *Erlebnis*/emotion "only ensnares the subject deeper and deeper in the state of subjection." Han etymologizes the subject as the *subjectum* — the one "cast under," subjected by a higher instance — so that liberation means tearing the subject out of subjection. Third, Han offers his positive program: the Foucauldian **ART OF LIVING** as a "practice of freedom," which unfolds as **DE-PSYCHOLOGIZATION** — "the art of killing psychology," the creation of "unnamed indi­vidualities, beings, relations, qualities." Because neoliberal psychopolitics is a technology of domination operating through psychological programming and steering, the praxis of freedom must disarm it by de-psychologizing and "de-voiding" (*ent-leeren*) the subject, opening onto "a mode of existence that still has no name: an unwritten future."

The chapter is the conceptual hinge between Han's critique of psychopolitics and his elsewhere-developed figure of the IDIOT/IDIOTISM (not named in this chapter, but clearly its destination). It is short, citationally dense (Nietzsche, Foucault on event/history, Foucault on experience via Blanchot and Bataille, Foucault on the art of living), and it inverts every term the book has used to describe domination: calculability becomes the event's incalculability; the colonized psyche becomes the de-psychologized self; subjection becomes the wrenching-free of the subject.

## Arguments

- **The event's incalculability is what wrests the subject from subjection.**
  - Reasoning structure: Psychopolitics (esp. its Big Data / dataist arm, developed in earlier chapters) rules through calculation and prediction. The event is by definition that which "defies all calculation and prediction." Therefore the event marks the limit and the outside of psychopolitical power; it is the structural site of emancipation because it cannot be pre-empted.
  - Evidence: Nietzsche's "naturification" and "readiness for what is absolutely sudden and thwarting"; Foucault's account of the event as reversal/usurpation after which "one suddenly speaks another language."
  - Source passage: > The event brings into play an outside, which breaks the subject open and wrests it from subjection. Events repre^ sent breaks and discontinuities; they open up new spaces for action.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The event is incalculable and breaks the subject open.md`

- **Erfahrung liberates where Erlebnis (emotion) subjugates.**
  - Reasoning structure: Experience (*Erfahrung*), founded on discontinuity, transforms and dissolves the subject (frees it from itself); the neoliberal psychopolitics of *Erlebnis*/emotion (the exploitable affect of earlier chapters) does the opposite, ensnaring the subject ever deeper in subjection. The same affective faculty that psychopolitics exploits is, in its negative/transformative form, the medium of liberation.
  - Evidence: Foucault on experience (via Nietzsche, Blanchot, Bataille) as bringing the subject "to its annihilation or its dissolution"; the explicit opposition to "the neoliberal psychopolitics of experiencing or emotion."
  - Source passage: > Experience tears the subject out from subjection - out of its downcast state. It signifies the opposite of the neoliberal psychopolitics of experiencing or emotion, which only ensnares the subject deeper and deeper in the state of subjection and subjugation.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Erfahrung frees the subject while Erlebnis deepens subjection.md`

- **The praxis of freedom against psychopolitics is de-psychologization.**
  - Reasoning structure: Neoliberal psychopolitics is "a technology of domination that stabilizes and perpetuates the prevailing system by means of psychological programming and steering" (note the structural echo of the *Transparency Society* thesis that "compulsive transparency stabilizes the existing system"). Because it operates *through* the psyche, the way to disarm it is to remove its object — to de-psychologize, to "de-void" (*ent-leeren*) the subject. De-psychologization is thus not therapy or self-optimization (which the book elsewhere reads as "healing as killing") but the killing of psychology as such.
  - Evidence: Foucault's "art of killing psychology"; Han's claim that de-psychologization "serves to disarm psychopolitics, which is a means of effecting submission."
  - Source passage: > Neoliberal psychopolitics is a technology of domination that stabilizes and perpetuates the prevailing system by means of psychological programming and steering. Accordingly, the art of living, as the praxis of freedom, must proceed by way of de-psychologization.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/De-psychologization is the praxis of freedom that disarms psychopolitics.md`

- **The de-voided subject opens onto a nameless, unwritten future.**
  - Reasoning structure: If the subject is constitutively subjection, and psychopolitics works by filling/steering the psyche, then emptying the subject (de-psychologizing, *ent-leeren*) does not produce a freer subject but something past the subject-form altogether — a mode of existence "that still has no name." Resistance here is figured not as a new identity but as the suspension of identity/nameability.
  - Evidence: the etymology of subject-as-subjected; the closing image of the de-voided self opening onto "an unwritten future."
  - Source passage: > When the subject is de-psychologized - indeed, de-voided (ent-leert) - it opens onto a mode of existence that still has no name: an unwritten future.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The de-voided subject opens onto an unwritten future.md`

- **Calculability vs the Event (Prediction vs Rupture)** — the core opposition of the chapter: psychopolitical prediction/calculation (Big Data positivism) against the event that "defies all calculation and prediction." This is a fresh, vault-worthy tension. Candidate `tensions/Calculability vs Event.md` (or `Prediction vs Event`).
  - Source passage: > It defies all calculation and prediction. When it occurs, an entirely new state of affairs begins.

- **Subjection vs the Event/Freedom (Subject vs Beyond-the-Subject)** — being a subject = being subjected; freedom = being wrenched out of the subject. The tension is internal to the very concept of the subject. Relates to the existing O'Gieblyn tension `Subject vs Object`, but is distinct (here the axis is subjection/liberation, not subject/object ontology). Candidate `tensions/Subjection vs Freedom.md`; see matches_existing.

- **Erfahrung vs Erlebnis (transformation vs emotional capture)** — recapitulated here as liberation vs subjugation; maps onto the existing definition note and onto `Positivity vs Negativity` (Erfahrung carries negativity/discontinuity; Erlebnis is positive, self-identical, consequence-free). Update the existing definition rather than creating a new tension.

- **Latent tension: de-psychologization vs psychological "healing"** — the chapter implies (without naming it here) the book's "healing as killing" critique: the de-psychologized self is the opposite of the therapeutically optimized self. An unresolved question the chapter leaves open is whether "killing psychology" is itself achievable from within a psychopoliticized subject, or only by the event that arrives from "an outside."
