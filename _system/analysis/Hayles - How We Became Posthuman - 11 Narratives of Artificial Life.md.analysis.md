## Executive Summary

This is Hayles's chapter on the **third wave of cybernetics** — Artificial Life (AL) — and it functions as the culminating case study for the book's master thesis: that "information lost its body," and that the construction of *life* itself is now being routed through a disembodied, form-over-matter assumption inherited from cybernetics and, ultimately, from Platonism. Hayles maps the third wave onto a figure (the **spiral**, vs. the second wave's autopoietic **circle**) and traces its lineage through Francisco Varela, who bridges the second (autopoiesis/reflexivity) and third (emergence/evolution) waves.

The analytic core is a *narratological* reading of science: Hayles argues that AL's "strong claim" — that computer codes are not merely simulating life but *are alive* — is not established by the programs themselves but is *constructed through stories told about and through them*. She dissects three narrative levels around Thomas Ray's **Tierra** program: (1) the first-level anthropomorphic naming of code-creatures ("ancestor," "parasite," "hyperparasite," "mother cell"); (2) the rhetorical positioning of AL as legitimate theoretical biology (Langton's "life-as-it-could-be"); and (3) the speculative third-level narrative about humanity's relation to its "silicon cousins" — where the **observer** becomes explicitly implicated and where, in the AL paradigm, "the machine becomes the model for understanding the human. Thus the human is transfigured into the posthuman."

Crucially, Hayles does *not* simply indict AL. She splits it. She identifies a **disembodied camp** (Langton, Fredkin, Moravec, Minsky — form separated from matter, consciousness downloadable, the "computational universe" where "information is king") and an **embodied countercurrent** (Varela, Rodney Brooks's "subsumption architecture" and situated robots, Mark Tilden's nervous-net robots, the evolutionary psychologists Barkow/Cosmides/Tooby, and Antonio Damasio's *Descartes' Error*). The chapter ends on the book's signature normative move: posthumanity "is already here"; the open question is "what kind of posthumans we will be" — and a defense of embodiment: "bodies can never be made of information alone, no matter which side of the computer screen they are on."

For vault purposes, this chapter is a *primary realizer* of several committed stubs: **Artificial Life**, **the observer**, **emergence/feedback loops**, **the three waves (homeostasis → reflexivity → autopoiesis → emergence)**, **Embodiment vs Information**, **Posthumanism**, **the liberal humanist subject**, and the **form/matter (Platonic) dichotomy** underlying the construction of "information."

## Definitions

- **Autonomy (Varela & Bourgine's reconception)** — the living's capacity to assert existence and "bring forth a world that is significant and pertinent," now allowing the system's organization to transform itself through emergent behavior.
  - > "“Autonomy in this context refers to [the living’s] basic and fundamental capacity to be, to assert their existence and bring forth a world that is significant and pertinent without being pre-digested in advance...”"

- **Artificial Life (Langton's definition)** — the study/synthesis of life-like behaviors in man-made media.
  - > "“Artificial Life is the study of man-made systems that exhibit behaviors characteristic of natural living systems... attempting to synthesize life-like behaviors within computers and other artificial media.”"

- **The three research fronts of AL** — wetware (artificial biological life), hardware (robots/embodied forms), software (computer programs instantiating emergent/evolutionary processes).
  - > "Wetware is the attempt to create artificial biological life... Hardware is the construction of robots and other embodied life-forms. Software is the creation of computer programs instantiating emergent or evolutionary processes."

- **Emergence** — see Key Concepts.
  - > "Emergence implies that properties or programs appear on their own, often developing in ways not anticipated by the person who created the simulation."

- **First-order vs second-order emergence (Steels)** — first-order = any property generated by component interactions; second-order = emergent properties that bestow additional functionality, especially the ability to process information ("the capacity to evolve").
  - > "Among all such emergent properties, second-order emergence grants special privilege to those that bestow additional functionality on the system, particularly the ability to process information."

- **Address by template (Tierra's virtual computer)** — matching one code segment to another by its binary inverse, modeled on DNA base-pairing; the containment mechanism.
  - > "address by template matches one code segment to another by looking for its binary inverse... the virtual computer searches nearby memory to find a matching segment with the code 0110."

- **The genotype = phenotype collapse in AL** — the "creatures" do not express codes; they *are* codes.
  - > "Rather, the “creatures” are their codes. For them, genotype and phenotype amount to the same thing; the organism is the code, and the code is the organism."

- **The computational universe** — see Key Concepts; everything (humans, machines, cosmos) is fundamentally information processing.
  - > "Indeed, the essential function of the universe as a whole is processing information."

  - > "the figure most apt to describe the third wave is a spiral... like a spring compressed and suddenly released, the processes break out of the pattern of circular self-organization and leap outward into the new."

- **The "soup" / primeval soup** — Tierra's allocated memory block, analogy to the origin of life on Earth.
  - > "a block of memory that Ray calls the “soup,” an analogy with the primeval soup at the beginning of life on Earth."

- **The "reaper" and the "slicer"** — Tierra's death/time-allocation programs (figured as agents of mortality and fate); the death's-head image in the video.
  - > "Ray introduced a program that he calls the “reaper.” The “reaper” monitors the population and eliminates the oldest creatures and those who are “defective”..."

- **Parasite / hyperparasite / ancestor / mother & daughter cell** — biomorphic naming that converts binary operations into "the high drama of a Darwinian struggle for survival and reproduction" / an "epic."
  - > "the narratives alter the binary operations that, on a physical level, amount to changing electronic polarities... transforming the binary operations into the high drama of a Darwinian struggle for survival and reproduction."

- **The disembodied flying POV (and the Genesis script)** — the Santa Fe video makes the viewer "a disembodied pov flying over the lifeworld of the 'creatures'"; the narrative "follows the script of Genesis."
