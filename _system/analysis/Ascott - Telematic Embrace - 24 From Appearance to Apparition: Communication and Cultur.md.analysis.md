## Executive Summary

This is Ascott's own essay (a primary text, NOT Shanken's editorial introduction), originally delivered at FISEA: Fourth International Symposium on Electronic Arts (Minneapolis, 1993). It is the title-essay for the book's central conceit and one of the cleanest statements of Ascott's mid-career thesis: that Western art's millennial preoccupation with **appearance** (surface, representation, the look of things, the retinal/optical gaze, ready-made reality) is dying, displaced by an art of **apparition** (emergence, becoming, transformation, the construction of reality). The essay opens with Schrödinger's Cat and Greg Egan's novel *Quarantine* to frame quantum-measurement / observer-created reality as the deep model for an art in which the observer constructs meaning. Ascott argues that in the "cybersphere" / cyberspace the artwork is no longer a finished object but a context — an open-ended, cybernetic, interactive **system** "structurally coupled" to the behaviour of its user. He aligns this with radical constructivism (Watzlawick, Heinz von Foerster), names connectivity / interaction / emergence / transformation as the new "watchwords," predicts the interface moving "closer and closer to the body and into the brain" (the **post-biological body as interface**), and locates the sole pre-telematic ancestor of this networked sensibility in Jackson Pollock. The essay is polemical (museums, galleries, critics, "cultural theory" presiding over the death of appearance-art) and political (the "democratisation of meaning" = shared ownership of reality). Its title antithesis, Appearance vs Apparition, is the durable durable contribution; it also strongly advances the Connectivity/Interaction/Emergence/Transformation cluster, network/structural-coupling cybernetics, the artist-as-context-designer, and the post-biological body.

This chapter cross-links powerfully to the just-ingested **Burnham** (the object→system thesis; Ascott's "from object to process" is the participatory/behavioural pole of 1960s cybernetic art beside Burnham's systems esthetics of the object), to **McLuhan** (the extended sensorium / global field of consciousness), to **Hayles/Haraway** (the dissolving natural/artificial boundary, the post-biological body), and to **O'Gieblyn** (consciousness, observer-created reality, re-enchantment / Taoist flux).

  - Source passage: > "Apparition implies action, just as appearance implies inertia. Apparition is about the coming into being of a new identity, which is often, at first, unexpected, surprising, disturbing. If appearance is claimed as the face of reality, of things as they are, apparition is the emergence of things as they could be."
  - Source passage: > "there is a radical shift in our perceived relationship with reality, where the emphasis has moved from appearance to apparition; that is, from the outward and visible look of things to the inward and emergent processes of becoming."

  - Source passage: > "Cyberspace is the space of apparition, in which the virtual and real not only co-exist, but co-evolve in a cultural complexity."

## Definitions

Terms explicitly defined or strongly clarified in this chapter:

- **Apparition** — "the coming into being of a new identity… the emergence of things as they could be"; opposed to appearance (the look of things, things as they are).
- **Appearance** — the surface look of things, representation, "ready-made reality"; "inertia"; the millennial concern of Western art.
- **Eigenstate** — "the quantum jump producing what is known as the eigenstate" (the either/or outcome after wave-function collapse). Borrowed physics term, not Ascott's coinage; supports `Observer-Created Reality` but probably plain text.
- **Structural coupling** — interactive art "constitutes a structural coupling between everyone and everything within the Net" (Maturana/Varela's term applied to networked art). Candidate definition note or cross-link to `Autopoiesis`/`Cybernetics`.
- **The post-biological body as interface** — the body whose interface to computer systems is set within the brain via biocontrollers, erasing the natural/artificial boundary.
- **Radical constructivism** — (via Watzlawick / von Foerster) the position that "the environment, as we perceive it, is our invention"; "as incompatible with traditional thinking as interactive art is with traditional art."

## Arguments

- **Claim: Western art's preoccupation with appearance is dying because it is irrelevant to a systems-based culture concerned with apparition, emergence, and transformation.**
  - Reasoning: Appearance-art served church/state ideologies and classical (clockwork-determinist) science, seeing man as a material object; a culture now concerned with complexity, systems, the invisible and immaterial finds no value in representation.
  - Evidence: the polemic against boutiques/galleries/museums/art magazines; "international art… is dying."
  - Source passage: > "And it is dying, because it is no longer relevant to a culture that is progressively concerned with the complexity of relationships and subtlety of systems, with the invisible and immaterial, the evolutive and the evanescent, in short, with apparition."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The art of appearance is dying because culture has shifted to apparition`

- **Claim: Representation in art is fraudulent; the processes of apparition are the authentic relation to reality.**
  - Reasoning: our insight into how reality is constructed in consciousness shows representation cannot disclose the material or spiritual state of things; "the retinal gaze can penetrate very little."
  - Evidence: "The mirror always lies"; "The surface of the world hides more than it discloses."
  - Source passage: > "the processes of apparition are authentic and that appearance is a fraud. Representation in art was always essentially mendacious, illusory, and counterfeit. The mirror always lies."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Representation is a fraud and apparition is the authentic relation to reality`

- **Claim: In the cyberculture, to construct art is to construct reality.**
  - Reasoning: human consciousness is the instrument for creating reality (radical constructivism); interactive art puts the observer at the centre constructing meaning; therefore making art = making reality, a shared/political act.
  - Evidence: the Schrödinger/Egan/von Foerster framing; the democratisation-of-meaning passage; closing line.
  - Source passage: > "In the cyberculture, to construct art is to construct reality, the networks of cyberspace underpinning our desire to amplify human cooperation and interaction in the constructive process."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/To construct art is to construct reality`

- **Claim: The artist's new role is to design context, not to create or transmit content — and the observer becomes the centre of the creative process.**
  - Reasoning: if reality/meaning is constructed by the observer, the artwork must be a context for that construction; "Art is no longer a window onto the world but a doorway through which the observer is invited to enter."
  - Source passage: > "Art is no longer a window onto the world but a doorway through which the observer is invited to enter into a world of interaction and transformation."
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The artist designs context not content and the observer constructs the work`

- **Claim: Telematic art is conceptually driven, not technologically led — its only pre-telematic ancestor is Jackson Pollock.**
  - Reasoning: networks are "a matter of attitude before… machines"; Pollock first made art as action/arena, brought the picture off the wall onto the Earth, modelled connectivity/networked imaging (provenance: Navajo / Native American art); the link is one of sensibility/attitude, not style/form.
  - Evidence: the extended Pollock passage; "If there is any link whatsoever between the art of cyberculture and the art of the pre-telematic era, it lies in the painting of Pollock."
  - Source passage: > "working with networks is a matter of attitude before it is anything to do with machines. Telematic art is conceptually driven, not technologically led."
  - Source passage: > "It was Pollock who first brought the tight-framed picture window of painting off the gallery wall and onto the surface of the Earth, marking out an arena for action and interactivity…"
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Telematic art is conceptually driven not technologically led` (Pollock as the sole ancestor folded in, or a distinct `arguments/Pollock is the only pre-telematic ancestor of networked art`)

- **Claim: The systems interface is moving into the body and brain, dissolving the natural/artificial boundary — the post-biological body as interface.**
  - Reasoning: keyboard/mouse/HMD/data-glove/data-suit are being consigned to the museum; artists want creative control of cyberspace through biocontrollers in their own nervous systems; but the conceptual shift (object→process) matters more than the neural-interface hardware.
  - Source passage: > "our technologies of perception, cognition, and communication… are moving closer and closer to the body and into the brain."
  - Confidence: high (the body-into-brain claim); medium on the relative priority claim.
  - Recommended wiki note: feeds `concepts/The Post-Biological Body`; cross-link to argument cluster around `The Body as Data` / `Embodiment`.

- **Claim: The collapse/confusion of late-20th-c. art (New York School's fall, neo-expressionism, postmodern solipsism, return to academicism) is evidence of a major paradigm shift away from appearance.**
  - Reasoning: Kuhnian paradigm-shift framing; the institutional consequences (galleries redundant, museums/academies to be rethought).
  - Source passage: > "the whole miserable confusion, demoralisation, and splintering of art at the fag end of this century is evidence of the major paradigm shift that we are undergoing."
  - Confidence: medium (rhetorical/polemical)
  - Recommended wiki note: optional; fold into the appearance-dying argument.

## Metaphors

- **Schrödinger's Cat** — the both-dead-and-alive boxed cat as the model of observer-created reality / the both/and→either/or quantum jump. Central organizing image of the essay. Candidate metaphor/symbol note: `Schrödinger's Cat (Observer-Created Reality)`.
  - Source passage: > "according to quantum theory, the cat is both dead and alive until we observe or measure it, at which point the wave function collapses…"
- **The mirror always lies** — representation as a lying mirror; appearance as counterfeit.
  - Source passage: > "The mirror always lies."
- **Window vs Doorway** — appearance-art as a window onto the world; apparition/interactive art as a doorway the observer enters. Strong candidate metaphor/tension figure.
  - Source passage: > "Art is no longer a window onto the world but a doorway through which the observer is invited to enter…"
- **Dark fibre / wings of light / the Holy Grail** — the Erbium Doped Amplifier sending "an infinity of messages through glass on wings of light," the "communications engineer's Holy Grail" (Gilder quote). The material infrastructure of connectivity.
  - Source passage: > "send an infinity of messages through glass on wings of light, is the communications engineer's Holy Grail"
- **The evolutionary spiral / Taoist flux** — return to "a more Taoist desire for flux and flow, for change and transformation"; no eternal verities for "manuscripts or monuments."
  - Source passage: > "We are on an evolutionary spiral that has returned us to a more Taoist desire for flux and flow…"
- **Critics as vultures at the periphery of the old order** — the polemic against "cultural theory" / critics who "wag endlessly their disapproving and despairing fingers," "hovering like vultures at the periphery of the old order of art."
- **Cyberspace as a matrix of human values / psychic charge** — "Cyberspace cannot remain innocent. It is a matrix of human values. It carries a psychic charge." (Anticipates technoetics / the spiritual.)

  - Source passage: > "Apparition implies action, just as appearance implies inertia."

  - Source passage: > "Can representation co-exist with constructivism?"
- **Window vs Doorway** — see metaphors; the spectator outside-looking-in vs inside-the-process.
- **Natural vs Artificial** — the boundary Ascott wants made "redundant technologically… conceptually and spiritually"; the post-biological body. Maps to Haraway's breached boundaries.
- **Visible/Retinal vs Invisible/Immaterial** — appearance-art = the retinal gaze, surface; cyberculture = the invisible, the forces/fields/relationships science addresses, the immaterial/spiritual. Maps onto `Material vs Immaterial`.
  - Source passage: > "with the invisible and immaterial, the evolutive and the evanescent, in short, with apparition."
