## Executive Summary

This short, pivotal chapter is Han's genealogical setup for the book's central thesis: the passage from **biopolitics** to **psychopolitics**. Following Foucault, Han narrates a periodization of power — from the **sovereign power** of the sword (the "privilege to seize hold of life in order to suppress it," a power to *deal death*) to **disciplinary power**, a power *over life* that "invests life through and through" via the "administration of bodies" and the "calculated management of life." He ties this transition to a change in production: the shift from agrarian to industrial economy required that the body be disciplined and "calibrated to be a production-unit," fashioned from "shapeless dough" into a "machine." Disciplinary power is **normative power** — it subjects the subject to norms, commandments and prohibitions and works through the *negativity* of training/drilling (Abrichtung); in this it shares the negativity of sovereign levying/conscription (Abschöpfung), and both pursue **allo-exploitation**, producing the **obedience-subject**.

Han then marks the decisive limit. Disciplinary technology reaches into the mental sphere ("industry" = "hard work"; "industrial school" = "reform school"; Bentham's panopticon promising "moral improvement"), but it is "too crude to penetrate into the deeper layers of the soul." Bentham's "Big Brother only observes inmates from the outside"; the panopticon is "bound to the optical medium" and "has no access to inner thoughts or needs." **Biopolitics** is then defined as "the governmental technology of disciplinary power" — devoted to the **population** as "a productive and reproductive mass," regulating "reproductive cycles, birth and death rates, levels of general health, and life expectancy." But precisely because biopolitics works through *population statistics*, it "has no access to the psychic realm": "Demography is not the same thing as psych-ography." The chapter ends by opening the door to the rest of the book: **statistics and Big Data lie worlds apart** — Big Data can establish "not just an individual but a collective psychogram — perhaps even the psychogram of the unconscious itself," and so "may yet shine a light into the depths of the psyche and exploit the unconscious entirely." Biopolitics governs the body; the new regime (psychopolitics, via Big Data) will colonize the psyche.

- **Big Data** — introduced at the chapter's close as the technology that *can* reach the psyche, where statistics/demography cannot.
- **The panopticon** — Bentham's disciplinary architecture, here characterized strictly as optical and exterior.

## Arguments

- **Claim:** Modern power shifted from a power to *deal death* (sovereignty) to a power *over life* (discipline/biopolitics).
  - Reasoning structure: historical-genealogical (after Foucault); the sword/negativity of death yields to the administration/investment of life.
  - Evidence: Foucault, *History of Sexuality* Vol. 1.
  - Source passage: > "Hereby, the 'old power of death' yields to the careful 'administration of bodies' and 'the calculated management of life'."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Power shifted from dealing death to administering life.md`

- **Claim:** The shift from sovereign to disciplinary power was driven by a change in the mode of production — from agrarian to industrial.
  - Reasoning: industrialization required that bodies be disciplined and fitted to machine production; discipline is a productive (economic) technology, not merely a juridical one.
  - Source passage: > "The transition from the power of sovereignty to disciplinary power followed from changes in forms of production, specifically the shift from agrarian to industrial production."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Discipline fits the body to industrial production.md`

- **Claim:** Disciplinary power is normative/negative power — it works through prohibition, norm, and the drilling-out of deviation.
  - Reasoning: discipline subjects the subject to rules and eliminates anomalies; its negativity (Abrichtung) aligns it with sovereign negativity (Abschöpfung).
  - Source passage: > "Such negativity of training and drills (Abrichtung) is constitutive of disciplinary power."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Disciplinary power is normative power that works through negativity.md`

- **Claim:** Disciplinary power cannot reach the psyche; it is "too crude" and remains external/optical.
  - Reasoning: even where discipline touches the mental sphere (moral reform, the "industrial/reform school," Bentham's moral-improvement claim), its "orthopaedic technology" cannot penetrate "the deeper layers of the soul"; Bentham's panopticon "only observes inmates from the outside" and "has no access to inner thoughts or needs."
  - Source passage: > "The orthopaedic technology of disciplinary power is too crude to penetrate into the deeper layers of the soul - with its hidden wishes, needs and desires - and take it over."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Disciplinary power cannot penetrate the psyche.md`

- **Claim:** Biopolitics is unsuited to the neoliberal regime because biopolitics governs the body/population while neoliberalism exploits the psyche.
  - Reasoning: biopolitics works through population statistics/demography, which "has no access to the psychic realm" and "can deliver no material for drawing up a psychogram of the population."
  - Source passage: > "this approach proves altogether unsuited to the neoliberal regime, which exploits the psyche above all. Biopolitics, which makes use of population statistics, has no access to the psychic realm."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated) — this is the chapter's load-bearing argument and the book's hinge
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Biopolitics governs the body but cannot govern the psyche.md`

- **Claim:** Statistics and Big Data are categorically different; Big Data can produce a psychogram (even of the unconscious) and so reach the psyche.
  - Reasoning: demography yields population-biology data; Big Data yields a map of the psyche — individual and collective — and so can "exploit the unconscious entirely."
  - Source passage: > "On this score, statistics and Big Data lie worlds apart. Big Data provides the means for establishing not just an individual but a collective psychogram - perhaps even the psychogram of the unconscious itself."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated)
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Big Data reaches the psyche where statistics cannot.md`

## Metaphors

- **The Sword / Might of the Sword** — sovereign power figured as the blade that deals death.
  - Source passage: > "The power of sovereignty is the might of the sword."
- **Shapeless Dough → Machine** — the body under discipline figured as raw dough kneaded into a machine; discipline as a "concerted orthopaedy."
  - Source passage: > "A 'concerted orthopaedy' fashions 'shapeless dough' into a 'machine'."
- **The Body as Production-Unit** — discipline figured as calibration/fitting of the body to machinic production.
  - Source passage: > "The body is calibrated to be a production-unit."
- **Orthopaedy / Orthopaedic Technology** — discipline figured as a corrective straightening of the body (and its limit: too crude to reach the soul).
  - Source passage: > "The orthopaedic technology of disciplinary power is too crude to penetrate into the deeper layers of the soul"
- **Depths of the Psyche / Light into the Depths** — the psyche figured as a dark interior (with "hidden wishes, needs and desires") that Big Data can finally illuminate.
  - Source passage: > "it may yet shine a light into the depths of the psyche and exploit the unconscious entirely."
- **Bentham's Big Brother** — Orwellian surveillance figure used to mark the *merely external/optical* gaze of discipline.
  - Source passage: > "Bentham's Big Brother only observes inmates from the outside."

## Symbols

- **The Panopticon (Benthamian)** — disciplinary surveillance architecture, here strictly characterized as optical/exterior, with no access to interiority. Sets up the book's later contrast with the *digital* panopticon and the "friendly Big Brother."
  - Source passage: > "His panopticon is bound to the optical medium. It has no access to inner thoughts or needs."
- **The Psychogram** — the symbolic figure of the *map of the psyche/unconscious* that Big Data can produce and demography cannot; the emblem of digital psychopolitics.
  - Source passage: > "Big Data provides the means for establishing not just an individual but a collective psychogram - perhaps even the psychogram of the unconscious itself."

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Power over death vs. power over life** — sovereignty (deal death) vs. discipline/biopolitics (invest/administer life).
  - Source: > "disciplinary power is not a power to deal death, but a power over life"
- **Body/population vs. psyche/unconscious** — the chapter's defining axis: biopolitics reaches the body and the population; only psychopolitics/Big Data reaches the psyche.
  - Source: > "this approach proves altogether unsuited to the neoliberal regime, which exploits the psyche above all."
- **Demography vs. psych-ography (statistics vs. Big Data)** — quantitative population-biology vs. a map of the psyche.
  - Source: > "Demography is not the same thing as psych-ography. It cannot tap into or disclose the psyche. On this score, statistics and Big Data lie worlds apart."
- **Exterior/optical surveillance vs. interior penetration** — Bentham's panopticon (outside, optical) vs. the Big-Data reach into "inner thoughts or needs."
  - Source: > "Bentham's Big Brother only observes inmates from the outside. His panopticon is bound to the optical medium."
- **Allo-exploitation vs. auto-exploitation (obedience-subject vs. achievement-subject)** — sovereign/disciplinary external domination vs. (implied) neoliberal self-exploitation; this chapter supplies the *allo-* pole and the obedience-subject.
  - Source: > "Both sovereign power and disciplinary power pursue allo-exploitation. They bring forth the obedience-subject."
- **Negativity of discipline vs. (implied) positivity of psychopolitics** — discipline works through prohibition/norm/drill (negativity); the neoliberal regime (per Han's broader thesis) works through positivity/permission. This chapter establishes the negativity pole.
  - Source: > "Such negativity of training and drills (Abrichtung) is constitutive of disciplinary power."
