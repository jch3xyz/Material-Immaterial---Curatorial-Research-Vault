## Executive Summary

This chapter is the culmination of Part III's argument about **Instrumentarian Power**: it shows instrumentarian power scaling up from the individual to **society itself** as a totalizing project. Where earlier chapters defined instrumentarianism as a new species of power that tunes, herds, and conditions behavior toward guaranteed outcomes, Chapter 14 reveals its *societal* endgame — **A Utopia of Certainty**, in which the whole of "society," "social relations," and "key societal processes" are annexed "as a fresh terrain for rendition, calculation, modification, and prediction." The pursuit of totality, begun in the **Reality Business** (extending extraction from the virtual to the physical world), now necessarily "includes society."

The chapter develops several intertwined threads. (1) **Two species of power**: instrumentarianism and totalitarianism are "distinct species" that both "yearn toward totality" but in "profoundly different ways" — totalitarianism seeks totality as a *political* condition and relies on *violence*; instrumentarianism seeks totality as a *condition of market dominance* and relies on its control over the **Division of Learning in Society**, enabled and enforced by **Big Other**. (2) **Totality includes society**: Zuboff inventories the totalistic ambitions of the surveillance-capitalist leaders — Microsoft's Satya Nadella, Google's Sundar Pichai and Larry Page, Facebook's Mark Zuckerberg — quoting their own words to show that "society," not merely individuals, is now subordinated to the market dynamic. (3) **Applied utopistics**: using Frank and Fritzie Manuel's scholarship on utopian thought, she argues the surveillance capitalists are *sui generis* utopianists who, unlike Marx (whose theory required a Lenin to apply it), "seize the world in practice," reversing the normal sequence of theory and practice — practice "at high velocity in the absence of an explicit and contestable theory." (4) **Confluence as machine relations**: through Nadella's factory and construction-site demonstrations, she derives the social vision implicit in the practices — a **hive mind** / **confluence** in which "the freedom of each individual machine is surrendered to the knowledge that they share," an "all-out war on accidents, mistakes, and randomness in general," explicitly tracing the lineage to **Skinner's Walden Two** and to the behavioral theorists Planck, Meyer, and Skinner. (5) **Confluence as society**: the machine hive becomes "the role model for a new human hive" in which "individual freedom is forfeit to collective knowledge and action," nonharmonious elements "preemptively targeted with high doses of tuning, herding, and conditioning." This is named the signature of the **Third Modationality** — corrected: the **Third Modernity** — "offered up by surveillance capital as its answer to our quest for effective life together."

This chapter is one of the two or three best primary anchors in the book for `Instrumentarian Power`, `Big Other`, `A Utopia of Certainty` / `Guaranteed Outcomes`, the `Two Species of Power` contrast, `The Hive` / `Confluence` / the instrumentarian collective, `Anticipatory Conformity` (here developed as the swarming, "social influence processes" that mobilize confluent human action), `Second Modernity and Third Modernity`, and the realized `Surveillance` keystone. It also deeply extends Han's `Digital Panopticon` and `Society of Control`, Harari's `Dataism` and `Society as Algorithm`, and resonates with Kurzweil's embraced ubiquitous computing (Mark Weiser's "hundreds of computers in every room"). The chapter is clean OCR (no corruption beyond the epigraph's W. H. Auden line); every quotation below is a verbatim contiguous substring.

> Note on `Third Modationality`: that token does NOT appear in the source — it is a typo I am flagging and correcting; the source term is **third modernity** (lowercase in the prose; canonical concept name `Second Modernity and Third Modernity`). Disregard the struck phrasing above; the verbatim source reads "This is the signature of the third modernity offered up by surveillance capital."
