## Executive Summary

The penultimate and shortest chapter of the book extends Han's theory of eros from the intimate/erotic register into the **political** register. The thesis: eros is the ground of the *We* — of communal action, of political revolt, of the courage for radical and utopian change — and so the contemporary erosion of eros (its degradation into sexuality and pornography under neoliberalism) is simultaneously the **erosion of the political**. Han runs the argument through three moves. (1) A **Platonic** frame: eros is "something universal"; it holds sway over the three parts of the soul (epithumia/desire, thumos/spiritedness-courage, logos/reason), and it is *thumos* — the seat of rage and rupture — "where eros and politics touch." Today desire (epithumia) dominates; thumos withers into mere annoyance/dissatisfaction that lacks "the negativity of rupture"; and logos, deprived of eros, "deteriorat[es] into data-driven calculation, which is incapable of reckoning the event, the incalculable." (2) A **Badiouian** frame: love is a "Two scene," an event marked by "the negativity of upheaval" and demanding "fidelity"; this eventfulness is what "connects love with politics and art," all three commanding fidelity to the event and interrupting "the Same in favor of the Other." Sexuality and pornography, by contrast, occupy the "One scene," reproduce the Same, erase otherness, and intensify narcissification. (3) A **surrealist** frame: for André Breton and the surrealists the reinvention of love is "an artistic, existential, and political gesture," and eros is "the energetic source of renewal, which also feeds political action" — "faithful to what is yet to come." The chapter is the book's most explicit statement that eros is a political and revolutionary energy, and that neoliberalism *depoliticizes* precisely by "replacing eros with sexuality and pornography."

The chapter is Han-authored throughout; its Badiou quotations (from *In Praise of Love* and *Ethics*) and its Breton quotation are Han citing his sources — **attribute those verbatim phrases to Badiou / Breton as their originators**, but the surrounding argument is Han's. (Unlike the book's Foreword, ch. 7 is not itself by Badiou.)

## Arguments

- **Claim: Eros is the ground of the political and the We; the erosion of eros is the depoliticization of society.**
  - Reasoning structure: eros holds sway over the soul's parts, including thumos, the seat of political rupture; neoliberalism replaces eros with sexuality/pornography (epithumia), so thumos and the capacity for communal action wither, and politics "degrade[s] into mere work."
  - Evidence: the tripartite-soul frame; the diagnosis of the burnout/achievement society; the "communal action — a we — now proves impossible" claim.
  - Source passage: > "Neoliberalism is depoliticizing society in general—and not least of all, by replacing eros with sexuality and pornography. It is based on epithumia. In a burnout society of isolated, self-alienated achievement-subjects, thumos is also withering away. Communal action—a we—now proves impossible."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The erosion of eros is the erosion of the political.md`

- **Claim: Thumos is where eros and politics touch; rage (thumotic negativity) is what inaugurates a new state of affairs, and its decay into mere annoyance leaves circumstances intact.**
  - Reasoning structure: contrast between thumotic rage (negativity of rupture, breaks with convention) and annoyance/dissatisfaction (lacks negativity, lets circumstances persist).
  - Evidence: "Rage is thumotic… it radically breaks with convention"; annoyance "allows circumstances to persist."
  - Source passage: > "Thumos, then, would be where eros and politics touch. However, contemporary politics—which lacks not only thumos, but eros as well—has degraded into mere work."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Thumos is where eros and politics touch.md`

- **Claim: Without eros, logos degenerates into data-driven calculation that cannot reckon the event or the incalculable.**
  - Reasoning structure: eros is what keeps reason open to the event/the Other; strip eros and logos becomes mere calculation, blind to the incalculable — the same critique of Big Data / additive positivity that runs through Psychopolitics and (in ch.8) the "end of theory."
  - Evidence: "logos is deteriorating into data-driven calculation, which is incapable of reckoning the event, the incalculable."
  - Source passage: > "Moreover, without eros, logos is deteriorating into data-driven calculation, which is incapable of reckoning the event, the incalculable."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Without eros logos degenerates into calculation blind to the event.md` (cross-link `Dataism`, `Big Data`, and the existing `Big Data is blind to the event and so blind to the future`). Human-review whether this is a distinct argument or a facet of the existing one.

- **Claim: Sexuality and pornography occupy the "One scene" and reproduce the Same; only love as a "Two scene" event admits the wholly Other and denarcissifies.**
  - Reasoning structure: the sexual object is self-identical, consumable, "not the Other," and "never calls me into question"; pornography "erases otherness altogether," "intensifies narcissification"; love-as-event generates "a 'rupture,' a 'hole' in the order of the Habitual and the Same."
  - Evidence: the One-scene/Two-scene contrast; "autoerotic contact and auto-affection protect the ego from being touched or seized by the Other."
  - Source passage: > "The sexual object always remains self-identical. No event occurs for it, since a sexual object that can be consumed is not the Other. It never calls me into question."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Sexuality occupies the One scene and reproduces the Same.md` (cross-link `Pornography (Han)`, `Hell of the Same`, `The Two Scene`).

- **Claim: Eventfulness (fidelity to the event) is the universal quality of eros that binds love, politics, and art.**
  - Reasoning structure: the event interrupts the Same in favor of the Other and demands fidelity; love, politics, and art all "command 'fidelity' to the event"; this transcendental fidelity is a universal quality of eros. Follows Badiou.
  - Evidence: "Eventfulness connects love with politics and art."
  - Source passage: > "Even though Alain Badiou rejects a direct link between politics and love, he presumes a 'kind of secret resonance' that arises between life lived wholly under the sign of a political idea and the intensity of love."
  - Confidence: high (Directly stated; Badiou-sourced).

  - Reasoning structure: Breton ascribes universal power to eros ("the only one capable of leading him further than the stars… is eroticism"); eros combines the artistic, existential, and political and remains "faithful to what is yet to come."
  - Evidence: the Breton quotation; "eros is the medium of a poetic revolution in language and existence."
