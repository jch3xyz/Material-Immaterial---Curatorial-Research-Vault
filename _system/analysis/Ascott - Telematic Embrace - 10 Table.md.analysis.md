## Executive Summary

A short 1975 essay, originally published in *House: The Journal of the London Architecture Club*, that sits squarely in Ascott's pre-telematic, **cybernetic/behaviourist** phase. Ascott takes the domestic *table* as a model object and reads it as a behavioural **matrix** — a horizontal "arena for behaviours," a "medium of exchange," "the grounds for change." The argument moves through three moves: (1) a semiotics of domestic behaviour (house, kitchen, table as nested analogues of the universe; objects as "pure ideas, realised in hardware"); (2) a sweeping art-historical polemic in which the **horizontal table-top** (holistic, multi-viewpoint, transactional, "bird's-eye," cosmic) is opposed to the **vertical plane** of the Renaissance "picture on the wall" / the mirror — an "archetypal convention of a whole culture of separation, competition, self-love" produced by perspective and the vanishing point; (3) the extension of this polemic to broadcast television and "video art," whose social/cultural "bankruptcy" Ascott blames on verticality, against which he proposes horizontal, all-round, top-and-bottomless table-top video as a route to "holistic consciousness." The essay closes with Ascott's signature behaviourist credo: art is now "a form of behaviour (rather than a simple array of images or objects)," it "aspires to the condition of exchange," it is an **open system** with "no final outcome," and "Art, then, resides not on a surface or within material objects alone, but between the behaviours of all parts of the system." The piece is a compact, vivid statement of the OBJECT→SYSTEM / image→behaviour move that defines Ascott's pole of 1960s cybernetic art — the participatory/behavioural counterpart to Burnham's systems esthetics of the object. It is also a sharp anticipation of his later anti-spectatorial, network-consciousness arguments (the table-top as a many-to-many transactional surface prefigures the telematic network) and connects strongly to McLuhan's holistic/acoustic-space versus visual-perspective opposition.

## Definitions

- **Table (Ascott's working definition)** — "A table exists … only in so far as it is the arena for behaviours"; it is "a medium of exchange, the space between behaviours and states, the grounds for change," "a matrix (and so at all levels)." Defined behaviourally/relationally, not as a physical object.
- **The horizontal table-top vs the vertical plane** — the table-top = horizontal, holistic, "bird's-eye," multi-viewpoint, transactional, all-round "without top or bottom"; the vertical plane = the picture/mirror on the wall, fixed viewpoint, isolating, narcissistic. This binary is effectively defined and is the chapter's analytic spine.
- **Matrix (in myth/religion)** — "It is always the matrix of transformation and transfer, of change and exchange, between two poles, two worlds … the dialectical surface is necessarily horizontal"; "In religion, the table is the mystical matrix."

## Arguments

- **Claim: Art is a form of behaviour, not an array of objects or images, and so takes on the quality of transaction/exchange.**
  - Reasoning structure: redefinition of art's ontology — from object/surface to behaviour/system — drawn from "the context of art now … concepts of behaviour, transaction, process and system."
  - Evidence: the table-top as the working model of an art of transaction; the analyst's-couch vs dream-table contrast.
  - Source passage: > Art, clearly established in our present culture as a form of behaviour (rather than a simple array of images or objects), now takes on the quality of a transaction. Art aspires to the condition of exchange.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Art is a form of behaviour not an array of objects.md` (Ascott).

- **Claim: Art resides not in objects or surfaces but BETWEEN the behaviours of all parts of the system; only the observer's intervention generates meaning.**
  - Reasoning structure: the open-system thesis — meaning is relational and participatory, not stored in the artifact.
  - Evidence: the table-top "open system"; "only the intervention of observers can generate meaning and value."
  - Source passage: > The table-top supports an open system, in which only the intervention of observers can generate meaning and value. Art, then, resides not on a surface or within material objects alone, but between the behaviours of all parts of the system.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Art resides between behaviours not in objects.md` (Ascott) — the keystone bridge to `Material vs Immaterial` / `Object vs System`.

- **Claim: Renaissance perspective and the vanishing point established the vertical "picture on the wall," which signified and enforced Western culture's isolation, alienation, competition, and self-love.**
  - Reasoning structure: art-historical genealogy of alienation — the shift from the horizontal (cosmic, holistic) to the vertical (self-reflecting, isolating) plane mirrors and reproduces a worldview of separation.
  - Evidence: "Mirror, mirror on the wall…"; the doctrine of the vanishing point intended to "obliterate" the holistic/integrative worldview; the fixed, immobilized viewing point.
  - Source passage: > As such the picture on the wall, the painting, has been the pre-eminent signifier of Western culture for as long as the values of alienation, competition, and narcissism have dominated our society. As those values recede, so does the importance of painting as a "mirroron-the-wall" signifier.
  - Source passage: > its semiotic was repressed during the Renaissance with the advent of perspective and its principal raison d'être, which was to demonstrate man's isolation from the universe, to close down mystical transactions.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Perspective made the vertical picture the signifier of Western alienation.md` (Ascott).

- **Claim: The social and cultural failure of television and "video art" is due to verticality; only horizontal, all-round table-top video can contribute to holistic consciousness.**
  - Reasoning structure: applies the horizontal/vertical thesis to electronic media — the monitor on the vertical plane reproduces the mirror's isolation; redesigning the viewing plane (horizontal, no top/bottom) is the cure.
  - Evidence: the "bankruptcy and irrelevance of 'video art'"; the separation of entertainment and art as "a consequence of this verticality."
  - Source passage: > Only when video is created to be viewed on the horizontal, table-top plane, seen all round without top or bottom, will it have the possibility of contributing to our holistic consciousness.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Vertical television fails and horizontal table-top video could give holistic consciousness.md` (Ascott).

- **Claim: Reciprocal, many-to-many transaction (the "dream table") is necessary for change and growth, replacing the one-way analyst's couch.**
  - Reasoning structure: contrast of communication topologies — one-way (couch, hierarchic, hidden analyst) blocks change; many-way (dream table, "infinite lines of communication") enables it. A precise behaviourist/cybernetic feedback claim.
  - Evidence: the couch "only permitted a one-way line of communication"; the dream table "has infinite lines of communication."
  - Source passage: > The dream table, unlike the couch, has infinite lines of communication, enabling transactions to occur between the inner worlds, the dreams or inventions of all those placed around it.
  - Source passage: > Without reciprocity and transaction, change and growth of either patient or analyst were impeded.
  - Confidence: high
  - Recommended wiki note: extend `arguments`/`Reciprocal Communication` — fold into the `Reciprocal Communication` update (one-way vs many-way / feedback).

## Metaphors

- **The table as matrix / mystical matrix / dream table** — the governing figure: matrix of transformation and exchange between two poles ("the dialectical surface is necessarily horizontal"; "In religion, the table is the mystical matrix"; "the dream table … absolutely replacing the analyst's couch").
- **The house and table as analogues of the universe** — "Each person's house is a received or created analogue of his/her perceived or wished-for universe. The table is the analogue of the house"; the table lets us "sit around our universe of discourse."
- **The table as test-bed / sounding board / device for divination** — "a device for divination, a sounding board for new relationships … a test-bed for change."
- **The soft table / table as a spread on the ground** — the picnic / Middle-Eastern cloth as "a shadow of a table," "removing the corporeality of the table to a mere slip of cloth"; bringing the table-top "literally to the surface of the earth."
- **"Mirror, mirror on the wall…"** — the folk-tale formula for the vertical, narcissistic picture; "the apotheosis of the vertical plane … reflecting only ourselves and talking to us only one at a time."
- **The Tarot / the Magician's table** — the table as the "metaphysical table-top," the arena of the major/minor arcana ("Tarot is the most metaphysically refined of table-top behaviours").
- **Going "through the looking glass"** — the false "higher level" entertainment "masquerading as art" on the vertical screen.
- **Domestic utensils as "pure ideas, realised in hardware"** — kitchen objects (fork, funnel, container, grater, sieve) as embodied ideas brought into "new assemblies."

## Symbols

- **The Table** — the central symbol/motif of the whole essay (round table, board table, high table, soft table, dream table, altar).
- **The Mirror / the Picture on the Wall** — the antagonist symbol: the vertical, isolating, narcissistic plane.
- **The Altar / the sacred horizontal** — "the sacred horizontal that stands between the vertical polarities of heaven and earth."
- **The Analyst's Couch** — the obsolete one-way, hierarchic communication device the dream table replaces.
- **The Monitor Screen** — the vertical-plane TV/video as a contemporary form of the mirror-on-the-wall.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Horizontal vs Vertical** — THE structuring opposition of the chapter: the horizontal table-top (holistic, all-round, transactional, cosmic, bird's-eye) vs the vertical plane (fixed viewpoint, isolating, narcissistic, one-at-a-time). Candidate canonical tension `Horizontal vs Vertical` (Ascott).
- **Behaviour/System vs Object/Image** — art as behaviour/transaction/open system vs art as "a simple array of images or objects" / "a surface or within material objects alone." Maps onto the existing `Object vs System` and `Material vs Immaterial` tensions.
- **Reciprocity vs Isolation (many-way vs one-way communication)** — the dream table's "infinite lines of communication" vs the couch's "one-way line"; reciprocity generating "trust and sympathy" vs the "culture of separation, competition, self-love."
- **Holistic/Integrative vs Partial/Fixed viewpoint** — "Our worldview is holistic and integrative … we resist the partial view" vs the perspectival "fixed viewing point."
- **Disclosure vs Alienation** — the table as "an arena for disclosure and analysis" vs the picture on the wall as "always totally alienating, always separate and apart from the viewer."
