## Executive Summary

A dense documentary anthology for the year 1968 — the pivotal year of the book. Lippard's own argumentative voice is sparse; she compiles books, exhibition records, artist statements, and quotes. The year is anchored by the publication of her own essay with John Chandler, "The Dematerialization of Art" (Art International, February 1968), which gives the book its name and thesis. The chronicle instantiates almost the entire conceptual-art vocabulary at once: the dematerialized/immaterial work (LeWitt, Barry, Weiner's "Statements," Ramsden's secret pieces), systems aesthetics (Haacke's first water/weather pieces and his "system is real" statement; Burnham's "Systems Aesthetics" essay), the exhibition/catalogue as medium (Seth Siegelaub's Bradford, Windham, and the November 1968 Huebler catalogue that "exists solely as a catalogue"), the document/documentation (On Kawara's "I met / I went," Burgy's *Rock #5*, Dibbets's "the work of art is the photo"), and the art-and-politics turn (Daniel Buren's "art is objectively reactionary," the Argentine Rosario/Tucumán group). A major internal dispute is recorded verbatim: the Art-Language group's March 1968 letter contesting the very metaphor of "dematerialization" on physical/matter-state grounds — the book's own thesis put under adversarial pressure.

## Definitions

- **Dematerialization** (Lippard/Chandler) — implicit definition: the de-emphasis of the physical object in favor of the thinking process / idea; matter "denied" (sensation read into concept) or "transformed into energy and motion." Recorded with its immediate counter-definition by Art-Language (see Tensions).

## Arguments

- **Claim: Ultra-conceptual art is dematerializing the art object — the idea displaces the physical work.**
  - Reasoning structure: historical periodization (1960s anti-intellectual intuitive art → conceptual/idea art) → object demoted to end-product → studio becomes study → "dematerialization."
  - Evidence: LeWitt, Barry, Weiner, Ramsden, Atkinson cited in the surrounding chronicle.
  - Source passage: > "Such a trend appears to be provoking a profound dematerialization of art."
  - Confidence: high (directly stated, in the book's own source essay).
  - Recommended wiki note: feed into `tensions/Material vs Immaterial.md` (art section) rather than a new argument note.

- **Claim (counter): "Dematerialization" is a false metaphor — all art remains matter in some state.**
  - Reasoning structure: physics of matter-states (solid/liquid/gas; matter as a form of energy) → a map or typewritten sheet is as solid-state as a Rubens → recording an idea in words produces a material by-product, not a "dematerialized" object.
  - Evidence: Andre's "empty space," Atkinson's "Map to not indicate."
  - Source passage: > "They may not be an art-object as we know it in its traditional matter-state, but they are nevertheless matter in one of its forms, either solid-state, gas-state, liquid-state."
  - Confidence: high (directly stated, Art-Language letter).
  - Recommended wiki note: cite in `Material vs Immaterial` as the internal dissent; strong human-review item (the book documents the critique of its own thesis).

- **Claim: Art is objectively reactionary; contesting society requires contesting art itself.**
  - Reasoning structure: art belongs to present society as ornament/safety-valve → even "subversive" art is recuperated when hung on the gallery line → therefore the artist who wants another society must rupture with art.
  - Source passage: > "Art is the most beautiful ornament of society as it is now, and not the warning signs for society as it should be—never that. … But art is objectively reactionary." (Buren)
  - Confidence: high (directly stated).
  - Recommended wiki note: art-and-politics thread; flag essay-level.

## Metaphors

- **"The studio is again becoming a study"** (Lippard/Chandler) — the workshop of the hand replaced by the workshop of the mind; figures dematerialization as a shift of locus from body to intellect.
- **"A Sedimentation of the Mind" / geological erosion of thought** (Smithson) — "mental rivers wear away abstract banks, brain waves undermine cliffs of thought, ideas decompose into stones of unknowing"; mind and earth in mutual erosion.
- **"The frame can be language … the frame can be documents"** (Huebler) — perception of nature as bounded by conventions; language and documentation as framing devices equivalent to the picture frame.

## Symbols

- **The map** (Huebler's *42nd Parallel*, Atkinson's "Map to not indicate," Smithson's *Double Nonsite*) — recurring conceptual stand-in: the document/notation as the work, a "convention" pointing to a location where "in actual physical fact … there was nothing there."
- **The certificate / receipt** (N.E. Thing Co. ACT/ART certificates; Huebler's certified postal receipts) — bureaucratic document as both medium and authentication of the dematerialized work.
- **The catalogue / printed page** (Weiner's *Statements*, the Xerox Book, Huebler's Nov 1968 catalogue) — the publication as the artwork's primary site rather than a secondary record.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Dematerialization vs. matter-state (the Art-Language dispute).**
  - Poles: idea/immaterial work ↔ everything is still matter (solid/liquid/gas, matter-as-energy).
  - Source passage: > "Matter is a specialized form of energy; radiant energy is the only form in which energy can exist in the absence of matter."
  - This is the book's own thesis under adversarial pressure — maps directly onto `tensions/Material vs Immaterial`.

- **Art as resistance vs. art as recuperated commodity.**
  - Poles: refusing the "property state" / showing art in factories ↔ even subversive art "relished" when "hung 'on the line' in galleries."
  - Source passage: > "it relishes the 'revolution' when it is hung 'on line' in galleries or nicely disposed of in its apartments." (Buren)

- **Object vs. system / process.**
  - Poles: discrete art object ↔ real interdependent processes that exceed the space they occupy (Haacke); anti-form / casual ordering (Morris).

## References Between Authors

- **Lippard/Chandler ↔ Art-Language (Atkinson, Baldwin, Burn, Ramsden)** — explicit, documented disagreement over the term "dematerialization" (the March 23, 1968 letter directly names and contests the February essay).
- **Lippard ↔ Hayles (cross-book, interpretive)** — Lippard's *dematerialization of the art object* is a striking parallel to Hayles's *disembodiment of information*: both narrate a (contested) flight of meaning/value from a material substrate, and both are historically contingent rather than inevitable. Flag for the references/cross-author layer.
- **Conceptual artists ↔ systems theory / cybernetics** — Haacke and Burnham import "system" language; bridges to Hayles's `Systems Theory` / `The Three Waves of Cybernetics`.
