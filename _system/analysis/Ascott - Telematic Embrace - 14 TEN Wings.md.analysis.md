## Executive Summary

This is a very short chapter (source pages 184–185): the surviving text of Ascott's 1982 proposal **TEN Wings**, a telematic art project contributed to **Robert Adrian's "The World in 24 Hours,"** a networked-art event staged at the **Ars Electronica** exhibition in Linz, Austria, 1982, running over the **Sharp APL / ARTBOX** electronic-mail timesharing service. The chapter consists of the editor's one-line gloss followed by the (heavily OCR-corrupted) operating *instructions* for the work itself — essentially an artwork **score** or **protocol** rather than an essay.

The work is a distributed **I Ching divination engine** run across a telecommunications network. ("The Ten Wings" — *Shih I* — is the traditional name for the ten classical commentaries appended to the *I Ching*.) Remote participants are directed to throw coins three times, transmit the numeric totals via ARTBOX to Ascott (address `<ASCOT>`), and receive back — by the same process of divination — a generated **"JUDGEMENT, A COMMENTARY AND AN IMAGE 'IN TEXT'"**, which is displayed at the Ars Electronica Center. All participants orient their attention toward a single shared **"WORLD QUESTION,"** then mail in five images responding to the resulting text within 24 hours, to be collated and documented back to the whole network.

The chapter matters to the book's arc as the **earliest concrete instantiation of telematic art** in the collection's chronology: the abstract 1960s thesis of the artwork-as-behavioural-system here becomes an *actual* networked, computer-mediated, participant-completed, geographically distributed event. It also seeds the later **technoetic / Shamantic Web** strand — divination, chance, oracle, and altered/expanded states of awareness fused with electronic networks — and the **network-consciousness** motif of many minds converging on one planetary question. The text ends with the machine handshake `ACCEPT (Y/N): Y MSG ACCEPTED: ASCOT 33`, foregrounding the computer-network medium itself as part of the work.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Object vs System / Material vs Immaterial** — TEN Wings has essentially no material art object; the "work" is the immaterial distributed process and its data transmissions (plus ephemeral mailed cards/documentation). A clear instance of the vault's `Object vs System` (Burnham) and the eight-author `Material vs Immaterial` tension, now on the telematic side.
- **Chance / Divination vs Computation** — the work yokes an ancient chance-oracle (coin-throwing, I Ching) to a deterministic digital network (APL timesharing). Productive tension: the irrational/oracular routed through the rational/computational. (Prefigures the technoetic "dry vs wet/spiritual" convergence.)
- **Distributed authorship vs the coordinating node** — authorship is dispersed across the participant network and outsourced to divination, yet everything funnels through the single address `<ASCOT>` (Ascott as hub/server). Tension between decentralized participation and a centralized coordinating point.
- **Local act vs planetary frame** — each participant performs a small local ritual (throw coins, mail five images) under a single shared planetary "WORLD QUESTION": the individual node vs the network whole.
