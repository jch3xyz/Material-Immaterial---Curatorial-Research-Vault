## Executive Summary

This chapter is Hayles's portrait of Norbert Wiener as the figure in whom **first-wave cybernetics collides with liberal humanism**. Her thesis is that cybernetics radically reconceived the human as a system of information flows whose boundaries are *constructed rather than given*, and that Wiener ‚Äî "the father of cybernetics" ‚Äî both authored this dissolution of the human/machine boundary and recoiled from it because it threatened the autonomous, self-regulating, possessive-individualist subject of liberal humanism. Hayles reads Wiener's intellectual machinery as built from three interlocking moves: (1) a **probabilistic worldview** inherited from Willard Gibbs (substituting "word for world"), out of which his **information construction** grows; (2) **analogy** as the master trope that lets animals, humans, and machines be treated as equivalent cybernetic systems by eliding embodied difference; and (3) an **entropy / negentropy** cosmology in which life is "an island of negentropy amid a sea of disorder" and good (flexible, feedback-using) machines are morally ranged against evil (rigid, engulfing) machines.

The chapter's deeper argument is about **the erasure of embodiment**. Wiener's analogical method abstracts to the level where pattern similarities appear and "erase[s] from view the very real differences in embodied materiality." Hayles ties this elision biographically to Wiener's own discomfort in his body and his abandonment of laboratory biology. The closing sections ("The Argument for Celibacy") read *Cybernetics* and *The Human Use of Human Beings* as texts where **erotic anxiety about body boundaries** erupts: Wiener constructs a homosocial fantasy of intimacy with an "intelligent savage" to re-secure himself as an autonomous liberal subject, and finally counsels that cybernetics remain "celibate" ‚Äî confined to the physical sciences ‚Äî to protect the self as locus of control. Historically, Hayles concludes, Wiener failed: the link between liberal humanism and self-regulation forged in the eighteenth century was "stretched thin" by the 1960s and "largely broken" by the 1980s, after which the cyborg "do[es] not speak as one." The chapter thus stages the transition from the **liberal humanist subject** to its **posthuman successor**, with Wiener as the anxious threshold figure.

## Definitions

- **Information (Wiener)** ‚Äî > "Wiener defined information as a function of probabilities representing a choice of one message from a range of possible messages that might be sent. In a sense, he took Gibbs's idea and substituted word for world."
- **Teleology (cybernetic)** ‚Äî > "teleological behavior means simply 'behavior controlled by negative feedback' (p. 24)." / "teleology implies a goal achieved through negative feedback."
- **Negentropy (negative entropy)** ‚Äî > "Brillouin therefore proposed that information be considered as negative entropy, or negentropy."
- **The ergodic hypothesis** ‚Äî > "an ensemble of dynamic systems in some way traces in the course of time a distribution of parameters which is identical with the distribution of parameters of all systems at a given time."
- **Information (Bateson's gloss)** ‚Äî > "Recall that Gregory Bateson defined information as a difference that makes a difference; if there is no difference, there is no information."
- **Augustinian vs Manichean opponent** ‚Äî > "the difference between an opponent who plays 'honorably,' that is, abiding by rules that do not change, and one who tries to win by manipulation." Nature is Augustinian; the chess player (and chess-playing machine) is Manichean.
- **Cybernetics (etymology)** ‚Äî > "The Greek root for cybernetics, 'steersman,' aptly describes the cybernetic man-machine: light on its feet, sensitive to change, a being that both is a flow and knows how to go with the flow."

## Arguments

- **Claim: First-wave cybernetics made the boundaries of the human subject constructed rather than given.**
  - Reasoning structure: If the human is defined by information flows (Bateson's cane), then epidermal surfaces no longer fix the subject's limits; cane, hearing aid, voice synthesizer, and pilot's firing helmet all join the human in a single system.
  - Evidence: the seductive list moving "from modifications intended to compensate for deficiencies to interventions designed to enhance normal functioning."
  - Source passage: > "Once this splice is passed, establishing conceptual limits to the process becomes difficult."
  - Confidence: high `Directly stated`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Cybernetics makes the boundaries of the human subject constructed.md`

- **Claim: Wiener tried to fuse cybernetics with liberal humanism, but the fusion was unstable and ultimately self-undermining.**
  - Reasoning structure: Self-regulating machinery and possessive individualism share an eighteenth-century lineage (Mayr, Adam Smith's Invisible Hand); Wiener wanted the cybernetic machine to *extend* the autonomous self, but the same logic dissolves the self that owns itself (Dick's question).
  - Evidence: Mayr's history; Wiener's contradictions; the closing historical verdict.
  - Source passage: > "liberal humanism, self-regulating machinery, and possessive individualism had come together in an uneasy alliance that at once helped to create the cyborg and also undermined the foundations of liberal subjectivity."
  - Confidence: high `Directly stated`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Cybernetics both created and undermined the liberal subject.md`

- **Claim: Analogy is not a flaw of cybernetics but its constitutive method ‚Äî and its method of erasing embodiment.**
  - Reasoning structure: Because meaning is relational (Saussurean), analogy *constitutes* both terms; "black box" behaviorism produces the equivalence of man and machine by constructing a viewpoint from which the equation must hold; this systematically elides embodied difference.
  - Evidence: "Behavior, Purpose, and Teleology"; the "appeared" slippage; Wiener's lab estrangement.
  - Source passage: > "the equation between organism and machine works because it is seen from a position formulated precisely so that it will work."
  - Confidence: high `Directly stated`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The man-machine equivalence is produced by where the observer stands.md`

- **Claim: Wiener's elision of materiality is bound up with his own estrangement from the flesh.**
  - Reasoning structure: Biographical-interpretive ‚Äî his physical clumsiness, abandonment of lab biology, and impatience with experimental detail predisposed him to abstract to pattern rather than attend to embodied particulars.
  - Evidence: horseshoes anecdote; "We cut the attachment of the muscle" plural; Rosenblith/Wiesner tribute.
  - Source passage: > "the emphasis on analogy went hand in hand with a certain estrangement from the flesh."
  - Confidence: medium `Strongly implied` / `Interpretive synthesis`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Wiener's erasure of embodiment reflects his estrangement from the flesh.md`

- **Claim: Entropy is morally coded ‚Äî life and good machines are negentropic; rigid machines are entropic, engulfing, and "evil."**
  - Reasoning structure: Wiener inherits the nineteenth-century thermodynamic sign (entropy = death) rather than Shannon's; he stages a cosmological "titanic struggle" of good cybernetic flexibility vs. rigid mechanism.
  - Evidence: "island of negentropy"; HU passages on rigid machines as cogs and levers.
  - Source passage: > "So firmly rooted is Wiener in this perspective that he comes close on several occasions to saying that entropic decay is evil."
  - Confidence: high `Directly stated`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Wiener morally codes entropy as evil and negentropy as life.md`

- **Claim: To protect the liberal subject, Wiener counsels cybernetic "celibacy" ‚Äî confining cybernetics to the physical sciences.**
  - Reasoning structure: Total interpenetration of micro (selfish units within) and macro (knaves/fools without) scales would reduce the individual to a "connective membrane" with no control; the only resolution is withdrawal, expressed through erotic metaphors of loose vs. tight coupling.
  - Evidence: the "intelligent savage" fantasy; "we are too much in tune with the objects of our investigations to be good probes."
  - Source passage: > "Wiener decides that however tempting the prospect of penetrating the boundaries of other disciplines might be, cybernetics is better off remaining celibate."
  - Confidence: high `Directly stated`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/Wiener counsels cybernetic celibacy to preserve the autonomous self.md`

- **Claim: Wiener failed to contain cybernetics within liberal humanism; the liberal/self-regulation link broke by the 1980s.**
  - Reasoning structure: Historical conclusion ‚Äî no individual controls what a discipline signifies "when it propagates through the culture by all manner of promiscuous couplings."
  - Evidence: the periodization (1940s‚Äì50s tension; thin by 1960s; broken by 1980s).
  - Source passage: > "By the 1960s, the link between liberal humanism and self-regulation, a link forged in the eighteenth century, was already stretched thin; by the 1980s, it was largely broken."
  - Confidence: high `Directly stated`
  - Recommended wiki note: `arguments/The link between liberalism and self-regulation broke by the 1980s.md`

## Metaphors

- **The mirror of the cyborg** ‚Äî Wiener builds a mirror inviting us to see ourselves as control-communication devices, then "glance[s] over his shoulder, sees himself as a cyborg, and makes a horrified withdrawal." Central figure of the chapter's ambivalence.
- **Word for world** ‚Äî Wiener "took Gibbs's idea and substituted word for world": one message from a cacophony of possible messages mirrors one world from a galaxy of possible worlds.
- **The baggy pair of pants** ‚Äî the probabilistic Gibbsian universe "operates like a baggy pair of pants, holding together all right but constantly rearranging itself every time one tries to sit down" (vs. Laplace's clock).
- **Island of negentropy / whirlpool in a river** ‚Äî life as "an island of negentropy amid a sea of disorder"; humans as "whirlpools in a river of ever-flowing water," "patterns that perpetuate themselves."
- **Dr. Zhivago and information death** ‚Äî the whimsical frozen-telegraph scenario where heat death and "information death" converge ("eeeeeee") to illustrate entropy as the erosion of difference.
- **Swimming upstream against the entropic tide / building dikes** ‚Äî organism as informational system running against entropy; men and machines "shoulder to shoulder in building dikes that temporarily stave off the entropic tide."
- **Maxwell's Demon / leaves studded with Demons** ‚Äî plants in photosynthesis act "as if their leaves were studded with Maxwell's Demons," sorting molecules to run uphill toward complexity.
- **Loose vs. tight coupling (erotic)** ‚Äî the precise sciences keep a "sufficiently loose coupling" with phenomena; tighter coupling risks loss of objectivity; eroticized anxiety about penetration of body/disciplinary boundaries.
- **Coitus interruptus / cybernetic celibacy** ‚Äî Wiener's withdrawal from the synthesis of physical and social sciences figured as sexual abstinence.

## Symbols

- **The blind man's cane** (Bateson) ‚Äî emblem of the constructed, prosthetic human boundary; the question that "aimed to spark a mind-shift."
- **The "intelligent savage" / two men alone in the woods** ‚Äî Wiener's homosocial fantasy of intimacy-through-gaze; symbol of liberal masculine autonomy and the appropriative plural of the liberal subject (recurs as Natty Bumppo/Chingachgook, Ishmael/Queequeg).
- **The rigid machine vs. the cybernetic brother** ‚Äî humans as "cogs and levers and rods" (the engulfing rigid machine) versus the flexible feedback machine "ranged alongside man as his brother and peer."
- **The steersman (kybernetes)** ‚Äî the etymological figure of the adaptive cybernetic man-machine that "is a flow and knows how to go with the flow."

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Liberal humanism vs. cybernetics** ‚Äî Wiener simultaneously equates humans with machines and "spoke up strongly for liberal humanist values"; the master contradiction of the chapter. Poles: autonomous self / boundary-dissolving system. (`Embodiment vs Information` adjacency.)
- **Pattern vs. presence (structure-free vs. structure-rich cybernetics)** ‚Äî cybernetics alternately elides internal structure (to assimilate men and machines) and foregrounds it (to distinguish them from "plain-vanilla mechanical systems").
- **Embodiment vs. abstraction** ‚Äî "the tension between the plenitude of embodiment and the sparseness of abstraction" (Wiener vs. McCulloch). Directly names the `Embodiment vs Information` stub.
- **Purpose vs. randomness** ‚Äî the cybernetic reframing displaces the old Newton-vs-God debate: "The important tension now is not between science and God but between purpose and randomness."
- **Information vs. entropy / order vs. chaos** ‚Äî Wiener's sign (information = order, entropy = death) vs. Shannon's identification of the two; the choice is "conventional" yet morally loaded.
- **Augustinian vs. Manichean opponent** ‚Äî honorable nature vs. manipulative chess-player; maps onto Wiener's pride in vs. horror at cybernetics as a "Manichean science."
- **Good machine vs. evil machine** ‚Äî flexible/feedback (brother) vs. rigid/engulfing (oppressor); a moral hierarchy smuggled into a descriptive science.
- **Control vs. dissolution (ecstasy vs. anxiety)** ‚Äî "At what point does the anxiety provoked by dissolution overcome the ecstasy?" The boundary-disruption that thrills also threatens to "get out of hand."
