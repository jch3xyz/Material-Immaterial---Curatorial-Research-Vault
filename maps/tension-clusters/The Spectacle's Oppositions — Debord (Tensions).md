# The Spectacle's Oppositions (Debord)

Debord stages the spectacle through a series of dialectical oppositions: reality against appearance, the lost unity of life against its fragmentation, use value against exchange value, and — running the length of the book — lived time against the reified time of things, encounter against isolation, and the critical method (détournement) against mere quotation.

- [[Reality vs Appearance]] — reality/essence vs appearance/representation; the spectacle is real and reality is spectacular (Ch1)
- [[Unity vs Separation]] — the lost unity of life vs fragmentation; the false unification that reunites only in separateness (Ch1, Ch3)
- [[Use Value vs Exchange Value]] — exchange value as a condottiere that ends up waging the war for its own sake (Ch2)
- [[Concentrated vs Diffuse Spectacle]] — the two forms of the spectacle, two masks of the same poverty (Ch3)
- [[Councils vs Party]] — revocable/federated self-organization vs the vanguard/hierarchical party that hardens into bureaucracy (Ch4)
- [[Cyclical vs Irreversible Time]] — myth-governed eternal return vs linear history (Ch5)
- [[Lived Time vs the Time of Things]] — experienced time vs the reified time capital eliminates (Ch5)
- [[The Reality of Time vs the Publicity of Time]] — time lived vs time consumed as image (Ch6)
- [[Space vs Time (Debord)]] — the spatialization and temporalization of the spectacle (Ch6–7)
- [[Isolation vs Reintegration]] — atomization and its controlled, pseudo-communal reintegration (Ch7)
- [[Détournement vs Quotation]] — the method of reversal vs the passive citation it inverts (Ch8, th.208)
- [[Abolition vs Realization of Art]] — the two inseparable aspects of a single transcendence of art (Ch8, th.191)
- [[Theory vs Practice (Debord)]] — ideas alone cannot destroy the spectacle; only a practical force can (Ch8, Ch9)
- [[Encounter vs Isolation]] — the destroyed faculty of encounter vs genuine dialogue (Ch9)
- [[True vs False (Debord)]] — the spectacle as the moment when the false becomes a real moment of falsity (Ch9, th.219)
