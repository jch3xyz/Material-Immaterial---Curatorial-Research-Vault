## By Book

### Donella Meadows — Thinking in Systems

The vault's first native-discipline systems-dynamics source. Meadows's arguments run from the definition of a system, through its feedback machinery and the epistemic reasons systems defeat our expectations, to the traps, leverage points, and the closing turn from analysis to engagement. See [[Donella Meadows (Arguments)]] for the full cluster page.

**Systems, structure, and behavior (Intro–Ch2)**

- [[A system causes its own behavior through its structure]]
- [[The systems lens is complementary to reductionist analysis not superior]]
- [[Intractable problems persist because they are systems problems not external failures]]
- [[The behavior of a system cannot be known from its parts alone]] — the Elephant parable
- [[Systems thinking is recovered intuition not new expertise]]
- [[The purpose of a system is the most crucial determinant of its behavior]]
- [[A systems purpose is deduced from its behavior not its stated goals]]
- [[A system persists as itself through complete turnover of its elements]]
- [[Systems with similar feedback structures produce similar behaviors regardless of appearance]]
- [[Acting faster on a delayed loop worsens oscillation high leverage wrong direction]]
- [[A model explores what would happen rather than predicts what will happen]]

**Why systems work — and surprise us (Ch3–Ch4)**

- [[Resilience self-organization and hierarchy are why systems work so well]]
- [[Resilience is invisible until it fails so we sacrifice it for legible stability and productivity]]
- [[Self-organization is suppressed because it threatens power structures]]
- [[Wildly complex structure can arise from just a few simple rules]]
- [[Complex systems can evolve only through stable intermediate forms]] — Simon's watchmaker fable
- [[A hierarchy exists to serve its originating subsystems]]
- [[Our models are strongly congruent with the world yet fall far short of it]]
- [[Events explain nothing only structure explains behavior]]
- [[There are no separate systems boundaries are drawn by the observer]]
- [[Perpetual growth in a finite environment is impossible]]
- [[Overshoot oscillation and collapse are always caused by delays]]
- [[Fix the system not the individual because rational actors produce outcomes no one wants]]

**System traps and leverage (Ch5–Ch6)**

- [[Problematic behavior is a property of system structure not of the actors]] — so blame and discipline cannot fix it
- [[The counterintuitive escape from policy resistance is to let go]]
- [[The tragedy of the commons is a failure of feedback not of morality]]
- [[A system obediently produces whatever its goal specifies]]
- [[An intervention becomes a trap when it atrophies the systems own capacity]]
- [[Leverage rises as the intervention point becomes more immaterial]]
- [[Paradigms are the sources of systems]]
- [[People intuit leverage points but push them in the wrong direction]]
- [[The highest leverage point is to transcend paradigms]]

**From analysis to engagement (Ch7)**

- [[We cannot control systems but we can dance with them]]
- [[Most of what goes wrong in systems goes wrong because of missing information]] — the eleventh commandment; the Toxic Release Inventory 40% drop
- [[What we can measure is not what is important]]
- [[We see only what we can talk about]]
- [[Systems thinking leads to the edge of analysis and points beyond to the human spirit]]
