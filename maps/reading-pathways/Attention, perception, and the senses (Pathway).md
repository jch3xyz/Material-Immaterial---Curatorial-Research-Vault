# Attention, perception, and the senses

This pathway follows a single claim across two thinkers: that media and social regimes reshape perception *beneath* the level of opinion or content — they reset what a culture is able to notice, attend to, and feel. It opens with Han's diagnosis of the contemporary attention-regime (deep contemplative attention giving way to scattered hyperattention and multitasking) and then crosses the bridge into McLuhan's older, deeper claim that every medium silently re-tunes the ratios among the senses and amputates the very faculties it extends. Curatorially it is useful for framing work about screens, distraction, immersion, slowness, and sensory overload — anything where the real subject is not *what* we perceive but the conditioning of perception itself, and where a piece wants to make its own re-tuning of attention felt rather than argued.

- [[Deep Attention vs Hyperattention]] — the framing tension for the whole path: two regimes of attention as the axis perception swings along.
- [[Deep Contemplative Attention]] — Han's baseline: the immersive, lingering attention that is the precondition of culture and of any deep experience.
- [[Hyperattention]] — its displacement: the broad-but-flat, rapidly switching attention-regime that the digital order rewards.
- [[Multitasking]] — Han's sharpest figure for hyperattention: not an advance but a regression to the divided vigilance of animals under threat.
- [[Profound Boredom]] — the negative capacity hyperattention destroys; deep boredom as the contemplative soil from which sustained attention grows.
- [[Vita Activa vs Vita Contemplativa]] — the underlying value-axis: Han's defense of contemplative stillness against the regime of restless activity.
- [[Acceleration]] — the speed that scatters attention and strips perception of narrative, the connective tissue between Han's diagnosis and a media-ecology reading.
- [[Marshall McLuhan and Byung-Chul Han]] — the explicit bridge: McLuhan as the media-ecology precursor whose sensory account underwrites Han's attention diagnosis.
- [[Sense Ratios]] — the hinge concept: McLuhan's claim that media alter the ratios and patterns of perception beneath opinion, before any content registers.
- [[Media as Extensions of Man]] — McLuhan's master frame: each medium extends a sense or faculty outward, reorganizing the whole sensorium around it.
- [[The Medium Is the Message]] — the corollary: a medium's real effect is the change of scale and sensory pattern it introduces, not the content it carries.
- [[Autoamputation]] — the cost of extension: every sense amplified by a medium is correspondingly numbed, perception self-anesthetizing under overload.
- [[Hot vs Cool Media]] — McLuhan's typology of how media engage the senses, calibrating how much they fill in versus demand of perception — the practical end of the path.
