# Acceleration and time

This pathway sets two valences of speed against each other and follows both into the question of time. Kurzweil reads acceleration as double-exponential progress to be embraced; McLuhan reads electric speed as the implosion that contracts the planet and rewires the nervous system; Han reads it as the crisis of a temporality scattered into atomized presences — and insists the problem is not speed but the loss of narrativity, which no mere deceleration can heal. It is useful curatorially for staging acceleration as both promise and pathology, and for grounding "slowness" work in the fragrance/scent-of-time figures rather than in nostalgia for the brake. Consolidates "Acceleration, two valences."

- [[Exponential vs Linear]] — the epistemic axis the affirmative case hangs on; why accelerating returns feel like rupture from inside a linear intuition.

- [[The Global Village]] — what electric speed produces — the planet contracted to a single participatory community, acceleration as compression of space.
