# Freedom, control, and power

This pathway traces the genealogy of power as a single migrating argument: from the enclosed, body-disciplining institutions Foucault diagnosed, through Han's claim that neoliberal freedom has itself become the subtlest coercion, to Deleuze's open "society of control," and finally to Zuboff's instrumentarian power, where control is no longer watched or willed but manufactured for profit. The through-line is the move from *enclosure* to *open modulation* — power leaving the cell and entering the network, the soul, and the behavioral future. Curatorially, it is the vault's spine for any project on surveillance, autonomy, and the politics of the self: it lets you stage how each thinker renames the same shift, and where their verdicts diverge (voluntary self-exposure vs. unilateral extraction; achievement vs. control; totalitarian terror vs. radical indifference).

- [[Freedom vs Control]] — the framing tension the whole genealogy turns on; not freedom *against* control but freedom *as* a mode of control.
- [[Disciplinary Society]] — the Foucauldian origin: power working through enclosure, the cell, and the despotic gaze, disciplining the body in milieus of confinement.
- [[Biopolitics]] — Foucault's regime of power over the body and the population, the baseline Han will periodize *past*.
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Michel Foucault]] — the hinge pairing: where Foucault's analysis of power, Han argues, can no longer account for the psychic and topological shift to the present.
- [[Smart Power]] — Han's friendly, permissive power that pleases and incites rather than forbids — coercion that no longer looks like coercion.
- [[Exploitation of Freedom]] — the core inversion: the neoliberal subject exploits *itself* voluntarily, because the feeling of freedom attends the exploitation.
- [[Auto-Exploitation]] — the mechanism that makes freedom efficient as control; the exploiter is simultaneously the exploited.
- [[Psychopolitics]] — power migrating from body to soul: control through motivation, optimization, and debt rather than discipline.
- [[Digital Panopticon]] — the apparatus of the new regime: a decentered, aperspectival, *loved* surveillance sustained by self-display, not isolation.
- [[Byung-Chul Han and Gilles Deleuze]] — the bridge to Deleuze: Han routes the mole/snake, subject/project, and body/soul distinctions through Deleuze's "Postscript on the Societies of Control."
- [[Society of Control]] — the genealogy's pivot and synthesis node: Deleuze's passage from confinement to continuous modulation, braided here with Han's voluntary thesis and Zuboff's actuation.
- [[Behavioral Surplus]] — the economic turn: lived experience claimed as raw material, the substrate that gives the next regime its profit motive.
- [[Instrumentarian Power]] — Zuboff's new species of power that knows and *shapes* behavior — modulation become actuation, control manufactured for guaranteed outcomes.
- [[Big Other]] — the material apparatus of instrumentarian power: the ubiquitous architecture that nudges, herds, and tunes behavior beneath awareness.
- [[Instrumentarian Power vs Totalitarianism]] — Zuboff's sharpest distinction: not the state's "total possession" but the market's "total certainty," ruling through radical indifference rather than terror.
- [[Shoshana Zuboff and Byung-Chul Han]] — the closing keystone pairing: Big Other beside the digital panopticon, unilateral extraction beside voluntary self-exposure — where the genealogy's two endpoints meet and disagree.
