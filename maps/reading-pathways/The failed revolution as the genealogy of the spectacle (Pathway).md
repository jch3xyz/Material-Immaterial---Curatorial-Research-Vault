# The failed revolution as the genealogy of the spectacle

Debord's Chapter 4 is not a digression but the spectacle's origin story. The pathway runs from the proletariat as history's self-producing subject, through its defeat into a representation that turned against it, to the vanguard party hardening into the bureaucracy that owns the class — the concentrated spectacle. It then reverses into the affirmative pole: the workers' councils as the one undefeated, anti-spectacular form.
