# Money, value, and the commodity

This pathway traces how *value* detaches from material substance across the vault — from the art object to attention, from coin to credit-information, from lived experience to behavioral data. It follows the value form as it grows progressively more immaterial yet more total: Han's exhibition value that liquidates the thing into its display, McLuhan's money dissolving from commodity into pure credit-information, Harari's fictions that out-power material things, and Zuboff's claim of human experience itself as the fourth fictional commodity feeding behavioral futures markets — closing on capital recast as religion. Curatorially it is a tool for staging the economic underside of the material/immaterial axis: useful for any project on commodification, attention economies, data extraction, or the question of what art is worth once it dematerializes.

- [[Exhibition Value]] — Han's entry point: value beyond the Marxian use/exchange pair, existing "thanks only to the attention it produces"; the object survives only as display.
