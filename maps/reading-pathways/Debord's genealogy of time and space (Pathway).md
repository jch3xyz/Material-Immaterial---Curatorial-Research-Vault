# Debord's genealogy of time and space

Chapters 5–7 give the spectacle a history and a geography. The pathway runs from the eternal-return time of myth-governed agrarian societies, through the linear time born as the private property of the masters, to the consumable falsified time of the spectacle — and then from time into space, where urbanism becomes the very technology of separation.

- [[Cyclical Time]] — the eternal-return time of static, myth-governed societies
- [[Irreversible Time]] — linear historical time born as the private property of the masters, generalized as labor time
- [[Irreversible time originates as the private property of the ruling class]] — the load-bearing thesis
- [[Spectacular Time]] · [[Pseudo-cyclical Time]] — the consumable, falsified time of the spectacle imitating natural rhythms

- [[Banalization]] · [[Pseudo-community]] · [[The Collapse of City and Country]] — the unification of space toward motionless monotony
