# Determinism, prediction, and the future

This pathway traces prediction as the capture of the future — the moment when computation stops describing tomorrow and starts producing it. It runs from O'Gieblyn's algorithm-as-providence (prediction reborn as predestination), through Han's charge that Big Data is blind to the event and pre-empts free will, to Zuboff's anatomy of a prediction economy that asserts a right to author your future tense — closing on the free-will/determinism axis itself. Curatorially it is the vault's spine for any project on forecasting, inevitability, agency, and the politics of the future: it pairs a theological reading of the black box with a political-economic one, so an exhibition can stage determinism as both a metaphysics and a market.
