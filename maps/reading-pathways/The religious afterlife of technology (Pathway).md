# The religious afterlife of technology

This pathway reads three of the vault's authors as theologians in disguise. O'Gieblyn's *God, Human, Animal, Machine* supplies the master diagnosis — that a disenchanted world recoded itself in computational terms, and that transhumanism is Christian eschatology in materialist dress, the immortal soul renamed "pattern." Harari names the resulting faiths outright (Dataism, Techno-Humanism) and narrates them with a diagnostician's irony; Kurzweil supplies the affirmative cosmology O'Gieblyn and Harari write against, a resurrection theology phrased as engineering. Curatorially, the sequence is useful for staging technology as secular religion — for any show or essay that wants to make the eschatological structure of "the algorithm-as-God" visible, and to hold the celebrant and the critic in the same frame.
