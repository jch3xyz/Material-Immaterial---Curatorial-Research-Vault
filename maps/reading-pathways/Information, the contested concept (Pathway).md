# Information, the contested concept

"Information" is the vault's most contested word: every author in the corpus uses it, and every one means something different. This pathway reads that single term across all eleven authors as a journey — from Han's positivized, theory-killing data, through Hayles's Shannon-Wiener pattern that "lost its body," O'Gieblyn's information-as-soul-substitute, Harari's data cosmology, Kurzweil's information-as-fundamental-reality, Haraway's universal translation into code, McLuhan's electric light as pure information, Burnham's systems-art key factor and Lippard's dematerialized "primary" idea-art, Ascott's connective data-flux, to Zuboff's axial principle of who-knows. Curatorially it is the vault's best instrument for showing that a word everyone shares can name a celebration, a diagnosis, a theology, and an economic weapon at once — useful for wall texts, panel framing, or any argument that turns on whether "information" affirms or empties the material world.

- [[Embodiment vs Information]] — the framing tension: the whole contest is whether information is a free-floating pattern or something that can only ever be enacted in a body.
- [[Hyperinformation]] — Han's opening move: the positivized, accumulating data-mass that does not converge on truth and ends theory; information as the friendly face of control.
- [[Pattern and Randomness]] — Hayles's Shannon-Wiener inheritance, the dialectic that displaces presence/absence; the technical core from which "information lost its body."
- [[The Body as Data]] — the recoding of the living particular into downloadable informational pattern; the hinge where Hayles's diagnosis meets every later author's dream of substrate-independence.
- [[Disembodied Information as the Soul]] — O'Gieblyn's theological reading: information as the secular substitute for the soul, the immortal pattern that survives the body.
- [[Order as Information That Fits a Purpose]] — Harari's cosmology in miniature: reality reread as data-processing, the universe as flow, the ground of Dataism.
- [[Freedom of Information]] — Harari's Dataist supreme value, prizing flow over substrate; information elevated from tool to creed.
- [[Patternism]] — Kurzweil's metaphysics: the self as a persisting pattern of information, information as the fundamental reality that matter merely instantiates.
- [[The World as a Problem of Coding]] — Haraway's universal translation, the common move of the communications sciences and modern biologies that recodes the world into a single language.
- [[The Informatics of Domination]] — Haraway's coded command-control grid; information as translation turned into a regime of power and its material substrate.
- [[The Light Bulb as Pure Information]] — McLuhan's clearest case: a medium that is pure information without content, the proof that the medium is the message.
- [[Systems Esthetics]] — Burnham's systems-art key factor: when sculpture migrates from object to system, information (not matter) becomes the determining term of the work.
- [[Dematerialization]] — Lippard's "primary information": the idea-art object emptied to the concept, the art-historical instance of information as the immaterial.
- [[The Datapool]] — Ascott's connective data-flux: information as the shared, living medium of network consciousness and the telematic embrace.
- [[The Division of Learning in Society]] — Zuboff's axial principle: who knows, who decides, who decides who decides — information as the contested ground of a new species of power.
- [[Information]] — the synthesis node where all eleven senses are gathered side by side; read last, it lets the celebration, the diagnosis, the theology, and the weapon be held in one view against the [[Material vs Immaterial]] axis.
