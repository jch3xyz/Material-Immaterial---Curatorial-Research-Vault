# The self, individualism, and its dissolution

This pathway traces the slow unmaking of the autonomous individual — the bounded, self-possessing, free-willing subject that liberal modernity took as its bedrock — across six authors who each remove one of its supports. It begins with the subject as a coherent inheritance, watches Han recast it as an exhausting self-project, sees Harari dissolve its free will into a biochemical fiction, follows Zuboff as market power liquidates the very conditions of individuality, and ends with Haraway and Ascott reframing the self's dispersal not as loss but as a distributed, networked, plural mode of being. Curatorially it is the vault's backbone for any project on identity, agency, autonomy, and the post-individual self — pairing the diagnostic (Han, Harari, Zuboff) with the affirmative (Haraway, Ascott) so the dissolution reads as both a wound and an opening.
