# Surveillance from the panopticon to Big Other

The surveillance thread runs the length of the vault, and this pathway follows it as a single argument about who watches, why, and to what end. It opens on Han's friendly, voluntary digital panopticon — surveillance that no longer needs coercion because we expose ourselves — passes through Harari's Dataism, which dissolves the human into a data source to be read, and arrives at Zuboff's full political economy of surveillance capitalism and Big Other, where lived experience is rendered into behavioral data and power shifts from watching to shaping. Along the way Kurzweil and Ascott supply the affirmative counterpoint: the same human–machine merger the others diagnose, here embraced as liberation. Curatorially this is the vault's spine for any project on data, prediction, and the future of the self — it lets surveillance be read across philosophy, history, and political economy rather than as a single author's claim.
