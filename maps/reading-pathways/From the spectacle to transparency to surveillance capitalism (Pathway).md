# From the spectacle to transparency to surveillance capitalism

The vault's ancestor→descendant spine. Debord's 1967 spectacle — a social relation mediated by images, capital become image, the spectator who lives less the more he contemplates — is the source text from which Han's transparency society and Zuboff's surveillance capitalism both descend. Debord cites the pornographic; Han names it as the spectacle's endpoint; Zuboff shows the same unilateral, administrator-concentrated media matured from managing appearance into managing behavior.
