# Systems Thinking (Meadows, Thinking in Systems)

Donella Meadows's *Thinking in Systems* is the vault's first native-discipline systems-dynamics primary source — the science that grounds the cybernetics the art/cybernetics books (Burnham, Ascott, Hayles) carry secondhand. The cluster runs from the definition of a system, through its stock-and-flow and feedback machinery, to why systems surprise us, the recurrent traps, and the leverage points where small immaterial changes shift everything.

## The system and its parts

- [[A System]] — interconnected things producing their own pattern of behavior over time
- [[Elements Interconnections and Purpose]] — the three constituents, ranked: elements < interconnections < purpose
- [[Structure Determines Behavior]] — the Slinky thesis: a system causes its own behavior

## Stocks, flows, and feedback

- [[Stocks and Flows]] — the stock-and-flow primitive (the bathtub); a stock is the memory of the history of changing flows
- [[Balancing Feedback Loop]] — the stabilizing, goal-seeking loop; parent of [[Homeostasis]]
- [[Reinforcing Feedback Loop]] — the amplifying, runaway loop; parent of [[Acceleration]] and [[The Law of Accelerating Returns]]
- [[Dynamic Equilibrium]] — the balanced-flow steady state (inflow equals outflow)
- [[Shifting Dominance]] — the strongest of several loops on one stock sets behavior
- [[Delays]] — pervasive strong determinants; a delay in a balancing loop causes oscillation
- [[Oscillation]] — the overshoot/undershoot a delay imposes on a balancing loop

## Growth and limits

- [[Overshoot and Collapse]] — the behavior modes of a growth system that overruns a limit
- [[The Limits-to-Growth Archetype]] — no physical system grows forever in a finite environment
- [[Stock-Limited vs Flow-Limited Resources]] — nonrenewable vs renewable
- [[Limiting Factor]] — Liebig's law of the minimum; growth changes the limit
- [[Layers of Limits]] — every physical entity is surrounded by limits
- [[The Systems Zoo]] — simple stock-and-flow systems whose behavior comes from feedback structure, not appearance

## Why systems work — and surprise us

- [[Resilience]] — the capacity to survive, persist, and bounce back; distinct from static stability
- [[Self-Organization]] — the capacity to make one's own structure more complex; distinct from [[Emergence]] and [[Autopoiesis]]
- [[Hierarchy (Systems)]] — nested subsystems that grant stability and reduce information load
- [[Suboptimization]] — a subsystem's goals dominating at the expense of the whole
- [[Mental Models]] — everything we know is a model, congruent with yet never the real world
- [[Bounded Rationality]] — reasonable decisions on incomplete, delayed information yield outcomes no one wants
- [[Nonlinearity]] — cause not proportional to effect; flips behavior modes
- [[No Separate Systems]] — boundaries are observer-drawn, not real
- [[Event Behavior Structure]] — events explain nothing; structure is the source

## System traps

- [[System Traps]] — the umbrella for the eight archetypes, each a trap and an opportunity
- [[Policy Resistance]] — the fixes-that-fail archetype
- [[The Tragedy of the Commons]] — a shared erodable resource with missing/weak feedback
- [[Drift to Low Performance]] — eroding goals / the boiled-frog syndrome
- [[Escalation]] — a reinforcing loop between competitors
- [[Success to the Successful]] — competitive exclusion; the rich-get-richer engine
- [[Shifting the Burden to the Intervenor]] — addiction/dependence; the intervention atrophies self-maintenance
- [[Rule Beating]] — evasive action honoring the letter not the spirit of a rule
- [[Seeking the Wrong Goal]] — a badly-defined goal makes the system produce the wrong result
