# Arguments — Marshall McLuhan

## Understanding Media

- [[The medium not its content is the message]] — the book's signature thesis: the form and scale of a medium, not its content, is the message
- [[The neutral-instrument view of technology is the numb stance of the technological idiot]] — the 'content' obsession as the somnambulism of the technological idiot
- [[All media must be studied together because any extension reorganizes the whole psychic and social field]] — the methodological premise of the survey
- [[Electric speed reverses explosion into implosion]] — mechanical fragmentation contracting into the electric nervous system
- [[Every medium has a break boundary triggered by cross-fertilization with another medium]] — Boulding's point of no return, released by the crossing of media
- [[Every extension of man is an autoamputation that numbs perception]] — the keystone physiological argument: extension as self-amputation under stress
- [[To perceive an extension of ourselves is necessarily to embrace it]] — the servomechanism trap: we worship and serve our own extensions
- [[Linguistic media shape social development as much as the means of production]] — the directly-stated subversion of the Marxian dialectic
- [[Media are make-happen agents not make-aware agents]] — media act on the whole field below the threshold of awareness
- [[All media are active metaphors that translate experience]] — every medium as a translator of experience into new forms
- [[Art is the only known immunity to technological trauma so the artist is indispensable to survival]] — the artist as integral awareness and cultural immune system
- [[We have leased our central nervous systems to private corporations]] — the surveillance/capture argument: the outered CNS as corporate property
- [[Electric implosion must be met by decentralism not expansion]] — the political corollary of the implosion thesis
- [[Only the phonetic alphabet not writing in general created the detribalized civilized individual]] — the alphabet, not writing as such, makes the visual civilized self
- [[The Western bias toward sequence as logic is a hidden effect of alphabetic technology]] — lineal logic as a media effect, not a law of thought
- [[Speed-up of information is the engine of social reorganization and past a threshold of breakdown]] — acceleration as the motor of social form, to the point of breakdown
- [[Spengler mistook a media effect for a metaphysical fate]] — The Decline of the West as a misread media-effect
- [[Electric light is the clearest proof that the medium is the message because light is information without content]] — pure information, a medium without content
- [[The shift from round to square architecture is a change in sense ratios not a stylistic accident]] — built form as a sensory, not stylistic, fact
- [[Money is a specialist visual technology underwritten by print literacy]] — money as a print-underwritten visual technology, arcing toward credit-information
- [[Abstract time was created by literacy reinforced by the clock not by the clock alone]] — the correction of Mumford: literacy, not the clock alone, makes abstract time
- [[Repeatability is the core of the mechanical principle that has dominated the West]] — exact repeatability as the single anchor of mechanization
- [[Detachment the power to act without reacting is the chief gift of typography and an embarrassment in the electric age]] — typographic detachment as gift and electric-age liability
- [[Urbanization was explosion not implosion]] — the explicit dispute with Mumford on the city
- [[Advertising will liquidate itself by its own success]] — at the terminus of programmed pre-established harmony, advertising self-liquidates
- [[Ads are the richest daily reflection a society ever made of itself]] — ad teams out-research sociologists; the acceptable ad dramatizes communal experience
- [[Games are extensions of the social man and are mass media]] — games extend social man, model a culture, and are therefore mass media
- [[Games are counter-irritants to the stress of specialist labor and stave off automatism]] — a society without games sinks into the zombie trance of automation
- [[Hitler's rise was a media effect of radio's form not its content]] — the formal (anti-Lazarsfeld) retribalization claim; a dated/dangerous causal assertion to attribute, not endorse
- [[Automation ends jobs and subjects but fuses work and learning]] — the structural argument anchoring automation as learning a living
- [[Automation liberates men into the role of artist]] — McLuhan's most utopian claim; cross-linked to its critics Han, Harari, and Lippard
