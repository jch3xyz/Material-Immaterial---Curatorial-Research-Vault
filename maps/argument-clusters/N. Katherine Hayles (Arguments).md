# Arguments — N. Katherine Hayles

## How We Became Posthuman

- [[The disembodiment of information was contingent not inevitable]] — reification won at the Macy Conferences through situated debate, not technological necessity
- [[The liberal subject and the posthuman both erase embodiment]] — the posthuman continues rather than disrupts the liberal privileging of mind over body
- [[Disembodiment is impossible there is only different embodiment]] — no presence in the world can be disembodied; embodiment can be destroyed but not replicated
- [[Conscious agency has never been in control]] — mastery through autonomous will is the story consciousness tells itself about emergent, distributed processes
- [[The posthuman is lethal only when grafted onto liberal humanism]] — Moravec's downloading fantasy expands rather than abandons the autonomous subject
- [[The strong claim of artificial life is constructed through narrative]] — codes are made to seem alive through anthropomorphic naming and speculative stories
- [[The disappearance of the body is the symptom of a new subjectivity]] — the body's discursive 'vanishing' is the materially-produced crossing of informatics and information
