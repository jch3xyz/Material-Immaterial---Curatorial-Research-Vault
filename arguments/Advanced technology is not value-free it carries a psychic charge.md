# Advanced technology is not value-free it carries a psychic charge

## Claim

Advanced technology is not innocent or value-free but carries a psychic charge, so that to interact with it is to change the psyche; and when the whole planet engages with a technology as widespread as telematics, a sea-change in world order can be expected.

## Expanded Explanation

In chapter 16 Ascott rejects the common assumption that a technology is a neutral tool, a mere instrument whose moral weight lies only in how it is used. For Ascott a technology is already charged before any particular use: it carries a "psychic charge" such that the very act of interacting with it reshapes the interior life — perception, cognition, the structure of mind itself. Technology is not external to consciousness; engaging it is already a transformation of consciousness.

From this premise Ascott draws a planetary consequence. [[Telematic Art|Telematics]] is not a marginal or specialist medium but a globe-spanning one. If a single interaction alters the psyche, then a technology in which "the whole planet engages" alters the collective psyche at scale — and so "a sea-change in world order can be expected." The argument moves from the individual nervous system to world order in one step, because for Ascott the network is precisely the apparatus by which individual minds are bound into a larger field of mind (see [[The Telematic Embrace]], [[Reciprocal Communication]]).

This is why, on the same page, Ascott insists that "much work must be done" to understand "the deep-rooted insights, the human meaning, implicit in this technology": if technology is value-laden, then its meanings are not optional features to be debated after the fact but are already operating, and the artist's task is to surface and shape them. The claim thus grounds Ascott's larger conviction that art is the right discipline for working at the level of [[New Media Art]] and [[Art as Experiential Technology]], where the medium is engaged precisely for what it does to mind.

## Reasoning Structure

1. A technology is not innocent or value-free — it carries a "psychic charge."
2. Because of that charge, to interact with the technology is already to change the psyche (not merely to accomplish an external task).
3. Telematics is a technology in which the whole planet engages.
4. Therefore a planet-wide psychic transformation follows, from which "a sea-change in world order can be expected."
5. Therefore the human meaning implicit in the technology must be actively understood and shaped rather than assumed neutral.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- The instrumental view of technology — that a tool is value-neutral and only its use carries moral weight — is exactly the position Ascott denies; it remains the standard counterposition. (McLuhan names this stance the "numb" reflex of "the technological idiot"; see [[The neutral-instrument view of technology is the numb stance of the technological idiot]].)
- The inference from individual psychic change to a "sea-change in world order" is asserted rather than argued; the magnitude and direction of the planetary effect are not specified or demonstrated within the passage. Label: `Strongly implied` for the planetary step.
- "Psychic charge" is metaphorical; the passage does not define a mechanism by which interaction alters the psyche, leaving the claim at the level of conviction and program rather than demonstration.

## Related Arguments

- [[The medium not its content is the message]] — McLuhan's foundational version of the same thesis: the form of a medium, not its content, is what reshapes us, so no medium is neutral. Ascott's "psychic charge" is the telematic-era restatement.
- [[The neutral-instrument view of technology is the numb stance of the technological idiot]] — McLuhan's direct refutation of the value-free-tool assumption that Ascott also rejects.

## Tensions

- [[Metaphor vs Literalism]] — the claim turns on technology being charged with meaning (metaphor, "human meaning implicit") rather than functioning as a literal, inert instrument.
- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — a value-laden, psyche-altering technology is more than its material substrate; its charge is immaterial (meaning, mind), consistent with the telematic shift from object to field.
