# Media are make-happen agents not make-aware agents

## Claim

Media operate below the threshold of consciousness — they are "make happen" agents but not "make aware" agents, "put out long before they are thought out," and their very externalization "tends to cancel the possibility of their being thought of at all"; McLuhan's whole project is to convert this make-happen into make-aware, "reducing these conflicts by an increase of human autonomy."

## Expanded Explanation

This is the methodological keystone of Chapter 5 and a recurring refrain of Part I, anchored here because it is the chapter that states it outright. Having argued that [[Media as Extensions of Man|media are extensions of man]] and that their crossings release [[Hybrid Energy|hybrid energy]] "as by fission or fusion," McLuhan draws the consequence that matters most for his method: "It has now been explained that media, or the extensions of man, are 'make happen' agents, but not 'make aware' agents." The effects are real and pervasive — they *happen* — but they are not accompanied by any native awareness of themselves. The medium does its work in the dark. `Directly stated`

The reason is temporal and structural. We externalize our faculties before we have understood them: "We can, if we choose, think things out before we put them out," but in fact we almost never do, because "they are put out long before they are thought out. In fact, their being put outside us tends to cancel the possibility of their being thought of at all." The act of putting an extension *outside* us is exactly the act that hides it from thought — the externalization is self-concealing. This is the same mechanism the vault tracks under [[Narcosis]] ("self-amputation forbids self-recognition") and behind the figure of [[Man as Servomechanism of His Extensions|man as servomechanism of his extensions]]: the user is shaped by what he cannot perceive. `Directly stated`

The argument is not fatalistic but programmatic — it states the task the book sets itself. There is "no blindness in these matters once we have been notified that there is anything to observe," and McLuhan closes the diagnosis with the book's governing hope: "The present book, in seeking to understand many media, the conflicts from which they spring, and the even greater conflicts to which they give rise, holds out the promise of reducing these conflicts by an increase of human autonomy." Understanding media is precisely the conversion of make-happen into make-aware; the reward of awareness is autonomy. This is the same ethical payoff that [[The Artist as Integral Awareness|the artist as integral awareness]] carries — the artist is the one who can "correct the sense ratios before the blow of new technology has numbed conscious procedures," i.e. who already operates in the make-aware register the rest of us must be raised to. The one naturally occurring break in the make-happen trance is the hybrid seam: "the parallel between two media holds us on the frontiers between forms that snap us out of the Narcissus-narcosis. The moment of the meeting of media is a moment of freedom and release from the ordinary trance and numbness imposed by them on our senses." `Directly stated`

## Reasoning Structure

1. Media are extensions of man whose effects on psyche and society are real and pervasive — they *make things happen*.
2. But those effects arrive without any accompanying awareness of the medium itself: media are "make happen" agents, not "make aware" agents.
3. The cause is that we externalize faculties before understanding them — "put out long before they are thought out" — and the externalization itself "cancels the possibility of their being thought of at all" (the self-concealing logic also named by [[Narcosis]]).
4. Therefore the default human relation to technology is unconscious servomechanism, not conscious use.
5. But blindness is not necessary "once we have been notified that there is anything to observe"; understanding can convert make-happen into make-aware.
6. Conclusion: the study of media "holds out the promise of reducing these conflicts by an increase of human autonomy" — awareness is the route to autonomy. (The hybrid seam and the artist's integral awareness are the two openings through which this awareness enters.)

## Evidence Used by the Author

- The Plato/Athens example: "the greatest school had been put out for human use before it had been thought out" — extensions precede their own understanding ([[raw/McLuhan - Understanding Media/08 Chapter 5 - Hybrid Energy - Les Liaisons Dangereuses]]).
- The chewing-gum/computer image of "data gathering" in which "each stick of chewing gum we reach for is acutely noted by some computer" — our private lives have "become information processes just because we have put our central nervous systems outside us in electric technology": externalization happening below awareness.
- The hybrid seam as the structural exception — "the meeting of media is a moment of freedom and release from the ordinary trance and numbness" — evidence that make-aware is possible, not just hoped for.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- The claim is asymmetrical and possibly self-undermining: if externalization "cancels the possibility of [media] being thought of at all," how can the book itself think them? McLuhan answers only that "there is none in these matters once we have been notified," and locates the genuine break narrowly — at the hybrid seam and in the rare [[The Artist as Integral Awareness|artist of integral awareness]] — which leaves it unclear whether broad, durable make-awareness is actually available to a culture or remains the privilege of a few. `Interpretive synthesis`
- "Reducing these conflicts by an increase of human autonomy" is asserted as a promise, not demonstrated; the chapter's own surrounding material (fission/fusion, the "A" bomb vs "H" bomb image of cultural implosion) dwells far more on the violence of make-happen than on any achieved make-aware. `Interpretive synthesis`

## Related Arguments

- [[The medium not its content is the message]] — the companion thesis: because the medium works unnoticed (make-happen), attention is captured by content while the medium reshapes us; both arguments locate the real action below awareness.
- [[The neutral-instrument view of technology is the numb stance of the technological idiot]] — O'Gieblyn's parallel diagnosis (after McLuhan) that treating a medium as a neutral tool is itself the numbed, make-happen stance this argument names.
- [[All media must be studied together because any extension reorganizes the whole psychic and social field]] — the methodological corollary: make-aware requires studying the whole field, since each extension's effect is hidden and systemic.
- [[Every medium has a break boundary triggered by cross-fertilization with another medium]] — the hybrid crossing is both a break boundary and the "moment of release" that momentarily makes a medium aware to us.

## Tensions

- [[Numbness vs Awareness]] — this argument is the methodological statement of that axis: media are make-happen (numb) by default, and McLuhan's project is the deliberate conversion to make-aware, with "an increase of human autonomy" as its stake.
