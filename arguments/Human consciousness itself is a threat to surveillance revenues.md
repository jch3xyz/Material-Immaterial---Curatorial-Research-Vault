# Human consciousness itself is a threat to surveillance revenues

## Claim

Because no autonomous judgment, consent, resistance, or self-regulation is possible without awareness, and behavior modification works precisely by evading awareness, surveillance capitalism structurally requires the suppression of awareness — making human consciousness itself an enemy of surveillance revenues.

## Expanded Explanation

In Chapter 10 ("Make Them Dance"), Zuboff turns from *what* [[Economies of Action]] do — actuate behavior toward guaranteed outcomes — to the precondition on which they depend: the dark. The Facebook emotional-contagion experiment and the leaked Australian ad-targeting document show that "tuning through suggestion" succeeds only when it operates "just beyond the rim of individual awareness." The contagion study's authors openly celebrate this, reporting that emotional states can be transferred "without their awareness"; the Australian operation pinpoints "the exact moment at which a young person needs a 'confidence boost' and is therefore most vulnerable." Crucially, the technique does not merely happen to be covert — for Zuboff "this evasion is neither accidental nor incidental, but actually essential to the structure of the whole surveillance capitalist project." *(Confidence: Directly stated.)*

The reason is a chain of dependencies. Awareness is "the necessary condition for the mobilization of cognitive and existential resources": "There is no autonomous judgment without awareness. Agreement and disagreement, participation and withdrawal, resistance or collaboration: none of these self-regulating choices can exist without awareness." Zuboff marshals self-regulation research showing that self-awareness is the foundation of empathy, volition, reflection, impulse control, and the capacity "to premeditate" — the single most important determinant of one's ability to resist persuasion. So the same faculty that grounds human autonomy is exactly the faculty that defeats [[Telestimulation]]. It follows that the competitive logic of behavior modification turns consciousness into an adversary: "human consciousness itself is a threat to surveillance revenues, as awareness endangers the larger project of behavior modification." *(Confidence: Directly stated.)*

This is the deepest stake of the [[The Means of Behavioral Modification|means of behavioral modification]]. Where industrial capitalism merely preferred compliant workers and consumers, surveillance capitalism is *structurally compelled* to "supplant autonomous action with heteronomous action" — to shift the locus of control over the future tense "from 'I will' to 'You will,'" exiling us "from our own behavior." Tuning also weaponizes the very capacities awareness sustains: it exploits empathy and the susceptibility to social influence ("I want to be like my friends") to manufacture the motivational conditions for actuation. The argument thus joins Zuboff's larger thesis — that surveillance capitalism is antithetical not only to democracy (which also "threatens surveillance revenues") but to the inner human conditions on which democratic and individual self-determination both rest. *(Confidence: Strongly implied — Zuboff explicitly pairs the democracy threat with the consciousness threat; reading the latter as the inner correlate of the former is a tight inference she invites.)*

## Reasoning Structure

1. Behavior modification by "tuning"/telestimulation succeeded in Facebook's experiments only by evading individual and group awareness (premise — evidence).
2. This evasion is essential, not incidental, to the structure of the surveillance capitalist project (premise — directly stated).
3. Awareness is the necessary condition for all self-regulating choices — autonomous judgment, agreement/disagreement, participation/withdrawal, resistance/collaboration — and for empathy, premeditation, and resistance to persuasion (premise — self-regulation research).
4. Therefore the faculty that grounds human autonomy (consciousness) is the same faculty that disables behavior modification.
5. Therefore awareness endangers behavior modification, and so human consciousness itself is a threat to surveillance revenues (conclusion — directly stated).
6. Therefore surveillance capitalists are competitively compelled to supplant autonomous (self-regulated) action with heteronomous (other-regulated) action — shifting control of the future tense from "I will" to "You will."

## Evidence Used by the Author

- The Facebook emotional-contagion study, whose own first paragraph celebrates transferring emotional states "without their awareness" — [[Telestimulation]] confirmed at scale.
- The leaked 2017 Australian/New Zealand Facebook document, which leverages "psychological insights" on 6.4 million young people to target moments of maximum vulnerability — [[Economies of Action]] feeding on [[Behavioral Surplus]].
- Self-regulation research (Wagner & Heatherton) holding that "the primary purpose of self awareness is to enable self-regulation," and that "every threat to human autonomy begins with an assault on awareness."
- Cambridge "susceptibility to persuasion" research: "the ability to premeditate" and conscious commitment are the top determinants of resistance to persuasion.
- The etymological contrast of *autonomy* ("regulation by the self") with *heteronomy* ("regulation by others"), which Zuboff maps onto the surveillance-capitalist substitution.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- The measured emotional-contagion effect was, by Zuboff's own report, "measurable, if weak" — a skeptic could argue the manipulative power, and thus the threat consciousness poses to it, is overstated. Zuboff's reply is structural and prospective: the *imperative* and the granular Australian effort show the trajectory, not the present ceiling.
- "Human consciousness itself is a threat to surveillance revenues" is a claim about the *logic* of the business model, not an allegation that any single firm sets out to abolish consciousness; the threat is to *revenues*, and the response is the evasion of awareness rather than its destruction.
- Self-regulation/persuasion research is enlisted as supporting evidence; the inference that surveillance capital is therefore *structurally* anti-consciousness is Zuboff's synthesis, drawing the empirical literature into her own frame.
