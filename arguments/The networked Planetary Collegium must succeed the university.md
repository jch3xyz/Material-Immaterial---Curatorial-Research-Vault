# The networked Planetary Collegium must succeed the university

## Claim

By the same historical logic of succession that moved the centre of learning from the church to the secular university, the bricks-and-mortar university must now give way to the networked, post-institutional [[The Planetary Collegium]] — a moist successor set in cyberspace and moistmedia, invested in behaviour and communication rather than physical real estate.

## Expanded Explanation

Ascott frames the future of learning as a problem of historical succession rather than reform. Twice he draws the same analogy: just as the church-as-centre-of-learning had to yield to the secular university, and just as the mediaeval monastery gave way to the university, so the university must now yield to forms of learning set within cyberspace and [[Moistmedia]]. The succeeding institution is not a building but a network. He calls it [[The Planetary Collegium]], a model "developed, tested, and proven within the limits of the university framework" that is now ready to "cut loose from the Mothership" as long-established institutions become "culturally redundant, commercially ineffective, and financially unviable."

The decisive contrast is between two kinds of investment. The twentieth-century university located its value "at the level of bricks and mortar," in "physical real estate"; the Planetary Collegium relocates that value to "behaviour and communication" and to an "interspatial cyber estate" with "fluid architecture, networked organisation and lateral structure." This is the educational instance of Ascott's broader [[Material vs Immaterial]] / [[Object vs System]] spine: as art moves from the field of objects to the field of behaviour, so learning moves from the physical campus to the post-institutional network. He pointedly dismisses "distance-learning" curricula as a half-measure that "hardly begin[s] to address this central problem of the university in the telematic age" — bolting a network onto a building is not the succession he means.

Because telematic culture is bottom-up, emergent, and connectivist, the successor institution must be too: not a digitized lecture hall but a "lateral structure," a network of nodes whose pedagogy is [[Network Consciousness]] and [[Cyberception]] rather than the transmission of object-knowledge. The succession is thus inseparable from Ascott's larger wager that telematic networking is a quantum leap in human consciousness — the Collegium is the institution proper to that leap.

## Reasoning Structure

1. Premise (historical pattern): The centre of learning has migrated by institutional succession — church → secular university; monastery → university.
2. Premise (current redundancy): In the telematic age, the bricks-and-mortar university is becoming culturally redundant, commercially ineffective, and financially unviable.
3. Premise (relocation of value): Educational value has shifted from physical real estate (bricks and mortar) to behaviour, communication, and an interspatial cyber estate.
4. Premise (insufficiency of reform): "Distance-learning" merely extends the old model and does not address the central problem.
5. Conclusion: Therefore the university must give way to a networked, post-institutional successor — the Planetary Collegium, set in cyberspace and moistmedia.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- The succession is asserted by analogy, not demonstrated; the church → university and monastery → university parallels may not entail that networks must replace (rather than supplement) campuses.
- Ascott ties the Collegium's future to "the new transnational marketplace of e-commerce" and to investment, IPOs, and start-ups — making post-institutional learning dependent on the very corporate-capital forces that other arguments in this book warn must be resisted (cf. [[The creative potential of telematic networks depends on artists resisting corporate and military colonisation]]).
- The claim is millennial and prophetic (written amid the dot-com "hysterical climate"); the predicted obsolescence of the bricks-and-mortar university has not, in the event, arrived as cleanly as forecast.
- "Behaviour and communication" as the locus of educational value is programmatic; how accreditation, embodiment, and care translate into a "fluid architecture" is left to be worked out.
