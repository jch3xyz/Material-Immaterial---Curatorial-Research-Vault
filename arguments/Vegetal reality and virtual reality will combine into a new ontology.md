# Vegetal reality and virtual reality will combine into a new ontology

## Claim

Ascott argues that the psychoactive plant — a "very ancient technology" — inserted into art at the edge of the Net will fuse with virtual reality so that vegetal reality and virtual reality combine to create a new ontology, dissolving the divide between inner and outer space into a single order of cosmography.

## Expanded Explanation

The argument falls within Ascott's "Moist Manifesto," whose program states that "MOIST REALITY combines Virtual Reality with Vegetal Reality" and that the moist domain is the "interspace" where "dry pixels and wet molecules converge." Into the complexity of art exploring consciousness and artificial life at the edge of the Net, Ascott foresees the insertion of "a new but very ancient technology, that of the psychoactive plant." The shamanic vine ayahuasca becomes the seed of a "cyberbotany," and its psychoactivity is treated not as escape but as an instrument coordinate with the network — a way of bringing inner (psychic) and outer (cyber) space into one field. This positions the plant as a technology of consciousness on equal footing with the digital, making [[New Media Art]] inseparable from a biological and "spiritually numinous" dimension.

The ontological wager is that these two realities are not merely juxtaposed but synthesized: a "new ontology" in which the digital and the vegetal, the virtual and the actual, are no longer separable domains. Ascott grounds the claim in his ayahuascatec.net project, designed "to explore this field," and in the figure of a "double consciousness" that "simultaneously constructs and navigates a world that is at once virtual and actual." The ayahuasceros are imagined as employing "their double consciousness to navigate both psychic space and cyberspace," linking disembodied entities, human users, and artificial agents across the Net. This is Ascott's culminating extension of art understood as [[Art as Experiential Technology]] — technology not as instrument upon the world but as the means of bringing a new world, and a new kind of self, into existence.

## Reasoning Structure

1. The convergence of the digital ("dry") and the biological ("wet") opens a "moist" interspace of potentiality.
2. Into this field Ascott inserts the psychoactive plant — "a new but very ancient technology" — giving rise to a cyberbotany (e.g. ayahuasca, "the vine of the soul").
3. Therefore vegetal reality and virtual reality will combine to create a new ontology, and inner/psychic space and outer/cyber space coalesce into one cosmography.
4. The double consciousness of the ayahuascatec.net practitioner is the subject who constructs and navigates this single virtual-and-actual world.

## Evidence Used by the Author

- The "Moist Manifesto" program statements (e.g. "MOIST REALITY combines Virtual Reality with Vegetal Reality"; "MOIST MEDIA is interactive and psychoactive"; "MOIST ENGINEERING embraces ontology") — see [[New Media Art]].
- The cyberbotany passage naming ayahuasca (Banisteriopsis caapi), "the vine of the soul," as the instrument of the new ontology.
- The ayahuascatec.net project as the concrete vehicle for exploring vegetal–virtual convergence.
- The "double consciousness" that navigates a world "at once virtual and actual," and the footnote describing ayahuasceros navigating "both psychic space and cyberspace" telematically and telepathically toward a "Psibernetic Arch."

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- The argument is offered as visionary "contention" and manifesto, "a textual portal to the future" with "no end or closure," not as demonstrated result — Ascott labels his method "visionary pragmatism," which embraces speculation.
- The synthesis depends on accepting psychoactivity and "psi-perception" as cognitive technologies on par with the digital; skeptics would treat the "spiritually numinous" and "telepathic" claims as unfalsifiable rather than ontological.
- The "new ontology" is asserted rather than specified; what it would mean for vegetal and virtual reality to be metaphysically one (rather than experientially combined for a subject) is left open.
