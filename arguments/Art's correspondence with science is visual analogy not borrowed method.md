# Art's correspondence with science is visual analogy not borrowed method

## Claim

Modern art's apparent kinship with science is a visual correspondence pulled "from the air" by perceptive innovators, not a method rationally borrowed from scientific sources.

## Expanded Explanation

By Chapter 6 of *Beyond Modern Sculpture*, [[Jack Burnham]] has watched the grid-and-field idiom of the New Tendency converge so closely on the imagery of modern physics that the temptation is to read one as the rational derivative of the other. He resists this. The field as "a repetitive set of elements" had already saturated environmental design — the "redundant array of solid-state components," the "sameness that prevails over glass and steel facades," the raster of computer display boards — and "Crude and distant though this image is from the theories of gravitation constructed by Einstein, it served to bring the invisible world of energy dynamics ... back to the level of visual comprehension." The resemblance is real, but it is a resemblance of *appearance*, not of derivation.

The decisive statement follows: "As intoxicating as it is to tie New Tendency field configurations to Einsteinian mechanics, the analogy stops short of physical reality and rests upon visual correspondences" (`Directly stated`). Burnham then specifies the mechanism by which such correspondences arise, and it is the opposite of borrowing: "In most instances it would seem that the innovators of an art style — usually extraordinarily perceptive beings — pull them from the air, but in any case do not borrow them from scientific sources as a matter of rational decision." The artist does not study relativity and then build a model of it; a style emerges, is "contagious," spreads — and the analogy to science is recognized only afterward.

This is the kinetic-chapter instantiation of Burnham's larger [[Reification]] thesis. Reification is "the constant resynchronization of artistic sensibility with a disclosed form-world of scientific theory" — but re-synchronization is precisely *not* citation. The sculptor seized [[The Scientific Model]] as an "exotic reservoir" of pure form, not as a body of method to be applied; the form-world of science enters art through the perceptive sensibility, below the level of rational decision. The correspondence is genuine and consequential, yet it runs through the eye and the era's collective sensibility, not through the laboratory.

## Reasoning Structure

1. The New Tendency's field-and-grid configurations visibly resemble the field conceptions of modern physics (Maxwell, Einsteinian space-time, wave-particle duality).
2. But the resemblance "stops short of physical reality and rests upon visual correspondences" — it is appearance, not physical or rational identity (and "many artists today will deny any psychical or physical connection between the two").
3. The innovators of a style "pull them from the air" and "do not borrow them from scientific sources as a matter of rational decision"; the style spreads by contagion after a period of incubation.
4. Vasarely confirms the temporal order from the inside: the analogy to physics is noticed *after* the plastic accomplishment, never consciously sought before it.
5. Therefore art's correspondence with science is a visual analogy carried by the perceptive sensibility (the engine of [[Reification]]), not a method rationally borrowed — re-synchronization, not citation.

## Evidence Used by the Author

- The field as "a repetitive set of elements" already pervades design (solid-state components, curtain-wall facades, raster boards); the New Tendency grid is "Crude and distant ... from the theories of gravitation constructed by Einstein," yet brings the invisible energy-world "back to the level of visual comprehension" (raw Chapter 6 — [[raw/Burnham - Beyond Modern Sculpture/08 Chapter 6 - Kineticism - The Unrequited Art]]).
- **Vasarely**: "devoured" works on relativity, wave mechanics, and cybernetics, yet "I have never consciously found my inspiration in the exact sciences. It is after the plastic accomplishment that I noted the existence of certain analogies." The analogy is retrospective, not the source.
- **Jesús-Rafael Soto**: "Not that Soto has ever appropriated any of the physicist's methodology, but his thought processes ... correspond very closely to that of the atomic scientist." Soto's own *World of Relations* statement — "Relations exclude the idea of the void. Relations have an autonomous existence" — is offered as a visual/intuitive parallel to field physics, not a borrowing of its method.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- Burnham himself concedes the correspondences are real and "difficult to say" precisely how they arise — the claim is about *route* (visual/intuitive vs. rational borrowing), not about whether the parallel exists. Soto "perfectly understood the laws and visual implications of field effects," so the line between informed intuition and borrowed method is not perfectly clean.
- Limited to the New Tendency / kinetic case; Burnham elsewhere argues that Constructivist dematerialization *did* derive from "the conceptual models of science" ([[Constructivist sculpture dematerialized its support from the conceptual models of science]]), so the strength of the "visual analogy not borrowed method" claim varies by movement — here the analogy is loosest (Einsteinian mechanics), there the model is a more direct formal reservoir.
- Vasarely's testimony is a single artist's retrospective self-report and could rationalize an influence after the fact rather than disprove it.
