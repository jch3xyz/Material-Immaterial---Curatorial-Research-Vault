# The world is being translated into a problem of coding

## Claim

Communications sciences and modern biologies are built by a single structural move — the translation of the world into a problem of coding — that searches for a common language in which all resistance to instrumental control disappears and the organism ceases to exist as such, surviving only as a "biotic component," a special kind of information-processing device.

## Expanded Explanation

For Haraway, the [[The Informatics of Domination]] — the "scary new networks" that replace the "comfortable old hierarchical dominations" — is not merely a list of historical transitions but rests on one underlying operation. Two seemingly distinct fields, the engineering of communication and the new molecular life sciences, are "constructed by a common move—the translation of the world into a problem of coding." The point of this move is *universal translatability*: once any object, body, or relation is rendered as code, it can be "submitted to disassembly, reassembly, investment, and exchange." Coding is thus the technical precondition of total instrumental control, because in a fully coded world there is, by design, no heterogeneity left that could resist being processed `(Directly stated)`.

Haraway works the claim out twice, symmetrically. In the communications sciences the move appears as cybernetic systems theory, where "the key operation is determining the rates, directions, and probabilities of flow of a quantity called information," and where [[Information]] is precisely "that kind of quantifiable element (unit, basis of unity) that allows universal translation, and so unhindered instrumental power." In modern biology the same move appears as molecular genetics and immunobiology, where "the organism has been translated into problems of genetic coding and readout" and biotechnology operates as "a writing technology." The consequence is ontological, not merely methodological: "In a sense, organisms have ceased to exist as objects of knowledge, giving way to biotic components, i.e., special kinds of information-processing devices." Haraway compresses the biological case into a single figure — "Biology here is a kind of cryptography" — making coding the literal substance of what life now *is* for knowledge `(Directly stated)`.

This is the operation that licenses the rest of the informatics of domination. Once the world is code, "control strategies" can "concentrate on boundary conditions and interfaces, on rates of flow across boundaries—and not on the integrity of natural objects." "Integrity" or "sincerity" of the Western self gives way to "decision procedures and expert systems"; human beings, "like any other component or subsystem, must be localized in a system architecture whose basic modes of operation are probabilistic, statistical." Crucially, "No objects, spaces, or bodies are sacred in themselves; any component can be interfaced with any other if the proper standard, the proper code, can be constructed for processing signals in a common language." Coding is therefore the dissolver of the sacred and the boundary alike — the move by which the difference "between machine and organism is thoroughly blurred." This is the structural root of the vault's [[The Cyborg]], and the term "informatics" Haraway coins here is the one [[N. Katherine Hayles]] later borrows `(Directly stated)`.

## Reasoning Structure

1. Two distinct domains — communications sciences and modern biologies — are constructed by one and the same move.
2. That move is the translation of the world into a problem of coding: a search for a common language.
3. A common code makes everything universally translatable, hence submittable to "disassembly, reassembly, investment, and exchange."
4. Universal translatability removes any heterogeneity that could resist instrumental control ("all resistance to instrumental control disappears").
5. Applied to life, the move converts the organism into a "biotic component," an "information-processing device" — so organisms "have ceased to exist as objects of knowledge."
6. Conclusion: coding is the structural operation underwriting the informatics of domination; control then works on rates, boundaries, and interfaces rather than on the integrity of "natural objects," and nothing is "sacred in itself."

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- Haraway is *not* endorsing the coding worldview as truth; she describes it as the structure of domination she means to inhabit and contest. The cyborg is offered as an ironic, oppositional figure born inside this same condition, so the argument doubles as both diagnosis and the ground of resistance — "double vision" rather than a thesis about how nature really is `(Strongly implied)`.
- She explicitly resists technological determinism: the move is enforced by "a historical system depending on structured relations among people," not by the machines themselves — "we are not dealing with a technological determinism" `(Directly stated)`. The reduction to code is a political construction, hence in principle contestable.
- The claim is asserted as a sweeping structural homology across very different sciences; the verbatim support is illustrative (telephone technology, weapons deployment, molecular genetics, immunobiology) rather than an exhaustive demonstration that *every* science performs the move `(Interpretive synthesis)`.

## Related Arguments

- [[The disembodiment of information was contingent not inevitable]] — Hayles supplies the genealogy Haraway presupposes: information became a disembodied, quantifiable pattern through situated debate (the Macy Conferences, Shannon over MacKay), so the "common language" of code that Haraway treats as the structure of domination was itself a contestable historical choice.
- [[We are not our bodies but a pattern of information that persists through material turnover]] — Kurzweil's patternism is the celebratory endpoint of the same move Haraway diagnoses: where she warns that the organism is reduced to a coded "information-processing device," he embraces the human-as-pattern as liberation.

## Tensions

- [[Material vs Immaterial]] — the move *is* the operation of immaterialization: it lifts the world off its material substrate into transferable code, so that "the boundary-maintaining images of base and superstructure, public and private, or material and ideal never seemed more feeble."
- [[Embodiment vs Information]] — translating the organism into "a problem of coding" is precisely the subordination of embodiment to information that this tension names; the body survives only as "biotic component."
