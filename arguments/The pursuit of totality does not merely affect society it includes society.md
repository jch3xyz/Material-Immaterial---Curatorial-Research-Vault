# The pursuit of totality does not merely affect society it includes society

## Claim

Because [[The Prediction Imperative|the prediction imperative]] mandates a continuous approximation to total information, the drive toward totality cannot stop at extracting data *from* society; it necessarily annexes "society," "social relations," and key societal processes themselves as a fresh terrain for [[Rendition]] and modification, so that society is not merely affected by the pursuit of totality but is *included* in it as one more object.

## Expanded Explanation

In the chapter's pivotal turn, Zuboff argues that the totalizing logic she has tracked through the online world now crosses a threshold. The pursuit of certainty — "the mandate of the prediction imperative" — "requires a continuous approximation to total information as the ideal condition for [[Machine Intelligence|machine intelligence]]." Having already enlarged its scope "from the virtual to the real world," the apparatus reaches the point where, "as the reality business intensifies, the pursuit of totality necessarily leads to the annexation of 'society,' 'social relations,' and key societal processes as a fresh terrain for rendition, calculation, modification, and prediction." The decisive shift is from society as the *source* of [[Behavioral Surplus|behavioral surplus]] to society as the *object* — the thing rendered. This is what the section title names: "Totality Includes Society."

Zuboff grounds this with an inventory of the surveillance capitalist CEOs' own self-description — "like generals delivering a chest-thumping tally of their armies." Nadella's roll call of Windows, Office, and Cortana users; Pichai's billion-user "products and platforms" and his "we must go deeper"; Larry Page's wish to make "people" a "first class object in search"; and Zuckerberg's frankness about "'society,' not just the individuals within it, as subordinate to Facebook's embrace" together evidence that the object of the project has become society as such. From these inventories Zuboff draws the load-bearing inference: "This pursuit, it becomes clear, does not merely have consequences for society; it includes society." The corollary is Page's stated goal of "people as a first class object" and Nadella's "first-class thing in the cloud" — the human and the social tie reconceived as computational objects in [[Big Other]].

The mechanism by which society is *included* rather than merely affected is the migration of *machine relations* into the place of social relations. Nadella's smart factory — where "every machine is the same machine marching to the same song," herded to preestablished norms in an "all-out war on accidents, mistakes, and randomness" — is, Zuboff says, "the proving ground for his social vision." It models a "collective mind — a hive mind" in which each element "operates in concert with every other element." The factory is "the economic petri dish in which a new division of learning mutates into life, ready for translation to society," exactly as the industrial division of labor once migrated from workplace to society. The conclusion is the chapter's image of the human [[The Hive|hive]]: "the machine hive becomes the role model for a new human hive in which we march in peaceful unison toward the same direction based on the same 'correct' understanding in order to construct a world free of mistakes, accidents, and random messes." Society is included in the totality because society is to be re-engineered in the image of the machine system.

## Reasoning Structure

1. Premise: The prediction imperative mandates certainty, which "requires a continuous approximation to total information."
2. Premise: This drive has already migrated "from the virtual to the real world," rendering "all people, things, and processes as computational objects."
3. Premise: As "the reality business intensifies," the only remaining frontier is the social itself, so the pursuit of totality "necessarily leads to the annexation of 'society,' 'social relations,' and key societal processes."
4. Premise (evidence): The CEO inventories (Nadella, Pichai, Page, Zuckerberg) treat society — not merely individuals — as the object: people as "a first class object," society as "subordinate to Facebook's embrace."
5. Premise: The vehicle of annexation is the transposition of *machine relations* (the "hive mind" factory) onto *social relations*, just as the industrial division of labor once migrated from workplace to society.
6. Conclusion: Therefore the pursuit of totality "does not merely have consequences for society; it includes society" — society becomes a terrain for rendition and modification, refashioned as a human hive.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- The CEO statements are promotional rhetoric; Zuboff herself calls the "magical age" discourse a "minesweeper" and "no mere hogwash." A critic could argue she reads strategic ambition as accomplished fact — the inventories show intent and reach, not that society has *already* been annexed. Zuboff's reply is that the rhetoric is itself an instrument that legitimates and advances the totalistic project.
- The factory-to-society transposition is an analogy (the industrial division of labor → the division of learning); whether social relations can in fact be engineered like networked machines is an empirical question Zuboff asserts as a tendency rather than demonstrates as accomplished.
- Zuboff distinguishes this annexation sharply from totalitarian totality — "instrumentarianism and totalitarianism are distinct species, they each yearn toward totality... in profoundly different ways." Conflating the two (Weiser's "make totalitarianism... seem like sheerest anarchy") would misname the power; the "includes society" claim is about market-driven, not violence-driven, totality. (See [[Instrumentarian Power vs Totalitarianism]].)
- The step from "machine relations as template" to a realized "human hive" is `Strongly implied` by the chapter's argument and imagery rather than presented as completed fact.

## Tensions

- [[The Individual vs The Hive]] — the human hive forfeits "individual freedom... to collective knowledge and action"; including society means dissolving the individual into the confluent whole.
- [[Instrumentarian Power vs Totalitarianism]] — both "yearn toward totality" but by distinct means; the argument names instrumentarian totality precisely to keep it from being mistaken for the totalitarian kind.
- [[Subject vs Object]] — to "include" society is to recast people, relationships, and social processes as "computational objects" and "a first class object" — the subject of social life rendered as an object of the system.
