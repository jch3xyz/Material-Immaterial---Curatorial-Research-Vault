# The shift from round to square architecture is a change in sense ratios not a stylistic accident

## Claim

The historical change from round, nomadic dwellings (the tent, the wigwam, the igloo) to the square house is not a matter of taste or style but a reorganization of the ratio among the senses: the round dwelling is a tactile-kinetic space that follows dynamic lines of force, while the square encloses an abstracted *visual* space that can be conceived only once the phonetic alphabet has specialized the senses and fragmented human work.

## Expanded Explanation

McLuhan takes up a fact anthropologists had long recorded but never accounted for — that "men live in round houses until they become sedentary and specialized in their work organization" — and supplies the missing cause through media analysis. For him the form of a dwelling registers the dominant ratio of the senses in a culture. The tent, wigwam, igloo, and cave are *not* enclosed or visual spaces; they "follow dynamic lines of force, like a triangle," and so retain a tactile, kinetic pressure. They are the architecture of the integral, participatory life of food-gathering communities, in which tribal man "had freely extended the form of his body to include the universe."

The square is a different order of space altogether. "A square is the enclosure of a visual space; that is, it consists of space properties abstracted from manifest tensions." Where the triangle "follows lines of force," the square "moves beyond such kinetic pressures to enclose visual space relations, while depending upon diagonal anchors." This abstraction of the visual *from* direct tactile and kinetic pressure is not a free invention; it "occurs only when men have learned to practice specialization of their senses, and fragmentation of their work skills." The phonetic alphabet is the engine of that specialization: "once men have adopted the visual dynamic of the phonetic alphabet," they begin to lose the tribal obsession with cosmic order embodied in the body and its extensions, and instead favor "separateness and compartmented spaces." The square room therefore "speaks the language of the sedentary specialist," while the round hut "tells of the integral nomadic ways of food-gathering communities."

The same logic, McLuhan argues, unlocks "a great many enigmas, past and present," including the architectural shift from the circular dome to the Gothic — "a change occasioned by alteration in the ratio or proportion of the sense lives in the members of a society." Style follows sensorium: a new extension of the body in social technology "sets up a new equilibrium among all of the senses and faculties," producing a "new outlook." The building is thus read as a medium whose form is its message.

## Reasoning Structure

1. The form of a dwelling encodes the dominant ratio of the senses in a culture (housing is a medium, an extension of the body's heat-control mechanism).
2. Round/nomadic dwellings (tent, wigwam, igloo, cave) are tactile-kinetic, not visual: they "follow dynamic lines of force, like a triangle" and retain manifest tension.
3. The square is the enclosure of a *visual* space — "space properties abstracted from manifest tensions" — i.e., the visual abstracted away from direct tactile and kinetic pressure.
4. This abstraction "occurs only when men have learned to practice specialization of their senses, and fragmentation of their work skills," a specialization driven by adopting "the visual dynamic of the phonetic alphabet."
5. Therefore the round-to-square change is caused by a shift in sense ratios (the rise of the visual under alphabetic literacy), not by stylistic preference — which is exactly the cause anthropologists missed.
6. The same cause explains the circular-dome-to-Gothic shift, since that too follows an "alteration in the ratio or proportion of the sense lives" of a society.

## Evidence Used by the Author

- The anthropological observation itself: "Men live in round houses until they become sedentary and specialized in their work organization. Anthropologists have often noted this change from round to square without knowing its cause." (`[[raw/McLuhan - Understanding Media/17 Chapter 13 - Housing - New Look and New Outlook]]`)
- The contrast of spatial types: "A tent or a wigwam is not an enclosed or visual space. Neither is a cave nor a hole in the ground. These kinds of space ... are not 'enclosed' in the visual sense because they follow dynamic lines of force, like a triangle."
- The definition of the square as abstracted visual space, set against the triangle that "follows lines of force."
- The phonetic alphabet as the cause of sensory specialization: "Once men have adopted the visual dynamic of the phonetic alphabet, they begin to lose the tribal man's obsession with cosmic order ... Indifference to the cosmic, however, fosters intense concentration on minute segments and specialist tasks."
- The extension to architectural history: the change "from circular-dome architecture to gothic forms, a change occasioned by alteration in the ratio or proportion of the sense lives in the members of a society."
- The igloo case study, showing how "the intensification of a single factor" (artificial heat from the white man's portable stove) introduces a new pattern into an ancient way of life — a model for how a new extension resets the equilibrium of the senses.

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- McLuhan himself flags the fragility of the case: "This entire discussion is offered at considerable risk of misapprehension because these are, spatially, highly technical matters," and he concedes the explanation "will not be obvious to people of visual culture."
- The argument is mono-causal where the historical record is multi-causal: climate, available materials, sedentism, agriculture, and population density are conventional explanations for round-to-square change that McLuhan subordinates to a single sensory cause. The igloo example even concedes a material trigger (artificial heat) before reading it as a sense-ratio shift.
- The correlation of "square" with "visual/alphabetic" and "round" with "tactile/tribal" is asserted as a structural homology more than demonstrated empirically; it is `Strongly implied` as a reading rather than independently verified.
