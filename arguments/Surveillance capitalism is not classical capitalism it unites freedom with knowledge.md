# Surveillance capitalism is not classical capitalism it unites freedom with knowledge

## Claim

Classical capitalism rests on the premise that the market is intrinsically unknowable, so freedom of action is justified by the actor's ignorance of the whole; surveillance capitalism reverses this by driving toward total knowledge, replacing mystery with certainty, and thereby fusing freedom and knowledge in a single unprecedented accumulation of power — which is exactly why surveillance capitalists "know too much to qualify for freedom."

## Expanded Explanation

Zuboff frames the question of whether surveillance capitalism is "merely 'capitalism'" by isolating its first departure from market capitalism's history: "it insists on the privilege of unfettered freedom *and* knowledge." Classical theory, she argues, grants capitalists wide-ranging freedom of action precisely *because* the market is held to be unknowable. She traces this to "two bedrock assumptions about capitalism made by its own theorists: The first is that markets are intrinsically unknowable. The second is that the ignorance produced by this lack of knowledge requires wide-ranging freedom of action for market actors." Freedom, on this account, is the practical consequence of ignorance — the two are "twin born," "two sides of the same coin called mystery." (`Directly stated`)

The lineage Zuboff cites is Adam Smith and Friedrich Hayek. Smith's "invisible hand" describes a "staggeringly complex pattern, a mystery that no one person or entity could hope to know or understand, let alone to direct." Hayek radicalizes the point, proposing that Smith's invisible hand "had perhaps better have been described as an invisible or unsurveyable pattern," and concluding that because the relevant information flows lie "beyond the span of the control of any one mind," individuals "must freely choose their own pursuits because there is no alternative, no source of total knowledge or conscious control to guide them." Freedom is mandatory *because* total knowledge is impossible. This is the same structure named in [[Freedom Is Ignorance]]. (`Directly stated`)

Surveillance capitalism breaks this link. Its competitive dynamics produce a "compulsion toward totality": "Total information tends toward certainty and the promise of guaranteed outcomes." In Zuboff's words, it "replaces mystery with certainty as it substitutes rendition, behavioral modification, and prediction for the old 'unsurveyable pattern.'" The market is no longer invisible, and so the classical justification for freedom dissolves. What emerges instead is "an unprecedented convergence of freedom and knowledge," whose scope "corresponds exactly to the scope of [[Instrumentarian Power]]." Surveillance capitalists "claim the freedom to order knowledge, and then they leverage that knowledge advantage in order to protect and expand their freedom" — a self-reinforcing cycle that hijacks [[The Division of Learning in Society]]. The normative conclusion follows: the cycle "will be broken only when we acknowledge ... that surveillance capitalists know too much to qualify for freedom." (`Directly stated`)

## Reasoning Structure

1. Classical capitalism assumes the market is intrinsically unknowable (Smith's invisible hand; Hayek's "unsurveyable pattern").
2. From that assumption it derives a normative entitlement: because no one can know the whole, market actors must be free to act — freedom is the corollary of ignorance.
3. Freedom and ignorance are therefore "twin born," "two sides of the same coin called mystery."
4. Surveillance capitalism's competitive struggle produces a compulsion toward totality; total information tends toward certainty and guaranteed outcomes.
5. It therefore replaces mystery with certainty — substituting rendition, behavioral modification, and prediction for the unsurveyable pattern.
6. This severs the link between freedom and ignorance and substitutes a new "convergence of freedom and knowledge," scaled to instrumentarian power.
7. Conclusion: because the original justification for capitalist freedom was ignorance, and surveillance capitalists are not ignorant, "surveillance capitalists know too much to qualify for freedom."

## Evidence Used by the Author

- The two "bedrock assumptions" of capitalism's own theorists — markets are intrinsically unknowable, and that ignorance requires wide freedom of action. (Ch. 18, [[raw/Zuboff - The Age of Surveillance Capitalism/20 Chapter 18 - A Coup from Above]])
- Adam Smith's "invisible hand" as a "mystery that no one person or entity could hope to know or understand, let alone to direct."
- Friedrich Hayek's reframing of the invisible hand as an "invisible or unsurveyable pattern," and his insistence that "Human design" is impossible because information flows are "beyond the span of the control of any one mind."
- Hayek choosing the market over democracy as "the only one compatible with freedom," deriding the "termite state" as incompatible with human freedom — the same hive figure surveillance capitalism's collectivism now emulates (see [[The Hive]], [[Radical Indifference]]).
- The "compulsion toward totality" of [[Behavioral Futures Markets]], which "replaces mystery with certainty."

## Counterarguments and Limitations

- A defender of the firms could note Zuboff's own concession that "Surveillance capitalists are no different from other capitalists in demanding freedom from any sort of constraint" — the rhetoric of "freedom to" and "freedom from" is continuous with classical capitalism, so the break is in the *knowledge* condition, not the freedom claim. Zuboff's argument depends on treating the unknowability premise as load-bearing for the whole justification.
- The claim "they know too much to qualify for freedom" is a normative inference Zuboff draws, not a position Smith or Hayek stated; it extends their logic against its heirs. Zuboff presents this as the conclusion of "our investigations" rather than as a neutral description. (`Strongly implied` that the inference is hers; the premises are `Directly stated`.)
- Whether total knowledge is actually achieved, or only asymptotically approached, bears on how completely mystery is "replaced" — a point Zuboff develops elsewhere as the drive toward ubiquity ([[A Utopia of Certainty]]).
