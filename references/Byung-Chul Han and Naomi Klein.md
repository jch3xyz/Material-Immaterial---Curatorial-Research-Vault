# Byung-Chul Han ↔ Naomi Klein

## Relationship Type

Direct citation / disagreement. In Ch.7 ("Shock") of *Psychopolitics*, Han makes Klein's *The Shock Doctrine* the central interlocutor: he adopts her account of shock as a real technology of power, including her two "Doctor Shocks" — the psychiatrist Ewen Cameron and the economist Milton Friedman — but reclassifies the phenomenon. Klein is a forward-stub author (no book in `raw/`); her citations are verified from this chapter's endnotes (*The Shock Doctrine*, Picador, 2008).

## Explanation

Han takes Klein's narrative seriously enough to reconstruct it at length: Cameron's CIA-funded electroshock experiments that "de-branded" and "re-branded" the psyche, and Friedman's neoliberal doctrine that exploits the disorientation following catastrophe to "depattern" whole societies. But where Klein offers shock as the explanatory key to neoliberalism ("disaster capitalism"), Han argues this "blinds her to the actual workings of neoliberal psychopolitics." For Han, shock therapy is a *disciplinary*, *biopolitical* technique whose essential trait is [[Negativity]] — it paralyzes and annihilates. Genuine neoliberal [[Psychopolitics]] operates instead through [[Positivity]]: it seduces, flatters, and preempts the soul rather than shocking it. So Klein, in Han's reading, has correctly diagnosed a real apparatus of power but mis-dated it, mistaking a disciplinary-society technology for the neoliberal present. `Interpretive synthesis` (the reclassification is the note's organizing claim; each component is directly stated below).
