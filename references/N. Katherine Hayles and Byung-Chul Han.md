# N. Katherine Hayles ↔ Byung-Chul Han

## Relationship Type

**Possible resonance — vault-internal, not textual.** [[N. Katherine Hayles]] and [[Byung-Chul Han]] are not in dialogue: neither cites the other, and the two books were written on different continents, in different disciplines, decades and a discourse apart. This note is an interpretive bridge built by the vault, not a relationship either author claims. The resonance is real but the convergence is shallow, and its load-bearing point is a *distinction*: both diagnose a regime in which disembodied, positivized "information" comes to stand for reality itself — but the word **information** means something different in each, and the two senses must not be merged. `Speculative connection`.

## Explanation

The shared diagnosis is easy to state and easy to overstate. Both thinkers describe a cultural condition in which an immaterial, abstract entity called *information* is elevated over the embodied, material, resistant world it supposedly leaves behind — and both treat that elevation as an illusion with costs. For Hayles the illusion is "the body as information," the "dream or nightmare" she sets out to contest by "attending to the material interfaces and technologies that make disembodiment such a powerful illusion." For Han the correlate is the [[Transparency|society of transparency]], in which language is "positivized, operationalized" and the world is smoothed into "streams of capital, communication, and information." On the [[Embodiment vs Information]] axis they line up on the same side: both refuse the equation of the real with the informational. `Interpretive synthesis`.

But the two senses of *information* are not the same object, and treating them as one would dissolve the very distinction that makes the pairing interesting:

1. **Hayles's information is the Shannon/Wiener probabilistic pattern.** It is a precise technical construction — the [[Pattern and Randomness|interplay of pattern and randomness]] (see [[Information (Hayles)]]) — which she *historicizes and contests* rather than denounces. Information here is "a pattern rather than a presence, defined by the probability distribution of the coding elements composing the message." Hayles's quarrel is not with the engineering but with the reification of the engineering into an ontology (see [[N. Katherine Hayles and Claude Shannon]], [[N. Katherine Hayles and Norbert Wiener]]). Her counter-move is to recover [[Embodiment|embodiment]] and [[The Materiality of Informatics|the materiality of the medium]]: "Information, like humanity, cannot exist apart from the embodiment that brings it into being as a material entity in the world." `Directly stated` (Hayles).

2. **Han's information is positivized, operationalized language stripped of negativity.** It is not a probability function but a *phenomenology of language under transparency*: "[[Information]] is a phenomenon as such insofar as it lacks all negativity. It amounts to positivized, operationalized language." Han's information is defined by what it lacks — [[Negativity|negativity]], hermeneutic depth, the capacity to wound or withhold — and his complaint is that its accumulation produces no truth: "[[Hyperinformation]] and [[Hypercommunication|hypercommunication]] bring no light into darkness." This is a different theoretical object, reached by a different route. `Directly stated` (Han, via [[Information]]).

The resonance lives in the overlap of *effects*, not of *definitions*. Where the two genuinely touch is the figure of the **body absorbed into a coding regime**. Hayles's cyberspace cowboy who regards his flesh as "meat," her insistence that the user "learns, kinesthetically and proprioceptively, that the relevant boundaries for interaction are defined less by the skin than by the [[Feedback Loops|feedback loops]] connecting body and simulation in a technobio-integrated circuit," and her reading of *Snow Crash* — where "humans are computers," language collapses into performative code and "saying is doing" — describe a positivization of the body and of language that rhymes with Han's [[Smoothness|smoothing]] of the world into operational information. In *Snow Crash* especially, Hayles's "infoworld" reduces "everything... to information production, storage, and transmission," which is structurally what Han means by language that has become information under the regime of [[Framing (Ge-Stell)|Framing]]. Both, in different vocabularies, are describing the disappearance of [[Negativity|negativity]] — the resistant, the opaque, the embodied — into a frictionless positive surface. `Speculative connection`.

The distinction matters most where it would be tempting to collapse. Han would call Hayles's [[Pattern and Randomness|pattern/randomness]] dialectic itself a piece of *information* in his pejorative sense — additive, operational, post-hermeneutic. Hayles would call Han's "positivized language" a cultural symptom whose technical substrate is precisely the Shannon information she anatomizes. Neither claim is the other's. The pairing is therefore best read as two non-communicating diagnoses of the same historical surface — useful as a [[Material vs Immaterial|material/immaterial]] cross-section of the vault, dangerous if read as a single shared concept of information. `Interpretive synthesis`.

## Source Passages

### Hayles — the body dissolved into feedback loops (Ch. 2)

The user's body is reconstituted as a node in a circuit; the skin ceases to be the relevant boundary:

> In these systems, the user learns, kinesthetically and proprioceptively, that the relevant boundaries for interaction are defined less by the skin than by the feedback loops connecting body and simulation in a technobio-integrated circuit.

This is Hayles's version of the body positivized into information — but it arrives through the [[Pattern and Randomness|pattern/randomness]] dialectic, not through Han's negativity/positivity axis. See [[The Body as Data]], [[Feedback Loops]]. `Directly stated` (Hayles).

### Hayles — information as probabilistic pattern (Ch. 10)

Hayles's term is the technical one, expanded but not abandoned:

> the interplay between pattern and randomness I will label information, intending the term to include both the technical meaning of information and the more general perception that information is a code carried by physical markers but also extractable from them.

The phrase "carried by physical markers but also extractable from them" is the whole problem in miniature: it is the *extractability* — the seeming separability of code from matter — that Hayles contests and that Han would call positivization. See [[Information (Hayles)]], [[The Materiality of Informatics]]. `Directly stated` (Hayles).

### Han — information as positivized, operationalized language

Against which the Hayles passages should be read, to mark the difference:

> The society of transparency is the society of information. Information is a phenomenon as such insofar as it lacks all negativity. It amounts to positivized, operationalized language.

Han's information is defined by *lack of negativity*; Hayles's is defined by *probability distribution*. Same word, different objects. See [[Information]], [[Negativity]], [[Hyperinformation]]. `Directly stated` (Han, via the existing concept note [[Information]]).
