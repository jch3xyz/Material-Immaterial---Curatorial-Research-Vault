# Yuval Noah Harari ↔ Charles Darwin

## Relationship Type

Historical influence. Harari positions Darwin's *On the Origin of Species* as a founding event in a century-and-a-half-long transformation of the life sciences — the transformation that ultimately produced [[Dataism]]. Darwin is not argued with; he is cast as the upstream source of the claim that [[Organisms Are Algorithms]]. Two threads run through Harari's engagement: (1) natural selection reinterpreted as the quality-control mechanism of biochemical algorithms; and (2) Darwin's publication as one of two founding waves that together generate Dataism, the other being Alan Turing's formulation of the Turing Machine (see [[Yuval Noah Harari and Alan Turing]]).

## Explanation

Harari argues that modern biology, following Darwin, has come to see every organism — from a giraffe to a human being — as a biochemical algorithm shaped by natural selection. On this view, natural selection functions not as a mystical creative force but as a quality filter: the feelings and algorithms that survived millions of years of selection are simply those that passed its "harshest quality tests." Harari then zooms out to intellectual history: he names Darwin's 1859 *On the Origin of Species* as the first of two tidal waves whose confluence produces [[Dataism]] — the doctrine that all organisms are algorithms and that life is data processing. The second wave is Alan Turing's Turing Machine. Together these two scientific traditions collapse the barrier between biochemical and electronic algorithms, enabling Dataism to claim that the same mathematical laws govern both.

This relationship is foundational for several of Harari's core concepts. The claim that [[Organisms Are Algorithms]] depends directly on the Darwinian inheritance; [[Evolutionary Humanism]] inherits Darwin as well, but Harari treats that lineage as a political danger rather than a scientific legacy. The present note concerns the epistemological and scientific inheritance — Darwin as the origin point of biology's turn toward information and computation.

## Source Passages

### Thread 1: Natural Selection as Quality Control

From *Homo Deus*, Chapter 3 ("The Human Spark"):

> when you listen to your feelings, you follow an algorithm that evolution has developed for millions of years, and that withstood the harshest quality tests of natural selection. Your feelings are the voice of millions of ancestors, each of whom managed to survive and reproduce in an unforgiving environment.

Label: `Directly stated`

Harari renders Darwinian natural selection as a quality-control mechanism operating on algorithms — specifically the biochemical algorithms that constitute feelings and behavior. The phrasing "harshest quality tests of natural selection" reframes evolutionary fitness as algorithmic filtering, anchoring the [[Organisms Are Algorithms]] thesis in Darwin's own theory.

### Thread 2: Darwin as a Founding Wave of Dataism

From *Homo Deus*, Chapter 11 ("The Data Religion"):

> Dataism was born from the explosive confluence of two scientific tidal waves. In the 150 years since Charles Darwin published On the Origin of Species, the life sciences have come to see organisms as biochemical algorithms. Simultaneously, in the eight decades since Alan Turing formulated the idea of a Turing Machine, computer scientists have learned to engineer increasingly sophisticated electronic algorithms. Dataism puts the two together, pointing out that exactly the same mathematical laws apply to both biochemical and electronic algorithms. Dataism thereby collapses the barrier between animals and machines, and expects electronic algorithms to eventually decipher and outperform biochemical algorithms.

Label: `Directly stated`

Darwin is named explicitly and dated precisely. Harari gives Darwin's publication a 150-year lineage that runs from 1859 directly into contemporary biological Dataism. The passage pairs Darwin with Turing as co-founders of the worldview: the life sciences (Darwin's wave) declare that organisms are biochemical algorithms; computer science (Turing's wave) engineers electronic ones. The convergence of the two waves is [[Dataism]].

### Thread 3: Biology as the More Important Dataist Discipline

From *Homo Deus*, Chapter 11 ("The Data Religion"):

> Dataism is most firmly entrenched in its two mother disciplines: computer science and biology. Of the two, biology is the more important. It was the biological embracement of Dataism that turned a limited breakthrough in computer science into a world-shattering cataclysm that may completely transform the very nature of life. You may not agree with the idea that organisms are algorithms, and that giraffes, tomatoes and human beings are just different methods for processing data. But you should know that this is current scientific dogma, and that it is changing our world beyond recognition.

Label: `Directly stated`

Harari ranks the Darwinian inheritance — biology's adoption of the algorithmic view — above the Turing inheritance in terms of world-historical consequence. The biological claim that organisms are algorithms is "current scientific dogma," not a fringe proposition; its roots run back through post-Darwinian biology to the publication that inaugurated the tradition.
