# Yuval Noah Harari ↔ Byung-Chul Han

## Relationship Type

Conceptual parallel (vault-internal interpretive bridge, **not** a citation). The two authors never cite each other and write in opposite registers — Harari is a sweeping historian-futurist describing a coming world, Han a polemical critic diagnosing the present pathology of one. But their objects converge precisely. Harari is the *worldview-builder* of the very [[Dataism]] that Han's [[Dataism (Han)|critique]] attacks: where Harari narrates "the data religion" from the inside, partly seduced by it, Han names the same configuration from the outside as a regime of domination. Reading them together makes the vault's keystone Dataism note legible from both sides.

## Explanation

Four convergences carry the bridge. (1) **Dataism / data-as-religion.** Harari directly states that "information flow" has become "the supreme value of this new religion"; Han's [[Dataism]] and [[Big Data]] notes name the same elevation of data over narrative meaning. (2) **Censorship-by-flooding ↔ [[Hyperinformation]].** Harari's claim that twenty-first-century censorship "works by flooding people with irrelevant information" is the same diagnosis as Han's Hyperinformation — that a surplus of information brings no clarity but disorientation. (3) **The growth imperative / capitalism-as-religion.** Harari calls "the belief in economic growth a religion" with the commandment "thou shalt invest thy profits in increasing growth"; this is the parallel to Han's [[Capital as Religion]] and to the realized Harari concept [[Capitalism as Religion (Harari)]] and [[The Growth Imperative]]. (4) **Desire-engineering and control experienced as freedom.** Harari's "hackable" will — feelings that are "just algorithms," desires we "don't choose" but can be manipulated by helmet, drug or algorithm so that "the supposedly free will of customers will become just another product we can buy" — is the same structure Han names as [[Smart Power]] and [[Friendly Big Brother]]: a power that does not coerce against the will but produces and pre-empts it, so domination is felt as freedom (Han's [[Freedom vs Control]] axis; cf. [[Free Will as Fiction (Harari)]] and [[Algorithms That Know Us Better Than We Know Ourselves]]). `Interpretive synthesis` (the parallels are the vault's organizing claim; each Harari component below is directly stated in the source, the mapping onto Han is the synthesis).

## Confidence

Medium. The parallels are genuine and load-bearing, but the relationship is a constructed resonance rather than a textual link: neither author names the other, and the registers differ (Harari describes Dataism with considerable sympathy as a historical inevitability; Han indicts the same formation as neoliberal domination). The Harari side of each parallel is `Directly stated` in the verbatim passages below; the mapping onto Han's [[Dataism]], [[Hyperinformation]], [[Capital as Religion]], [[Smart Power]] and [[Friendly Big Brother]] is `Interpretive synthesis`. The note exists because it is structurally necessary for the keystone Dataism merge, not because the authors are in dialogue.
