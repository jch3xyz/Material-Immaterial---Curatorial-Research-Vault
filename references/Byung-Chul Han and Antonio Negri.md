# Byung-Chul Han ↔ Antonio Negri

## Relationship Type

Direct citation and disagreement. In *Psychopolitics*, Han names Negri explicitly and rejects his post-Marxist theory of the "Multitude" as a description of the contemporary neoliberal mode of production. The disagreement is an extension of Han's broader "with Marx beyond Marx" critique (see [[Byung-Chul Han and Karl Marx]]): if Marx's own revolutionary scheme is refuted by immaterial auto-exploitation, then Negri's attempt to salvage that scheme through the "Multitude" inherits the same failure.

## Explanation

Negri's central political wager — advanced in *Empire* (with Michael Hardt) and in the broader "Multitude" project — is that immaterial and affective labour has produced a new cooperative subject, the "Multitude," which can overthrow the "Empire" of global capital and realize a communist or common form of life. Han rejects this on diagnostic grounds: the cooperative, collective subjectivity Negri posits does not exist. The contemporary mode of production is not marked by networked cooperation but by radical isolation — "the solitude of an entrepreneur who is isolated and self-combating and practises auto-exploitation voluntarily." Because neoliberalism converts workers into self-exploiting entrepreneurs, there is no collective exploited subject and therefore no political "We" capable of collective action against Capital. (Label: `Directly stated`.)

The rejection travels through Han's concept of [[Dictatorship of Capital]]. Where Marx expected a "dictatorship of the proletariat" to resolve capitalism's contradictions, and where Negri hoped the "Multitude" would replace the proletariat as the emancipatory subject, Han argues that the dissolution of the proletariat by neoliberalism removes the social basis for either scheme. Because [[Auto-Exploitation]] is now universal and classless, "no political We is even possible that could rise up and undertake collective action." The system's stability derives precisely from the fact that exploitation is directed inward: aggression turns against the self and produces depression rather than revolt. The cooperative collectivity Negri theorizes is therefore not suppressed or deferred — it structurally cannot arise under conditions of immaterial production as Han diagnoses them. (Label: `Directly stated`.)

Han's disagreement with Negri is sharper than his disagreement with Marx. With Marx, Han works from the inside — keeping Marx's critique of free competition and capital's ruse of individual freedom while denying the dialectical *Aufhebung* and the revolutionary prognosis. With Negri, the break is total: Negri's departure from classical Marxism does not go far enough, because he retains the Marxist "scheme" of an emancipatory collective subject, merely substituting "Multitude" for "proletariat." For Han, this is "yet another illusion" in the same series. (Label: `Interpretive synthesis` — the framing of Negri as inheriting rather than escaping Marx's error is Han's explicit claim but the formulation here draws the implication out.)

The reference note for [[Immaterial Labour]] is also relevant here: both Han and Negri take immaterial production as the decisive feature of contemporary capitalism, but they draw opposed conclusions. Negri reads immaterial labour as socially cooperative and therefore as the potential ground of collective emancipation; Han reads it as producing the privatized, self-commodifying entrepreneur who has no ground for solidarity. The concept of [[Immaterial Labour]] is therefore the pivot on which the disagreement turns. (Label: `Interpretive synthesis`.)

## Source Passages

### 1. The "Multitude" does not describe the contemporary mode of production

This is the central rejection passage, where Han names Negri, identifies the claim he rejects ("Multitude" as post-Marxist successor to the "proletariat"), states his counter-diagnosis (isolated, self-combating auto-exploitation), and passes his verdict on Negri's Marxist scheme.

> The cooperative 'Multitude' that Antonio Negri has exalted as the post-Marxist successor to the 'proletariat' does not describe the contemporary mode of production. Rather, conditions are defined by the solitude of an entrepreneur who is isolated and self-combating and practises auto-exploitation voluntarily. As such, it is a mistake to believe that the cooperative 'Multitude' will overthrow the parasitic 'Empire' and bring forth a communist social order. The Marxist scheme to which Negri adheres will prove to have been yet another illusion.

Label: `Directly stated` — Han names Negri, identifies the "Multitude" and "Empire" by name, and explicitly passes judgment on "the Marxist scheme to which Negri adheres."

### 2. The classless auto-exploiting subject forecloses the political "We"

The structural argument against any cooperative subject — which applies to Negri's "Multitude" as much as to Marx's proletariat — is stated two paragraphs later:

> The neoliberal regime transforms allo-exploitation into auto-exploitation; this process affects all 'classes'. Such classless self-exploitation - which was something utterly unknown to Marx - renders impossible any social revolution based on the difference between the exploiters, on the one hand, and the exploited, on the other. Indeed, given the auto-exploiting achievement-subject's isolation, no political We is even possible that could rise up and undertake collective action.

Label: `Directly stated` — the "political We" that cannot arise under auto-exploitation is the precise category that Negri's "Multitude" was meant to supply.

### 3. Context: neoliberalism as transformation of workers into entrepreneurs

The premise underlying both rejections — Han's description of the isolated, self-exploiting neoliberal subject that neither Marx nor Negri accounts for:

> As a mutant form of capitalism, neoliberalism transforms workers into entrepreneurs. It is not communist revolution that is now abolishing the allo-exploited working class - instead, neoliberalism is in the course of doing so. Today, everyone is an auto-exploiting labourer in his or her own enterprise. People are now master and slave in one. Even class struggle has transformed into an inner struggle against oneself.

Label: `Directly stated` — this passage supplies the empirical description Han opposes to Negri's account of cooperative immaterial labour.
