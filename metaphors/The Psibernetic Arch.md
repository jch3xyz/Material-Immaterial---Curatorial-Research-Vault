# The Psibernetic Arch

## The Metaphor

The arch — specifically a *rainbow arch* — spanning and uniting two "apparently opposed spheres": **cybernetics** and **parapsychology**. This is the governing figure of Roy Ascott's 1970 essay "The Psibernetic Arch" (first published, untitled, in *Studio International*, April 1970), and the visual carrier of his coinage `[[Psibernetics]]` and of the `[[Cyb vs Psi]]` opposition. The two piers of the arch are named in paired terms — "technology and telepathy; provision and prevision; cyb and psi" — "the west and east sides of the mind." A "general systems approach" is the keystone that lets the arch stand: it investigates both poles as "a whole system of prediction, control, and communication." The arch is load-bearing in the most literal metaphoric sense — it "could support a whole artistic culture." `Directly stated`

## What It Compares

- **Target:** the prospective fusion of cybernetic technology and psychic (extrasensory) communication into a single coming culture — the "psibernetic era" — and the new art that would express it (the art of `[[Futuribles]]`).
- **Source domain:** an arch, a single span resting on two piers, the form rising precisely because two opposed sides press against and complete each other; and a *rainbow* arch, an ephemeral, immaterial, light-borne bridge that appears in the air between separated grounds.

The mapping is structural: the two piers are the opposed spheres (`[[Cyb vs Psi]]` — cybernetics/technology/provision on the "west," parapsychology/telepathy/prevision on the "east"); the keystone is the "general systems approach" (`[[Cybernetics]]`, general systems theory) that locks them into one structure; the spanned void beneath is "a whole system of prediction, control, and communication"; and what the completed arch carries overhead is "a whole artistic culture." `Strongly implied`

## What It Reveals

- **Opposites can be load-bearing.** An arch turns two things that lean *against* each other into a structure that holds weight precisely *because* of that opposition. The figure reframes the cyb/psi antithesis not as a contradiction to be resolved but as a tension to be exploited — the same move the vault tracks in `[[Cyb vs Psi]]`. The crisp characterisation of the two poles ("ESP is instant, but sporadic. Cybernetics is universal, but entropic") shows each pier compensating for the other's defect. `Directly stated`
- **It makes an immaterial, speculative claim picturable.** A "rainbow arch" gives a single, hopeful, visible image to an otherwise abstract and contestable prediction (that telepathy and computing belong to one science of communication). The arch is itself "immaterial" — a thing of light and air — which suits Ascott's claim that the perfect medium of feedback, ESP, "involves no media." `Strongly implied`
- **It positions art as the keystone, not the cargo.** Because the arch "could support a whole artistic culture," the figure casts the psibernetic synthesis as the *structure* and art as what it carries — and, reciprocally, casts the artist as the one who can already see and sketch the span ("art . . . has anticipated the fact"). This is the seed of Ascott's lifelong technology-plus-consciousness programme, the conceptual ancestor of his later `[[Network Consciousness]]` and technoetics. `Interpretive synthesis`

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It smooths a real epistemic gulf into a continuous curve.** A graceful arch implies the two grounds are commensurable and equally solid. But one pier (cybernetics) rests on an established science and the other (parapsychology/ESP) on disputed, "erratic" phenomena — the visual symmetry lends the unproven pole borrowed structural credibility from the proven one. `Strongly implied`
- **The rainbow's beauty launders speculation as inevitability.** Calling the bridge "very beautiful" and a uniting "rainbow" recruits aesthetic pleasure and the rainbow's connotations of promise/covenant to make a 1970 visionary forecast feel already accomplished ("the psibernetic era . . . is upon us"). The figure hides how much of the synthesis is wish. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **An arch is static, finished, monumental — but the thing it pictures is erratic and unrealised.** ESP's "manifestation is erratic," the clairvoyant-computer linkage is "speculation," cybernetics is "entropic"; the serene permanence of an arch occludes the instability, fragility, and provisionality of both poles it claims to join. `Interpretive synthesis`
