# The Body as Congealed Metaphor

## The Metaphor

In the Conclusion of *How We Became Posthuman*, Hayles names the body itself "a congealed metaphor, a physical structure whose constraints and possibilities have been formed by an evolutionary history that intelligent machines do not share." The figure folds two registers together: the body is read *through* metaphors resonant with cultural meanings, but it is also *itself* a metaphor that has set — congealed — into matter. Where a living metaphor is a mobile crossing between domains, this metaphor has hardened into a load-bearing physical form, so that the body's anatomy and behaviors are the sedimented residue of solutions evolution found "within given parameters." `Directly stated`.

## What It Compares

Target → source-domain mapping:

- **The body** → a *metaphor* (a figure that stands in for something else) that has been *congealed* (frozen, set, solidified) into a *physical structure*.
- **The body's constraints and possibilities** → the fixed shape the metaphor took as it cooled — what the form can and cannot do.
- **The forming process** → an *evolutionary history*, "thousands of years of sedimented evolutionary history," acting as the heat-and-pressure that shaped the figure.
- **The crucial differentiator** → that history is one "that intelligent machines do not share." The machine may run the same patterns of pattern/randomness, but it lacks the congealing process; its embodiment is "distinctively different."

The body is thus not raw nature opposed to culture, nor pure information abstractable from its substrate, but a *figure made flesh* — meaning incarnated as structure. This is why Hayles can say elsewhere in the chapter that the surviving organism's internal structure is "a good metaphor for the complexities without": the congealed metaphor of the body is the long-run product of that metaphor-making by evolution. `Directly stated` / `Strongly implied`.

## What It Reveals

The figure makes thinkable a *limit* to the posthuman fantasy of seamless human-machine fusion. Because the body carries an evolutionary history machines do not, Hayles argues "there is a limit to how seamlessly humans can be articulated with intelligent machines, which remain distinctively different from humans in their embodiments." The metaphor gives that limit a positive content rather than leaving it as mere resistance: the body is not a deficiency to be uploaded past but a dense archive of contingent solutions. `Directly stated`.

It thereby reframes the terror of the posthuman. If the body were only a "support system for the mind," then [[Hans Moravec]]'s dream of downloading consciousness would be a mere engineering problem. Figuring the body as congealed metaphor turns the terror "away from the apocalyptic and toward a more moderate view of seriated social, technological, political, and cultural changes" — the body's sedimented history is exactly what cannot be downloaded. `Directly stated`.

Crucially, the figure is *distinct from* [[The Body as Data]]. Where the body-as-data is the reduction Hayles rejects — the body abstracted into transmissible information — the congealed metaphor insists on irreducible, non-replicable particularity. It is the affirmative ground of [[Embodiment]] that the data-reduction erases, and so the pivot of [[Embodiment vs Information]]. `Interpretive synthesis`.

## What It Hides or Distorts

The metaphor risks naturalizing the body as a stable given, when Hayles is careful that the very evolutionary models reading the body are themselves reflexively shaped by the history they analyze: "the models proposed by evolutionary biologists have encoded within them cultural attitudes and assumptions formed by the same history they propose to analyze." The figure of *congealing* can suggest a finished, fixed object and so soften this [[Reflexivity]] — the danger of treating an interpreted body as simply the body. Hayles flags the risk herself by noting the computer-module model of evolutionary psychology "testifies at least as much to the importance of information technologies in shaping contemporary worldviews as it does to human brain function." `Directly stated` / `Strongly implied`.

The word "congealed" can also overstate fixity in the other direction, occluding how much human cognition is already *not* contained in the body at all — distributed across the environment and nonhuman actors, as in [[Distributed Cognition]]. The body as congealed metaphor names the limit of fusion; it must not be read as a boundary sealing off an autonomous self, the very move Hayles attributes to [[The Liberal Humanist Subject]] and warns against. The figure marks a difference of embodiment, not an inviolable container. `Interpretive synthesis`.
