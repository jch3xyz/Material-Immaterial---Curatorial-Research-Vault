# All That Is Moist and Alive Must Hand Over Its Facts

## The Metaphor

Shoshana Zuboff's poetic statement of rendition's demand for total illumination: "All that is moist and alive must hand over its facts. There can be no shadow, no darkness. The unknown is intolerable. The solitary is forbidden." The figure casts the living world as a tributary population summoned to surrender — every wet, breathing thing commanded to "hand over its facts" as if paying a levy — while every refuge of shadow, darkness, and solitude is abolished as a matter of principle (Directly stated, Ch.8).

## What It Compares

The metaphor maps a specific economic-technical operation onto an image of totalizing extraction and forced disclosure:

- **Target** — *extension*, which Zuboff names as "the first dimension of scope": the movement of rendition operations "into the real world, seizing fresh unexpected chunks of human experience." Extension "wants every corner and crevice, every utterance and gesture on the path to dispossession" (Directly stated, Ch.8).
- **Source domain** — a levy or tribute exacted from the whole of organic life. "All that is moist and alive" stands for living human experience in its wet, embodied, breathing particularity — body, gesture, utterance, emotion. To "hand over its facts" is the act of [[Rendition]]: experience compelled to give up its data. The accompanying prohibitions — "no shadow, no darkness," "the unknown is intolerable," "the solitary is forbidden" — map the elimination of every unrendered remainder onto a regime of mandatory, universal illumination.

The image belongs to the chapter's wider argument that rendition is "Step One" in the construction of behavioral surplus, "the concrete operationalization of the 'original sin of simple robbery,'" pursued through practices Zuboff elsewhere calls "unauthorized, unilateral, gluttonous, secret, and brazen" (Directly stated, Ch.8). The metaphor compresses that whole machinery into a single command issued to the living.

## What It Reveals

- **The totality of the ambition.** By making the subject of the sentence "all that is moist and alive" — not "users," "data," or "customers" — Zuboff reveals that the object of rendition is *life itself*, not a market segment. There is no natural boundary the demand respects; the very next sentences in the chapter explain that "the prediction imperative makes boundaries and borders intolerable" (Directly stated, Ch.8). The metaphor names that intolerance.
- **Disclosure as compulsion, not exchange.** "Must hand over" frames rendition as a non-negotiable levy rather than a transaction. This exposes the emptiness of the consent vocabulary Zuboff dismantles in the same chapter — "There are no more fig leaves" — and shows that the language of "opt in/opt out" misdescribes what is actually a demand backed by power (Strongly implied, Ch.8).
- **The illumination this regime requires.** "There can be no shadow, no darkness" reveals that surveillance capitalism's logic is not merely to watch what is visible but to *forbid invisibility*. This is the constructive counter-image to [[The Right to Sanctuary]]: where sanctuary names "a space of inviolable refuge," this metaphor names the force that would abolish all such space, leaving "a world of 'no exit.'" The line "The solitary is forbidden" is the direct denial of the inward, "disconnected" time Zuboff argues a self requires (Interpretive synthesis).
- **Why nothing can be left out.** "The unknown is intolerable" reveals the epistemic root of the demand: under the prediction imperative, any unrendered remainder is a gap in certainty, and certainty is the product. The metaphor thus links extension to [[Economies of Scope]] and the pursuit of experience "monetized as certainty" (Directly stated, Ch.8).

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **The agent disappears into a law of nature.** The sentence has no grammatical subject who issues the command — facts "must" be handed over, the solitary "is" forbidden — which lends the demand an air of impersonal inevitability. This rhetorically mirrors the very "inevitabilism" Zuboff criticizes elsewhere; read carelessly, the metaphor can make rendition sound like a force of nature rather than a contingent corporate project pursued by named firms ("Google rendered the Earth… Facebook rendered the social network"). Zuboff intends the line as indictment, but its grandeur risks naturalizing what she means to denounce (Interpretive synthesis).
- **It dramatizes seizure and understates seduction.** The figure of a levy violently extracted ("hand over its facts") foregrounds coercion; it is less able to capture the smiling, frictionless, "smart"-branded mode in which much rendition actually proceeds — the marionette charm Zuboff describes when she calls "smart" "a euphemism for rendition." The poetry of conquest can eclipse the banality of the consenting click (Speculative connection).
- **"Moist and alive" risks a residual vitalism.** By opposing the wet, living particular to the dry abstraction of data, the metaphor implies a clean line between an authentic organic interior and the facts extracted from it — an implied inviolable inwardness. Authors elsewhere in the vault (Hayles, Haraway) question precisely that opposition between embodied life and informatic pattern; the metaphor's force partly depends on a humanist intuition those thinkers complicate (Speculative connection).

## Related Symbols and Concepts

- [[Rendition]] — the operation the metaphor poeticizes: the concrete practices by which experience is "claimed as raw material for datafication." This metaphor is the lyric statement of rendition's first dimension, *extension*.
- [[Economies of Scope]] — extension and depth are the two dimensions of scope; "All that is moist and alive…" names the scope ambition of the extension dimension.
- [[The Right to Sanctuary]] — the elemental right this metaphor inverts: "no shadow… the solitary is forbidden" is the precise abolition of the "inviolable refuge" sanctuary protects. The two notes are mirror images of one demand and one defense.
- [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — the economic order whose imperatives issue the command; rendition is "Step One" in its supply chain.
- [[Digital Dispossession]] — extension proceeds "on the path to dispossession"; the metaphor names the appetite that drives the dispossession of human experience.
- [[Big Other]] — the ubiquitous sensing apparatus that materially enforces the demand that nothing remain in shadow.
- [[Digital Panopticon]] — Han's figure of total, voluntary visibility; Zuboff's image converges with it from the side of the watcher's imperative ("there can be no shadow"), where Han stresses the watched subject's joyful self-exposure (Speculative connection).
