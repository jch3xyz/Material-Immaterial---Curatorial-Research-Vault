# The Seamless Universal Web of Mystics and Madmen

## The Metaphor

Ascott figures the networked computer as a loom that can "weave in digital space that seamless universal web of which, previously, only mystics and madmen would speak." The web is the old esoteric image of cosmic interconnection — the all-binding fabric of mystical or visionary intuition — now claimed to be *literally fabricable* through telematic technology. In the same breath the computer is credited with the power to "call a halt to entropy, bending time round, tying it, in effect, in a butterfly bow, to bring distant, separate events and minds into new conjunctions." The metaphor thus fuses three images: a *web* (universal interconnection), an *anti-entropic act* (halting the slide into disorder), and a *bow tied in time* (the bending and binding of duration itself). `Directly stated` — Ascott uses the exact phrase "that seamless universal web of which, previously, only mystics and madmen would speak."

## What It Compares

- **Target**: what the networked computer does — knit "distant, separate events and minds into new conjunctions," producing what Ascott elsewhere in the chapter calls a "global consciousness" out of telematic, parallel, asynchronous connection.
- **Source domain**: a *seamless universal web*, woven in digital space — the figure of total, edgeless cosmic interconnection that until now belonged only to the visionary speech of "mystics and madmen," paired with the gestural image of time "tied... in a butterfly bow."

The mapping takes a *mystical* image (the universe as one continuous, secretly ordered web) and remaps it onto a *technical* substrate (the computer as "extra-sensory device dealing invisibly with the invisible," simulating "non-linear, non-local, layered, and discontinuous" patterns of events). What was metaphysical intuition becomes, in the figure, engineerable fact. `Directly stated` for both poles.

## What It Reveals

- **Re-enchantment as the explicit project.** The metaphor makes thinkable a *re-enchantment* of technology: the computer is enlisted not to disenchant the world but to realize what only "mystics and madmen" could previously voice. This is the figural heart of Ascott's claim that telematic art restores the visionary, occult, and consciousness-expanding dimension to media. See [[Re-enchantment]] and the tension [[Disenchantment vs Re-enchantment]]. `Strongly implied`
- **The computer as instrument of the invisible.** The web image rides on the preceding claim that "The computer is an extra-sensory device dealing invisibly with the invisible," able to "glimpse the unseeable, to grasp chaos and the secret order in the disordered." The web is the *product* of that extra-sensory power — the unseen connections made into a visible, weavable fabric. `Directly stated`
- **Anti-entropy as redemption.** By tying entropy off in a "butterfly bow," the figure recasts the second law of thermodynamics as something art-and-computation can suspend — binding the scattered (distant events, separate minds, runaway disorder) back into conjunction. The metaphor reveals Ascott's conviction that the network is a *negentropic*, world-gathering force. `Directly stated`
- **It names the aspiration of an entire art form.** The web of mystics and madmen is the visionary horizon of [[New Media Art]] and [[Art as Experiential Technology]] as Ascott conceives them — art that does not depict connection but technologically *enacts* it, weaving minds into [[Reciprocal Communication]]. `Interpretive synthesis`

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **"Seamless" hides the seams.** The figure of an edgeless, jointless fabric occludes the very material infrastructure Ascott has just described — clocks releasing electrical charge at "nanosecond" intervals, signals taking time "to move from point to point on a silicon chip," "billions of electronic pulses" — a substrate that is nothing but discrete units and seams. The metaphor smooths a profoundly granular, machinic process into mystical continuity. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **It borrows the authority of vision while disowning its risk.** Invoking "mystics and madmen" lends the network the glamour of prophetic insight, but quietly converts what those figures *spoke* (intuition, vision, even delusion) into what the machine *delivers* (fact). The slide from visionary speech to technical accomplishment can disguise utopian assertion as engineering. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **"Halt to entropy" overstates the machine.** No computer reverses the second law; the figure of bending and bowing time is poetic, not physical. Taken literally — as the surrounding rhetoric of "global consciousness" invites — it risks promising a redemption from disorder and finitude that the technology cannot underwrite. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **Universality erases the local and the asymmetric.** A "universal web" implies every mind equally and frictionlessly woven in, hiding who has access to the network, who authors and who is authored, and the asymmetries of "intelligence amplified, authorship dispersed" — distortions that surveillance-and-power readings elsewhere in the vault make visible. `Speculative connection`
