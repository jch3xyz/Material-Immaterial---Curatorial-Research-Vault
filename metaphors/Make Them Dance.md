# Make Them Dance

## The Metaphor

"We are learning how to write the music, and then we let the music make them dance." The apparatus is a composer; human behavior is a body that dances to a score it neither wrote nor hears. The surveillance capitalist *writes the music* — engineers the choice architecture, the cues, the shut-off switch, the schedule of reinforcements — and then *lets the music make them dance*, so that the resulting movement looks autonomous while being remotely choreographed toward "guaranteed outcomes." It is the chapter's title figure for what Zuboff calls **economies of action**: the completion of the *means of behavioral modification*. `Directly stated` (the quotation is verbatim from an "internet of things" software developer; Zuboff frames it as the signature image of actuation/herding).

## What It Compares

| Source domain (music / dance) | Target (economies of action) |
|---|---|
| The composer who writes the music | The surveillance capitalist / engineer who designs the intervention |
| The music (a score that plays automatically) | The choice architecture, nudges, herding cues, conditioning schedules — embedded in software, sensors, and devices |
| Letting the music "make them dance" | Actuation: sensors that are also *actuators*, modifying real-time action in the real world |
| The dancer who moves to the music | The user whose behavior is "tuned," "herded," and "conditioned" toward outcomes set by others |
| Dancing that looks spontaneous | Behavior that feels self-chosen but is "remote orchestration of the human situation" |

The mapping turns Skinner's "technology of behavior" into a commercial composition: the three approaches Zuboff names — **tuning** (subliminal cues and the **nudge**), **herding** (controlling the immediate context, e.g. shutting down a car's engine), and **conditioning** (operant reinforcement, "data pellets") — are the instruments through which the music is written. `Directly stated` for the three approaches; `Interpretive synthesis` for the instrument-mapping.

## What It Reveals

- **Actuation as the turning point.** The figure dramatizes the shift Zuboff's engineers describe: "sensors can also be actuators," so that "the real aim is ubiquitous intervention, action, and control." Prediction is no longer enough; the apparatus now *acts on* the world to make the prediction come true. This is the completion of the "means of behavior modification." `Directly stated`
- **Choreography at a distance.** "Herding enables remote orchestration of the human situation, foreclosing action alternatives and thus moving behavior along a path of heightened probability that approximates certainty." The dancer's freedom is narrowed to the steps the music permits. `Directly stated`
- **The evasion of awareness is structural.** The music must work "just beyond the rim of individual awareness," because "individual awareness is the enemy of telestimulation" and "human consciousness itself is a threat to surveillance revenues." A dancer who hears the strings being pulled stops dancing. `Directly stated`
- **From "I will" to "You will."** By writing the music, surveillance capitalism "effectively exiles us from our own behavior, shifting the locus of control over the future tense from 'I will' to 'You will.'" The metaphor names a transfer of authorship over action — the supplanting of *autonomy* (regulation by the self) with *heteronomy* (regulation by others). `Directly stated`

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It launders coercion as art.** "Writing the music" carries the innocent glamour of composition; what it conceals is "an element of the user's loss of self-control" and "automated engineered behavior modification" designed to meet the company's "own revenue and growth objectives," not the dancer's interests. The aesthetic frame anaesthetizes the violence Zuboff calls "engineering." `Strongly implied`
- **It implies consent to the dance.** A dance is something one chooses to join; the metaphor obscures that the score is "indecipherable and therefore uncontestable," administered "covertly, at scale, and in the absence of social or legal mechanisms of agreement, contest, and control." `Directly stated`
- **It flatters the composer's omniscience.** Spoken from inside the apparatus, the phrase casts engineers as masters of a benign craft. Zuboff re-narrates the same line as the Greeks "secreted deep in the belly of the Trojan horse" — the "economic orientation obscured behind the veil of the digital." The metaphor hides the asymmetry of knowledge and power that makes the choreography possible. `Strongly implied`
