# Technology as a Self-Propelled Time-Binding Web

## The Metaphor

In the closing pages of *Beyond Modern Sculpture*, Jack Burnham figures technology not as a tool wielded by humanity but as a self-creating organism: a **self-propelled, energy- and time-binding web** spun over the surface of the earth — "and now beyond." On this image technology weaves itself; it has its own purposiveness; and human beings are not its authors but merely "the catalyst for its happening." The "immense core of facts and data" that we call technological achievement is, in this figure, not the substance of what is occurring but a "camouflage" draped over it — concealing the real event, which is a transformation happening *to* biological man. `Directly stated`

| What is actually at stake | "What is actually happening to biological man" — a change to the species, not to its instruments |

The metaphor extends the autonomy Burnham already ascribes to [[Technics]] (his welded coinage for science-and-technology) into a frankly biological and cosmological register. Where the [[Technics]] note records technology as "self-aggrandizing," "irreversible," and indifferent to "the ambitions and contentments of the human race," this figure adds the decisive turn: the web *makes itself*, and the human is demoted from instigator to catalyst. `Directly stated`

## What It Reveals

- **Autonomy as the deep truth of technics.** The image makes thinkable a technology with its own teleology — a process that "appears to be an extremely purposeful phenomenon" without that purpose being anyone's intention. This is the climax of Burnham's case that technics "moves almost as if men were not its instigators." `Directly stated`
- **The species, not the artifact, is the real object of change.** By calling the data-cloud "camouflage for what is actually happening to biological man," the metaphor relocates the event from the machines to the organism that builds them — the move that grounds [[Sculpture as Biological Signal]] and Burnham's speculation about a "post-human species." `Strongly implied`
- **Time-binding as continuity across epochs.** Naming the web "time-binding" frames technology as something that does not merely occupy space but threads generations together — the long evolutionary pattern Burnham reaches for via depth psychology, which "penetrate[s] the facade of customs, techniques, and notions which each society erects around itself in the name of culture." `Strongly implied`
- **A demystification of "achievement."** It reverses the usual valuation: the proud "immense core of facts and data" is not the achievement but the disguise. What looks like mastery is the surface under which something is being done to us. `Directly stated`

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It evacuates agency and accountability.** If technology "create[s] itself" and man is "simply the catalyst," then no one is responsible — a quietism Burnham elsewhere resists when he calls for "the control, if not rehabilitation, of industrial technology." The web-image risks naturalizing as destiny what are in fact choices, institutions, and interests. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **It smuggles in teleology it cannot prove.** "Extremely purposeful" attributes intention to a process; Burnham himself flags the surrounding theory as speculation ("it may seem presumptuous for anyone to offer a theory uniting all human efforts") and floats the counter-reading that the whole drive may be "merely a large-scale death wish." The metaphor's confident purposiveness overstates what the text will actually assert. `Strongly implied`
- **It dissolves the very distinctions it depends on.** A single planet-spanning "web" elides the difference between weapons and consumer goods, between the labs that spin it and the populations it falls upon — distinctions Burnham keeps alive elsewhere ("the motive forces behind technology have made life comfortable for a relatively few humans while they have unintentionally but progressively destroyed the biosphere"). `Interpretive synthesis`
- **"Camouflage" presumes a hidden truth the metaphor cannot itself disclose.** The figure asserts that the data conceals "what is actually happening to biological man" but supplies that hidden event only as conjecture (self-inflicted evolution, the post-human species). The metaphor stages a revelation it then withholds. `Interpretive synthesis`

This is the register in which Burnham's figure differs sharply from O'Gieblyn's later [[Technology as Metaphor]]: O'Gieblyn diagnoses a *figurative* slide by which a technological model becomes the literal language for mind and cosmos, whereas Burnham insists his web is not a metaphor for an autonomous force but the description of one — a power that needs no metaphor to act, even as he can only reach it *through* metaphor. `Interpretive synthesis`
