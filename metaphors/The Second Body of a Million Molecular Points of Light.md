# The Second Body of a Million Molecular Points of Light

## The Metaphor

In the keystone late chapter "Weaving the Shamantic Web" (1998), Roy Ascott reports a first-person ayahuasca vision in which he was "at one and the same time conscious of inhabiting two bodies, the familiar phenomenology of my own body sheathed, as it were, in a **second body** made up of a mass of multicoloured particles, a **million molecular points of light**." The figure is a *double body*: the everyday lived body, and over it (sheathing it, like a second skin or aura) a body resolved into pure particulate luminosity — matter pixelated down to a swarm of moving points. It is the embodied, phenomenological correlate of his concept of `[[Double Consciousness]]` ("the state of being that gives access, at one and the same time, to two distinctly different fields of experience"): just as consciousness splits into two simultaneous fields, the body splits into two simultaneous bodies, and the `[[The Double Gaze|double gaze]]` moves "more or less at will" between them. `Directly stated`

The image arrives inside Ascott's structuring analogy between two "technologies of consciousness" — the digital ("an extremely powerful computer") and the shamanic ("a particularly potent plant (ayahuasca, the 'vine of the soul')"). The second body is the shamanic-trance counterpart of the avatar body the virtual-reality user wears in cyberspace; Ascott names this the `[[The Post-Biological Body|post-biological]]` condition, "our ability, aided by computer technology, to move effortlessly through the infinities of cyberspace, while at the same time accommodating ourselves within the structures of the material world." `Directly stated`

## What It Compares

- **Target:** the dematerialized, data-rendered, or `[[The Post-Biological Body|post-biological]]` body — the body as it is re-experienced under a technology of consciousness (VR immersion, or the plant), no longer felt as solid mass but as information, pattern, or field; and, more broadly, the phenomenology of `[[Double Consciousness]]` registered at the level of embodiment.
- **Source domain:** a *second body* of light — a luminous particulate shell ("a mass of multicoloured particles, a million molecular points of light") that *sheathes* the ordinary body, like an aura, a cloud of fireflies, or the pixel-swarm of a screen image, present at the same time as and superimposed upon the flesh.

The mapping is structural and bodily, not merely visual: the *two bodies* map onto the *two fields of experience* of double consciousness; the *sheathing* (one body inside/over the other, both occupied at once) maps onto the *simultaneity* of the double gaze; the *resolution of flesh into discrete points of light* maps onto the conversion of body into data/information/pattern that the vault tracks as `[[The Body as Data]]`; and the freedom to "move in and out of them more or less at will" maps onto the navigability of cyberspace and of trance alike. `Strongly implied`

## What It Reveals

- **It gives embodiment, not just consciousness, to the post-biological claim.** Where much transhumanist and `[[Posthumanism|posthumanist]]` rhetoric treats the data body abstractly (uploading, the body "left behind"), Ascott's figure insists the second body is *felt* — it is "the familiar phenomenology of my own body" that is sheathed, not abandoned. The dematerialized body is reported as a lived experience, an addition rather than a subtraction. This makes the figure a rare phenomenological — rather than engineering — image of `[[The Body as Data]]`. `Strongly implied`
- **It pictures the collapse of the material/immaterial distinction at the scale of the body.** Elsewhere in the same chapter Ascott argues, via the scanning tunnelling microscope's atom-by-atom construction, that "the immaterial and material lose their categorical distinction." The second body of light *is* that collapse made bodily: matter (flesh) and information (points of light) are not opposed but co-present, each a way of being the same self. The figure is one of Ascott's strongest contributions to the `[[Material vs Immaterial]]` tension. `Strongly implied`
- **It models the dual citizenship of the networked subject.** "A million molecular points of light" reads equally as a description of a body dissolved into screen-pixels and of a node in a luminous network. The second body is the body one wears in `[[Full-Immersion Virtual Reality|virtual reality]]` and on the Net — the avatar, the data-double — and the figure shows the telematic subject inhabiting flesh and network *at once*, the embodied form of `[[Cyberception]]` and the `[[The Shamantic Web|shamantic web]]`. `Interpretive synthesis`

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It aestheticizes dematerialization as luminous and benign.** "A million molecular points of light" is a beautiful, almost sacred image (light, particles, shimmer); it lends the dissolution of the solid body the connotations of revelation and transcendence. It thereby hides what other vault authors foreground as loss or domination — that rendering the body as data is also what makes it `[[The Body as Data|trackable, storable, and manipulable]]`. The figure's beauty launders the data body of its political weight. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **It universalizes a chemically and ritually induced state.** The second body is reported under ayahuasca, within the precise architectural and social protocols of the Santo Daime ritual; Ascott himself notes that "specific protocols and conditions control or construct a given reality." Read as a general truth about the post-biological body, the figure smooths over the fact that this phenomenology is a *constructed*, drug- and ritual-dependent event, not the ordinary condition of embodiment. `Strongly implied`
- **It implies a clean separation of "two bodies" where there may be one continuous, ambiguous field.** The crisp duality (familiar body / second body, sheathed one inside the other) imposes a tidy two-term structure on what the surrounding prose describes as a fluctuating, fractal, "thousand repetitions of the same image" flux. The metaphor of *sheathing* makes the relation sound architectural and stable; the experience as narrated is unstable and recursive. `Interpretive synthesis`
