# The Pentecostal Company

## The Metaphor

The society of the future imagined as a **Pentecostal company that received the Holy Ghost as tired to a man** — a community whose shared inspiration is *tiredness* rather than activity, and whose revelation tells its members not what they *should* be but what they *need not* be. Han borrows the figure from Peter Handke's "Essay on Tiredness" and proposes it as a name for the society to come: a *society of tiredness* set against the contemporary *society of activity* (the doping, performance-driven [[Achievement Society]]).

The Pentecost image inverts its biblical sense. In Acts, the descent of the Holy Ghost fills the apostles with the power and the tongues to *act* and to *speak*; here the inspiration descends on a company that is *tired*, and what it inspires is **not-doing** — `Directly stated`.

The metaphor maps the structure of a religious revelation onto a social condition: just as Pentecost founds a community through a shared descent of spirit, fundamental tiredness founds a [[Community of Singularities]] through a shared, non-coercive in-difference. Han frames it as an "immanent religion of tiredness" — `Directly stated` that the figure is religious in form, `Interpretive synthesis` in reading the structural mapping.

## What It Reveals

- **Inspiration can be subtractive.** Where the [[Achievement Society]] understands inspiration and energy as additive — as fuel for more performance — the Pentecostal company's inspiration *removes* the imperative to perform: "The inspiration of tiredness tells them not so much what they should, as what they need not, be." This is the figure of [[Negative Potency]] (the capacity *not-to*, the "not-to" of the Sabbath) raised to a social principle — `Directly stated` for the quote, `Interpretive synthesis` for the link to negative potency.
- **A community without belonging or kinship.** The Pentecostal company gathers people without requiring relation or "familial or functional connections" — Han's [[Community of Singularities]], "a community that requires neither belonging nor relation," prefigured for Handke in a Dutch still life where beetle, snail, bee, and butterfly are "all there together at the moment" — `Directly stated`.
- **An alternative to violence and isolation.** Against the "solitary," "divisive" I-tiredness that "destroys all that is common or shared" and "drove us to violence," the inspired tiredness of the company is peaceable: "my gaze disarmed every intimation of a violent gesture, a conflict, or even of an unfriendly attitude, before it could get started" — `Directly stated`.

The figure thus folds together several of Handke's smaller images that Han threads through the chapter: the **new Orpheus** ("A certain tired man can be seen as a new Orpheus; the wildest beasts gather around him and are at last able to join in his tiredness"), the **playing hand** set against the "laboring, gripping hand," and the **disarming gaze** of the tired person in whom "resolution yields to a state of calm." See [[Fundamental Tiredness]] for the underlying condition these images describe.

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It aestheticizes exhaustion.** By naming a desirable "fundamental tiredness," the figure risks blurring into the very burnout it opposes. Han is careful to distinguish the two — inspiring tiredness "hardly amounts to a state of exhaustion in which one proves unable to do anything"; the tiredness of exhaustion is "the tiredness of positive potency" that "makes one incapable of doing something," whereas inspired tiredness is the tiredness of [[Negative Potency]] — but the single word "tiredness" carries both, and the metaphor's appeal can collapse the distinction — `Directly stated` for Han's distinction, `Interpretive synthesis` for the risk.
- **It offers a contemplative exit, not a structural one.** The Pentecostal company is a vision of redeemed coexistence ("a form of salvation, a form of rejuvenation"), but it names no mechanism by which the doping, performance-machine society would actually give way to it. The image works at the register of an "immanent religion," not of political or economic transformation — `Interpretive synthesis`.
- **Pentecost's own content is suppressed.** The biblical Pentecost is the gift of *tongues* and of *mission* — language and going-out. Han's borrowing keeps the form (a company inspired in common) while emptying it of speech and action, which is the point; but the inversion can read as a quietism that mistakes withdrawal for community — `Speculative connection`.
