# The Private CIA and State Department

## The Metaphor

The self is run like a government with two badly coordinated branches. A covert agency — "my own private CIA" — takes actions on its own, without the approval or even the knowledge of the official spokesbody — "my State Department." The State Department, confronted afterward and having no real idea why the action was taken, "cooks up a story that presents me in the best possible light." Harari states it directly: "Again and again my own private CIA does things without the approval or knowledge of my State Department, and then my State Department cooks up a story that presents me in the best possible light." (`Directly stated`)

The image generalizes Michael Gazzaniga's split-brain findings to everyone. In the clinic, the speech-controlling left hemisphere would confabulate reasons for actions actually driven by data only the mute right hemisphere had seen ("the chicken claw goes with the chicken, and you need a shovel to clean out the chicken shed"), leading Gazzaniga to conclude that the left hemisphere houses "an internal interpreter that constantly tries to make sense of our life, using partial clues in order to concoct plausible stories." Harari sets the geopolitical version immediately alongside it: "It's as if the CIA conducts a drone strike in Pakistan, unbeknown to the US State Department. When a journalist grills State Department officials about it, they make up some plausible explanation." The narrating self *is* that State Department — a press office, not a witness. (`Directly stated`)

## What It Compares

- **The covert agency (CIA)** → the sub-personal, sub-conscious processes that actually initiate action: the "genes, hormones and neurons," the "biochemical processes in my brain," the right-hemisphere data the speaking self never sees. These are the [[Organisms Are Algorithms|biochemical algorithms]] that decide; in the brain-scanner experiments their "neural events… begin from a few hundred milliseconds to a few seconds before the person is aware of this choice."
- **The drone strike / the action taken without approval** → the decision or desire that "wells up" already finished — "I don't choose my desires. I only feel them, and act accordingly."
- **The State Department spokesperson** → the **narrating self**, "akin to Gazzaniga's left-brain interpreter," the verbal module we identify *as* ("When they say 'I', they mean the story in their head").
- **The cooked-up story / plausible explanation** → confabulation: the post-hoc rationale "that presents me in the best possible light," generated by a press office that "don't have a clue why the strike was ordered, so they just invent something."
- **The State Department believing its own spin** → self-deception: "Often enough, the State Department itself becomes convinced of the pure fantasies it has invented."

The mapping is target → source-domain: the self's account of itself is bureaucratic public relations, not introspective truth. (`Directly stated`; the desire/timing details are `Strongly implied` as elements of the same mechanism Harari is illustrating.)

## What It Reveals

- **Self-knowledge is retrospective spin, not direct access.** The metaphor dramatizes that the "I" who explains my behavior was not in the room when the behavior was decided. Reasons are issued after the fact by a department structurally cut off from operations — which is why introspective confidence is no evidence of introspective accuracy. (`Directly stated`)
- **The narrating self is a confabulation engine, not a reporter.** It exposes the [[The Experiencing Self vs The Narrating Self|narrating self]] as a story-spinner whose product is governed by the need for a flattering, coherent plot rather than by fidelity to the record — continuous with the peak-end rule and duration-neglect that distort its memories elsewhere in the chapter. (`Directly stated`)
- **It motivates the political payoff of the book.** If the self's own account is unreliable cover-story, then an external system that actually logged every operation could represent my interests better than my press office does — the warrant Harari builds toward [[Algorithmic Determinism]] and [[Dataism]] ("Google will not make decisions on the basis of cooked-up stories"). (`Interpretive synthesis`; the Google line is from [[raw/Harari - Homo Deus/12 The Great Decoupling]], adjacent to this chapter.)
- **It pictures the self as plural and governmental.** Rendering the person as a multi-agency state makes vivid Harari's claim that "humans aren't individuals. They are 'dividuals'" — a "tug of war between different and often conflicting inner entities," not a single sovereign chooser. The self becomes an [[Intersubjective Reality|imaginary story]] running inside one skull. (`Directly stated`)

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It smuggles agency back in while denying it.** A CIA and a State Department are *intentional* institutions with goals, secrets, and strategies. Harari's larger argument is that the underlying processes are "deterministic or random," i.e. *not* agential at all (see [[Free Will as Fiction (Harari)]]). The metaphor humanizes the mechanism it is meant to demystify — the "covert agency" is really just firing neurons with no plan, yet the image lends it the cunning of a spy service. (`Interpretive synthesis`)
- **It implies a unified perpetrator behind the spin.** "The CIA" and "the State Department" are each singular bodies, suggesting two coherent inner persons. But Harari's own view dissolves even these into "a stream of consciousness" with "no permanent self who owns the desires" — the two-branch picture is a useful simplification that re-introduces exactly the bounded selves it should eliminate. (`Strongly implied`)
- **The "best possible light" framing implies a vanity motive.** Casting confabulation as flattering PR suggests the interpreter's distortions are self-serving by design. Gazzaniga's interpreter, by contrast, simply seeks *any* plausible coherence (the snow-shovel story is not flattering, just credible) — so the metaphor over-reads ego where the clinical mechanism shows only pattern-completion. (`Interpretive synthesis`)
- **It naturalizes a covert-state model of mind.** The figure presupposes a familiar (and ideologically loaded) image of government as a duplicitous deep state plus a lying press office. That picture carries connotations — secrecy, propaganda, manufactured consent — that may say as much about the metaphor's geopolitics as about the brain. (`Speculative connection`)
