# Capitalism Should Not Be Eaten Raw

## The Metaphor

Capitalism is food — specifically sausage — that "is meant to be cooked by a democratic society and its institutions because raw capitalism is antisocial." Zuboff offers this as a one-line précis of Thomas Piketty's research: just as raw meat is dangerous and must be cooked before it is fit to eat, capitalism is dangerous and must be processed through democratic institutions before it is fit for human society. To eat it raw is to ingest something that sickens the body politic.

## What It Compares

- **Target:** capitalism's relationship to the democratic institutions that govern it.
- **Source domain:** the culinary distinction between raw and cooked food (with sausage — composite, processed, only safe once cooked — as the specific image).

The mapping:

| Source domain (food) | Target (political economy) |
|---|---|
| Raw, uncooked meat | Capitalism untethered from countervailing law and policy |
| Cooking | The work of democratic society and its institutions — `[[The Double Movement]]` |
| The cook | A democratic society and its institutions (legislation, regulation, social contest) |
| Eating raw → illness | Releasing raw capitalism → "antisocial" consequences: divergence, inequality, threat to democracy |
| Cooked, edible food | Capitalism "harnessed… to society's needs" — market democracy |

The image sits inside `[[The Double Movement]]` argument: the double movement *is* the cooking, the democratic institutional labor that renders capitalism social. Its deliberate "demolition under the neoliberal flag" (`[[Neoliberalism]]`) is what serves capitalism raw — and `[[Surveillance Capitalism]]` is the contemporary mutant form that grows in the absence of countervailing institutions. `Interpretive synthesis`

## What It Reveals

- **Capitalism is not self-justifying or self-regulating.** The metaphor makes vivid Polanyi's and Piketty's shared claim that a self-regulating market, "left to itself," "contains powerful forces of divergence, which are potentially threatening to democratic societies." Raw capitalism is the *default*, dangerous state, not a neutral baseline; safety is something *added* by institutions. `Directly stated`
- **Domestication is necessary, ongoing labor.** Cooking is active work that must be done before consumption and done again each time. This frames the double movement not as a one-time achievement but as continual institutional cooking — and explains why its "demolition" is catastrophic rather than merely a return to nature. `Strongly implied`
- **Surveillance capitalism is "raw" capitalism.** By naming the un-cooked state as "antisocial," the figure positions surveillance capitalism as undomesticated capitalism let loose by the failure to re-tether — pairing with Han's `[[Dictatorship of Capital]]` and `[[Capital as Religion]]` (capital freed of external check). `Interpretive synthesis`

## What It Hides or Distorts

- **It naturalizes "cooked" capitalism as wholesome.** The metaphor concedes that capitalism, once processed by democratic institutions, is acceptable — even nourishing. It frames the problem as one of *preparation*, not of the ingredient itself. This may understate whether surveillance capitalism's novelty (claiming human experience as raw material) exceeds anything a "cooking" could domesticate. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **It implies a clean cook/ingredient separation.** Cooking is external to the meat; but democratic institutions are not external to capitalism — they are entangled with it, funded by it, and (per Piketty) subject to "political capture" by "financial elites." The kitchen analogy hides the way the eater's institutions can be bought by what is on the plate. `Strongly implied`
- **It domesticates antagonism into hygiene.** "Eating raw" frames the harm as a food-safety problem — a matter of correct procedure — rather than as structural conflict, class power, or the active dismantling of protections by interested parties. The neoliberal "demolition" of the double movement was a political project, not a cooking accident. `Interpretive synthesis`
- **It treats capitalism as a single dish.** Industrial capitalism and surveillance capitalism are very different "meats"; a recipe (double movement) adequate to one may be useless against the other. The figure's reassuring familiarity can obscure that the contemporary problem may require a new cuisine entirely. `Strongly implied`
