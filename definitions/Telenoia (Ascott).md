# Telenoia — Roy Ascott

## Author's Definition

Telenoia is Ascott's own 1992 coinage — the term he invented "to describe both the means and the meaning" of the telematic process. He builds it from the Greek roots *tele* ("far off") and *nous* ("mind"), and defines it tersely as **"networked consciousness, interactive awareness, mind at large (to use Gregory Bateson's term)."** (*Directly stated*, ch. 23.)

The word is constructed as the deliberate antonym of **paranoia**. Where paranoia names the suspicious, isolated, alienated self of the old industrial order — "the secrecy and suspicion sustained by the paradigm of paranoia, which is what the smokestack culture engendered" — telenoia names the connective, shared mind of the networked culture: "Telenoia is thus set to replace the paranoia of the isolated, alienated self of the old industrial society." (*Directly stated*, ch. 23.) Ascott makes the opposition explicit and almost playful elsewhere — "Telenoia: computer-mediated, distributed mind-at-large: asynchronous global connectivity. Not paranoia!" (*Directly stated*, ch. 26.)

## Context

Ascott coined "telenoia" in November 1992 to name a specific twenty-four-hour global networking project at the V2 Organisation in 's-Hertogenbosch, Holland (bulletin boards, e-mail, computer conferencing, fax, videophone), and to "stand for the nature of the telematic process in general" (*Directly stated*, ch. 23). The term therefore does double duty: it names an artwork-event and a condition of consciousness.

The definition sits inside Ascott's larger argument that telematic art "generates meaning through our intimate participation in its evolution" and "cannot be viewed, from the outside, as a discrete entity" — telenoia is the felt, from-inside-the-network state that such art both requires and produces. He gives it an evolutionary and almost mythic provenance ("if, in the beginning, the Word was Telenoia"), aligning it with right-hemisphere, "visual, mosaic, all-at-once" thinking and "the non-linear, intuitional, fast-as-lightning distributed mind of networks," against the "linear, mechanical thinking" of deterministic industrialisation. (*Directly stated*, ch. 23.)

Across the book the same word recurs as a structuring principle. In ch. 26 it is one of "four new ways of being and doing" arising in the telematic culture (alongside cyber-estate, inter-reality, and cyberception) that constitute the planetary collegium. In ch. 27 it is the thing a future cyber-city should be built to "fully celebrate" — "the product of networked consciousness, interactive awareness, thought at a distance, or 'mind-at-large.'" In ch. 29 it names "the new society, the society of connectivity, open-ended creativity, collaborative consciousness," glossed as "consciousness-at-large." Telenoia is thus the consciousness-side companion to the technology-side term [[Telematics (Ascott)]] and the felt experience named by the concept [[Network Consciousness]].

## Differences from Other Uses

Telenoia is a neologism unique to Ascott, so there is no rival authorial definition of the *word* in the vault. It is best distinguished by the existing notes it is closest to:

- **vs the concept [[Network Consciousness]]**: Network Consciousness is the broader, fuller exposition of the felt expansion of mind in the net (the noosphere / global-brain register, Bateson's "thinking system" scaled to the planet). Telenoia is Ascott's compact, coined *name* for that state, pointedly defined against paranoia and rooted in the V2 project. The two are cross-linked; Network Consciousness is the default conceptual target. (*Interpretive synthesis*.)
- **vs Gregory Bateson's "mind at large"**: Ascott explicitly borrows Bateson's term as the gloss for telenoia, but extends it from Bateson's ecology of mind ("human plus computer system plus environment constitutes a thinking system") to a planet-scale, computer-mediated *art* and *consciousness* program. (*Directly stated*, ch. 23/27.)
- **vs [[Distributed Cognition]]** (the cross-author cognitive-science register): telenoia overlaps with distributed cognition but is deliberately spiritual and visionary — "Telematic technology is the technology of consciousness; it is spiritual before it is in any way utilitarian" — courting the [[Re-enchantment]] cluster rather than a neutral information-processing account. (*Strongly implied*.)
- **vs Teilhard de Chardin's noosphere / Peter Russell's global brain**: telenoia shares the cosmic, planetary-mind ambition but is grounded in a specific network practice and named in opposition to a specific pathology (paranoia), rather than as a stage of evolutionary teleology. (*Interpretive synthesis*.)
