# Negative Feedback — Jack Burnham

## Author's Definition

Burnham, reporting Wiener and Bigelow's foundational insight, defines negative feedback parenthetically as the technical core of cybernetic control:

> negative feedback (in both animals and machines, this is a fractional return of the energy output of a system in the form of information guiding the system's future activities) was the key concept to control.

`Directly stated`. The definition fuses two claims: (1) feedback is a *fractional* return of *output energy* re-entered as *information*, and (2) this information *guides the system's future activities* — and crucially it operates identically across the animal/machine divide ("in both animals and machines"). Burnham further specifies the *negative* qualifier against its opposite: negative feedback is "the process whereby any number of coupled systems are designed to maintain a relatively constant input-output relationship," whereas positive feedback (Wiener's "hunting" symptom; the dynamite explosion) "is caused when a mechanical or chemical chain reaction exceeds the boundaries of restraint" (`Directly stated`).

## Context

The definition appears in Chapter 8 ("Robot and Cyborg Art"), where Burnham narrates the 1943 Rosenblueth–Wiener–Bigelow paper "Behavior, Purpose and Teleology" and the birth of [[Cybernetics]] from Wiener's *Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine* (1948). For Burnham this is not a digression into engineering but the technical foundation of his art-historical thesis: that sculpture is moving "toward a systems view of art" and ultimately toward a [[The Cyborg|cyborg]] art capable of genuine two-way exchange with the observer. Negative feedback is the mechanism that would let an art object stop being "an inert object" receiving "one-way stimulation" and instead "engage in a series of interactions with a human observer." Burnham explicitly ties the desire of Kinetic Artists "for a flexible interrelationship between their work and the viewer" to the requirement of negative feedback — `Strongly implied` that, for Burnham, negative feedback is the technical precondition of [[Reciprocal Communication]] and responsive art.

Burnham anchors the biological side of the definition in Walter Cannon's *The Wisdom of the Body* (1932): negative feedback is the principle behind [[Homeostasis]], "the ability of the body to keep its quantities of liquids, solids, and gases in a state of equilibrium" through "various strategies, which include feedback mechanisms" (`Directly stated`). The machine side is the "servo system" or "closed-loop communication regulated as are Cannon's homeostasis functions." Negative feedback is thus the hinge of Burnham's organicism: the shared organizational principle that makes "both natural and man-created systems" elicit "related behavioral responses."

## Differences from Other Uses

The vault grounds feedback chiefly through Hayles (*How We Became Posthuman*), where [[Feedback Loops]] and [[Homeostasis]] are read within the history of [[The Three Waves of Cybernetics]] and the contest between [[Homeostasis vs Reflexivity|homeostasis and reflexivity]]. Differences:

- **Burnham draws the same Wiener/Cannon lineage to an aesthetic end.** Where Hayles tracks feedback as it carries the *erasure* of [[Embodiment|embodiment]] into the [[The Liberal Humanist Subject|liberal-humanist]]/[[Posthumanism|posthuman]] story, Burnham takes feedback as the *enabling* technology of a new, more-than-mimetic art — the "key concept to control" that lets sculpture cross from inert object to responsive [[Cybernetics|cybernetic]] organism. `Interpretive synthesis`.
- **Burnham's emphasis is on the energy/information fusion.** His parenthetical insists feedback is energy output *re-entered as information* — a formulation close to the period's information theory (he cites Shannon and Weaver on the same page) but put to the service of [[Systems Theory|a systems view of art]]. `Strongly implied`.
- **Distinct from [[Negative Potency]]** (Han, after Agamben): that is the dialectical "power not-to," a faculty of refusal. Burnham's *negative feedback* is a control-theoretic, stabilizing return of signal. The shared word "negative" is coincidental; the concepts do not overlap. `Directly stated` (the two texts are unrelated).

- [[Homeostasis]] — Cannon's bodily equilibrium, Burnham's biological exemplar of negative feedback.
- [[Reciprocal Communication]] — the two-way exchange negative feedback makes possible; Burnham's goal for responsive/cyborg art.
