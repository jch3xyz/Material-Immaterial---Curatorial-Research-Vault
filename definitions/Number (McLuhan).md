# Number — Marshall McLuhan

## Author's Definition

For Marshall McLuhan, number is not an abstract quantity but a **technology** — an extension and separation of the human body, specifically of the sense of touch. Like every medium in *Understanding Media*, number is unintelligible while treated as a self-standing object and becomes legible only once it is read as an outering of a bodily faculty:

> In isolation, number is as mysterious as writing. Seen as an extension of our physical bodies, it becomes quite intelligible.

McLuhan then names the precise faculty number extends, pairing it against writing in a single sentence. Where writing extends the eye, number extends the hand:

> Just as writing is an extension and separation of our most neutral and objective sense, the sense of sight, number is an extension and separation of our most intimate and interrelating activity, our sense of touch.

This faculty of touch he glosses with the Greek term "haptic," and — crucially for the definition — he refuses to confine touch to skin contact. Touch is reconceived as the **interplay among the senses**, i.e. the sense-ratio itself:

> It may very well be that in our conscious inner lives the interplay among our senses is what constitutes the sense of touch. Perhaps touch is not just skin contact with things, but the very life of things in the mind?

So number is the extension of an interrelating, integrating faculty, which is why McLuhan repeatedly insists that it "originates in the sense of touch, of which it is an extension," and that it is "the tactile and quantitative form" of a medium. `Directly stated`

## Context

The definition appears in Part II's chapter on number ("Number: Profile of the Crowd"), one of the case studies in which McLuhan applies the book's master equation — that every medium is an [[Media as Extensions of Man|extension of man]] — to a specific technology. Number is set inside a longer historical argument: phonetic letters and number "were the first means of fragmenting and detribalizing man," the technologies that pulled the individual out of the tribal group in space (privacy), in thought ("point of view"), and in work (specialism). Number is thus enlisted in McLuhan's account of [[Detribalization and Retribalization]]: it is part of the "shadow of number, the language of science" that runs alongside literacy as a detribalizing force. `Directly stated`

The pairing of number-with-touch against writing-with-sight is what makes the definition do work in the larger thesis. Because a medium acts by re-proportioning the senses (see [[Sense Ratios]]), defining number as the extension of touch positions it as the **tactile, integrating** counter-pole to the **visual, fragmenting** medium of phonetic writing. McLuhan tracks the two through history — the medieval positional zero becoming Renaissance infinity once print intensified the visual faculty, Euclidean space as "a direct result of the action of the phonetic alphabet on the human senses" — to show technologies translating into one another "through the agency of our own senses." The chapter closes the loop by noting that the electric age "brings number back into unity with visual and auditory experience," dethroning the visual sense and restoring "the dominion of synesthesia." `Directly stated`

The redefinition of touch as the interplay among the senses is the conceptual hinge. It lets McLuhan equate touch with the Greek "consensus or a faculty of 'common sense' that translated each sense into each other sense, and conferred consciousness on man." Touch, so understood, is the integrating sense — which is why a technology that extends and *separates* touch (number) participates in the "state of disintegration" he diagnoses, "putting us out of touch with ourselves." `Strongly implied`

## Differences from Other Uses

- This is the **definition** note for number-as-technology. It sits beside McLuhan's broader concept [[Media as Extensions of Man]] and the master definition [[Extensions of Man (McLuhan)]], applying that equation to the single case of number; it should not be collapsed into them. `Interpretive synthesis`
- McLuhan's number is the opposite direction of travel from the vault's posthuman and Dataist authors. Where [[N. Katherine Hayles]], [[Ray Kurzweil]], and [[Yuval Noah Harari]] (in [[The Body as Data]], [[Dataism]], [[Organisms Are Algorithms]]) treat number/quantification as the medium *into which* the body and world are abstracted and dematerialized, McLuhan treats number as a faculty pushed *outward from* the body — an extension that remains continuous with the sensorium, rooted in touch, rather than a disembodiment. His "mysterious in isolation, intelligible as an extension of the body" formula is in effect a re-embodiment of what those authors read as pure information. `Interpretive synthesis`
- McLuhan's "haptic" is idiosyncratic. He does not mean cutaneous sensation but the **interplay among the senses** — the sense-ratio as such. This is a private redefinition: ordinary usage and most aesthetics treat the haptic as the sense of skin-contact and grasp, whereas McLuhan makes it the integrating "common sense" of the whole sensorium. The link [[Sense Ratios]] is therefore not incidental: for McLuhan touch *is* the ratio. `Strongly implied`
- Number here is not Pythagorean or magical in McLuhan's own voice. He reports the "ancient world" that "associated number magically with the properties of physical things," and Spengler's claim that "number is the essence of all things perceptible to the senses," but he reframes both — number's apparent magic is the iconic, tactile compression of a bodily extension, not a metaphysical property of things. `Directly stated`
