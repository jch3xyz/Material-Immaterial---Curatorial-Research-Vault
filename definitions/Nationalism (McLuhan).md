# Nationalism — Marshall McLuhan

## Author's Definition

McLuhan defines nationalism, in a parenthetical aside that is the crispest statement of the idea in the chapter, as **"continuity and competition in homogeneous space"** — and, as a felt psychic form, as "an intense new visual image of group destiny and status" that "depended on a speed of information movement unknown before printing."

> In the Renaissance it was the speed of print and the ensuing market and commercial developments that made nationalism (which is continuity and competition in homogeneous space) as natural as it was new.

> Nationalism itself came as an intense new visual image of group destiny and status, and depended on a speed of information movement unknown before printing.

The definition has three load-bearing terms. *Homogeneous space* is the uniform, repeatable, additive field that print produces by turning each vernacular into "an extensive mass medium" and by re-training the blood tribe into "an association of men homogeneously trained to be individuals." *Continuity* and *competition* name how interchangeable individuals are aggregated within that space — added one to another in a continuous, measurable extent, and set in atomistic rivalry. The nation, on this account, is not an ancient organic given but a *new* product of a *medium*: it is "as natural as it was new" because print made the homogeneous space feel like the natural order of things. (*Directly stated*.)

## Context

This is the headline thesis of *Understanding Media*'s Part II case-study chapter on the printed word, and the definition is the precise, citable kernel of the larger concept [[Print as the Architect of Nationalism]]. McLuhan introduces it as one of "the many unforeseen consequences of typography," indeed "perhaps, the most familiar," arguing that "political unification of populations by means of vernacular and language groupings was unthinkable before printing turned each vernacular into an extensive mass medium." (*Directly stated*.)

Within the book's apparatus, the definition does specific work. It is the print instance of [[Detribalization and Retribalization]]: "The tribe, an extended form of a family of blood relatives, is exploded by print, and is replaced by an association of men homogeneously trained to be individuals." It is an expression of the homogenizing, uniform, repeatable order of [[Typographic Man]] — the same "principle of extension by homogenization that is the key to understanding Western power." And it is dated by the medium that produced it: nationalism, McLuhan notes, "today... still depends on the press but has all the electric media against it," so that under [[Explosion vs Implosion|electric implosion]] and "even jet-plane speeds" the "older national groupings of social organization" become "quite unworkable." Nationalism is thus a mechanical-age, [[Mechanical vs Electric|print-age]] form on the far side of its peak, not a permanent condition.

This definition note sits *beside* the concept [[Print as the Architect of Nationalism]] — the same pairing pattern the vault uses for [[Information]] / [[Information (Han)]]. The concept carries the full causal mechanism (print → vernacular mass medium → exploded tribe → homogeneous national space); this note holds the bare, quotable definition.

## Differences from Other Uses

McLuhan's definition is distinctive in the vault for being a **media-determinist** account: nationalism is defined not by ethnicity, ideology, or sovereignty but as an *effect of a medium's form* — the homogeneous, continuous, repeatable space that movable-type print imposes. The unit of explanation is the technology, not the political idea, consistent with [[The Medium Is the Message]].

No other ingested author in the vault offers a competing definition of *nationalism* as such, so the contrast is by adjacency rather than head-to-head dispute:

- It is a primary-source upstream statement of the homogenization that [[Byung-Chul Han]] later diagnoses critically as [[Gleichschaltung]] and the [[Hell of the Same]]: where Han reads the smoothing-away of difference into the same as pathology, McLuhan reads the homogenizing of vernacular populations as the neutral mechanical cause of a powerful new social form. The valuation differs; the homogenization is the shared object. (*Interpretive synthesis*.)
- Its "continuity... in homogeneous space" and "add individual to individual" logic is the lineal, additive habit that Han's [[Narration vs Addition]] opposes to narrative — print's additive aggregation prefigured. (*Interpretive synthesis*.)
- Keep this note distinct from the concept [[Print as the Architect of Nationalism]] (the mechanism) and do not collapse it into the general concept [[Typographic Man]] (the broader subject print produces); nationalism is one specific social product of typographic man.
