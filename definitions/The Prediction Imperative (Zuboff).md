# The Prediction Imperative — Shoshana Zuboff

## Author's Definition

Zuboff names and fixes the term in Chapter 7 ("The Reality Business"), where she traces the competition over markets for future behavior past the point at which sheer volume of supply suffices. The defining passage is the moment of crystallization (*Directly stated*):

> It crystallizes a second economic imperative—the prediction imperative—and reveals the intense pressure that it exerts on surveillance capitalist revenues.

The full definitional work, however, is done by the passage that contrasts the new imperative with the extraction imperative and fixes its content as a matter of *quality* rather than *scale* (*Directly stated*):

> Competition for surveillance revenues eventually reached a point at which the volume of surplus became a necessary but insufficient condition for success. The next threshold was defined by the quality of prediction products. In the race for higher degrees of certainty, it became clear that the best predictions would have to approximate observation. The prediction imperative is the expression of these competitive forces.

The term is a coinage built to pair with — and succeed — Zuboff's first imperative. Where [[The Extraction Imperative]] is "the competitive forces that drive the need for surplus *at scale*," the prediction imperative is the expression of the competitive forces that drive the need for prediction *quality*, once volume has become "a necessary but insufficient condition for success." Its whole content is compressed into one law: "the best predictions would have to approximate observation." An imperative, not a strategy, it is a structural compulsion built into the logic of accumulation rather than a choice a firm can decline.

## Context

Zuboff introduces the prediction imperative at the hinge of Part II, just after summarizing the extraction imperative as the scale-governing logic of the first wave of prediction products (targeted online advertising). The prediction imperative is what takes over once that scale-threshold is crossed: it is "a second economic imperative," logically subsequent to extraction and concerned not with the *amount* of supply but with the predictive *certainty* it can yield (*Strongly implied*).

The same chapter draws out the imperative's fateful escalation. Because "the best predictions would have to approximate observation," and the surest observation is to *make* the behavior, the imperative pushes beyond knowing into doing (*Directly stated*):

> The prospect of guaranteed outcomes alerts us to the force of the prediction imperative, which demands that surveillance capitalists make the future for the sake of predicting it. Under this regime, ubiquitous computing is not just a knowing machine; it is an actuating machine designed to produce more certainty about us and for them.

This compulsion toward certainty is also what drives surveillance capitalism off the screen and into the physical world. The internet-of-things apparatus is, for Zuboff, "the material expression of the prediction imperative," and the convergence of ubiquitous computing with the economic compulsion toward certainty "signals the metamorphosis of the digital infrastructure from a thing that we have to a thing that has us" (*Directly stated*).

This definition note isolates the explicit definitional passages in Chapter 7; the broader development of the imperative across Chapters 8 and 9 — its making "boundaries and borders intolerable," its preference for "individual ignorance," its transformation of "the things that we have into things that have us," and its drive "deeper into the self" — is carried by the concept note [[The Prediction Imperative]], following the `Information` / `Information (Han)` and [[The Extraction Imperative]] / `The Extraction Imperative (Zuboff)` precedent (*Interpretive synthesis*).

## Differences from Other Uses

- The prediction imperative is *not* a synonym for [[The Extraction Imperative]] — it is the *second* of surveillance capitalism's economic imperatives, deliberately kept in a separate note and cross-linked. Extraction governs *scale* (the volume of [[Behavioral Surplus]]); prediction governs *quality* (the certainty of [[Prediction Products]]). Zuboff is explicit that the second threshold is "defined by the quality of prediction products," and that volume alone became "a necessary but insufficient condition for success" (*Directly stated*).
- It is not a synonym for [[Surveillance Capitalism]] itself. Surveillance capitalism is the whole economic order; the prediction imperative is one of its constituent compulsions — the part defining the metabolism by which knowing escalates into doing on "the path to profit" (*Strongly implied*).
- It is distinct from the everyday sense of "prediction" as passive forecasting. Zuboff's imperative dissolves the forecasting/fabricating distinction: it "demands that surveillance capitalists *make* the future for the sake of predicting it," so ubiquitous computing becomes "an actuating machine," not merely "a knowing machine" (*Directly stated*).
- Against Han's [[Digital Panopticon]], the locus of compulsion differs. Han's subject illuminates and exploits *itself* — a phenomenology of voluntary self-exposure. Zuboff's prediction imperative is a *firm-side* economic compulsion that converts that exposure into manufactured certainty; what is at stake is not the subject's self-display but the capitalist's drive toward guaranteed outcomes (*Interpretive synthesis*).
- Against Harari's [[The Growth Imperative]], the parallel is structural but the object differs. Harari's growth imperative is the never-satisfied expansion built into modern capitalism in general; Zuboff's prediction imperative is its surveillance-capitalist specification — what must expand is not output or capital but *certainty about the future* (*Interpretive synthesis*).
- It is distinct from [[Dataism]] / [[Big Data]] as ideology. Dataism valorizes data flow as a worldview; the prediction imperative is a concrete economic necessity with named owners and revenue stakes. The ideology may normalize the rendition the imperative compels, but the imperative is what the firm is structurally compelled to obey (*Interpretive synthesis*).
