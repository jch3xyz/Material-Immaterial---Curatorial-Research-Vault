# The Uncontract — Shoshana Zuboff

## Author's Definition

For Zuboff, the *uncontract* is the annihilation of contract: surveillance capitalism's substitution of guaranteed, machine-executed outcomes for the uncertain, social, promissory relations that the institution of "contract" was built to manage. She derives the term from Google chief economist Hal Varian's celebration of "new contractual forms due to better monitoring," which she rejects as a misnomer — the "horseless-carriage syndrome." Varian's invention, she argues, "cannot be understood as simply another kind of contract. It is, in fact, the annihilation of contract; this invention is better understood as the uncontract."

The keystone definitional statement:

> The uncontract is not a space of contractual relations but rather a unilateral execution that makes those relations unnecessary.

The uncontract *desocializes* the contract: it manufactures certainty by replacing "promises, dialogue, shared meaning, problem solving, dispute resolution, and trust" — the expressions of solidarity and human agency institutionalized in the notion of contract "over the course of millennia" — with automated procedures. It "bypasses all that social work in favor of compulsion" (Directly stated).

## Context

The uncontract appears in Chapter 7, *The Reality Business*, in the section "Executing the Uncontract." Zuboff presents it as "a feature of the larger complex that is [[The Means of Behavioral Modification|the means of behavioral modification]], and it is therefore an essential modality of [[Surveillance Capitalism|surveillance capitalism]]." It is the contractual face of [[Economies of Action]]: it "contributes to economies of action by leveraging proprietary behavioral surplus to preempt and foreclose action alternatives, thus replacing the indeterminacy of social processes with the determinism of programmed machine processes" (Directly stated).

Zuboff is emphatic that the stakes are not mere automation: "This is not the automation of society, as some might think, but rather the replacement of society with machine action dictated by economic imperatives" (Directly stated). The uncontract is thus the contractual expression of the broader [[A Utopia of Certainty|utopia of certainty]] — guaranteed outcomes substituted for the open-endedness of human promising — and it is made possible by the asymmetries of knowledge and power that flow from surveillance capitalism's dominance over [[The Division of Learning in Society|the division of learning in society]] (Strongly implied). When we celebrate the uncontract, Zuboff warns, "we celebrate the asymmetries of knowledge and power that produce these new possibilities" (Directly stated).

## Differences from Other Uses

In ordinary legal and economic usage a *contract* is a voluntary, bilateral instrument that coordinates future action under uncertainty through mutual promise and recourse. Varian and the optimists treat continuous monitoring as merely improving contract (better enforcement, lower transaction cost). Zuboff's coinage inverts this: the uncontract is not an improved contract but the *abolition* of the contractual form, because it removes the very uncertainty — and therefore the dialogue, trust, and shared meaning — that contract exists to negotiate. Where the contract presumes two agents capable of promising and possibly defaulting, the uncontract presumes one party with the power to execute outcomes unilaterally and the other reduced to a behaving object.

This sharpens, within the vault, the surveillance and control cluster. Where Han's [[Digital Panopticon|digital panopticon]] and Harari's [[Dataism]] diagnose voluntary self-exposure and the data worldview, Zuboff's uncontract names a specific *mechanism* of compulsion that goes beyond observation to **execution** — closer to a tightening of [[Society of Control|the society of control]] from monitoring into guaranteed behavioral outcomes (Interpretive synthesis). It is the contractual hinge between Zuboff's [[Big Other (Zuboff)|Big Other]] (the ubiquitous sensate apparatus) and [[Instrumentarian Power (Zuboff)|instrumentarian power]] (the modification of behavior toward others' ends).
