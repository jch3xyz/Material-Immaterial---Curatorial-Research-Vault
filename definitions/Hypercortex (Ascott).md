# Hypercortex — Roy Ascott

## Author's Definition

For Ascott the **hypercortex** is the brain of the museum: the museum reconceived "as a brain," whose own "very tangible cortex" is extended into a planetary-scale organ of hyperlinked connectivity. It is not the building, not "smart interfaces" or "intelligent architecture," but the museum's *associative cognition* itself — "the cognitive nexus of all those ideas, forms, structures, and strategies that are generated in interspace … the hyperlinks of a profound connectivity, that constitute the field of becoming between the virtual and the real that is our global domain" (`Directly stated`).

The prefix *hyper-* carries two senses at once: the museum's organic cortex *raised to a higher power* (a meta-cortex, the cortex of an emergent collective mind), and the literal *hyper*links of the Net as its synaptic tissue. The hypercortex is the cognitive substrate of `Network Consciousness` — the planetary extension of the individual neural cortex into **interspace**, the "field of becoming between the virtual and the real."

It is the load-bearing figure of the title thesis `The Mind of the Museum`: that "the museum must become intelligent, must get smart," that "we have to stop thinking for the museum and allow it to think for itself." The hypercortex names *what* the museum thinks with once it does (`Strongly implied`).

## Context

Ascott introduces the term in his 1996 keynote "The Mind of the Museum" (file-ordinal 29; Shanken's internal ch. 25), an address to "The Total Museum" conference at the Art Institute of Chicago. The argument: the museum, born as a site of "spiritual transformation," "went wrong when it adopted the values of the old industrial culture" — "a society of paranoia." To survive the emerging **technoetic culture** it must model itself on the brain and learn the cultural consequences of `Cyberception`, "the technologically amplified faculties of perception and cognition."

The hypercortex is the pivot of Ascott's three-stage museum typology, **the museum of the third kind**: not the first kind (where one "could hang out with the muse"), nor the second (the mere transposition of art's "discrete objecthood … into its virtual other," which "won't work"), but a third where "the close encounter of minds with minds, *cortex to hypercortex*, virtual with the actual, leads to interactivity at the level of the spirit." The hypercortex is thus the *destination* term of a mind-to-mind encounter — the cognitive nexus an individual cortex meets when it enters interspace (`Directly stated`).

It is also the addressee of Ascott's positive program: the museum-building is to be replaced by **gardens of hypotheses** "laid out in the interspace between the virtual and the actual," which stand "to the hypercortex and to human cyberception … what the neo-Georgian and postmodern boxes of the old museum were to the Cartesian conformity of classical culture." The hypercortex is what Cartesian, compartmentalized, object-bound culture is being superseded *by* (`Directly stated`).

This makes the chapter a primary anchor for `Material vs Immaterial` (the museum/Net-gallery as the literal object→data site: "art now inhabits both the interspace between material and immaterial worlds"), and a strong cross-link to `Distributed Cognition` and `Network Consciousness`. It also extends `New Media Art` and `Art as Experiential Technology` — the latter directly, in "interactivity at the level of the spirit."

## Differences from Other Uses

- **vs. `Distributed Cognition` (Hayles, cross-author).** Distributed cognition is the general thesis that thinking is spread across body, tools, and environment. The hypercortex is Ascott's *specific* organ-figure for that distribution at planetary scale — the museum's (and the Net's) cortex as a single emergent brain. The hypercortex is one named instantiation of distributed cognition, not the field itself (`Interpretive synthesis`).
- **vs. `Network Consciousness` (Ascott).** Closely allied but distinct: network consciousness is the *collective awareness* / "telematic society of mind" the Net substrates; the hypercortex is its *neural organ* — the cognitive tissue (hyperlinks as synapses) that consciousness runs on. Mind vs. the cortex of that mind. Cross-linked, not merged (`Strongly implied`).
- **vs. `Sense Ratios` / `Media as Extensions of Man` (McLuhan).** McLuhan's electric age "extends the central nervous system" of man; Ascott explicitly inherits this lineage (he quotes *Understanding Media* in this very essay). The hypercortex is the cortical / higher-cognitive specification of McLuhan's nervous-system extension — not just sensory extension but *associative cognition* and *hyperlinked memory* raised into a collective brain (`Strongly implied`).
- **vs. The Mind of the Museum (concept).** `The Mind of the Museum` is the title thesis (the museum should think for itself); the hypercortex is the *what-it-thinks-with* — the cognitive nexus / organ named within that thesis. The definition note sits beside the concept, same pattern as `Information` / `Information (Han)` (`Directly stated`).
