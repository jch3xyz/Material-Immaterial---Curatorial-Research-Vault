# Technoetic Aesthetic — Roy Ascott

## Author's Definition

Ascott self-glosses a **technoetic aesthetic** — a new aesthetic so named because, on his account, *technology plus mind* both lets us explore consciousness more thoroughly and yields distinctly new forms of art, mind, cognition, and perception:

> a technoetic aesthetic, so named because I believe we need to recognize that technology plus mind not only enables us to explore consciousness more thoroughly but may lead to distinctly new forms of art, new qualities of mind, new forms of cognition and perception.

`Directly stated`. The name follows the same etymology as his cognate adjective `[[Technoetic (Ascott)|technoetic]]` — *techne* (art, craft, technology) joined to *noetic* (from *nous*, mind, consciousness) — but here it names not the culture as a whole but its specifically **aesthetic** dimension: the criterion that art is now made and judged by what it does to and with consciousness rather than by how it looks.

In his glossary *Technoetic Aesthetics: 100 Terms and Definitions for the Post-Biological Era*, Ascott gives the term its sharpest one-line form, defining the aesthetic as a succession away from the surface image of the world toward "creative consciousness and artificial life":

> Technoetic aesthetics The classical concern with the surface image of the world gives way to the technoetic aesthetics of creative consciousness and artificial life.

`Directly stated`. The two passages are complementary: the first names the *enabling principle* (technology + mind), the second states the *displacement* (surface image → creative consciousness and `[[Artificial Life]]`).

## Context

Ascott introduces the technoetic aesthetic in "Weaving the Shamantic Web" as the aesthetic correlate of his broader `[[Technoetics|technoetic]]` programme. Where the umbrella adjective names a whole convergent *culture* (the technological/scientific strands of twentieth-century art intertwining with the spiritual/conceptual strands), the technoetic *aesthetic* names the specific value-criterion that culture installs: art is no longer concerned with the appearance of objects but with the construction, navigation, and distribution of mind. He describes the technoetic artwork as "an enactment of mind" requiring "an intimate level of human interaction within the system, which constitutes the work of art" — an art that, eschewing "the passive voyeur, the traditional gallery viewer," instead "speaks to a kind of widespread intimacy, closeness on the planetary scale" `Directly stated`.

The aesthetic is thus inseparable from Ascott's faculty of `[[Cyberception]]` (technologically amplified perception and cognition) and from the participatory, system-constituting model of art he shares with the vault's `[[Reciprocal Communication]]` and `[[Art as Experiential Technology]]` clusters: a technoetic work is not a surface to be looked at but a system of mind to be entered `Interpretive synthesis`. Ascott is programmatic about the stakes: "the technoetic principle will be at the centre of art as it develops, and consciousness in all its forms will be the field of its unfolding" `Directly stated`. The classical "game of images is over"; what replaces the surface concern is an aesthetics of *creative consciousness* and *artificial life* — making `[[Emergence]]`, self-assembly, and `[[Autopoiesis]]` proper aesthetic categories rather than merely technical ones `Strongly implied`.

## Differences from Other Uses

This note is deliberately distinct from the existing definition `[[Technoetic (Ascott)]]`, which glosses the broad *adjective* / culture (technoetic culture = techne + noetic). Here the scope is narrowed to the **aesthetic** itself — the specific displacement of surface by consciousness, and the criterion that art's medium is now mind. Both are Ascott's coinages; Obsidian disambiguates the two by their `(Ascott)` titles, the same coexisting-notes pattern the vault uses elsewhere.

No other author in the vault uses "technoetic aesthetic(s)" — it is Ascott's term `Directly stated`. Its neighbours among other authors throw the contrast into relief:

- Against Burnham's `[[Systems Esthetics]]`, with which it shares the move from the discrete art object to the work-as-system, the technoetic aesthetic relocates the system's substance from physical/informational process to *consciousness*: where Burnham's spine runs object → system, Ascott's runs system → mind `Interpretive synthesis`. It explicitly extends Burnham's realized `[[New Media Art]]` and `[[Art as Experiential Technology]]` stubs toward a consciousness-centred horizon.
- Against the classical, surface- and image-bound aesthetic of representation, the technoetic aesthetic is its declared successor — "the classical concern with the surface image of the world gives way" — making it a position on, not a refinement of, the visual tradition `Directly stated`.
- It sharpens the vault's keystone `[[Material vs Immaterial]]` tension: by replacing the material surface image with the immaterial work of "creative consciousness," the technoetic aesthetic stakes out art's place "in the interspace between material and immaterial worlds" rather than resolving the opposition `Directly stated`.
- It overlaps O'Gieblyn's `[[Re-enchantment]]` in treating technology as the means by which mind and spirit return to the foreground of art, but routes that re-enchantment through `[[Artificial Life]]` and `[[Cyberception]]` rather than through metaphysics or theology `Strongly implied`.
