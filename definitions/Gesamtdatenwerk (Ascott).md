# Gesamtdatenwerk — Roy Ascott

## Author's Definition

Ascott coins **Gesamtdatenwerk** — "integrated data work" — as the deliberate, named successor to Wagner's **Gesamtkunstwerk** ("total artwork"). It is the planet-scale, permanently open, reciprocal networked artwork: a total *data* work in place of a total *art* work. Where Wagner's Gesamtkunstwerk was performed in an opera house, the site of the Gesamtdatenwerk is "the planet as a whole, its data space, its electronic noosphere." Its duration is "indeterminate, since this must be a work in flux and flow, permitting an infinity of interactions, inputs and outputs, collaborations and conjunctions between its many participants" (`Directly stated`, ch.18).

Because "reciprocity and interaction are of its essence," the Gesamtdatenwerk cannot differentiate "artist" from "viewer," producer from consumer: to participate in such a network "is always to be involved in the creation of meaning and experience" (`Directly stated`, ch.18). In the later "Is There Love in the Telematic Embrace?" (ch.20), Ascott specifies its sensorial-aesthetic ambition: the bringing together of "imaging, sound, and text systems into interactive environments that exploit state-of-the-art hypermedia and that engage the full sensorium, albeit by digital means" — a synthesis of the arts whose content "is in a state of flux, of endless change and transformation" and "is embodied in data that is itself immaterial" (`Directly stated`, ch.20).

## Context

The term appears in Ascott's central telematic-art manifesto (ch.18, 1989, originally in *Kunstforum*), structured by its subtitle triad **Connectivity, Transformation, Transcendence**. Ascott argues that computer networking is the constitutive medium and metaphor of late-twentieth-century culture, and that its true vocation is "to make the invisible visible." The Gesamtdatenwerk is the artwork-form proper to this medium: it requires the artist to "incorporate human and artificial movements, environmental dynamics, and ambient transformations all together into a more seamless whole." It is grounded in Ascott's redefinition of the computer as "not primarily a thing, but a set of behaviours" and of the interface as "a doorway into data space, a synaptic interval in a human-computer symbiosis" rather than a membrane walling off a discrete machine-object (`Directly stated`, ch.18).

In ch.20 the concept does concrete work: Ascott names his 1989 Ars Electronica project "Aspects of Gaia" as a Gesamtdatenwerk — "An energising stream of integrated digital images, texts, and sounds (a Gesamtdatenwerk) would then constitute a kind of invisible cloak, a digital noosphere that might contribute to the harmonisation of the planet" (`Directly stated`, ch.20). There the immateriality of the work is sharpened: its content "is pure electronic différance, until it has been reconstituted at the interface as image, text, or sound."

The Gesamtdatenwerk is the telematic culmination of Ascott's long-standing principle that the spectator completes the work; it is the direct extension point for the committed art/new-media notes the vault realized through Burnham — [[New Media Art]] and [[Art as Experiential Technology]] — and a primary instance of [[Reciprocal Communication]] scaled to the planetary network (`Interpretive synthesis`).

## Differences from Other Uses

- **Wagner's Gesamtkunstwerk** is Ascott's explicit, named model and foil (a `Directly stated` reference). The Gesamtkunstwerk unifies music, image, and poetry within a bounded performance site (the opera house) and preserves the composer-author / spectator division. The Gesamtdatenwerk transposes the synthesis onto global data space, makes its duration indeterminate, substitutes data for art-material, and — crucially — abolishes the artist/viewer (producer/consumer) distinction that Wagner's staged total artwork retains.

- **Burnham's [[Systems Esthetics]] and [[Object and System]]** (*Beyond Modern Sculpture*, 1968): Burnham theorizes the artwork moving from object to system. Ascott's Gesamtdatenwerk is the next move — from system to *network*: the artwork-as-planetary-data-field. Both insist the artwork is a behaviour or system the spectator completes; the Gesamtdatenwerk is a specific work-form within telematic / new-media art, not coextensive with the broad field (`Interpretive synthesis`). Kept DISTINCT from `Systems Esthetics` and from the broad concept [[New Media Art]].

- **Lippard's [[Dematerialization]]** and **Conceptual Art**: where conceptual art dematerialized the *object*, the Gesamtdatenwerk dematerializes the work into "data that is itself immaterial," "pure electronic différance" reconstituted only at the interface — a networked, participatory, sensory immateriality rather than a documentary one (`Interpretive synthesis`).

> The question of content must therefore be addressed to the Gesamtdatenwerk—the integrated data work— and to its capacity to engage the intellect, emotions, and sensibility of the observer. In a telematic art, meaning is not created by the artist, distributed through the network, and received by the observer.
