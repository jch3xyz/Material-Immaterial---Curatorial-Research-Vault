# The Credit Card

## Literal Meaning in the Source

In *Understanding Media*'s chapter on money, "the poor man's credit card" is the chapter's subtitle and the credit card is its closing figure — the terminal form in a historical progression of money. McLuhan stages money as a single medium evolving through stages of decreasing materiality: from commodity money (whales' teeth, rats, leather, cigarettes — "Money always retains something of its commodity and community character"), through metallic coinage, to "representative money, or Paper money," and finally to "the informational forms of credit and credit card." The credit card appears literally as the image of money at the moment it ceases to store work and becomes pure information: "As work is replaced by the sheer movement of information, money as a store of work merges with the informational forms of credit and credit card." `Directly stated`

It is named explicitly as the endpoint of a directional series: "From coin to paper currency, and from currency to credit card there is a steady progression toward commercial exchange as the movement of information itself." `Directly stated`

## Possible Symbolic Meanings

- **Money become pure information.** The credit card is the image of money emptied of its commodity and work-storing functions, retaining only its function as translator and transmitter — money as "the movement of information itself." Where the coin was a portable store of congealed labour, the card stores nothing; it is a node in an information network. This is the chapter's instance of the vault-wide [[Material vs Immaterial]] arc: money's slide from commodity (eaten, drunk, worn) through paper to a credit-image with no commodity body at all. `Directly stated`

- **The electric reversal of money.** Read through McLuhan's [[Reversal of the Overheated Medium]] principle, the card is what money flips into once it is pushed past the threshold of the mechanical, print-literate "Time is money" economy into "the instant electric interdependence of all men on this planet." At electric speed "money begins to be less and less a means of storing or exchanging work and skill"; the visual, fragmenting form of money (coin, price, ledger) reverses into the integral, organic, informational form of credit. `Interpretive synthesis` (the reversal principle is McLuhan's, but he does not name "reversal" in this chapter; the connection is built from his own framework.)

- **A return to tribal money — the retribalizing image.** The card's deepest symbolic charge is regressive-as-progressive: it carries money *back* toward its pre-literate, undifferentiated character. "This trend toward an inclusive information is the kind of image represented by the credit card, and approaches once more the character of tribal money. For tribal society, not knowing the specialisms of job or of work, does not specialize money either." The card is thus an emblem of [[Detribalization and Retribalization]] in the monetary sphere — print-literate man specialized money to separate social functions; electric man, becoming an "information gatherer" in the [[The Global Village|global village]], de-specializes it again into inclusive, tribal-like flow. `Directly stated`

- **"The poor man's credit card."** The subtitle's irony folds the whole argument into a phrase: in the electric age "the richest man is reduced to having much the same entertainment, and even the same food and vehicles as the ordinary man," recalling tribal societies where, money buying little, "Rich and poor necessarily live in much the same manner." The card universalizes access the way tribal money did — credit-as-information is, in principle, everyone's. `Strongly implied`

## Context

The symbol recurs as the organizing figure of the chapter's final movement, where McLuhan turns from the history of money to its electric future. The chapter's spine is a developmental analogy between money and the other "extensions of man" — money "like writing" and "like the clock" separates and specializes social functions; see [[Money as a Medium]] and the metaphor [[The Midas Touch]] coined in the same chapter. The credit card is where that analogy completes itself: just as the [[The Phonetic Alphabet|phonetic alphabet]] and print drove the move "from the closed world of the tribe" to the "open society," electric media (the telegraph named beside the card) reverse the trajectory, putting "the very concept of money in jeopardy." The card is the small, concrete object onto which McLuhan loads that reversal — the everyday emblem of money dissolving into [[Information]]. It coexists with the chapter's argument note `Money is a specialist visual technology underwritten by print literacy` and the definition [[Money (McLuhan)]], standing as their image rather than their statement.
