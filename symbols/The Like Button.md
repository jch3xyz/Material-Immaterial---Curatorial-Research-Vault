# The Like Button

## Literal Meaning in the Source

For [[Byung-Chul Han]], the Facebook "Like" button is a concrete interface element that he uses as the emblem of the Society of Positivity. He notes that Facebook "has consistently refused to introduce a 'Dislike' button," treating this refusal as diagnostic rather than incidental. In the chapter on Exhibition, "Like" reappears as a paradigm of an "anesthetic" judgment of taste — a verdict that "requires no sustained contemplation."

## Possible Symbolic Meanings

- **Emblem of consensus-positivity.** "Like" stands in for the general consensus of a society that "avoids negativity in all forms because negativity makes communication stall." It is the affirmative click that lets the Smooth flow of communication continue uninterrupted.
- **The structural absence of "Dislike."** The missing "Dislike" button is not an oversight but a systemic exclusion: rejection is a form of [[Negativity]] that "cannot be exploited economically." The symbol is defined as much by what it forbids as by what it permits. See Dislike Button.
- **Anesthetic judgment.** "Like" symbolizes a verdict stripped of Aesthetic Contemplation — judgment reduced to reflex, "infection, abreaction, or reflex," with no lingering, no brokenness, no friction with the Other.
- **Acceleration token.** Each "Like" is a unit that speeds the circulation of information; "further communication occurs more quickly following 'Like' than 'Dislike.'" The button is thus an instrument of [[Acceleration]] within [[Hypercommunication]].
- **Currency of [[Exhibition Value]].** "Like" measures attention, the only value left when [[Cult Value]] has been displaced. It is the economic register through which the visage-on-display is priced.
- **Sign of The Same.** "Like" is the reply that matches like-to-like; it is the communicative form of "the inferno of the same," where the resistance of the Other is dissolved into a chain reaction of agreement.

## Context

The symbol appears at the close of Han's first chapter on [[Positivity]], as the culminating image of a society that has organized itself around the dismantling of [[Negativity]]. Han has just argued that the Pirate Party and "liquid democracy" represent a postpolitical mode of opinion without Ideology; "Like" is the consumer-facing counterpart of that political flattening — opinion reduced to a single positive gesture.

It returns in the chapter on Exhibition in a different register: not as the consensus mechanism of a depoliticized public, but as the aesthetic verdict of a viewer who can no longer dwell with an image. There, "Like" is paired with images that are "unambiguous — that is, pornographic" and lack "all brokenness, which would trigger physical or mental reflection." (Interpretive synthesis across the two chapters: the same button operates as both a political and an aesthetic anesthetic.)

The symbol recurs whenever Han needs a single concrete artifact to point at the otherwise diffuse logic of [[Positivity]], [[Transparency]], and [[Exhibition Value]]. It is the visible tip of an invisible systemic compulsion.
