# C3I

## Gloss

**C3I** stands for *command-control-communication-intelligence* — the United States military's own symbol for its operations theory. [[Donna Haraway]] adopts it in *A Cyborg Manifesto* as a found acronym: a piece of real military jargon she lifts intact and turns into the condensed emblem of the entire coded-control regime she calls [[The Informatics of Domination]]. In her hands C3I names not a technology but a *metaphor* into which "the fundamentals of this technology can be condensed" — the four-fold operation (commanding, controlling, communicating, gathering intelligence) by which a world translated into information is rendered governable.

`Interpretive synthesis` — that Haraway treats the military acronym *as a symbol* of the whole regime, rather than as a mere technical reference, is supported across the three chapters but is the system's framing.

## Literal Meaning in the Source

In "The Informatics of Domination," C3I arrives as the distillation of a coding-and-control theory of the world. Haraway has just argued that communications sciences translate "the world into a problem in coding," that "the key operation is determining the rates, directions, and probabilities of flow of a quantity called information," and that "the biggest threat to such power is interruption of communication." It is at this point that she names the acronym: "The fundamentals of this technology can be condensed into the meta phor C3I, command-control-communication-intelligence, the military's symbol for its operations theory." Haraway is explicit that she is borrowing *the military's own symbol* — the term is theirs; she repurposes it.

In the opening section, the acronym is given a dollar figure and a body: "modern war is a cyborg orgy, coded by C3I, command-control-communication-intelligence, an $84 bil lion item in 1984's U.S. defense budget." Here C3I is not abstract — it is a budget line, the literal cost of a regime, and the code by which "modern war" (a "cyborg orgy") is run. The same section later folds C3I into a list of the "hardest" sciences — "the realm of pure number, pure spirit, C3I, cryptography, and the preservation of potent secrets" — aligning it with cryptography and secrecy as the domain of "greatest boundary confusion."

## Possible Symbolic Meanings

- **The emblem of coded control.** C3I condenses the four verbs of the [[The Informatics of Domination|informatics of domination]] into one acronym: a world reduced to information becomes a world that can be *commanded*, *controlled*, *communicated*, and surveilled (*intelligence*). It is the regime's self-description, captured in its own initials.
- **Militarism as the ground of the cyborg.** Because the cyborg is "the illegitimate offspring of militarism and patriarchal capitalism," C3I marks the cyborg's paternity. "Modern war is a cyborg orgy, coded by C3I" makes the acronym the genetic code of [[The Cyborg]] itself.
- **The grid on which bodies are written.** In "Cyborgs: A Myth of Political Identity," C3I becomes a *grid* — bodies are "textualized... as code problems on the grid of C3I." The acronym names the coordinate system onto which biotechnology and microelectronics project the body as a "code problem."
- **The site of resistance.** Crucially, Haraway does not leave C3I as a closed apparatus. By breaking the acronym into its components, she finds leverage: "Feminist cyborg stories have the task of recoding communication and intelligence to subvert command and control." The C and I (communication, intelligence) are recoded to subvert the other C and C (command, control). C3I becomes the very material of an oppositional [[Cyborg Writing]].

## Context

C3I recurs across three movements of the manifesto, and the *symbol's meaning shifts* with each appearance:

1. **Naming (Ch. 1).** First introduced as the code of "modern war," a "cyborg orgy," with its 1984 defense-budget price tag — establishing it as a concrete, costly, militarized regime, not a figure of speech.
2. **Condensing (Ch. 3).** Reintroduced as the *condensation* of the whole coding theory of power — "the military's symbol for its operations theory" — at the heart of [[The Informatics of Domination]]. This is where C3I becomes the emblem for the regime as such.
3. **Recoding (Ch. 6).** Turned into the *site of resistance*. Having become the grid on which bodies are "textualized... as code problems," C3I is disassembled into recodable parts: feminist cyborg stories "recode communication and intelligence to subvert command and control." The symbol thus carries Haraway's signature irony — the same acronym that names domination supplies the terms of its own subversion.

This trajectory mirrors the manifesto's larger refusal to mourn "the comfortable old hierarchical dominations": one does not escape C3I by leaving it, but by retelling it from inside. C3I is the technical-military face of the coded regime that the vault tracks downstream into the surveillance-era worldview of [[Dataism]] and the voluntary self-coding of the [[Digital Panopticon]] — but in Haraway it is still, emphatically, a *war* acronym with a body count and a budget.
