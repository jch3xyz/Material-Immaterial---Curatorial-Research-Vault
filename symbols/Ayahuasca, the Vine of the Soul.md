# Ayahuasca, the Vine of the Soul

## Literal Meaning in the Source

In *Telematic Embrace*, ayahuasca — the Amazonian psychoactive brew Ascott names "the 'vine of the soul'" (and, botanically, the liana *Banisteriopsis caapi*) — appears literally as the **plant pole** of a sustained parallel between two technologies of consciousness. In "Weaving the Shamantic Web" (ch. 30, 1998) Ascott reports first-person fieldwork at both poles: virtual-reality laboratories on the one side and, on the other, the Kuikuru *pagés* (shamans) of the Xingu River Region of the Mato Grosso and his initiation into the Santo Daime ritual in Brasília. He frames the encounter as being "under the influence of the computer and of the plant, albeit an extremely powerful computer and a particularly potent plant (ayahuasca, the 'vine of the soul')" (`Directly stated`).

In "Art @ the Edge of the NET: the Future Will Be Moist!" (ch. 31, the "Moist Manifesto," 2000) the same plant returns not as fieldwork but as a **program**: the psychoactive plant is "a new but very ancient technology" to be *inserted* into the art at the edge of the Net, the seed of a "cyberbotany… around the instrumentality of such plants as the shamanic liana, ayahuasca (*Banisteriopsis caapi*), the vine of the soul." Here it is the basis of his project **ayahuascatec.net** (`Directly stated`).

## Possible Symbolic Meanings

- **Plant technology / the ancient technology of consciousness.** Ayahuasca symbolizes Ascott's claim that the computer is not the first instrument for navigating consciousness — that the shaman's brew is an older "technology" doing the same work. It lets him place the digital and the vegetal on a single continuum of consciousness-altering tools (`Directly stated`, ch. 31: "a new but very ancient technology, that of the psychoactive plant").
- **The figure of [[Double Consciousness]].** The "vine of the soul" is the emblem of being in two fields of experience at once — the shamanic trance mirrored by the VR user's simultaneous inhabitation of cyberspace and the material world. The plant is what makes the parallel *experiential* rather than merely analogical (`Strongly implied`).
- **The seed of [[Cyberbotany]] and the moist synthesis.** As the named substance of ayahuascatec.net, ayahuasca symbolizes the convergence of "vegetal reality and virtual reality" into "a new ontology" — the [[Moistmedia|moist]] union of artificial and natural systems, "hyperculture seen as horticulture" (`Directly stated`).
- **The re-enchanting / numinous pole of [[Technoetics]].** The vine stands for the frankly spiritual, shamanic register Ascott folds into the technoetic aesthetic ("technology plus mind"), against a purely engineering account of art and machines — an art-side analogue of the technological [[Re-enchantment]] O'Gieblyn diagnoses (`Interpretive synthesis`; the word "re-enchantment" is not in either chapter).

## Context

The symbol recurs across the two late, technoetic chapters that bracket Ascott's "moist" turn. **Ch. 30 is the fieldwork exposition**: ayahuasca is encountered, the trance described, and the structuring analogy between shamanic and telematic technologies of consciousness derived — the source of [[Double Consciousness]], the double gaze, psi-perception (the shamanic analogue of [[Cyberception]]), and the title figure [[The Shamantic Web]] (shamanic + semantic). **Ch. 31 is the cyberbotany insertion**: the same plant is re-deployed as an explicit future program for art at the edge of the Net, named with its Latin binomial and tied to ayahuascatec.net, the Santo Daime, and the União do Vegetal. The plant is thus both an experience Ascott had and a tool he proposes — the hinge by which the shamanic enters his theory of art (`Directly stated`).
