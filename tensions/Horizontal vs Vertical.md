# Horizontal vs Vertical

## The Poles

- **Horizontal** — the table-top, the arena, the altar: a surface "seen all round without top or bottom," offering a "bird's-eye or holistic viewpoint," that mediates transaction between people across or upon it. For Ascott it is "always the matrix of transformation and transfer, of change and exchange," the "mystical matrix," and it "reflect[s] the cosmos." It "faces up, not only to us, but to the whole universe."
- **Vertical** — the picture, the painting, the mirror, the monitor screen on the wall: a plane that "demands a fixed viewing point," "immobilises the viewer," "looks back only at us," and is "always totally alienating." It reflects "only [man] himself, an isolated viewpoint," and is "the archetypal convention of a whole culture of separation, competition, self-love."

## Why It Matters

For Ascott this is not a formal preference about how to hang work but a spatial diagnosis of an entire civilization's "conception of its relation to the universe." He reads the Renaissance — "the advent of perspective and its principal raison d'être" — as the moment "the horizontal plane of the table-top was abandoned in favour of the vertical plane of the rectangle on the wall," a switch whose purpose "was to demonstrate man's isolation from the universe, to close down mystical transactions" `Directly stated`. The vertical plane thus becomes, for him, the genealogy of "the values of alienation, competition, and narcissism" that have "dominated our society": geometry as the carrier of an ideology `Directly stated`.

What is at stake, then, is whether art situates the viewer as an isolated, mirrored consumer of "a fixed message" or as a participant in an all-round, "holistic consciousness." The opposition is the spatial form of Ascott's wider [[Connection vs Narcissism]]: the vertical mirror stages the solitary, self-loving viewer ("Mirror, mirror on the wall, who is the fairest of them all"), while the horizontal arena stages reciprocal transaction. It also gives him a sharp critical instrument: he argues "the bankruptcy and irrelevance of 'video art' are assured just as long as the convention of the monitor screen on the vertical plane is maintained," extending the diagnosis from painting to the new electronic media `Directly stated`. Understanding the tension is what lets Ascott reframe the [[Cybernetic Art Matrix]] and [[Telematic Art]] not as content delivered on a screen but as a horizontal "creative arena" — and what binds his art-historical claim to a cosmological and ethical one.

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Roy Ascott]] in [[Telematic Embrace]] — the opposition is the spine of ch.10 ("Table," 1975), which narrates the Renaissance shift from the horizontal "mystical matrix" to the vertical "mirror-on-the-wall," and recurs in ch.13 ("Towards a Field Theory for Postmodernist Art"), where Ascott contrasts "the painting on the wall" that "looks back only at us" with "the arena and the table-top" that "have a more cosmic outlook."

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

Ascott does not hold the poles in symmetrical balance — he treats the horizontal as a recovery and the vertical as a historical aberration to be superseded ("As those values recede, so does the importance of painting as a 'mirror-on-the-wall' signifier") `Directly stated`. He concedes in ch.13 that "the vertical surface can provide sufficient possibility for ambiguity and, thus, greater viewer participation," but immediately reasserts the greater "potential in the open-endedness ... of unfixed, changeable objects and elements on the horizontal surface" `Directly stated`. The tension is therefore offered as **dissolvable through media change**: re-orient the plane — "Only when video is created to be viewed on the horizontal, table-top plane ... will it have the possibility of contributing to our holistic consciousness" — and the alienation built into the vertical convention is undone `Strongly implied`. Whether re-orienting a surface can by itself carry the ethical weight Ascott assigns it — whether narcissism is really an artifact of geometry rather than of culture using geometry — is a connection he asserts more than demonstrates, and so remains open `Interpretive synthesis`.
