# I-Tiredness vs We-Tiredness

The redemptive opposition that closes *The Burnout Society*. Reading Peter Handke's *Essay on Tiredness*, Han stages two utterly different tirednesses: the solitary, divisive, mute, blind, violent, world-destroying tiredness of the exhausted ego — and an eloquent, seeing, reconciliatory, world-trusting tiredness that founds community. These are the two faces of the single keystone concept [[Fundamental Tiredness]]: the first is the tiredness of [[Positivity|positive potency]] (exhaustion), the second the tiredness of [[Negative Potency]] (`Directly stated` — Han names them "I-tiredness" / "we-tiredness" and "the tiredness of positive potency" / "tiredness of negative potency").

## The Poles

- **I-Tiredness** — "solitary tiredness," the tiredness of an exhausted ego. It is the symptomatic tiredness of the [[Achievement Society]]: it separates and isolates, strikes one "mute and blind," destroys language, proximity, and all that is shared, and turns violent. Han glosses it as "worldless, world-destroying tiredness" — the tiredness of *positive potency*, which makes one incapable of doing anything (`Directly stated`).
- **We-Tiredness** — "eloquent, seeing, reconciliatory tiredness." By loosening the strictures of the ego it opens a "between," shifts the gravity of being from the I to the world ("Less I means more world"), and restores duality: one sees and is seen, touches and is touched. It is "tiredness that trusts in the world," and it founds [[Community of Singularities|a community that requires neither belonging nor relation]]. It is the tiredness of *negative potency* — of "not-to" — and it inspires rather than incapacitates (`Directly stated`).

## Why It Matters

The whole therapeutic arc of the book turns on this single hinge. Han has diagnosed the [[Achievement Society]] as a regime of excess [[Positivity]] that drives the auto-exploiting subject to exhaustion. The closing move does not prescribe more rest of the same kind; it distinguishes *two* tirednesses so that the cure is not the disease intensified. I-tiredness is the exhaustion the achievement society produces — more of the same isolating burnout. We-tiredness is its inversion: a tiredness rehabilitated as a "singular capacity," a [[Negative Potency|capacity of not-to]] that re-opens the world, restores the [[Negativity|negativity]] that hyperpositive life has flattened, and makes a non-coercive community thinkable. Reading the two as poles of one concept is what lets [[Fundamental Tiredness]] be both the name of the malady and the name of its redemption (`Interpretive synthesis` — Han does not draw a single diagram, but the chapter explicitly opposes the two and ties both to fundamental tiredness).

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Byung-Chul Han]] in [[The Burnout Society]] — frames the closing chapter "The Society of Tiredness" around Handke's opposition, recasting it in his own vocabulary of positive vs. negative potency and reading the redemptive "we-tiredness" as the seed of a coming "society of tiredness."
- [[Peter Handke]] in *Essay on Tiredness* — the source text Han reads throughout. Handke is the one who names "I-tiredness" and "we-tiredness," "divisive tiredness," "tiredness that trusts in the world," and "fundamental tiredness," and who supplies the Pentecost, Odysseus/Nausicaä, Orpheus, and Dutch-still-life images Han draws on.

## Related Concepts

- [[Fundamental Tiredness]] — the keystone concept whose two poles this tension names.
- [[Negative Potency]] — the "not-to" that distinguishes inspiring we-tiredness from incapacitating I-tiredness.
- [[Community of Singularities]] — the "all in one," the friendly *and*, that we-tiredness founds.
- [[Achievement Society]] — the regime that produces solitary I-tiredness.
- [[Positivity]] / [[Negativity]] — I-tiredness as the tiredness of positive potency; we-tiredness as that of negative potency.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

The tension is not meant to be dissolved into one term but held as a *conversion*: the same word, "tired," names both the worldless exhaustion of the ego and the world-trusting calm that opens onto the Other. Han's own gesture is a synthesis under one concept — [[Fundamental Tiredness]] — where the destructive pole is the privation and the reconciliatory pole the fulfillment. Whether the conversion from I- to we-tiredness can actually be willed, or whether it only arrives as a kind of grace (Han's "immanent religion of tiredness," the Sabbath, the Pentecost), is left open: the book ends by *imagining* a "society of tiredness," not by specifying how the achievement society's burnout becomes the community's repose (`Interpretive synthesis`).
