# Reductionism vs Holism

## The Poles

- **Reductionism** — the science that proceeds by analysis: tearing nature "up into little pieces," operating through linear systems, rigid causality, and analytical certitude. It "encounter[s] all things through a rigorous story line," the literate/visual-culture penalty McLuhan diagnosed.
- **Holism** — the anti-reductionist, intuitive, metaphor-driven sciences that attempt instead to "unify it into wholly new configurations": Rupert Sheldrake's morphic resonance and morphogenetic fields, James Lovelock's Gaia hypothesis of an organic, self-regulating planet. These offer "a vision of reciprocity and connectivity in the world to which the telematic art of our time aspires."

## Why It Matters

This is the epistemic opposition that undergirds Ascott's whole connectivist paradigm. For Ascott, the move into the twenty-first century is a move out of reductionist certitude into "a more playful interaction with non-linear systems." The shift is not merely scientific but esthetic and perceptual: as our interface with the world becomes computer-mediated and "virtualised," we stop encountering things through a single rigorous story line and begin navigating layered, many-trajectoried meaning. The holistic, anti-reductionist sciences (Sheldrake, Lovelock) are the natural-philosophical correlates of the connectivist network — they model the same reciprocity that telematic art seeks to enact. The tension matters because it names what `[[Connectivism]]` is *for* and *against*: the connective network is precisely the cultural form that abandons reductive analysis for holistic, emergent interaction. `Interpretive synthesis` for the framing; the pole content is `Directly stated`.

Crucially, Ascott does not stage the two poles as simply good-versus-bad. He notes that *both* reductionist and holistic science have "irreversibly altered the common view of nature" — the one by analysis, the other by unification — and that classical, god-given nature "will be dead" by the twenty-first century either way (`Directly stated`). The holistic pole is the one telematic art aspires to, but the supersession of pastoral nature is mutual.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

Ascott does not so much resolve the tension as *displace* it. He treats both reductionist and holistic science as twin agents that have together killed classical nature, then bets the future on the holistic pole — the "science of metaphor, anti-reductionist, holistic, and intuitive" — as the one whose vision of reciprocity and connectivity telematic art can realise. The synthesis, such as it is, is the connective network itself: a form that, being non-linear and inconclusive by design, cannot be reduced without being betrayed. The opposition therefore remains productively open — held, not dissolved — as the live epistemic stake of the connectivist program. `Interpretive synthesis`.
