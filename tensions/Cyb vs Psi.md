# Cyb vs Psi

The organizing opposition of Roy Ascott's 1970 essay "The Psibernetic Arch": the two apparently opposed spheres that a coming **psibernetic** culture would span — **cybernetics** (technology, provision, prediction-control) on one side, **parapsychology** (telepathy, prevision, ESP) on the other. Ascott calls them "the west and east sides of the mind" and proposes that a rainbow arch — a general-systems approach — unites them into a single whole system of prediction, control, and communication.

## The Poles

- **Cyb** — cybernetics, technology, provision (prediction-control). The science of feedback, organisation, and entropy; applied cybernetics is "efficient and reliable" and "universal," but it is "entropic" and, "due to the limitations of media," can never attain perfect communication absolutely. The pole of the engineered, the planned, the mediated.
- **Psi** — parapsychology, telepathy, prevision. Extra-sensory perception (telepathy, clairvoyance, psychokinesis) framed as "the most perfect form of communication and social feedback imaginable" precisely because "it involves no media" — but its "manifestation is erratic": ESP is "instant, but sporadic." The pole of the medialess, the anticipatory, the divinatory.

The contrast is crisp and deliberately symmetrical: *Directly stated* — "ESP is instant, but sporadic. Cybernetics is universal, but entropic." Each pole has the defect of the other's virtue.

## Why It Matters

The tension is the load-bearing structure of [[Psibernetics]], Ascott's neologism fusing *psi* and *cyb*. It frames the whole "Visionary Theories of Art, Technology, and Consciousness" program of *Telematic Embrace* at its 1970 seed-point: cybernetic, behavioural art (the [[The Cybernetic Vision]] / feedback pole, the *other* 1960s pole to Burnham's [[Systems Esthetics]]) is here wedded not yet to telecommunications networks but to *psychic* communication. Holding both poles as one "whole system of prediction, control, and communication" is what lets Ascott treat ESP as the limit-case of cybernetic [[Feedback Loops]] — "more instant than instant electronic communications, because it involves no media" — and so anticipates his later move from feedback to [[Network Consciousness]] and the network-as-prosthesis-of-mind. The cyb side connects to the vault's [[Information]] / [[Pattern and Randomness]] (organisation vs entropy) themes; the psi side's stronger claims (an "instant global sympathy, a galactic sympathy perhaps") remain Ascott's avowedly visionary register and are labelled *Speculative connection* throughout.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

Ascott does not leave the poles opposed: the essay's entire move is **synthesis under a general-systems frame** — the "rainbow arch" that lets both be investigated as "the two aspects of a whole system of prediction, control, and communication." The proposed resolution is [[Psibernetics]] itself, a culture and an art that arches across both. But the synthesis is *programmatic and predictive*, not achieved: it is offered as a prediction ("perhaps we should call it the psibernetic era"), and its psi pole carries claims — galactic sympathy, out-of-time behaviour as "the only real hope of travel around the galaxy" — that stay firmly in Ascott's visionary mode (*Speculative connection*). The tension is therefore best read as **held, not dissolved**: a deliberately unstable arch in which the medialess perfection of psi and the universal-but-entropic reliability of cyb each supply what the other lacks. (Interpretive synthesis on the held-tension reading; the synthesis-as-aim is *Directly stated*.)
