# Job vs Role

In the Telephone chapter McLuhan draws a sharp opposition between two ways a person can be socially defined: the **job** and the **role**. The job is the unit of the mechanical and print order — a "delegated," fragmentary, specialist function within a "pyramidal structure of job-division and description and delegated powers." The role is what electric media *restore*: a "nonvisual," inclusive, collective investiture that "delegated power" had displaced. The hinge is the telephone, which "by-pass[es] all hierarchical arrangements" and so dissolves the delegated chain of command; "in place of delegated power, there tends to appear / again with electric media, the role."

This is a social/political specialization of the vault's master opposition [[Fragmentation vs Wholeness]] (and the [[Mechanical vs Electric]] schema it sits under): the job is fragmentation applied to human function — a person split into a specialist task and granted only borrowed, lineal authority — while the role is wholeness applied to function — "the whole person" reinvested with collective, "nonvisual character." McLuhan's two emblems are the **king/emperor** as "the collective ego of all the private egos of their subjects" and the **movie star** as the "jobless image" on whom the electric culture confers "a mystic role."

## The Poles

- **Job** — The delegated, fragmentary, specialist function of the mechanical/print order. Authority here is "delegated," and "delegated authority is lineal, visual, hierarchical": the office-holder must "always get clearance from the chain-of-command" and exists as one fragment in "the pyramidal structure of job-division and description and delegated powers." The job is the form that Western "freedom" took — "the explosive, the divisive, advancing the separation of the individual from the state" — and it is what "Western man" still uses to "keep individuals in delegated jobs."

- **Role** — The collective, inclusive, nonvisual investiture that electric media restore. With electric media "a person can now be reinvested with all kinds of nonvisual character," because the chain-of-command pyramid cannot survive the telephone's speed and depth-involvement. The role's authority is "the authority of knowledge," which "is nonlineal, nonvisual, and inclusive." Its archetypes are the king and emperor "legally endowed to act as the collective ego of all the private egos of their subjects," and the movie star — a "jobless image" given "a mystic role" as a "collective embodiment[] of the multitudinous private lives of their subjects."

## Why It Matters

This tension is where McLuhan's media schema becomes a thesis about *political form and personhood*. The two modes of authority that pair with the poles are stated flatly: "Delegated authority is lineal, visual, hierarchical. The authority of knowledge is nonlineal, nonvisual, and inclusive." The job belongs to the first; the role to the second. Because the telephone "demands complete participation" and involves people "in depth," the "chain-oi-command pyramids cannot obtain support from electric technology" — the institutional scaffolding of the job loses its medium. What returns is not anarchy but the role: a person reinvested with collective character, with restraint now coming "not by the separation of powers, but by a pluralism of centers" (the political signature of electric [[Decentralization]]).

The stakes for the vault's concerns are twofold. First, the opposition continues the [[Center vs Margin]] reversal: delegated power "radiat[es] out to remote margins," whereas in an electric structure "there are... no margins," so "there can... be dialogue only among centers and among equals." Second, it touches embodiment and the immaterial directly — the role is "nonvisual," and the movie star's "jobless image" is precisely a *de-jobbed*, dematerialized public body that nonetheless carries an intensely collective charge. McLuhan reads the cult of the movie star as a half-conscious return of an archaic, tribal-collective mode of personhood that print-culture specialism had banished, even as Western man "still manages to keep individuals in delegated jobs." It is a smaller, social-political instance of the same retribalizing pull described in [[Detribalization and Retribalization]] and [[The Global Village]].

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Marshall McLuhan]] in [[Understanding Media]] — The job/role opposition is stated explicitly in Chapter 27 (The Telephone), as the chapter's culminating point about electric authority. McLuhan builds it from the telephone's destruction of "delegated chain-of-command authority" ("It is not feasible to exercise delegated authority by telephone"), pairs the two kinds of authority ("Delegated authority is lineal, visual, hierarchical. The authority of knowledge is nonlineal, nonvisual, and inclusive"), and lands on the reversal: "in place of delegated power, there tends to appear / again with electric media, the role." The king/emperor and the movie star are his two illustrations of the restored, collective role.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

McLuhan presents the relation as *reversal in progress*, not synthesis, and explicitly as **incomplete and unstable**: "So far, Western man has encountered the restoration of the role only tentatively. He still manages to keep individuals in delegated jobs." The two modes therefore coexist in friction during the changeover — the institutional job-pyramid persists even as the electric medium hollows out its authority — and the most visible site of the restored role (the cult of the movie star) is described as something done "somnambulistically," half-consciously, by a culture "abandon[ing] our Western traditions" without grasping what it is doing. There is no proposed fusion of job and role; rather, the job is the residue of a receding order and the role its electric successor, with the period of overlap marked by anxiety and misrecognition. `Interpretive synthesis` (that McLuhan frames this as an unfinished reversal rather than a reconcilable balance): the text states the restoration is "only tentative[]" and ongoing, with delegated jobs still in force.
