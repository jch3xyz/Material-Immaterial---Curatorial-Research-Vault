# Awareness vs Evasion

## The Poles

- **Awareness** — Human consciousness, self-awareness, and the capacity for premeditation are the necessary condition for autonomous judgment and self-regulation. Awareness is what makes agreement, disagreement, participation, withdrawal, resistance, and collaboration possible at all. It is the bulwark against persuasion and the literal meaning of *autonomy* ("regulation by the self").
- **Evasion** — Surveillance capitalism's economies of action require behavior modification that *bypasses* human awareness: subliminal cues, tuning, nudges, and emotional contagion that operate "without their awareness." For Zuboff this evasion is "neither accidental nor incidental" but structurally essential — which means consciousness itself becomes an obstacle to surveillance revenues.

## Why It Matters

This is the opposition behind one of Zuboff's most pointed claims: that under surveillance capitalism *human consciousness itself is a threat to surveillance revenues* (`Directly stated`). The argument inverts the usual liberal assumption that awareness is the citizen's protection. If behavior modification at scale works only when the target does not know it is happening, then the commercial imperative is to keep the target unaware — to engineer action through `[[Telestimulation]]` rather than persuade a deliberating subject. The stakes are the foundations of freedom: Zuboff aligns "self-regulation," "self-determination," and "autonomy" with the philosophers' "freedom of will," and frames every assault on awareness as an assault on autonomy itself, shifting the locus of control over the future tense from "I will" to "You will" (`Directly stated`).

The tension also distinguishes surveillance capitalism's power from older forms. Where disciplinary and persuasive power addressed a conscious subject to be convinced or coerced, evasion-based power sidesteps the subject entirely — a structural resonance with Han's `[[Smart Power]]`, which likewise operates not against the will but seductively beneath it (`Interpretive synthesis`).

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Shoshana Zuboff]] in [[The Age of Surveillance Capitalism]] (ch. 10, "Make Them Dance") — Facebook's emotional-contagion and voter-mobilization experiments succeeded precisely because "the evasion of individual and group awareness was critical." Zuboff generalizes this into a structural law: individual awareness is "the enemy of telestimulation," because awareness is the precondition for the self-regulating choices (resistance, withdrawal, participation) that economies of action must foreclose.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

Zuboff does not resolve the tension; she sharpens it into a warning. The two poles cannot be productively held together because evasion is, by her account, *constitutive* of surveillance capitalism's growth — it is not a regrettable side effect that better practices could mitigate, but the very condition of behavior modification "under the authority of the prediction imperative." The implied path is political and epistemic rather than synthetic: awareness — premeditation, commitment, "rights consciousness," democratic contest — is named as the bulwark, but it is precisely what the architecture is built to neutralize. The tension therefore remains open as a struggle, with consciousness cast as both the last line of defense and the designated target. `Interpretive synthesis`
