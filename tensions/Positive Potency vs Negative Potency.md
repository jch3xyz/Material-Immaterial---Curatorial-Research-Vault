# Positive Potency vs Negative Potency

## The Poles

- **Positive Potency** — the power *to do* something; the faculty of the Can. Useful and necessary, but when absolutized to the exclusion of all else it collapses into "fatal hyperactivity," and when it exhausts itself it leaves one *incapable*.
- **Negative Potency** — the power *not to do*, the power to say no. Not mere impotence (the inability to act) but a sovereign capacity that "reaches beyond" positivity; it is the condition of contemplation, of pausing, of seeing, and — in its tiredness — of inspiration.

This is the faculty-level opposition that underwrites *The Burnout Society*'s therapeutic turn. Where the broader axis [[Positivity vs Negativity]] is Han's *diagnostic* lever (society's excess positivity, its loss of negativity), Positive vs Negative Potency names the two *capacities of the subject* whose imbalance produces burnout and whose rebalancing offers a cure. Cross-link both: this tension is the human-faculty correlate of that diagnostic axis.

## Why It Matters

Han locates the pathology of the achievement subject not in too much restriction but in a runaway *Can*. "If one had only the power to do (something) and no power not to do, it would lead to fatal hyperactivity" (Directly stated, ch.6). The achievement society reads being-more-active as being-freer, but Han argues the opposite: "Paradoxically, hyperactivity represents an extremely passive form of doing, which bars the possibility of free action. It is based on positive potency that has been made absolute to the exclusion of all else" (Directly stated, ch.6). Burnout is thus not the failure of positive potency but its *absolutization* — and the cure is not more doing but the recovery of the negative power of not-to.

The stakes are also a theory of attention and thought. Without negative potency the senses "stand utterly at the mercy of rushing, intrusive stimuli," thinking "scatters among endless series of objects," and reflection becomes impossible because pure positivity "only permits anticipation and thinking ahead" (Directly stated, ch.6). Negative potency is therefore the enabling condition of the [[Vita Contemplativa]], of the "pedagogy of seeing," and — crucially for the diagnosis — of any genuine turn to the Other, which "presupposes the negativity of an interruption."

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Byung-Chul Han]] in *The Burnout Society* — defines the two forms of potency in ch.6 ("The Pedagogy of Seeing") and, in ch.8 ("The Society of Tiredness"), maps them onto two kinds of tiredness: the exhausting tiredness of positive potency that makes one *incapable*, versus the inspiring tiredness of negative potency, "namely of not-to." He attributes the "power to say no" formulation to Nietzsche and grounds negativity's life-giving role in Hegel.

The pole-distinction draws explicitly on Friedrich Nietzsche (the "power to say no," the "excluding instincts") and Hegel (negativity as "precisely what keeps existence alive"); both remain forward-stub figures in the vault.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

The tension is **not symmetrical and not meant to be dissolved by abolishing one pole.** Negative potency does not replace positive potency; it conditions and frames it. Han is explicit that negative potency "differs from simple impotence" — impotence is "merely the opposite of positive potency," still positive because tied to a something it cannot do, whereas negative potency "reaches beyond such positivity" (Directly stated, ch.6). The synthesis Han gestures toward is therefore a restored *ratio* between the two faculties: a subject that retains the power to do but recovers the power not-to, so that action does not "sink to the level of laboring" and tiredness can inspire rather than exhaust. The "society of tiredness" is the utopian name for this rebalanced order. Whether such a rebalancing is achievable within the achievement society — or only nameable against it — Han leaves open; the figure of the "Pentecostal company . . . tired to a man" is offered as a prefiguration, not a program.
