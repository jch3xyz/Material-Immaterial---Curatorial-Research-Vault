# Eros vs Sexuality

## The Poles

- **Eros** — love as a *Two scene*, an encounter with the **wholly Other** whose otherness cannot be assimilated. It is the site of the **event**: a rupture in the order of the Habitual and the Same, marked by negativity, distance, secrecy, and the veil. In Plato's tripartite soul it exceeds mere pleasure-desire (*epithumia*) and works through *thumos* — the spiritedness that "radically breaks with convention and inaugurates a new state of affairs." Eros calls the self into question and de-narcissifies it.
- **Sexuality (and its endpoint, pornography)** — the *One scene*, the "love of the One for another One." Its object is a consumable, self-identical thing that reproduces the **Same**. Grounded in *epithumia*, it lacks the negativity of the Other; no event can occur for it. Pushed to its limit as porn — "bare life on display" — it is auto-affective, narcissifying, and finally annihilates even sexuality itself.

## Why It Matters

This is [[Byung-Chul Han]]'s book-wide diagnosis in *The Agony of Eros*: that contemporary culture is liquidating love not by prohibiting sex but by *profaning eros into sexuality* — "if love is profaned into sexuality, as is happening today, the universal quality of eros vanishes" (`Directly stated`). The stake is the survival of the **Other** as such. Eros is the one experience structured around a negativity that a positivized, transparent, achievement-driven society cannot metabolize; sexuality is what remains when that negativity is drained off, leaving a smooth field of self-identical, consumable objects. Han treats this not as a private erotic matter but as a *political* one: without eros there is no *thumos*, and without *thumos* there is no communal "we," so "neoliberalism is depoliticizing society in general—and not least of all, by replacing eros with sexuality and pornography" (`Directly stated`).

The tension also secures a distinction Han insists on throughout: eros is **not** the same as *epithumia* / fleshly desire. Collapsing eros into sexuality is therefore not a change of degree but the destruction of a different order of experience — the shift from a relation that ruptures the Same to a transaction that reproduces it (`Strongly implied`).

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Byung-Chul Han]] in [[The Agony of Eros]] — the sole source. Chapter 7 ("The Politics of Eros") states the opposition most sharply: "The negativity of transformation or the wholly Other is foreign to sexuality. The sexual object always remains self-identical." Chapter 5 ("Porn") names pornography as "the antagonist of eros" and traces sexuality's terminal form. Han routes the *Two scene* / *One scene* vocabulary through Alain Badiou's *In Praise of Love* and reads porn against Baudrillard, Agamben, and Bataille.

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

Han holds the poles as a genuine **antagonism**, not a spectrum to be balanced: pornography "annihilates even sexuality," so the movement runs one way, toward the liquidation of eros, unless something interrupts it. He offers no synthesis in which eros and sexuality reconcile. Instead he points to a *restoration* of the erotic through fidelity to the event — the surrealists' "reinvention of love," eros as "the revolutionary yearning for an entirely different way of loving and another kind of society." The tension is thus **unresolved by design**: eros is not the moderation of sexuality but its outside, recoverable only as a rupture that re-admits the Other, distance, and the veil into a world that has grown "more naked and more obscene" (`Interpretive synthesis`).
