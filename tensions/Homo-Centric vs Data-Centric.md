# Homo-Centric vs Data-Centric

## The Poles

- **Homo-Centric** — the humanist axis, on which the human being and human experience are the source of all value and meaning. Value originates inside the person; the universe is infused with meaning by human feeling and judgement. Its command, in Harari's phrasing, is "Listen to your feelings!"
- **Data-Centric** — the Dataist axis, on which value is determined not by experience but by contribution to data processing. Meaning migrates out of the human interior and into the flow of information; the algorithm, not the self, becomes the authority that interprets the person to themselves. Its command is "Listen to the algorithms! They know how you feel."

## Why It Matters

This tension names what [[Yuval Noah Harari]] calls a "religious revolution" — a structural repetition, one rung lower, of the move humanism itself made. Harari's claim is symmetrical: just as eighteenth-century humanism dethroned God by relocating the source of value from the divine to the human, twenty-first-century [[Dataism]] threatens to dethrone the human by relocating that source again, from the human to the data flow. The human is positioned to be sidelined *by Dataism* in exactly the way God was sidelined *by humanism* — not refuted, but made peripheral to where value is now held to reside (`Directly stated`).

What is at stake is the seat of authority over the self. Under the homo-centric view, knowing yourself means turning inward — feelings are "complex algorithms honed by evolution," the trustworthy voice of millions of ancestors. Under the data-centric view, your feelings are merely an "outdated biochemical algorithm" that newer, external algorithms outperform; self-knowledge is outsourced to Google and Facebook, which "not only know exactly how you feel" but know "a million other things about you that you hardly suspect" (`Directly stated`). The tension therefore stages the displacement of the human interior as the privileged site of meaning — the bridge between humanism and a possible [[Posthumanism]] in which "Homo sapiens will vanish" as a value-bearing category.

## Authors and Books That Discuss It

- [[Yuval Noah Harari]] in *Homo Deus: A Brief History of Tomorrow*, ch. 14 "The Data Religion" — frames the shift homo-centric → data-centric as the third in a sequence of value-revolutions (deo-centric → homo-centric → data-centric), each one sidelining the prior source of authority. Harari does not endorse Dataism; he stresses it "is neither liberal nor humanist" yet "isn't anti-humanist" — it simply denies that human experiences are "intrinsically valuable" (`Directly stated`).

## Possible Synthesis or Unresolved Status

Harari presents this as an open and possibly irreversible historical drift rather than a stable opposition to be balanced. He is careful to mark it as a *possibility*, not a prophecy: Dataism "may sideline humans," the revolution "will probably take a few decades, if not a century or two," and he closes the chapter with critique — that it is "doubtful whether life can really be reduced to data flows," and that "we have no idea how or why data flows could produce consciousness and subjective experiences" (`Directly stated`). The tension is thus held open: data-centrism is portrayed as the ascendant paradigm, but one whose central reduction (organisms-as-algorithms) remains contestable, leaving the homo-centric pole undefeated rather than refuted. There is no synthesis offered; the two poles are staged as successive religions competing for the seat of value (`Interpretive synthesis`).
