# About — Dispersion (Seth Price)

**Work:** *Dispersion*, an essay by the artist Seth Price.
**Text dated:** 2002– (ongoing); **this PDF edition:** 2007.
**Source file:** `Dispersion_2007.pdf` (20 pages, 582×690 pt, set in Futura).

## Structure
*Dispersion* is a **single continuous essay** with no chapters, sections, or
internal headings. It has therefore been extracted into one content file,
`01 Dispersion.md`, holding the complete essay body (PDF pages 4–17) followed by
the colophon (page 18). Pages 1–3 are the cover, a blank, and a half-title; pages
19–20 are blank.

The essay is laid out in a magazine-style format in which image captions are
interleaved into the running text. The body prose has been reflowed into clean
reading order; captions and the two set-off epigraphs/pull-quotes have been
separated out from the body flow.

## Extraction notes / caveats
- **Figures are images, not text.** Every artwork/illustration reproduced in the
  essay is a raster image with no extractable text layer. Each is represented in
  place by a marker of the form `*[Figure — <caption>]*` carrying its original
  italic credit line. There are 21 such figure captions.
- Two figures contain text that is *part of the image* and so is **not** captured
  here (no transcription has been fabricated):
  - p14, *i-D Magazine. 2002.* — a scanned magazine clipping reviewing Price's
    "Game Heaven" CD (text rasterized in the image).
  - p12, the *Daniel Pearl Video* still — its Arabic caption is part of the image.
- **Epigraphs / pull-quotes** are rendered as block quotes:
  Marcel Broodthaers (opening epigraph), an uncredited hermeneutics quotation
  (p8), and Benjamin Buchloh (p16).
- **Typesetting fixes applied during extraction:** drop-cap initials re-joined to
  their words; spurious intra-word spaces from Futura kerning removed via
  character-gap word reconstruction (e.g. "histor y"→"history",
  "obser ved"→"observed"); line-break hyphenation resolved by a dictionary rule
  that joins soft-broken words ("prac-tice"→"practice") while preserving genuine
  compounds (kept: *as-if*, *tomb-like*, *ever-sharpening*, *profit-driven*);
  overprinted/doubled glyphs de-duplicated; inline italic work-titles preserved as
  markdown emphasis.
- **No symbol-font mojibake** exists in this PDF's text layer, so zero
  "content not captured" gibberish markers were emitted (only the figure-caption
  markers described above).
- Text was extracted from the file on disk (pdfminer.six, geometry- and
  font-aware); reading order was verified paragraph-by-paragraph against the page
  layout.
